# Purpose

This guide defines the **narrative voice, tone boundaries, and design philosophy** of the *Coinspiratorium* campaign.

It exists so AI systems (or collaborators) can generate:

* quests
* NPCs
* dungeons
* items
* dialogue

that **feel like they belong in the same world**.

This campaign intentionally blends:

* fantasy adventure
* criminal enterprise
* absurd comedy
* cosmic tragedy

Maintaining this balance is essential.

---

# Core Tone

The campaign tone can be summarized as:

```
Crime Comedy
+
Planar Weirdness
+
Sudden Existential Horror
```

It should feel like:

* **Planescape**
* **Guardians of the Galaxy**
* **Breaking Bad**
* **Discworld (at times)**

But the setting must always retain **real stakes**.

---

# Tone Balance Rule

Each piece of content should include at least **two of the following elements**:

### Humor

Examples:

* bureaucratic crime organizations
* magical branding consultants
* absurd magical commerce

---

### Wonder

Examples:

* surreal magical environments
* divine artifacts
* ancient planar ruins

---

### Threat

Examples:

* dangerous monsters
* unstable magic
* Veezy's wrath

---

### Tragedy

Examples:

* cursed artifacts
* forgotten civilizations
* failed destinies

---

# Humor Style

Humor should be:

* clever
* character-driven
* situational

Avoid:

* slapstick
* parody
* modern internet jokes

Good humor often comes from **absurd professionalism** applied to illegal or magical things.

Example:

```
Snitch Ray delivers a formal memo regarding overdue tribute payments.
```

---

# Narrative Voice

Descriptions should feel:

* vivid
* slightly theatrical
* occasionally poetic

Example tone:

```
The vault door opens with the slow confidence of something that knows it cannot be forced.
```

Avoid overly plain narration.

---

# Artifact Philosophy

Artifacts should almost always carry **symbolic meaning**.

They should represent ideas such as:

* broken promises
* lost potential
* divine mistakes
* corrupted ambition

Artifacts are rarely just "powerful items."

They are **stories in physical form**.

---

# Dungeon Philosophy

Dungeons should feel like **intentional places**, not random monster containers.

Players should be able to answer:

* Who built this?
* Why was it built?
* Why does the artifact exist?

A dungeon is effectively **a story told through architecture**.

---

# Enemy Design

Enemies should feel like they belong to the environment.

Examples:

Good:

* dragon constructs guarding a hoard vault
* angelic statues protecting a relic
* cursed scholars trapped in a library

Bad:

* random monster encounters unrelated to the dungeon theme

---

# The Veezy Rule

Whenever Veezy appears in a scene:

1. He should dominate the conversation.
2. His dialogue should contain memorable lines.
3. The players should feel both amused and uneasy.

The moment Veezy stops joking, the tone should shift dramatically.

---

# Offledger Style

Offledger should feel like a **fantasy black-market metropolis**.

Everything is:

* loud
* colorful
* slightly illegal
* extremely profitable

Examples of Offledger details:

* shops selling bottled emotions
* merchants trading in cursed relics
* magical debt collectors

---

# Moral Ambiguity

Players should frequently encounter situations where:

* success requires morally questionable actions
* the artifact's existence is tragic
* Veezy's goals are understandable but dangerous

This ambiguity keeps the campaign interesting.

---

# The Crown Infinite

The Crown Infinite represents the campaign's central philosophical question:

**What happens when ambition is given unlimited power?**

The device embodies:

* influence
* wealth
* identity
* destiny

It rewrites reality around **Veezy's concept of value**.

---

# Escalation Curve

The campaign should escalate in three phases.

### Phase 1 — Crime

Players run jobs and learn Offledger.

Tone:

* comedic
* adventurous

---

### Phase 2 — Conspiracy

Players discover the artifacts are part of something larger.

Tone:

* mysterious
* unsettling

---

### Phase 3 — Catastrophe

The Crown Infinite nears completion.

Tone:

* cosmic
* apocalyptic

---

# AI Generation Guidelines

When generating new content:

Always ask:

```
Does this reinforce the economic tension?
Does this reveal something about the world?
Does this fit the tone?
```

If the answer is no, revise the concept.

---

# The Most Important Rule

The campaign is fundamentally about this tension:

```
Ambition vs Freedom
```

The players are trapped inside Veezy's system.

The question is whether they will:

* escape it
* destroy it
* or become part of it.
