# Purpose

This file defines **non-negotiable worldbuilding rules** for the *Coinspiratorium* campaign so that AI-generated content remains consistent with the setting.

This campaign mixes:

* fantasy crime
* magical economics
* cosmic absurdity
* sudden horror

Content generation should always respect these tone and world constraints.

---

# Core Setting Rules

## Rule 1 — Everything is About Money

The central theme of the campaign is **magical capitalism**.

Everything ultimately ties back to:

* debt
* profit
* trade
* contracts
* ownership

Examples:

Good:

* a cursed artifact used as collateral
* a dragon renting out part of its hoard
* a demon selling prophecy futures

Bad:

* a random monster with no economic or narrative purpose

Every location or NPC should be tied to **commerce, ownership, or value**.

---

## Rule 2 — Offledger is Outside the Law

Offledger exists outside normal planar jurisdiction.

This means:

* criminals operate openly
* law enforcement rarely enters
* everything is negotiable

However:

**Offledger has its own rules.**

Breaking those rules results in consequences from:

* Veezy
* rival merchants
* extraplanar forces

---

## Rule 3 — Veezy is the Sun of the System

Everything ultimately revolves around **Vizzok the Opulent (Lord Veezy)**.

NPCs should:

* fear him
* admire him
* owe him money
* want to work for him

But **no one fully trusts him**.

His personality combines:

* mob boss
* celebrity
* philosopher
* narcissist

He should be funny until he suddenly isn't.

---

## Rule 4 — Humor Makes the Horror Worse

Comedy is a key tone element.

Examples:

* bureaucratic crime syndicates
* magical shop branding consultants
* artifact insurance claims

But the humor should never remove the threat.

The **funniest characters should still be dangerous**.

When the tone suddenly becomes serious, it should feel chilling.

---

## Rule 5 — Artifacts Should Have Meaning

Magical objects should rarely be generic.

They should represent:

* broken promises
* cosmic irony
* metaphysical concepts

Example:

A dragon egg that never hatches but contains infinite potential.

Bad artifact design:

```
+2 flaming sword
```

Good artifact design:

```
A sword that only burns when its wielder lies.
```

---

## Rule 6 — Dungeons Should Be Thematic

Each dungeon should have a **strong conceptual identity**.

Examples:

* a dragon hoard vault
* a celestial museum
* a memory archive
* a cursed casino

Avoid generic dungeon structures.

Players should understand the **story of the place** through its design.

---

## Rule 7 — Every Victory Creates New Problems

The campaign is about **systems of power**.

Success should often lead to:

* new debts
* new obligations
* new enemies

The players should never feel completely free until the final act.

# NPC Generation Rules

NPCs should fall into recognizable archetypes:

### Criminal Operators

Smugglers, dealers, fences, collectors.

### Corporate Weirdos

Auditors, consultants, brand managers.

### Arcane Specialists

Artificers, magical accountants, spell engineers.

### Desperate Clients

People who need something stolen.

### Enforcers

People who make problems disappear.

Every NPC should have:

1. a **motivation**
2. a **relationship to money**
3. a **relationship to Veezy**

---

# Dialogue Style

Characters in this setting speak in distinctive styles.

Examples:

### Veezy

Prophetic entrepreneur.

```
You call it theft.
I call it asset relocation.
```

### Eyecons

Corporate crime professionals.

```
Your payment schedule has entered the concern phase.
```

### Offledger Merchants

Fast-talking traders.

```
Listen, I'm not saying it's cursed.
I'm saying the curse adds resale value.
```

---

# Artifact Design Framework

Artifacts should follow one of these themes:

### Broken Destiny

Something that was supposed to be great but failed.

### Weaponized Potential

Something that contains unrealized power.

### Conceptual Magic

Objects tied to abstract ideas like memory, fate, identity.

### Cosmic Irony

Objects that punish the type of person who wants them.
