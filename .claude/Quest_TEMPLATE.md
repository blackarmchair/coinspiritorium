# Purpose

This template defines the structure for **Coinspiratorium missions**.

AI-generated quests should follow this format.

---

# Quest Title

A short, memorable title.

Often slightly comedic.

Example:

```
Steal a Solar's Halo
Borrow a Dragon's Egg
The Beholder Convention Job
```

---

# Adventure Summary

A short overview of the mission.

Include:

* the objective
* the location
* the primary challenge

Example structure:

```
Veezy sends the PCs to [LOCATION] to retrieve [OBJECT].

The location is guarded by [THEME OR ENEMY].

The mission becomes complicated when [TWIST].
```

---

# Target Item

Describe the key artifact.

Include:

* appearance
* magical properties
* narrative significance
* why Veezy wants it

Artifacts should feel unique.

---

# Scene 1 — The Briefing

The quest always begins with a briefing.

Usually delivered by:

* Veezy
* an Eyecon
* a criminal client

The briefing should include:

* explanation of the job
* potential dangers
* equipment provided

Optional:

* Veezy delivering strange philosophical commentary

---

# Scene 2 — Travel

The players travel to the dungeon location.

This section establishes:

* environmental hazards
* visual atmosphere
* tone

Examples:

* volcanic islands
* celestial temples
* cursed deserts

---

# Scene 3 — Dungeon Entry

The first puzzle or obstacle.

Examples:

* riddles
* guardians
* negotiation encounters

The dungeon's **theme should be immediately clear**.

---

# Scene 4 — Interior Challenges

Include a mix of:

* puzzle encounters
* social encounters
* combat

Encounters should:

* drain resources
* reinforce the dungeon theme

---

# Scene 5 — Artifact Chamber

The location of the target item.

This scene should include:

* emotional or narrative weight
* unusual mechanics
* a moral twist if possible

---

# Scene 6 — Escape

Retrieving the item triggers complications.

Examples:

* alarms
* awakened guardians
* environmental collapse

The escape should feel tense.

---

# Scene 7 — Debrief

Return to Offledger.

Possible outcomes:

* payment
* tribute demands
* new plot revelations

---

# Encounter Guidelines

Aim for **3–5 meaningful encounters per dungeon**.

Encounters count if they consume:

* HP
* spells
* consumables
* time

---

# Treasure Guidelines

Treasure should be split between:

1. sellable goods
2. magical curiosities
3. plot artifacts

Remember:

The players need treasure to:

* level up
* pay Veezy
* run the shop

---

# Campaign Progression

Early Campaign:

* small jobs
* establishing the shop
* learning Offledger

Mid Campaign:

* more dangerous heists
* factions interfering
* hints of the Crown Infinite

Late Campaign:

* discovering Veezy's plan
* deciding whether to stop him
* multiversal consequences
