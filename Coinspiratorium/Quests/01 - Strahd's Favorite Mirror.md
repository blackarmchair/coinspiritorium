# "You Have 30 Minutes to Steal [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s Favorite Mirror"

> *"I need to see my potential. Not my reflection. Past vibes only."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
The PCs' first real contract. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends them to Castle Ravenloft to steal a mirror from [[Strahd von Zarovich]]'s private chambers — during a masquerade ball attended by vampires, ghosts, and temporal aristocrats. They must infiltrate the ball, slip away unnoticed, bypass the wards on [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s private wing, survive the mirror's unsettling revelations, and escape — ideally before [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] catches them. He will catch them.

**Tone arc:** Comedy (briefing) → Gothic dread ([[Barovia]]) → Social tension (ball) → Creeping horror (mirror) → Quiet menace ([[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]) → Relief/comedy (debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Social infiltration, stealth, puzzle/wards, roleplay negotiation
**Combat:** Light (one encounter in the mirror chamber; [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is NOT a combat encounter)

---

## Target Item
**[[The Vanishing Mirror]]** — A full-length dressing mirror in an ornate wrought-iron frame shaped like interlocking thorns. The glass is faintly violet. It does not show the present — it shows what the viewer was doing exactly 24 hours ago, rendered with perfect clarity and sound.

[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] uses it as a window into yesterday. He watches his guests arrive before they arrive. He reviews conversations he wasn't part of. He studies himself from angles he couldn't have seen.

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Temporal resonance — allows the Crown to reach backward through time, anchoring its authority in history as well as the present. Without this component, the Crown only operates in the now. With it, the Crown's laws become retroactively true — as if they had always been true.

**Properties (while in PCs' possession):**
- Gazing into the mirror shows a vivid scene of what the viewer was doing 24 hours ago (no save, automatic)
- Viewer must succeed on a Will save (DC 14) or become fascinated for 1 round, watching the scene play out
- Other people present in the scene are rendered with perfect accuracy — including things the viewer didn't notice at the time
- The mirror weighs 40lbs and is 5 feet tall; it can be shrunk with *reduce item* or wrapped and carried by two people
- It is immune to mundane damage but can be broken by a *disintegrate* or similar effect

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The PCs are summoned to the back room of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] — the "office," which is really just a space where [[Iris Major]] has set up a folding table and a ledger. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is already present, floating near the ceiling, examining one of his eyestalk rings with performative boredom.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"I need a mirror. Not a mirror-mirror. Not a vanity. THE mirror. [[Strahd von Zarovich]] has it — dressing room, upper floors, big gothic castle, you can't miss it."*

He describes the mirror's properties in vague terms — *"It shows yesterday. I want to see my yesterday. My yesterday was INCREDIBLE."* — and deflects any questions about why he actually needs it.

**If PCs ask why this specific mirror:**
> *"Because I said so. You want a reason? Check Article III of your contract — 'procurement of designated assets at the Creditor's discretion.' That's me. I'm the Creditor. I'm also the discretion."*

**If PCs point out that [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is a legendary vampire lord:**
> *"Bitch! I'm a legendary me! You'll be fine. He throws parties. Sneak in, grab the mirror while he's doing his little vampire waltz, sneak out. I did the hard part already — I found it. You just the hands."*

### Logistics (delivered by [[Iris Major]])
After [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s pitch, [[Iris Major]] takes over with cold efficiency:
- **Portal:** A one-way portal from [[Offledger]]'s Portal Hub will deposit the PCs in the forest outside Castle Ravenloft, approximately one hour before sunset
- **Extraction:** A return portal will activate at a designated clearing two miles south of the castle at dawn — it stays open for ten minutes, then closes. Miss it and they're stuck in [[Barovia]]
- **Disguises:** [[Iris Major]] provides a *forged invitation* (masterwork quality, Forgery DC 25 to detect) to the Midnight Masquerade, addressed to "Lord and Lady Ashworth and retinue" — minor Darklord nobility from a distant domain
- **Masks:** Four ornate masquerade masks enchanted with *disguise self* (CL 3rd, 3 hours duration, activated by putting them on). The illusion defaults to pale, gaunt, aristocratic features — plausibly vampiric
- **Time limit:** The ball runs from sundown to midnight. After midnight, [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] retires to his private chambers. The PCs need to get the mirror *before* that

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Parting Words

> *"Oh — and while you're there, see if they serve that [[Barovia]]n red. I heard that shit exquisite. Bring me a bottle. Consider it a... supplemental deliverable."*

He's serious about the wine. He'll ask about it later.

### DM Notes
- This scene should be short and funny. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s charm is at full blast — he's sending them on an impossible errand and acting like it's a grocery run
- [[Iris Major]]'s clinical logistics contrast perfectly with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s bluster. She's the one who actually makes the operation possible
- Don't let the PCs over-plan. They don't know the castle layout. They don't know who's at the ball. They have masks, an invitation, and a deadline. Go.
- If PCs try to negotiate better terms (more pay, better gear, backup), [[Iris Major]] says: *"Your compensation is defined in Article IV, Section 2. Supplemental requisitions require a Form 7-C filed 30 days in advance."* [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] adds: *"What she said. But also, no."*

## Scene 2: Arrival in [[Barovia]]

### Setup
The portal deposits the PCs in a dense, fog-choked forest. The trees are black and leafless. The air smells like wet stone and something faintly sweet — old flowers, or old blood. The mists of [[Barovia]] press close.

Through the canopy, Castle Ravenloft is visible on its crag, lit from within by hundreds of candles. Faint music drifts down — strings, a harpsichord, something that might be laughter.

### The Approach
The road to the castle is well-traveled tonight. The PCs can see carriages — some horse-drawn, some pulled by nothing at all — winding up the switchback road. Figures in elaborate dress move through the mist.

**Encounter: The Gates**
The castle gates stand open, flanked by two **wight sentinels** (do not attack guests) who inspect invitations. They don't speak — they simply extend a clawed hand, examine the paper, and gesture guests through.

- The PCs' forged invitation passes inspection automatically ([[Iris Major]]'s work is impeccable)
- If a PC does something suspicious at the gate (casting a spell, drawing a weapon, refusing to show the invitation), one sentinel turns to stare and does not stop staring. It follows that PC with its gaze for the rest of the evening. This is unnerving but not immediately dangerous — it's a warning
- The sentinels *can* detect living creatures by scent (Spot +8 equivalent), but the mask enchantments and the press of arriving guests provide cover. No check required unless a PC actively draws attention

### Tone Guidance
This is the tonal pivot. The comedy of the briefing is gone. [[Barovia]] is oppressive, quiet, and wrong. Describe the silence between the music. The way the fog moves against the wind. The way the other guests don't seem to breathe.

The PCs should feel like they've stepped out of a heist comedy and into a horror movie. That contrast is the point.

## Scene 3: The Midnight Masquerade

### The Ballroom
The grand ballroom of Castle Ravenloft is cavernous and candlelit. Hundreds of guests fill the space — waltzing, mingling, drinking wine the color of garnets. The ceiling is lost in shadow. An orchestra of ghostly musicians plays from a raised gallery, their instruments moving of their own accord when the players' hands pass through the strings.

**Key sensory details:**
- The room is warm but no one is sweating — because almost no one is alive
- The wine smells like iron. It is not wine
- Mirrors line the walls but many of the guests cast no reflection
- Conversations are polite, formal, and subtly predatory — everyone is sizing everyone else up
- [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is visible across the room, holding court near a grand fireplace, surrounded by admirers. He is beautiful, pale, and utterly in control

### The Social Challenge
The PCs must survive approximately **2-3 hours of mingling** without being exposed as living mortals. This is a skill-based challenge, not a single check — it's a series of interactions, any of which could go wrong.

**The Core Problem:** The PCs are alive. Their hearts beat. They breathe. They're warm. In a room full of undead, this is conspicuous.

**Passive Defenses:**
- The *disguise self* masks handle visual appearance (pale, aristocratic, plausibly undead)
- The masquerade format means everyone is somewhat disguised — eccentricity is expected
- The crowd is large and the guests are self-absorbed

**Active Threats:**

| Threat         | Trigger                                 | Detection Method                                                | DC to Avoid                                                                                            |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Vampire guests | Close proximity (dancing, conversation) | Smell living blood, hear heartbeat                              | Bluff DC 15 to excuse flushed appearance; or stay at arm's length                                      |
| Ghost guests   | Attempting to touch or be touched       | Hand passes through PC (or doesn't)                             | Reflex DC 12 to smoothly avoid contact                                                                 |
| [[Rafail]]     | Strahd's chamberlain                    | Observing unfamiliar guests                                     | Sense Motive +14, trained observer; Bluff DC 18 if directly questioned                                 |
| The wine       | Social pressure to drink                | It's blood — drinking it requires Fort DC 13 or PC visibly gags | Sleight of Hand DC 12 to fake drinking                                                                 |
| Dancing        | Invitation to waltz                     | [[Barovia]]n waltzes are complex and specific                   | Perform (dance) DC 13 or Bluff DC 15 to gracefully decline                                             |
| The mirrors    | Walls are lined with them               | Living PCs cast reflections; vampires don't                     | Spot DC 14 for nearby vampires to notice; PCs can avoid mirrors with Spot DC 12 to identify them first |

### Key Ball NPCs

**[[Countess Lysara Veldritch]]** — Vampire Socialite
A stunning, ice-blonde vampire in a gown of black silk and silver thread. She gravitates toward whoever in the party seems most charismatic (highest Cha) and is relentlessly, dangerously flirtatious. She compliments their "warmth" and "vitality" — the words could be metaphorical. They are not.

- **Purpose:** Social pressure. She wants to dance, to talk, to get close. Every moment in her company is another chance to be detected
- **If she realizes they're alive:** She is delighted, not hostile. Living guests are *exotic*. She covers for them — for now — in exchange for "a favor, to be named later." This debt should feel uncomfortably familiar
- **Personality:** Amused, predatory, lonely. A smaller-scale [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]

**[[Lord Mordinev]]** — Ghost Noble
A portly, translucent ghost in outdated finery. Friendly, chatty, and a terrible gossip. He claims to remember the PCs from "[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s last gathering" — which none of them attended. He's confusing them with someone [[The Vanishing Mirror|the Vanishing Mirror]] showed him.

- **Purpose:** Comic relief and information. He'll happily describe the castle layout if asked ("*The west wing? Oh, that's [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s private quarters. No one goes up there during parties — well, almost no one*"). He's a goldmine of gossip, most of it inaccurate
- **If pressed on how he "remembers" them:** He gets confused and changes the subject. He genuinely doesn't know where the memory came from. This is the mirror bleeding its temporal properties into the castle — a subtle foreshadowing of its power
- **Personality:** Jovial, oblivious, harmless
- He will give hints if asked:
	- About the West Wing: "*Of course, one always greets the Lord's portrait — it's only polite*"
 
**[[Rafail]]** — [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s Chamberlain
A tall, silent bald man with dead eyes. He moves through the ball like a shark through calm water — greeting guests, managing servants, and quietly cataloguing everything he sees. He is the most dangerous person in the room who isn't [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]].

- **Purpose:** Tension. He's the active threat. If too many social checks fail, [[Rafail]] notices and begins watching the PCs specifically. He doesn't confront them directly — he simply appears nearby, observing, and the PCs feel it
- **If [[Rafail]] becomes suspicious (3+ failed social checks):** He approaches the party and politely asks about "Lord Ashworth's" recent travels. This is a Bluff DC 18 interaction — he's testing them. Failure doesn't mean immediate exposure, but he will follow up, and their window to slip away narrows
- **Stat reference:** Use stats from 3.5e for a high-level ranger/rogue (CR 8-10). He should be clearly beyond the PCs' ability to fight

### The Window
At some point, the PCs need to leave the ball and head upstairs. Possible triggers:
- They find a natural break in conversation
- Mordinev mentions the west wing and they seize the opportunity
- The orchestra begins a particularly elaborate piece that draws all attention to the dance floor
- A spectacle occurs — a ghost performing an aria, a vampire duel over a perceived slight, [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] himself making a toast — that creates a 5-10 minute window of distraction

**Slipping away:** Requires a Stealth check (Move Silently DC 14) or a reasonable cover story (Bluff DC 14 — "seeking the powder room," "feeling faint," "admiring the architecture"). The PCs should leave one or two at a time, not as a group.

### DM Notes
- The ball should feel like a sustained, simmering pressure cooker, not a single pass/fail. Let the PCs have some successes — charming a vampire, dodging a ghost's handshake, smoothly refusing the wine. Then ratchet up the tension with [[Rafail]]'s attention or Lysara's proximity
- Track social "heat" informally. Each failed check raises suspicion slightly. At 3+ failures, [[Rafail]] takes notice. At 5+, the PCs' window to escape is closing fast
- If a PC is genuinely exposed as living: panic is not required. Lysara might cover for them (for a price). Mordinev might be too oblivious to care. Even [[Rafail]] might simply note it and file it away — [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] doesn't throw people out of his parties. He prefers to *deal* with them privately
- The ball is the heart of the adventure. Don't rush it. Let the players roleplay, sweat, improvise. The longer they're in the ballroom, the more they'll appreciate the relief of getting out

## Scene 4: The Ascent

### The West Wing
Once clear of the ballroom, the PCs enter the quieter corridors of Castle Ravenloft. The music fades. The candlelight thins. The stone is older here, rougher, and the walls are lined with portraits whose eyes follow movement.

**Layout:**
The path from the ballroom to [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s private chambers passes through three areas:

### 4A: The Portrait Gallery
A long corridor of oil paintings depicting [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] across centuries — conquering, ruling, brooding, and one where he's clearly trying to paint himself and failing. The portraits whisper to each other as the PCs pass but do not raise alarm. They're gossiping.

- **Hazard:** The final portrait — a life-sized painting of [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] in full armor — has *magic mouth* cast on it. If a creature passes without saying "Good evening, my lord," it announces loudly: *"INTRUDERS IN THE WEST WING."*
- **Detection:** Spot DC 16 or Knowledge (arcana) DC 14 to notice faint abjuration magic on the painting's frame before triggering it
- **Bypass:** Saying the passphrase (overheard at the ball if they talked to Mordinev, who mentions it offhandedly: "Of course, one always greets the Lord's portrait — it's only polite"). Alternatively, *dispel magic* (CL check DC 14), *silence* on the painting, or simply covering it with a cloak
- **If triggered:** The alert is audible on this floor only. Two **animated armors** (see 4B) activate and await the PCs in 4B. Rafail is also aware.

### Encounter: Portrait Shadows
If the magic mouth alarm was triggered, OR if the PCs take longer than 5 minutes in the Portrait Gallery, the ambient dark magic of the west wing responds. Two of the portraits — a painting of [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] standing over a battlefield, and one of him alone in an empty ballroom — go dark. The canvases become voids of pure shadow, one of the **Shadows** peels free and glides silently toward the nearest living creature. The second shadow will only attack if the PCs linger or make a truly heroic amount of noise.

**Portrait Shadows (x1-2)** — MM p.221, CR 3 each
- Medium Undead (Incorporeal) | HP 19 | AC 13 (touch 13) | Speed fly 40 ft. (good)
- **Incorporeal Touch +3 melee** (1d6 Strength damage)
- **Strength Damage (Su):** Each hit deals 1d6 Str damage. A creature reduced to 0 Str dies and rises as a shadow in 1d4 rounds
- Incorporeal: 50% miss chance from corporeal sources; immune to nonmagical weapons
- Turn resistance +2 ([[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s domain empowers undead)
- Darkvision 60 ft., undead traits

**Tactics:** The shadows drift along the walls, clinging to the edges of the candlelight. They attack whoever is closest to the paintings — usually the rear of the marching order. They don't pursue beyond the Portrait Gallery. If turned, they retreat into the canvases and do not return for 10 minutes.

**If the alarm was NOT triggered:** The shadows remain dormant. A DC 16 Spot check notices the two dark paintings ("Those canvases are... emptier than the others"), and a DC 14 Knowledge (religion) check identifies them as shadow-haunted. PCs who avoid lingering pass through without incident.

### 4B: The Trophy Hall
A wide chamber displaying [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s collected curios — weapons from defeated rivals, taxidermized monsters, and six suits of plate armor standing in alcoves along the walls.

- **Two of the six suits are animated armors** (CR 1 each, MM p.13). They are dormant unless the magic mouth alarm was triggered OR a creature touches any displayed item
- **If dormant:** PCs can pass through with a Move Silently DC 12 (the armors "listen" even while dormant — loud noise or detected intruders wake them)
- **If active:** They attack with halberds and pursue intruders through the west wing. They do not leave the west wing and cannot descend to the ballroom
- **Loot opportunity:** A display case holds several curios:

### 4C: The Sealed Door
The entrance to [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s private chambers is a heavy oak door reinforced with iron bands and three distinct locks.

**Protections:**
1. **Arcane Lock** (CL 5th) — increases Open Lock DC by +10
2. **Mundane lock** — Open Lock DC 20 (DC 30 with arcane lock)
3. **Glyph of warding** — Blast glyph (3d8 sonic, Reflex DC 14 half), triggers when the door is opened without speaking the word "Tatyana"
4. **The word "Tatyana":** Can be learned from Mordinev (who mentions it wistfully — "He always did love that name... passwords, poems, the lot"), from the journal in the Trophy Hall (the entry about the mirror ends with "I whispered her name and the visions ceased, as they always do"), or from a successful Knowledge (history) or Knowledge (nobility) DC 16 check about [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s history

**Bypassing the door:**
- **Full bypass:** Speak "Tatyana" and pick the mundane lock (DC 20). The arcane lock and glyph both suppress
- **Brute bypass:** *Knock* or *dispel magic* (CL check DC 16) handles the arcane lock; detecting the glyph requires Search DC 18, and it can be disarmed with Disable Device DC 18 or any targeted *dispel magic*
- **Destructive bypass:** The door has Hardness 5, 30 HP, but breaking it is *loud* and guarantees [[Rafail]]'s arrival in 1d4 rounds

### DM Notes
- The Ascent should feel like a classic heist infiltration — quiet, tense, methodical. The comedy of the ball is behind them. The castle is watching
- Reward players who paid attention during the ball. Mordinev drops three useful pieces of information (the passphrase, Tatyana, the west wing layout) but only if the PCs engaged with him. Players who treated the ball as pure stealth and talked to no one will have a harder time here — and that's fair
- The animated armors are the only real combat encounter in the adventure. They should be manageable for level 2-3 PCs but cost resources (HP, spells, time)
- If the PCs are struggling badly with the locks and wards, have the journal clue be slightly more obvious, or let a successful Bardic Knowledge or similar check reveal Tatyana's significance

## Scene 5: The Mirror Chamber

### The Room
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s private dressing chamber is smaller and more intimate than expected. It's not a throne room — it's a bedroom antechamber. A vanity. A wardrobe. A chair facing a fireplace that burns without heat. And against the far wall, in a frame of wrought-iron thorns:

**[[The Vanishing Mirror]].**

It's five feet tall, slightly taller than a person, and the glass has a faint violet tint, like viewing the world through twilight. It doesn't show the room. It shows *yesterday's* room — the same space, 24 hours ago, [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] standing before it in different clothes, studying his own reflection with an expression that's hard to read.

### The Mirror's Effect
When a PC looks into the mirror, they see themselves — but 24 hours ago. The scene is vivid, with sound and ambient detail. Read or improvise a brief vignette for each PC who looks:

**What the mirror shows (tailor to your PCs):**
- A PC sees themselves in [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]], talking about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] behind his back. [[Snitch Ray]] is visible in the background, listening. The PC didn't notice him at the time
- A PC sees themselves pocketing a few extra coins during inventory. Minor, but the mirror doesn't judge — it just *shows*
- A PC sees themselves sleeping fitfully, dreaming. The dream is visible as a faint overlay — and it involves something the PC hasn't told the party about
- A PC sees themselves studying their copy of the [[Terms of Incoinspiration]], and the mirror zooms in on a clause they hadn't noticed before — one that mentions "procurement of temporal assets"

**Mechanical effect:** Each PC who gazes into the mirror must make a **Will save DC 14** or become fascinated for 1 round. While fascinated, they're flat-footed and unaware of their surroundings. Success means they can look away freely.

**The important revelation:** The last PC to look (or any PC who stares for more than 2 rounds) sees the mirror flicker — and for a brief moment, instead of showing 24 hours ago, it shows **[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] looking directly at them, in the present, from somewhere else in the castle.** He smiles faintly. Then the image returns to normal.

This means [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] has another way to see through the mirror. He knows they're here.

### Taking the Mirror
The mirror can be removed from the wall with a DC 14 Strength check (it's mounted with iron brackets) or by using tools to unbolt the frame (2 minutes of work, no check). It weighs 40 lbs.

**Complication:** When the mirror is removed from the wall, every other mirror in Castle Ravenloft — and there are hundreds — goes dark for one second. The ballroom guests notice. [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] *definitely* notices.

The PCs now have approximately **5 minutes** before [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] arrives. This is not enough time to reach the extraction point. It's barely enough time to reach the ballroom.

### Encounter: Dread Reflections
When the mirror is removed from its mounted position it begins projecting the 24hr-old echoes of the PCs who looked into it; not a clone, a *wrong* reflection.

**Dread Reflections (x0-4)** — CR 2 each
- These reflections match the exact stats and abilities of the PCs with the following changes:
	- Undead traits, can be turned
	- Immunity to mind-affecting spells, illusions, and psychology-based effects (including bluff/diplomacy/etc); they are nearly mindless
	- The reflection encounter will last a total of 12 rounds (or less if the PCs destroy all the shadows); split these 12 rounds evenly between the total number of shadows: 1 for each PC who looked in the mirror.

### DM Notes
- The mirror vignettes are a chance for individual character moments. Tailor them to your PCs' backstories and secrets. The mirror doesn't weaponize information — it just reveals it, neutrally. Let the players decide what to do with what they've seen
- The "[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is watching" moment should land like a cold hand on the back of the neck. Don't oversell it. Just describe it and move on. The players will provide the panic
- If a PC tries to use the mirror strategically (looking into it to learn what [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] was doing 24 hours ago, for example), reward that creativity. They might see [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] preparing for the ball, talking to [[Rafail]] about "*the troublesome visions the mirror has been showing lately*", or pacing in front of it, muttering about "*uninvited guests*"

## Scene 6: [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]

### The Confrontation
The PCs are carrying a 40-pound mirror through Castle Ravenloft. They're not going to make it to the exit before [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] finds them. He's not chasing them — he's already there.

**How he appears:** The PCs round a corner (in the Trophy Hall, the Portrait Gallery, or even the ballroom entrance) and [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is leaning against a wall with his arms crossed, looking mildly inconvenienced. He's been there for a while. He might have been there before they entered the corridor.

> *"You could have asked. I would have said no, of course. But the courtesy would have been appreciated."*

He is not angry. He is not afraid. He is a man whose favorite pen has been borrowed without permission by the office intern. This is beneath him, and he wants them to know that.

### [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s Demeanor
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is operating in **host mode**, not combat mode. He has no intention of killing the PCs — not because he can't (he emphatically can, and the PCs should feel this), but because:
1. Killing guests at his own ball is *gauche*
2. He's curious about who sent them
3. He already knows the answer (he saw them in the mirror) and wants to see if they'll lie

**What [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] knows:**
- He's been watching the PCs through the mirror's temporal echo. He saw them receiving the contract from [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] 24 hours ago (the mirror showed him yesterday's events in [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]])
- He knows who [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is — a beholder crime lord with delusions of grandeur. He finds [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] *amusing*, in the way a wolf finds a particularly bold rat amusing
- He knows the PCs are indentured servants, not willing agents. He finds this... relatable? Pitiable? Useful?

**What [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] does NOT know:**
- The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s full scope
- Why [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] specifically needs THIS mirror (though he suspects, and the mirror's recent visions have been "distressing" — it's been showing him glimpses of a possible future where reality itself is rewritten, gold-tinged and wrong)

### The Conversation
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] wants to talk, not fight. He's a conversationalist. He's been alone with his thoughts for centuries and the PCs are, at minimum, *novel*. Play this scene as a tense parlor discussion. [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] pours wine (real wine, this time), offers them seats they don't dare take, and asks questions in the manner of someone who already knows the answers.

**Key dialogue beats:**

**On their employer:**
> *"A beholder. How quaint. I've met a few. Grand ambitions, limited by myopia. Tell me — does yours still think gold makes him interesting?"*

**On their debt:**
> *"You owe him your freedom. I understand obligation perhaps better than anyone in this castle. Perhaps better than anyone in this plane. The difference between us, of course, is that my chains are self-forged. Yours were assigned."*

**On the mirror:**
> *"That mirror has been showing me... peculiar things, of late. Visions that feel wrong. Saturated. Gold where there should be shadow. It's been making me uneasy, and very few things make me uneasy."*
>
> He pauses.
>
> *"I don't know what your employer is doing. But the mirror does. And whatever it is, the mirror is afraid of it."*

### [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s Deal
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] offers the PCs a choice:

**Option A: Walk away clean.**
They put the mirror back and leave. No consequences from [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]. But they return to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] empty-handed, and that has consequences of its own.

**Option B: Take the mirror. But report back.**
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] lets them leave with the mirror. In exchange:
- The PCs agree to serve as [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s informants regarding [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s activities — specifically, what the artifacts are being used for
- [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] provides each PC with a **[[Barovia]]n signet ring** (worth 25gp; functions as a *sending stone* — once per week, the wearer can whisper a 25-word message to [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]], and he can reply)
- He does not demand loyalty. He does not demand exclusivity. He simply wants *information*. "*Consider me a concerned neighbor.*"
- **The catch:** If [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — or [[Snitch Ray]] — ever discovers these rings, the PCs are in catastrophic trouble. Possessing a communication link to an outside power violates at least three articles of the [[Terms of Incoinspiration]]

**Option C: The PCs propose something else.**
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is willing to negotiate. If the players come up with a creative counter-offer, entertain it. [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] respects audacity, even from mortals.

### If the PCs refuse all deals:
[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] sighs.

> *"Disappointing. But not surprising. Servants rarely think beyond the next paycheck."*

He lets them go — without the mirror. If they try to take it anyway, he simply takes it from them. He is CR 15+ and they are level 2-3. This is not a fight. This is a cat allowing a mouse to leave.

However: the mirror ends up in their possession regardless. When they return through the extraction portal, the mirror is already there, leaning against a tree, with a note pinned to the frame in elegant handwriting:

> *"A gift. No strings attached: the most dangerous kind."*

This means **[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] wanted them to have it all along.** Why? Because the mirror has been showing him things that frighten him, and he wants it out of his castle. He's not being generous — he's disposing of a problem.

If the PCs took the deal (Option B), this doesn't happen — they carry the mirror out themselves.

### DM Notes
- **[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is not the villain of this adventure. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is.** [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is a mirror (thematically appropriate) — he reflects the PCs' situation back at them. He's a more refined, more powerful, more self-aware version of someone trapped by their own choices
- **Do not let this become a combat encounter.** If a PC attacks [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]], he catches their weapon mid-swing, looks at it, looks at them, and says: *"No."* Then he returns to the conversation. If they persist, he dominates the attacker and has them sit quietly while the adults talk
- **[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s deal is the adventure's lasting consequence.** If the PCs accept, they now have a secret alliance with a vampire lord that conflicts with their obligation to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. This creates tension for the entire campaign — are they using the rings? Is [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] using them? When does it come back around?
- **The "gift" variant (if they refuse the deal) is arguably worse.** Now [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] has done them a favor, unprompted, and they owe him nothing... which means he can call in a social debt whenever he wants. And [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] is patient

## Scene 7: The Extraction

### Getting Out
**If the PCs took [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s deal:** [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] gestures them toward a servant's passage that leads directly out of the castle, bypassing the ballroom entirely. He wishes them a pleasant evening. [[Rafail]] watches them leave and says nothing.

**If the PCs are fleeing (alarm triggered, no deal):** They need to get out of the castle and to the extraction clearing. The ball is still ongoing — they can try to blend back in and walk out the front door (risky — they're carrying a five-foot mirror), or find an alternate exit:
- **Servant's entrance:** Kitchen staff (zombies, harmless) don't care about intruders. Leads to the courtyard. Move Silently DC 10 to pass through without knocking over pots
- **Windows:** The castle has tall windows in every corridor. The drop from the west wing is 40 feet (4d6 falling damage), reduced by any *feather fall*, rope, or creative solution
- **The front door:** Bluff DC 16 to walk out with a mirror during a masquerade. Surprisingly possible — "*It's a party favor*"

### If Combat Breaks Out — The Gate Sentinels
When [[The Vanishing Mirror|the Vanishing Mirror]] went dark (Scene 5), every undead in Castle Ravenloft felt it — including the **wight sentinels** at the front gate. They are no longer passive. If the PCs attempt to leave through the main gate or any ground-level exit within sight of the sentinels, the wights step forward and block the path.

**If the PCs took [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s deal:** The sentinels hesitate — [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s will conflicts with the castle's defensive instincts. They freeze for **2 rounds** before attacking, giving the PCs time to slip past (Move Silently DC 12) or sprint through. This is [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s parting gift: not safety, but a head start.

**If the PCs did NOT take [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s deal:** The sentinels attack immediately. They position at the gate and will not let the PCs pass without a fight.

**Ghast Sentinels (x2)** — MM p.255, CR 3 each
- HP 29 | AC 17 | Speed 30 ft.
- **Bite +5 melee** (1d8+3 plus paralysis)
- **Ghoul Fever (Su)**: Disease—bite, Fortitude DC 15, incubation period 1 day, damage 1d3 Con and 1d3 Dex. The save DC is Charisma-based.
- **Paralysis (Ex)**: Those hit by a ghast’s bite or claw attack must succeed on a DC 15 Fortitude save or be paralyzed for 1d4+1 rounds. Even elves can be affected by this paralysis. The save DC is Charisma-based.
- **Stench (Ex)**: The stink of death and corruption surrounding these creatures is overwhelming. Living creatures within 10 feet must succeed on a DC 15 Fortitude save or be sickened for 1d6+4 minutes. A creature that successfully saves

**Tactics:** The sentinels fight at the gate and do not pursue beyond the castle grounds. They attack whoever is carrying the mirror first. If reduced below 10 HP, they fight defensively — [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s property instinct is "delay," not "destroy guests."

**Bypassing:** PCs who chose a window exit, the servant's entrance, or any route that avoids the front gate skip this encounter entirely. Reward creative escape planning. The encounter exists to tax resources and make the two-mile hike to the extraction point feel perilous.

### The Clearing
Two miles south, in a hollow between dead oaks. The return portal is a faint golden shimmer — recognizably the same arcane signature as [[Offledger]]'s portals. It activates at dawn and stays open for 10 minutes.

If the PCs arrive early, they wait in [[Barovia]]n darkness. The mist is thick. Things move in it. Nothing attacks — this is atmosphere, not a combat encounter. But the PCs should feel like they're being watched. Because they are. [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] always watches.

## Scene 8: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal deposits them in [[Offledger]]'s Portal Hub. [[Iris Major]] is waiting with a clipboard. She inspects the mirror without expression, marks something on the clipboard, and says:

> *"Satisfactory. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]] will receive this personally. Report to the shop floor for your post-contract assessment."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is in the back room, trying on a new ring. He glances at the mirror with studied nonchalance — as if he didn't just send level-2 adventurers to steal from one of the most dangerous beings in the multiverse.

> *"See? Easy. I told you it was easy."*

**If PCs try to describe the danger they were in:**
> *"Danger is subjective. Risk is objective. And your risk was fully hedged by the fact that I believed in my plan. That's worth at least two levels of protection. Spiritually."*

**If PCs ask why he wants the mirror:**
> *"Nostalgia. Self-improvement. The aesthetic possibilities of hindsight. Don't overthink it; leave the thinking to me."*

**If PCs mention [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s comments about "disturbing visions":**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] pauses. His central eye dilates slightly. Then:
> *"[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] sees what I want the multiverse to see. Eventually."*

Beat. Then the charm snaps back on:
> *"Did you get my drink?"*

If they brought the [[Barovia]]n red (from the Trophy Hall or the ball), [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is genuinely delighted — perhaps the most sincere positive emotion the PCs have seen from him. He doesn't thank them. He simply says: *"You have taste. That's bankable."* Their next tribute payment is reduced by 10%. If they forgot the wine, he's quietly disappointed and their next tribute payment is unaffected but [[Snitch Ray]] makes a note.

**If the PCs performed well** (mirror acquired, minimal complications), [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] adds, almost as an afterthought:

> *"Oh — I got another crew running a job for me this week. Totally different thing. Somewhere else. Just mentioning it so you know: I got options."*

He says this like it's nothing. It's not nothing. This is the first mention of [[The Gilt Edge]] — though the PCs won't know that name yet. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] wants them to know they're replaceable. Competition motivates. If the PCs performed poorly, he omits this — no need to threaten someone who's already scared.

## Foreshadowing Checklist

This adventure plants seeds for the broader campaign. Track which ones your PCs encountered:

- [ ] **The mirror shows the past with perfect clarity** — first hint that time is relevant to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s plans

- [ ] **Lysara's "favor to be named later"** — a personal debt, mirroring the campaign's themes
- [ ] **[[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s deal / the "gift"** — an outside power is now watching [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], and possibly watching the PCs
- [ ] **[[Snitch Ray]] was listening when the PCs talked about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]** — he's always listening
- [ ] **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] mentions "another crew"** — first hint that the PCs aren't [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s only operatives. Seeds the [[The Gilt Edge]] rivalry

## Appendix: [[The Vanishing Mirror]] — Ongoing Campaign Use

After this adventure, the mirror is in [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s possession (and eventually, integrated into the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]). But its effects may linger:

- **If [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s deal was accepted:** The PCs have a communication line to a vampire lord who is independently tracking [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s activities. He contacts them after every 2-3 heists with increasingly pointed questions
- **If the "gift" variant occurred:** [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] contacts the PCs once, via dream, approximately 3 adventures later. He simply asks: *"Have you learned what he's building yet?"* — and then silence. This is terrifying regardless of whether the PCs know the answer
- **The mirror as Crown component:** When the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] begins activating in later phases, the PCs may notice that [[Offledger]]'s "sky" occasionally flickers and shows scenes from the past — the mirror's influence bleeding into the Crown's architecture. This is a callback to this adventure and a sign that the components are being assembled
