# O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.
*Orderly Planar Enforcement, Retrieval, and Tactical Intervention Vigilance Executive*

> *Extradimensional Men in Black crossed with paladins.*

---

## Overview
O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. is an interplanar law enforcement agency tasked with maintaining order across dimensional boundaries. They are bureaucratic, relentless, well-funded, and utterly humorless — which makes them the perfect foil to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy's]] flamboyant chaos.

They've been tracking Veezy for a long time. They don't know exactly what he's building in [[Offledger]] (the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]) but they're terrified he'll finish it.

## Tone & Aesthetic
- **Men in Black meets Paladins**: perfectly pressed extraplanar fabric, geometric insignias, monotone briefings
- Speak in acronyms, codes, and legalese
- Radiate competence and moral certainty: but are also deeply inflexible and willing to crush small people to catch a big target
- They're not evil, but they're not *good* to the PCs either; the party is a means to an end
- Think FBI handlers running a reluctant informant: polite threats, implied consequences, "we can protect you...if you cooperate"

## Goals
- **Primary:** Apprehend Vizzok the Opulent and dismantle his operation
- **Secondary:** Determine what he's building and prevent its completion
- **Tertiary:** Recover stolen planar artifacts and extradimensional contraband
- **Regarding the PCs:** Flip them into informants. They don't want the small fish; they want the beholder.

## Methods
- Surveillance across planes
- Inserting agents into criminal networks
- Leveraging indentured operatives (like the PCs) as non-consensual informants
- Legal pressure — they can make life very difficult through planar warrants, asset freezes, and dimensional travel restrictions
- Raids when they have enough evidence

## The Pitch to the PCs
*"We don't want you — we want him."*

They'll approach the PCs after the party has been working for Veezy long enough to be complicit but not so long that they're irredeemable. The offer:
- Cooperate, feed us intel, and when we take Veezy down, your debts are cleared and your records expunged
- Refuse, and you go down with him

The catch: Veezy has eyes everywhere (literally). Informing on him is a death sentence if he finds out.

## The Core Tension for PCs
Once O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. makes contact, the players are trapped between two masters:

| | Veezy | O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. |
|---|---|---|
| **Demands** | Tribute, loyalty, dungeon contracts | Intelligence, cooperation, betrayal |
| **Threatens** | Vaporization, spell slot repossession | Arrest, planar imprisonment |
| **Offers** | Power (via the shop and leveling) | Freedom (debt cleared, records expunged) |
| **Trust level** | None (but pays well) | None (but has jurisdiction) |

The players must eventually reclaim their agency and decide: side with Veezy, side with O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E., play both, or burn it all down.

## Resources
- Planar travel capability
- Divination networks
- Legal authority across multiple planes
- Anti-magic countermeasures (they've fought beholders before)
- Informant networks

## Relationship to the Coinspiratorium
They know the shop is a front. They're building a case. The PCs are their way in.

## Relationship to Veezy
Veezy calls them *"jealous former associates with a fear of evolution."* They call him a Class-7 Extradimensional Threat. Both are accurate.

## The IOU Connection
[[Auditor Pentival]] accidentally reveals the Crown Infinite's vulnerability: *"If Lord Vizzok ever possesses outstanding obligations — unpaid, binding, compounded — the Crown would destabilize instantly."* This offhand comment is what makes the PCs realize what [[Veezy's IOU]] actually is.

## Plot Beats
1. **Introduction:** O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. agents show up — maybe during a dungeon mission, maybe at the shop, maybe they pull a PC aside. They know everything.
2. **The Squeeze:** They make their offer. Cooperate or go down with Veezy.
3. **Double Life:** PCs begin feeding intel while maintaining their cover. Tension escalates.
4. **Discovery Pressure:** As they get closer to understanding [[The Device|what Veezy's building]], O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. gets more aggressive — and Veezy gets more paranoid.
5. **The Choice:** O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. wants to move in. Veezy's device is near completion. The PCs must choose a side — or forge a third option.

## Notes
- O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. should feel like a different kind of threat than Veezy — cold where he's hot, procedural where he's chaotic, boring where he's flamboyant
- They're not villains, but they're not heroes either. They'll sacrifice the PCs if it means getting Veezy.
- The acronym is intentionally absurd — lean into the bureaucratic comedy
- Possible recurring bit: every time a new agent introduces themselves, the acronym seems to stand for something slightly different
- Casseel is the only character in the campaign with genuine moral authority — use that sparingly and carefully
- Pentival is the endgame key — make sure the PCs meet him early enough to remember him, but don't make him feel important until it's too late
