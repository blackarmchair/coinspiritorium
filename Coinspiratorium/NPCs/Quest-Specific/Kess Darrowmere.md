# Kess Darrowmere
*AKA "the Fang"*

> *"You talk a lot for someone who still owes the same debt I do."*

---

## Overview
Kess "the Fang" Darrowmere is the leader of [[The Gilt Edge]], a rival adventuring party under contract to [[The Gold Standard]]. A former pit fighter from Calimshan, Kess borrowed from Veezy's organization to buy her freedom from the arena circuit — only to discover that she'd traded one cage for another. She leads Gilt Edge because nobody else in the crew can make hard calls fast enough, and because she'd rather die giving orders than live taking them.

She is the PCs' most direct rival: the one who talks shit, the one who gets there first, the one Veezy quotes when tribute is late.

---

## Appearance
- Mid-thirties, weathered brown skin, short-cropped black hair with a single streak of premature grey
- A constellation of small scars across her knuckles, forearms, and jawline — pit-fighting souvenirs
- Paired short swords worn crossed at her lower back, hilts wrapped in stained leather
- Moves like a fighter who learned footwork before she learned to read
- Permanent scowl — not anger, just the resting state of someone who stopped being surprised a long time ago
- Wears practical leathers, no ornamentation except a single gold tooth (a "gift" from Veezy she can't remove — it's enchanted)

## Personality
- **Direct.** Kess doesn't bluff, doesn't posture, doesn't waste words. She says what she means and means it immediately
- **Competitive.** Everything is a contest. Every room the PCs clear is a room she should have cleared first. She keeps score
- **Protective.** Underneath the scowl, she's fiercely loyal to her crew. Whisper, Ashenveil, and Torque are hers. Threatening them is the fastest way to turn a cold rival into a lethal enemy
- **Honest about dishonesty.** She knows they're all criminals. She doesn't pretend otherwise. She finds the PCs' rationalizations insufferable
- **Respects strength.** Not cruelty, not cleverness — *strength*. The willingness to take a hit and keep standing. Show her that, and her scowl thins by a fraction

## Role
Kess is the face of the PCs' rivalry with Gilt Edge. When Veezy says *"Gilt Edge brought back their contract in half the time,"* he's talking about Kess making it happen. She's the one who leaves the smug notes in pre-cleared dungeon rooms. She's the one who'll stand across from the PCs when both crews converge on the same artifact and say, *"Walk away. You don't want this."*

**DM Guidance:**
- Early game: a name the PCs hear, a shadow they chase. Evidence of Gilt Edge's work (cleared rooms, sprung traps, Kess's boot prints) but no face-to-face until Quest 3
- Mid game: direct encounters. Kess is professional about it — she's not trying to kill the PCs, she's trying to beat them. The difference matters
- Late game: cracks show. Kess has nightmares about the dragon egg's scream (Quest 8). She starts asking questions she shouldn't. She's the Gilt Edge member most likely to punch a PC *and* most likely to stand beside one when it matters

## Connections
- **Faction:** [[The Gilt Edge]] (leader), contracted to [[The Gold Standard]]
- **Allies:** [[Whisper]], [[Brother Ashenveil]], [[Torque]] — her crew
- **Enemies:** Anyone between her and the contract objective. Potentially the PCs, depending on the day
- **Relationship to Veezy:** Debtor. Kess borrowed from The Gold Standard to escape the Calimshan fighting pits. The debt transferred her ownership from one master to another. She knows this. She hates this. She pays on time anyway, because the alternative is worse

---

## Notes
- The gold tooth is enchanted — Veezy can hear through it when he chooses. Kess suspects this but can't prove it. She sometimes talks to it directly: *"I know you're listening, you floating bastard."* Veezy finds this charming
- She remembers debts the way Veezy does — who owes what, who paid, who didn't. This is not a coincidence. It's a survival mechanism learned from watching what happens to people who forget
- If the PCs ever save one of her crew members, her entire posture toward them shifts. She won't say thank you. She'll just stop leaving the smug notes
- Her nightmare about the dragon egg is real and recurring: she hears it screaming inside the shell, and in the dream, it's screaming her name
