# Brother Ashenveil

> *"I used to pray to the goddess of coin for guidance. Now I work for a beholder who thinks he IS a coin. The multiverse has a sense of humor, and it's not a kind one."*

---

## Overview
Brother Ashenveil is the face and healer of [[The Gilt Edge]], a tiefling cleric of the Trickery domain who once served as a priest of Waukeen, the goddess of trade. He embezzled temple funds — not out of greed, but out of a spectacularly bad plan to help someone he shouldn't have helped — and the resulting cascade of consequences landed him in [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s ledger through a chain of increasingly terrible deals.

He's the talker. The one who tries negotiation before bloodshed, charm before force, lies before truth. He's the reasonable member of an unreasonable crew — which makes him the most dangerous, because people believe him.

---

## Appearance
- Tiefling: deep grey-purple skin, short curved horns (left one chipped), a thin prehensile tail he uses for gesturing when agitated
- Lean build, angular features, a con artist's smile that reaches his eyes just enough to be convincing
- Wears vestments that look priestly at first glance but are subtly wrong — the holy symbol of Waukeen sewn inside his collar where nobody can see it, the outer embroidery purely decorative
- Carries a worn wooden holy symbol on a chain around his neck — blank on both sides. He carved Waukeen's face off it himself. He kept the shavings
- A small scar through his left eyebrow from the day the temple guards caught him
- Smells faintly of temple incense — a habit he never broke

## Personality
- **Charming.** Ashenveil could sell sand in Calimshan. He talks the way a lockpick works: finding the mechanism, applying just enough pressure, letting the tumbler fall
- **Amoral (with guilt).** He'll lie, cheat, and manipulate without hesitation — but he'll feel bad about it later. This doesn't stop him. It just makes him drink
- **Conflicted.** He served Waukeen. He betrayed Waukeen. He still believes in Waukeen. He works for a beholder who considers himself the apex of commerce. The theological dissonance is slowly eating him alive
- **The mediator.** When Kess wants to fight and the situation calls for words, Ashenveil steps forward. When the PCs and Gilt Edge are at each other's throats, he's the one who says *"Let's talk about this."* Whether he means it depends on whether talking is more advantageous than fighting
- **Genuinely kind (when he can afford to be).** There's a real person under the grift — someone who became a priest for sincere reasons before everything went wrong. The PCs might glimpse this person in unguarded moments. He will deny it immediately

## Role
Ashenveil is the Gilt Edge member most likely to *talk* to the PCs. He's the negotiator, the diplomat, the one who proposes splitting the objective rather than fighting over it. Whether his proposals are genuine or tactical depends on the situation — and that ambiguity is the point.

**DM Guidance:**
- Ashenveil is the "almost ally" — the rival party member who seems reasonable enough to work with, if only the circumstances were different. Play this tension hard
- In social encounters, he's Gilt Edge's primary speaker. He's good at it. The PCs should sometimes wonder if they're being played — and they should sometimes be right
- He heals Gilt Edge and lies to everyone else. He's good at both
- Late game: Ashenveil starts praying to Waukeen again — quietly, privately, for the first time in years. He won't tell anyone unless asked directly, and even then he'll deflect. If pressed, he'll say: *"I'm hedging my bets. It's what she'd want."* He's lying. He's terrified, and he's asking his goddess what to do about the Crown Infinite

## Connections
- **Faction:** [[The Gilt Edge]], contracted to [[The Gold Standard]]
- **Allies:** [[Kess Darrowmere]], [[Whisper]], [[Torque]]
- **Enemies:** The Temple of Waukeen (formally excommunicated), various creditors from his pre-Veezy debts
- **Relationship to Veezy:** Debtor. Ashenveil embezzled from the Temple of Waukeen — the *goddess of trade*. The resulting debt chain (temple to creditors to intermediaries to Veezy) is so convoluted that Ashenveil once tried to map it and gave up after the third page. Veezy loves the irony: a priest of commerce who committed the one sin his goddess can't forgive. He brings it up constantly

## Notes
- The embezzlement wasn't for himself — he funneled temple funds to help someone escape a bad situation. Who that person was and what happened to them is a thread to pull if the PCs invest in Ashenveil's story. He won't volunteer it. It still hurts
- His blank holy symbol is a deliberate choice. He could carve a new deity's face on it. He could throw it away. He does neither. He wears it blank because he hasn't decided whether he's still Waukeen's or not — and the indecision is the most honest thing about him
- Veezy specifically chose Ashenveil as Gilt Edge's face *because* of the Waukeen connection. A disgraced priest of commerce working for a beholder crime lord is great branding. Veezy has referred to Ashenveil as *"my chaplain"* and *"proof that even the divine can be disrupted"*
- If the PCs share information about the Crown Infinite with Ashenveil, he's the most likely Gilt Edge member to take it seriously — and the most likely to try to do something about it. His faith, however lapsed, gives him a framework for understanding that what Veezy is building is *wrong* in a way that goes beyond crime
