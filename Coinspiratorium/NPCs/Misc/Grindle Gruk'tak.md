# Grindle Gruk'tak
*The most important nobody in the multiverse.*

> *"Y'can have it. I'm never collecting that one."*

> **DM EYES ONLY** — This NPC holds the key to the entire endgame.

---

## Overview
Grindle Gruk'tak is a middle-aged goblin who runs a shabby potion stall in a totally different city from [[Offledger]]. He is deeply unremarkable. He is also, unknowingly, the holder of the single most valuable debt instrument in existence: [[Veezy's IOU]].

Before [[Vizzok the Opulent|Vizzok]] became "Lord Veezy," Grindle was his hype-man, assistant, and reluctant friend. He lent young Vizzok **49gp** for a sequined cape and a mixtape pressing. Vizzok never paid him back. Then Vizzok became terrifying. Grindle ran.

He has no idea the IOU is important. He's just a scared goblin with a drawer full of old junk and a memory he'd rather forget.

## Appearance
- Small, hunched, nervous — the opposite of Veezy in every way
- Wears a stained apron over patched clothes
- Fidgets constantly, avoids eye contact
- His potion stall is cramped, disorganized, and smells like dried herbs and anxiety

## Personality
- **Timid.** Flinches at loud noises. Apologizes preemptively.
- **Loyal (to a fault).** Even after everything, he won't badmouth Veezy directly. Just gets quiet.
- **Surprisingly warm.** If the PCs are kind to him, he opens up. He's lonely.
- **Terrified of Veezy.** Convinced Veezy would disintegrate him on sight. Won't say his name above a whisper.
- **Unaware of his importance.** Has no idea the IOU has metaphysical value. Thinks it's a worthless scrap.

## The IOU
Grindle keeps [[Veezy's IOU]] in a drawer, wrapped in old rags. He's:
- Too embarrassed to tell anyone about it
- Too scared to destroy it
- Too timid to realize its metaphysical value
- Absolutely unwilling to remind Veezy he owes him money

When the PCs help him with a problem, he mentions it casually:

> *"I used to hang with someone you might know. Big guy. Lots of eyes. Talks like a preacher with a gemstone addiction. He owed me forty-nine gold once. Never paid it back. I still have the note, I think."*

He rummages in a drawer. Pulls out the rag-wrapped scrap. Hands it to them:

> *"Y'can have it. I'm never collecting that one."*

His only condition: *"If you ever see him again... don't tell him about me."*

## How the PCs Meet Him
Possible hooks for the side quest:
- **Hired help:** Grindle needs them to move crates, run security for his stall, or recover a stolen herb shipment
- **Random encounter:** They stumble into his shop while on another mission in the city
- **O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. lead:** A cold case file mentions a "former associate" of Veezy's in this area
- **Veezy's mother:** *(If she exists in the campaign, Grindle might live near her)*

The key: **this encounter should feel like a throwaway side quest.** Grindle should seem completely unimportant. The IOU should seem like a curiosity, not a weapon — until the PCs connect the dots later.

## Connections
- **Former associate of:** [[Vizzok the Opulent]] (pre-fame era)
- **Holds:** [[Veezy's IOU]]
- **Faction:** None — he's completely off the grid
- **Known by:** Nobody important. That's the point.

## Notes
- Grindle is the emotional counterweight to Veezy — everything Veezy discarded to become what he is
- Playing him well means making the PCs care about him *before* they realize he matters
- He should feel real: a small person crushed by proximity to greatness
- If the PCs tell him what the IOU is worth, his reaction should be priceless — terror, disbelief, and maybe a little pride
- Consider giving him a quirk the players remember: a catchphrase, a nervous habit, a terrible potion he's proud of

### Special Abilities

**Goblin Traits**
Darkvision 60 ft., +4 to Hide checks (size)

**Potion Dabbler (Ex)**
Grindle can brew basic potions and alchemical remedies (CL 1st equivalent, limited to *cure light wounds*, *endure elements*, antitoxin, smokestick, and tanglefoot bags). His success rate is approximately 70%. The other 30% produces something that "works, technically, but tastes like regret."

**Forgettable (Ex)**
Grindle is so thoroughly unremarkable that creatures who interact with him briefly must make a DC 10 Intelligence check to remember specific details about him 24 hours later. This is not magical — he's just that easy to overlook.

## Notes
- Grindle is not a combat encounter. He is a *narrative* encounter. His statblock exists solely so you have it if things go sideways.
- His CR 1 is generous. He would lose a fight to a moderately aggressive housecat.
- The most important line on this statblock is under Equipment: the drawer. Everything else is flavor. That drawer is the endgame.
