## Agent Quill
*Primary Handler / PC Contact*

Agent Quill is a changeling; and he uses that nature to devastating professional effect. He always appears as the most trustworthy, relatable version of whatever the viewer expects to see: a friendly face from your hometown, a clerk you'd confide in, someone who looks like they *get it.* This isn't deception in Quill's mind. It's "rapport optimization."

Quill is the agent the PCs will interact with most. They're the one who makes the pitch, checks in for intel drops, and maintains the relationship. He is very, very good at making the PCs feel like he's on their team. He is not on their team.

**Appearance:**
- Shifts subtly depending on who he's talking to — nothing dramatic, just small adjustments that make him feel familiar
- In his "default" form: nondescript, medium build, forgettable face, sensible clothes
- Always carrying a thin leather satchel that seems to contain exactly the right document for any conversation
- Smile that reaches his eyes but never quite reaches sincerity

**Personality:**
- **Warm.** Disarmingly so. Asks about your day. Remembers details. Uses your first name.
- **Patient.** Never pushes too hard in one conversation. Lets the pressure build over weeks.
- **Calculating.** Behind the warmth, every interaction is an asset management exercise. He likes the PCs the way a farmer likes productive livestock.
- **Genuinely believes in the mission.** Quill isn't cynical. He truly thinks flipping the PCs is the right move for everyone. That conviction makes him more persuasive — and more dangerous.
- **Occasionally lets the mask slip.** Rare moments where the changeling facade cracks and something colder shows through. These should be unsettling.

**Vibe:** The FBI agent who brings you coffee during the interrogation. Your favorite middle manager. The friend who turns out to have been filing reports on you the whole time.

**Sample Quotes:**
- *"I'm not here to threaten you. I think we both know you're smart enough that I don't have to."*
- *"How's the shop? Good week? ...Good. That's good. Listen, while I have you—"*
- *"I know this is hard. I know you didn't choose this. But the choices you make from here? Those are yours."*
- *"Veezy doesn't know your name. I do. I know all your names. Think about what that means."*
- *"We're not the bad guys. We're just... the other guys."*

**Role at the table:** The primary point of contact. Quill is how O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. feels personal. The PCs might actually like him; which makes the betrayal-tension so much worse. Every time they share intel with Quill, they should wonder: *is this going to come back around?*

**Contrast with Iris Major:** Both are smooth operators. But Iris Major is ice-cold corporate authority — she doesn't pretend to care. Quill pretends beautifully. The question is whether Quill's care is entirely pretend, or if some of it is real. That ambiguity is the point.

### Special Abilities

**Minor Change Shape (Su)**
As a changeling, Quill can alter his appearance at will as if using *alter self* (CL 9th), but with no duration limit. This is a supernatural ability, not a spell, and is not affected by *antimagic field* or *dispel magic*.

**Rapport Optimization (Ex)**
Quill can make a Sense Motive check (DC 15) as a free action when first meeting a creature. On success, he gains +4 to all Charisma-based checks against that creature for the rest of the encounter. He intuitively adjusts his mannerisms, vocabulary, and body language to build trust.

**Dead Drop (Ex)**
Quill always has a prepared escape route. Once per day, if he is reduced to 0 hp or below, or is grappled/restrained, he can use an immediate action to activate a concealed *dimension door* talisman (CL 9th, 900 ft. range) and vanish. He leaves behind a small card that reads: *"We'll talk soon."*

## Notes
- Quill's Bluff +23 and Disguise +33 make him nearly undetectable in social situations — the PCs may interact with him multiple times before realizing it's the same person
- He is not built for combat and will avoid it. If forced to fight, he disengages immediately via Dead Drop
- His *Suggestion* (both bard spell and bardic music) is his most dangerous tool — a friendly suggestion from a trusted face
- The sleep poison bolts are for "difficult extractions," not assassinations. O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. prefers live informants
- Quill has been assigned to the PCs' case specifically. He knows their names, their debts, their fears, and their favorite tavern orders. He's been watching for weeks before first contact.
