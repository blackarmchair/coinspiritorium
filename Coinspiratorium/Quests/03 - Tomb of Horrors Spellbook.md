# "Borrow a Spellbook from the Tomb of Horrors"

> *"Even geniuses have drafts. I need to see what didn't make the cut."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs to the Tomb of Horrors — the most infamous dungeon in existence — to steal [[Acererak]]'s personal research journal from a neglected archive wing. The notebook contains the demilich's failed experiments, abandoned theories, and embarrassingly rough early work on lichdom. It also insults anyone who reads it. When [[Acererak]] notices someone rifling through his drafts, he has a psychic meltdown and the dungeon starts collapsing around the PCs.

This is the campaign's comedy episode. The comedy makes the danger funnier. The danger makes the comedy sharper.

**Tone arc:** Comedy (briefing) → Dread (approach) → Dark comedy (the rejected wing) → Absurdist horror (the notebook) → Chaos (the tantrum) → Breathless laughter (escape) → Uneasy comedy (debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Traps (adapted), exploration, a psychic assault/collapsing dungeon escape
**Combat:** Light (the dungeon fights you environmentally, not with monsters)

---

## Target Item
**[[Acererak's Warm-Up Notebook]]** — A battered leather journal, 200 pages, written in a cramped, furious hand that shifts between Draconic, Abyssal, Infernal, and a personal cipher [[Acererak]] invented to keep people from reading his notes. (The cipher is breakable with a Decipher Script DC 20 or *comprehend languages*. [[Acererak]] was not as clever at age 90 as he thought.)

The notebook contains:
- **Failed lich transformation formulas** (margins full of crossed-out equations and self-deprecating notes: *"This one just makes you dead. Regular dead. Pathetic."*)
- **Theoretical frameworks for soul persistence** — how to make consciousness survive the destruction of its vessel. The early math is wrong but the conceptual architecture is brilliant
- **Trap designs** that didn't make the final Tomb — some embarrassingly simple, others abandoned because even [[Acererak]] thought they were too cruel (there are three)
- **Personal diary entries** — scattered among the arcane theory. [[Acererak]] complaining about his rivals, his phylactery supplier, and the quality of available corpses

The notebook is **semi-sentient** — not alive, but imprinted with enough of [[Acererak]]'s personality to have opinions. Strong ones.

**The Notebook's Voice (read aloud at the table):**
The notebook communicates through text that appears on whatever page you open to. It writes in real time, in elegant script that drips with contempt:

- *"Oh. A reader. How optimistic of you."*
- *"Page-turner? Not you."*
- *"Your Intelligence score is an act of violence against literacy."*
- *"I've been read by liches, archmages, and demigods. And now... this."*
- *"If you're going to hold me, at least have the decency to wash your hands. I can feel your hit points. There aren't many."*
- On a fighter: *"You read with your lips moving. I can tell."*
- On a rogue: *"A thief? At least you're honest about what you are. Wait. No you're not."*
- On a cleric: *"Divine magic. The participation trophy of the arcane world."*
- On a wizard/sorcerer: *"Finally, someone who should be able to appreciate— no. No, I was wrong. Close me."*

The insults are constant, personalized, and surprisingly perceptive. The notebook KNOWS things it shouldn't — it reads the reader as thoroughly as the reader reads it.

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Undeath bypass — the notebook's theoretical framework for soul persistence allows the Crown to make [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s authority survive even his own destruction. If someone kills [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], the Crown's laws persist. He becomes a law of nature that outlasts its author — like gravity after the Big Bang.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
Same back room. [[Iris Major]] is already present and — for the first time — she's standing rather than sitting. Her clipboard is held slightly tighter than usual. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] floats in late, which is unusual. He's been in a good mood. This is worse than a bad mood.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"Quick one. In-and-out. I need a notebook from a tomb."*

Pause. He's smiling.

> *"The Tomb. Of Horrors."*

He watches their faces.

> *"Don't make that face. It's not the main tomb. It's like... the back room. The storage unit. Nobody goes there because there's no treasure — just some old notes from a guy who got famous later. I need those notes."*

**If PCs point out that the Tomb of Horrors has killed more adventurers than any dungeon in history:**
> *"See, that's marketing. [[Acererak]] got a reputation and now everybody thinks his whole crib is lethal. The FRONT is lethal. The showcase. That's the part he designed after he got good. I'm sending you to the part he built when he was still figuring shit out. His rough drafts. His B-sides."*

**If PCs ask why [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] wants a failed lich's early notes:**
> *"Because failure is instructive. Because rough drafts show the process. Because I need to understand how a man designed himself to survive death, and the final product don't show the work. The work shows the work."*

This is the most genuine thing [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has said about why he wants an artifact. He catches himself.

> *"Also it's rare. Rare is valuable. Don't overthink it."*

**If PCs ask what the notebook does:**
> *"It's a book. It sits there. You read it. That's what books do. Did nobody teach y'all about books?"*

### Logistics (delivered by [[Iris Major]])
[[Iris Major]]'s briefing is uncharacteristically short:

- **Portal:** Deposits the PCs in the Vast Swamp, approximately 1 hour's hike from the Tomb's exterior. This portal is two-way — the PCs can return through the same location at any time within 24 hours
- **Entry:** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has identified an **auxiliary entrance** — not the main entrance (which is a gauntlet of lethal traps), but a maintenance access point on the Tomb's eastern face. It leads to what [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] calls "the reject wing"
- **Intel:** [[Iris Major]] provides a partial map of the auxiliary entrance and first corridor, purchased from a survivor (the only one). The map has a note at the bottom in shaky handwriting: *"DON'T TOUCH THE WALLS"*
- **Special equipment:** Two *potions of gaseous form* (for emergency escape through cracks) and a *lead-lined scroll case* to contain the notebook once acquired. [[Iris Major]]: *"The notebook is reported to be... communicative. The case should reduce this."*
- **Warning:** *"The Tomb of Horrors is classified as a Level 5 Existential Hazard by [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]'s planar registry. Our operational mandate does not extend to recovery of remains. Return alive or do not return."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Parting Words

> *"One more thing. When you find the notebook — don't read it."*

He pauses.

> *"I mean, you're gonna read it. I know you're gonna read it. Just don't believe what it says about you. It's... opinionated."*

Longer pause.

> *"And if it says anything about beholders, that's outdated scholarship and I reject it categorically."*

### DM Notes
- [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s line about failure being instructive is a real window into his thinking. He's not just collecting components — he's *studying* the work of other beings who tried to transcend their nature. [[Acererak]] achieved undeath. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] wants to achieve something bigger. He's doing his homework
- The two-way portal is intentional — this adventure is shorter and more contained than [[Menzoberranzan]]. The PCs can retreat. The question is whether they'll want to once [[Acererak]] notices them
- [[Iris Major]] standing instead of sitting is a small detail that players may notice. If they ask, she says nothing. She's worried. She won't say that
- The lead-lined scroll case for the notebook is both practical (it actually muffles the notebook's commentary) and funny (the idea that they need containment equipment for a rude book)

## Scene 2: The Approach

### The Vast Swamp
The portal deposits them in knee-deep marsh water under a grey, sunless sky. The air is warm, wet, and smells like rot. The Vast Swamp is one of the most miserable environments in the campaign — not dangerous in an exciting way, just *unpleasant*.

**The hike:**
One hour through sucking mud, biting insects, and the occasional half-submerged skeleton wearing adventurer's gear. Some of the skeletons are very old. Some are not. None of them made it to the Tomb. That's how bad the Tomb's *neighborhood* is.

### The Tomb Exterior
The Tomb of Horrors is built into a hillside — a low, artificial mound rising from the swamp like a burial cairn. The main entrance is a yawning darkness flanked by carved demon faces. Even from a distance, the PCs can feel it: a weight, a *wrongness*, like the air itself knows this is a place where people come to die.

**The auxiliary entrance** is around the east side — a narrow crack in the hillside, partially concealed by overgrowth. It's not hidden so much as *ignored*. There's no carved entrance. No demon faces. No warning. Just a gap in the stone, three feet wide, leading down into darkness.

This is the door [[Acererak]] didn't bother to decorate. The door to the work he didn't want anyone to see.

> A crude inscription above the crack, in [[Acererak]]'s hand (Draconic): *"STORAGE. DO NOT ENTER. THIS MEANS YOU. (IT ESPECIALLY MEANS YOU, VECNA.)"*

### DM Notes
- The contrast between the Tomb's main entrance (legendary, imposing, lethal) and the auxiliary entrance (a crack in a hill with a passive-aggressive sign) sets the tone for the entire adventure. This is the Tomb's junk drawer
- The skeletons in the swamp are a reminder: the Tomb's reputation is earned. Even the approach is littered with the dead. The PCs are going to a slightly less lethal section, but "slightly less lethal" in the Tomb of Horrors is still extremely dangerous
- The Vecna callout is a real detail — [[Acererak]] and Vecna are rivals in undeath. The pettiness is authentic

## Scene 3: The Rejected Wing

### Overview
The auxiliary entrance leads into a section of the Tomb that [[Acererak]] built early in his career — before he perfected his craft. It's where he prototyped traps, tested theories, and stored everything he considered beneath his final vision. It has been neglected for centuries.

The wing is smaller than the main Tomb — five rooms connected by corridors, leading to the Archive at the far end. The architecture is the same black stone as the main Tomb, but the craftsmanship is... less. Carvings are unfinished. Joints are visible. One wall has a penciled layout sketch still faintly visible beneath the stone facade. This is the rough cut.

### Room 1: The Entry Corridor
A 60-foot corridor of black stone. The walls are lined with alcoves — some containing ceramic urns, some empty, one containing a skeleton posed in what [[Acererak]] apparently thought was an intimidating position (it's slouching).

**Trap: The Shallow Pit**
Midway through the corridor, a 10-foot section of floor is a pit trap.

- Spot DC 14 to notice the seams
- The pit is **5 feet deep**. Not 50. Not 100. Five
- At the bottom, scratched into the stone in [[Acererak]]'s handwriting: *"DEEPER (TODO)"*
- Falling in deals 1d6 damage (it's still a 5-foot drop onto stone) and is *embarrassing*, not lethal
- **The real trap:** The alcove nearest the pit contains a pressure plate (Search DC 16). Stepping on it fires a **poison dart** from the opposite wall (ranged attack +8, 1d4 + poison: Fort DC 15 or 1d6 Con damage). This one works. The pit was a distraction

### Room 2: The Flame Hall
A 40-foot corridor with scorched walls. Iron nozzles line both sides at waist height — the remnants of a flame trap. When the PCs enter, flames erupt from the nozzles.

**The twist:** The flames are **mostly illusory** (Will DC 13 to disbelieve). [[Acererak]] prototyped the trap with illusion magic before investing in real fire.

- The first 30 feet of flames are completely fake. Walk through them for 0 damage
- The last 10 feet of flames are **real** — [[Acererak]] finished that section. 3d6 fire damage (Reflex DC 14 half)
- There's no visual difference between the fake and real flames. A PC who disbelieves the first section might assume the whole thing is illusory and walk into the real fire
- **Detection:** A DC 16 Spellcraft check while observing reveals the final section has a different magical signature (evocation vs. illusion). Alternatively, throwing an object through each section works — the object burns in the last 10 feet
- A handwritten note on the wall near the entrance (Knowledge [arcana] DC 12 or Search DC 14 to find): *"FINISH REAL FLAMES LATER. BUDGET ISSUE."* This is genuine — [[Acererak]] ran out of materials. Even demiliches have budget problems

### Room 3: The Riddle Door
A stone door blocks the corridor. Above it, carved in ornate script:

> *"I am the end of all things, the beginning of eternity, the—"*

The inscription stops mid-sentence. Below it, in smaller, angrier handwriting:

> *"This riddle is terrible. Starting over. Leave door unlocked for now."*

The door is unlocked. Push it open (no check required).

- Behind the door: a 20-foot chamber with three pedestals. Two are empty. One holds a **small stone sphere** covered in dust. It radiates faint necromancy
- The sphere is one of [[Acererak]]'s prototype phylacteries. It doesn't work — the soul-binding matrix is incomplete. But it's still a necromantic artifact worth **150gp** to the right buyer. Handling it requires a Will save (DC 12) or the PC hears a faint whisper of [[Acererak]]'s voice saying *"Put that back"*
- **The real threat in this room:** The floor in front of the third pedestal is a **pressure plate** (Search DC 18) that triggers a **scything blade** from the wall (attack +12, 2d6+4 slashing). This trap works perfectly. It was the one thing in this room [[Acererak]] finished

### Room 4: The False Sphere
A larger chamber, 30 feet square, with a domed ceiling. In the center of the room, suspended in a metal frame: a **matte black sphere**, 2 feet in diameter.

Every adventurer in the world knows what a sphere of annihilation looks like. This looks exactly like one.

**It's not.** It's a painted wooden sphere. [[Acererak]] was prototyping the visual before installing the real thing in the main Tomb.

- Any PC who has heard of the Tomb of Horrors (Knowledge [dungeoneering] DC 10 — most adventurers have) knows about the sphere of annihilation at the main entrance. Seeing this one should cause immediate panic
- **Detecting the fake:** *Detect magic* shows no magic whatsoever. Physical inspection (touching it — DC 0 Bravery check, functionally) reveals it's wood. Knowledge (arcana) DC 14 confirms: no annihilation field, no nothing. It's a prop
- Scratched into the metal frame: *"PLACEHOLDER. ORDER REAL ONE FROM SOULMONGER CATALOG. ITEM #4471."*
- **The comedy:** The PCs just spent 30 seconds terrified of a painted ball. The relief should be enormous
- **The horror:** This means the REAL sphere is in the main Tomb. And someone else might be in there right now

**The real threat in this room:** The frame holding the fake sphere is rigged. Removing the sphere (even though it's worthless) activates a **teleportation trap** — the PC who touched it last must make a Will save (DC 16) or be teleported to a sealed stone coffin elsewhere in the rejected wing. The coffin is airtight. The PC has approximately 10 rounds of air (more with Survival or Con checks). The rest of the party can hear muffled banging if they succeed on a Listen DC 12. Finding the coffin requires a Search DC 16 or *locate creature*. Opening it requires Strength DC 18 or *knock*

### Room 5: The Hallway of Faces
A long corridor (50 feet) with carved faces on both walls — demonic, skeletal, and grotesque. In the original Tomb, this is where the green devil face with the sphere of annihilation sits. Here, the faces are...

**Unfinished.** Some are fully carved and menacing. Some are half-done, with chisel marks still visible. One is clearly [[Acererak]]'s own face but carved badly — he did it himself and the proportions are wrong. Another is labeled *"SCARY FACE #7 (REDO)"* in pencil on the stone.

**Spectral Sentinels (x2):**
The hallway is haunted. When any living creature enters, two **spectral sentinels** rise from the floor — translucent, screaming figures whose mouths move in constant maddened babbling. These are the remnants of adventurers who died testing [[Acererak]]'s prototype traps centuries ago, their souls tethered to the corridor as involuntary guards.

- Medium Undead (Incorporeal), CR 3 each (modified Allips, MM p.10)
- HP 26 | AC 15 (touch 15) | Speed Fly 30 ft. (perfect)
- **Incorporeal Touch +3 melee** (1d4 Wisdom drain — Will DC14 negates)
- **Babbling (Su):** 60 ft. radius. All sane creatures must make **Will DC 14** or be *fascinated* for 2d4 rounds. Creatures that save are immune for 24 hours
- Incorporeal: Only harmed by magic weapons, spells, or force effects. 50% miss chance from corporeal sources

**The Babbling:** Not random — it's fragments of trap warnings from the rooms the PCs have already passed through, delivered too late and in the wrong order:
- *"...don't step on the... the fire is only real at the... the door is unlocked but the BLADE..."*
- *"...DEEPER TODO... the sphere is WOOD but the frame... the coffin... no air..."*
- *"...he wrote 'do not enter'... three languages... he MEANT it..."*

A **Listen DC 16** while fascinated reveals one useful detail buried in the babble: *"...the archive... five rounds... he WAKES UP..."* — a garbled warning about the alarm in Scene 4.

**Tactics:** The sentinels attack the nearest living creature and don't coordinate. They cannot leave the Hallway. If both are destroyed, the babbling echoes for 1d4 rounds before fading.

**DM Guidance:** The Wisdom drain is thematic setup for [[Acererak]]'s psychic tantrum (Scene 5), which also targets Wisdom. PCs who lose Wisdom here are more vulnerable later — this creates resource tension. The babbling-as-trap-warnings is dark comedy: the dungeon's own ghosts trying to help, too broken to do it right.

- **The faces that are finished** each have a small dark hole where the mouth is. Three of the seven finished faces have active traps:
  - **Face 1 (demon):** *Burning hands* (3d4 fire, Reflex DC 13 half) triggered by proximity (5 feet)
  - **Face 4 (skull):** Emits a *fear* effect (Will DC 14, 30 ft. cone, shaken 1d4 rounds)
  - **Face 7 ([[Acererak]]'s self-portrait):** A *gust of wind* pushes the nearest PC backward 15 feet. This was supposed to be something more lethal — the margin note says *"REPLACE WITH DISINTEGRATE LATER"*
- **The unfinished faces** are harmless. One still has a note taped to it: *"Ask Vecna's people about the green fire effect. DO NOT credit him."*
- **Detection:** Search DC 15 reveals which mouths have active mechanisms. The active faces have a faint smell of ozone (burned magic components)

**At the end of the corridor:** A heavy iron door, slightly ajar. Beyond it: the Archive.

### DM Notes
- The Rejected Wing should feel like walking through a genius's embarrassing early work. Every room is a trap [[Acererak]] abandoned, a design he wasn't satisfied with, or a prototype that didn't work as intended. The comedy is natural — don't oversell it. Let the players find the *"DEEPER (TODO)"* note and laugh on their own
- **The real traps still hurt.** The poison dart, the scything blade, the real flames, the teleportation coffin — these are all potentially dangerous to level 4-5 PCs. The comedy of the broken traps lowers the players' guard, and then a working trap reminds them where they are. This is the Tomb of Horrors. Even the rejects can kill
- The fake sphere of annihilation is the wing's best moment. Build the tension — describe it exactly like a sphere of annihilation. Let the players panic. Let the rogue make a 40-word speech about how they're all going to die. Then someone detects no magic and touches it and it's *wood*. The laughter should be enormous. Then the teleportation trap fires and someone's sealed in a coffin and the laughter stops
- [[Acererak]]'s notes and margin scribbles are DM flavor — improvise more if the players are enjoying them. The demilich's early career is a goldmine of comedy: budget problems, rivalry with Vecna, bad riddles, prototype phylacteries

## Scene 4: The Archive

### The Room
A chamber carved from the bedrock, 25 feet square, with stone shelving covering every wall. The shelves are filled with:
- Scrolls (most crumbling, some still legible)
- Failed phylactery prototypes (ceramic jars, crystal orbs, a taxidermied raven that clearly didn't work)
- Stacks of loose parchment covered in equations, diagrams, and crossed-out incantations
- One shelf labeled *"GOOD IDEAS"* that is completely empty
- One shelf labeled *"BAD IDEAS"* that is overflowing

In the center of the room: a stone lectern. On the lectern: **[[Acererak]]'s Warm-Up Notebook**.

It's smaller than expected. Battered leather, pages yellowed, spine cracked. It looks like something you'd find in a used bookshop. It is the most dangerous book the PCs have ever held.

### The Notebook Wakes Up
The moment a PC touches the notebook, text appears on the open page:

> *"Someone's touching me."*

Pause. More text:

> *"Someone with... let me assess... oh. Oh, this is unfortunate."*

The notebook begins its running commentary. From this point forward, any time a PC opens the notebook, looks at it, or carries it, the text updates. It can hear them (it senses vibration through its pages). It responds to conversation, though there's a slight delay — it has to write fast.

**Sample first interactions:**

If the PC is a fighter or barbarian:
> *"A martial. You found the archive. Congratulations. Did someone lead you here, or was it the pictures on the wall?"*

If the PC is a wizard or sorcerer:
> *"An arcanist. Finally. What's your specialty? ...Don't tell me. I can feel your spellbook from here. Thin. Like your understanding of metamagic."*

If the PC is a cleric:
> *"A priest. Delightful. Tell your god I said the afterlife infrastructure is shoddy. I should know — I redesigned it."*

If the PC is a rogue:
> *"Ah. A thief. At least you're on-brand for what you're doing right now."*

**If multiple PCs are present:**
> *"There are several of you. A party. How quaint. Like a field trip to a graveyard. Which is literally what this is."*

### Reading the Notebook
The notebook's actual content is visible between the insults — dense arcane theory, soul mechanics, and necrotic engineering. A PC with Spellcraft DC 18 or Knowledge (arcana) DC 20 can parse the theoretical framework and identify:

- [[Acererak]]'s early theory of **soul persistence** — how to make a consciousness survive the destruction of its vessel. The math is wrong in places but the concepts are sound
- **The phrase "contractual persistence"** appears in a marginal note on page 74: *"Investigate whether binding agreements survive death of the parties. If soul anchors function through obligation rather than necromancy, the applications are... significant. TODO: find a lawyer."*
- A diagram labeled *"ALTERNATIVE PERSISTENCE MODELS"* that includes lichdom (completed, circled), divine ascension (crossed out, annotated *"requires worship, degrading"*), and a third option labeled *"ECONOMIC BINDING"* with a question mark

PCs who read this won't fully understand it yet. But the phrase "contractual persistence" and the "economic binding" model will become deeply relevant when they learn about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]].

### What Else Is in the Archive

| Item | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Scroll of *vampiric touch* (CL 5) | 375gp | Intact, usable |
| Scroll of *gentle repose* (CL 3) | 150gp | Slightly singed |
| Failed phylactery (crystal orb) | 200gp (collector's item) | Non-functional, radiates faint necromancy, whispers when held |
| [[Acererak]]'s sketches of trap designs | 50gp (to an engineer or dungeon-builder) | Includes three traps "too cruel even for me" — genuinely horrifying ideas |
| A letter from Vecna, unsent reply | 75gp (to a historian) | Vecna wrote asking for collaboration. [[Acererak]]'s draft reply is six pages of increasingly creative insults. He never sent it. Possibly his greatest regret |

### The Alarm
**5 rounds after the notebook is touched**, the PCs feel it: a pressure change in the air, a drop in temperature, and a low vibration that comes from everywhere and nowhere.

The notebook's text changes:

> *"Oh no."*

New line:

> *"He knows. He felt me wake up. You need to leave. Now."*

Then, more slowly:

> *"I take back 40% of the things I said about you. Please run."*

**[[Acererak]] is psychically present.** He's not here physically — his demilich form is deep in the main Tomb, dreaming in its throne of bone. But his consciousness has noticed someone in his reject pile. Someone touching his *early work*. His *drafts*. His *process*.

This is the worst thing that has ever happened to him.

### DM Notes
- The Archive scene has two layers: comedy (the notebook, the empty "GOOD IDEAS" shelf, the Vecna letter) and horror (the contract persistence theory, the alarm). Let the comedy play out first. Give the PCs time to laugh, explore, and read the notebook's insults. THEN the alarm hits
- **The notebook's personality shift when the alarm triggers is important.** It goes from arrogant and contemptuous to genuinely scared — not of the PCs, but of [[Acererak]]. The notebook doesn't want to be destroyed, and [[Acererak]] WILL try to destroy it rather than let anyone see his early work. This gives the PCs an unexpected ally: the book that's been insulting them now needs them to survive
- The "contractual persistence" note is the adventure's most important foreshadowing beat. It connects [[Acererak]]'s research directly to the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s mechanics. A PC who reads this and later learns about the Crown will have a moment of horrifying clarity: [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] didn't just want [[Acererak]]'s research — he wanted his *blueprint*
- The letter from Vecna is pure gold at the table. If a PC reads [[Acererak]]'s draft reply aloud, encourage them to really commit to the contempt. Six pages of creative insults directed at the god of secrets

## Scene 5: [[Acererak]]'s Tantrum

### The Psychic Assault
[[Acererak]]'s consciousness floods the rejected wing. He cannot physically manifest here — his awareness is spread thin, reaching across the Tomb from his throne room — but he can warp reality within his domain. And he is *furious*.

**Phase 1: The Voice (Rounds 1-3)**
[[Acererak]]'s voice fills the dungeon — not from any direction, but from inside the PCs' skulls. It's cold, precise, and vibrating with restrained rage.

> *"WHO IS IN MY ARCHIVE?"*

Silence. Then:

> *"THOSE ARE ROUGH DRAFTS."*

> *"THOSE NOTES ARE PRELIMINARY. THEY DO NOT REPRESENT MY CURRENT CAPABILITIES."*

> *"I EXPLICITLY LABELED THAT SECTION 'DO NOT OPEN.' IN THREE LANGUAGES."*

**Mechanical effect:** At the start of each round during Phase 1, every PC must make a **Will save (DC 15)** or take **1d4 Wisdom damage** from the psychic pressure of a demilich's concentrated embarrassment. This is not an attack — it's the emotional equivalent of standing next to a supernova of narcissistic rage. PCs who succeed on the save feel the pressure but resist it. PCs who fail feel [[Acererak]]'s mortification as their own — they're suddenly, horribly aware of their own worst moments, their own early failures, their own rough drafts.

**The notebook responds** (text appears frantically):
> *"Ignore him. He's always like this when someone finds the early work. Just MOVE. NOW."*

### Phase 2: The Dungeon Warps (Rounds 4-8)
[[Acererak]] can't kill the PCs directly at this range, but he can reshape his Tomb around them. The rejected wing begins to change:

**Round 4:** The walls start **closing in** — the corridor from the Archive to Room 5 narrows by 1 foot per round. PCs must move through at normal speed or begin making Escape Artist checks (DC 14) to squeeze through. This is a time pressure mechanic, not a death trap — the walls stop at 2 feet apart (uncomfortable but passable for Medium creatures, easy for Small)

### Encounter: Test Subject Remains (Round 4-5)
As the walls close in, they crack open alcoves that weren't visible before — sealed niches containing the remains of [[Acererak]]'s **test subjects**. These were adventurers [[Acererak]] captured centuries ago to beta-test his rejected traps. The psychic tantrum reanimates them as **skeletal husks** — barely coherent undead still wearing their testing harnesses (leather straps, numbered tags, one still clutching a clipboard).

**Test Subject Skeletons (x4)** — Modified Skeletons (MM p.226), CR 1 each
- Medium Undead | HP 6 each | AC 13 (natural armor + rusted chain fragments) | Speed 30 ft.
- **Claw +1 melee** (1d4+1)
- **Testing Harness:** Each skeleton has a random malfunctioning trap component strapped to it (roll d4 or choose):

| d4 | Trap Component | Effect |
|----|---------------|--------|
| 1 | Flame nozzle (broken) | On death, 5-ft. burst of fire — 1d6 fire, Reflex DC 12 half |
| 2 | Dart launcher (jammed) | 50% chance each round to fire a dart at a random creature within 10 ft. — +4 ranged, 1d4 damage |
| 3 | Glyph plate (cracked) | On hit, Fort DC 13 or *dazed* 1 round from a weak shock |
| 4 | Spring mechanism | On death, launches the skeleton's upper body 15 ft. in a random direction — anyone in the path takes 1d6 bludgeoning, Reflex DC 11 to dodge |

- Undead traits: immune to mind-affecting, poison, disease, death effects, critical hits, nonlethal damage
- **Not intelligent.** They don't coordinate. They stumble out of the walls and attack whatever they can reach. They're less "guards" and more "dungeon debris that happens to be hostile"

**Tactics:** They emerge into the narrowing corridor (which is now 4 feet wide and closing), creating a terrible traffic jam. The PCs must fight through them AND escape the closing walls simultaneously. The skeletons don't care about the walls — they're already dead. They just block passage and occasionally explode.

**DM Notes:**
- This encounter is funny and lethal in equal measure. Numbered test subjects with clipboards. Trap components strapped to their bodies like lab equipment gone wrong. One of them has a name tag that reads "SUBJECT 7: DISAPPOINTING"
- The real danger isn't the skeletons (6 HP each, easily dispatched) — it's the TIME they cost. Every round spent fighting is a round the walls close further and a round closer to the exit sealing shut. The PCs should be hacking through skeletons while shouting about corridor width
- The trap components firing randomly add chaos to an already chaotic scene. A dart launcher that shoots at random creatures — including the skeleton's own allies — is peak Tomb of Horrors energy: even the test subjects are booby-trapped

**Round 5:** **Gravity rotates 90 degrees** in Room 4 (the False Sphere room). The floor becomes a wall. The ceiling becomes the floor. Everything not bolted down falls sideways. PCs in the room must make Reflex saves (DC 14) or take 2d6 damage from falling into the "new" floor (the former wall). The fake sphere rolls across the room like a bowling ball

**Round 6:** The faces in the Hallway of Faces (Room 5) **all activate simultaneously** — every face, finished or not, fires its trap. The finished faces deal their normal effects; the unfinished faces produce random, incomplete magical effects:
- One emits a cloud of butterflies (harmless, obscures vision for 1 round)
- One sprays ice-cold water
- One speaks a half-formed incantation that gives everyone in 10 feet a splitting headache (-1 to attacks for 2 rounds)
- One shoots a beam of light that turns the nearest PC's hair white for 24 hours

**Round 7:** **The Floor Becomes Transparent** in Room 3 (the Riddle Door room). The PCs can see through the stone floor to the chambers below — the REAL Tomb of Horrors, with its lethal traps, its treasures, and at the very bottom, in a throne room of bone, a tiny gemlike skull surrounded by fading light. **[[Acererak]]'s demilich form.** It's looking up at them. For one second, the PCs and the most dangerous undead in existence make eye contact through a transparent floor.

Then the floor goes solid again.

> The notebook: *"DON'T LOOK DOWN. Why did you look down? I SAID DON'T."*

**Round 8:** The exit corridor (Room 1, the Entry Corridor) begins to **seal shut** — the walls are extruding stone to close the crack the PCs entered through. They have **3 rounds** before the exit closes completely. After that, they're trapped in the rejected wing with an angry demilich's psychic attention focused on them

### Phase 3: [[Acererak]]'s Last Demand (Round 9+)
As the PCs approach the exit, [[Acererak]]'s voice changes. The rage is still there, but underneath it — barely — there's something else. Something almost vulnerable.

> *"PUT IT BACK."*

Quieter:

> *"That notebook is... preliminary. It's not... it doesn't represent... I was YOUNG. I was still developing my methodology."*

Quieter still:

> *"Those notes were never meant to be published."*

**The PCs feel it:** beneath the fury, beneath the cosmic power, beneath the centuries of undeath — [[Acererak]] is *embarrassed*. Mortified. A genius forced to confront his early, imperfect work. The most powerful undead mind in existence is having the emotional equivalent of someone reading your high school poetry.

**The notebook's text, one final line before they escape:**
> *"He'll get over it. He won't forget. But he'll get over it."*

Then:

> *"...Thank you for not leaving me."*

The text fades. The notebook goes quiet — for now.

### DM Notes
- [[Acererak]]'s tantrum is the adventure's set piece and it should be played for maximum chaos. The dungeon is warping around the PCs, gravity is shifting, faces are firing, and the most powerful undead in the multiverse is screaming about his creative process. It's terrifying and hilarious simultaneously
- **The Wisdom damage is the real threat.** 1d4 Wis damage per round for 3 rounds can seriously impair a party. If a PC hits Wis 0, they're *feebleminded* — catatonic from the psychic pressure. This keeps the danger real while the comedy runs
- **The transparent floor moment is the adventure's horror beat.** The PCs see [[Acererak]]'s true form and it sees them. This should land with absolute silence at the table. For one second, the comedy stops and the PCs remember what this place is
- **[[Acererak]]'s vulnerability at the end is intentional.** He's not sympathetic — he's a demilich mass murderer. But the moment of exposed vanity makes him human (figuratively). This mirrors [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]: both are beings of enormous power and enormous ego who cannot bear to be seen at less than their best. The notebook is [[Acererak]]'s rough draft; the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s
- The notebook thanking the PCs is the only genuine emotional beat the book offers. It should land gently. Then it goes back to insulting them next time they open it

## Scene 6: The Escape

### Getting Out
The PCs burst out of the closing crack in the hillside and into the Vast Swamp. Behind them, the stone seals shut. The auxiliary entrance is gone — [[Acererak]] has sealed it permanently. No one is entering his rejected wing again.

The swamp is exactly as miserable as it was on the way in. The fog is thicker now. It's raining. The notebook, from inside the lead-lined scroll case, produces muffled text that only the carrier can faintly read through the case's seams:

> *"The humidity is unacceptable. I'm a first edition."*

### The Contested Exit — [[The Gilt Edge]]
The PCs burst out of the sealing crack — and they're not alone.

Four figures are already in the swamp clearing outside the auxiliary entrance. They're muddy, armed, and carrying their own lead-lined scroll case. They got here first. They're trying to get in. And the entrance just sealed behind the PCs.

This is **[[The Gilt Edge]]** — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s other crew.

**The Setup:** [[The Gilt Edge]] was sent to retrieve the notebook on the same contract, on a parallel timeline. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] told both crews about the auxiliary entrance and let them race. (This is his management philosophy: competition produces results.) [[The Gilt Edge]] arrived 30 minutes after the PCs entered and have been waiting outside, growing increasingly nervous as the dungeon began to rumble.

**[[The Gilt Edge]] (CR 3 each, 4 members):**
- **[[Kess "the Fang" Darrowmere]]** — Human fighter 3. Leader. Paired short swords, permanent scowl. She steps forward first: *"That's our contract. Hand it over."*
- **[[Whisper]]** — Changeling rogue 3. Hangs back, face shifting through subtle expressions. Watching everything, saying nothing
- **[[Brother Ashenveil]]** — Tiefling cleric 3 (Trickery). The talker. *"Let's all just take a breath. Nobody needs to bleed in a swamp."*
- **[[Torque]]** — Warforged artificer 3. Standing perfectly still. Holding an adamantine wrench the size of a short sword. Not blinking (never blinks)

**Resolution Options (5-Round Timer):**

The entrance is sealed. [[Acererak]]'s psychic pressure is fading but the swamp is getting darker. Both crews need to reach the extraction portal. The PCs have what [[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]] wants. This can resolve several ways:

1. **Combat:** [[The Gilt Edge]] fights as a team — [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] engages the strongest PC, [[Torque]] flanks, [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] heals and buffs, [[Whisper]] goes for the scroll case. They're professional but not suicidal. **If 2+ members drop below half HP, [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] calls retreat.** They disappear into the swamp. This isn't worth dying over — yet.

2. **Negotiation (Diplomacy DC 18 or Bluff DC 16):** [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] is open to talking. Options:
   - *"We got here first. The contract's fulfilled. Take it up with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]."* — [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] respects strength. She doesn't like it, but she accepts it
   - *"Split the credit — we cleared the dungeon, you got the intel."* — [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] can sell this to the crew. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] will decide who gets the bonus
   - *"We'll owe you one."* — Dangerous. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] remembers debts the way [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] does

3. **Intimidation (DC 16):** The PCs just survived a demilich's tantrum. If they can convey that convincingly, [[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]] backs off. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]]: *"Fine. Next time."*

4. **Speed:** The PCs just run. They have a head start if they move immediately. [[The Gilt Edge]] pursues for 3 rounds, then falls back — they don't know the exact portal location.

**After the Encounter:**
However it resolves, [[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]] exists now. The PCs have rivals. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] will compare the two crews at the next debrief. The competition has begun.

The notebook, from inside the scroll case:
> *(muffled)* *"Were those more of you? There are MORE of you? How many indentured idiots does one beholder need?"*

### The Walk Back
The return hike to the portal is uneventful — [[Acererak]]'s influence doesn't extend beyond the Tomb. But the PCs are rattled, potentially Wisdom-damaged, and carrying a sentient book that won't shut up.

**Optional encounter:** A group of adventurers heading TOWARD the Tomb — 4 well-equipped NPCs, clearly experienced, asking the PCs if they've "come from in there." They're planning to tackle the main entrance. The PCs can warn them, lie to them, or ignore them.

- If warned: the adventurers thank them, look at each other, and go in anyway. Adventurers
- If asked for the party's name: "The Platinum Shield." A high-level party that has a real chance. Or doesn't. The PCs will never know
- **The point:** Other people die here. Regularly. The PCs got what they came for and lived. That's not nothing

### DM Notes
- The escape is short and the walk back is breathing room. The PCs just survived a psychic assault from a demilich — let them decompress, joke about it, and marvel at the notebook's continued rudeness
- The Platinum Shield encounter is optional but effective. It grounds the Tomb's lethality — these aren't beginners going in. And the PCs can't do anything about it. The world keeps turning
- The notebook in the scroll case is a running gag from here to the debrief. Muffled insults. Let the carrier improvise what they can read through the seams

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal returns them to [[Offledger]]. [[Iris Major]] is waiting. She takes the scroll case, examines it at arm's length, and notes:

> *"The asset appears active. Quarantine protocols may be required."*

The notebook, muffled: *(unintelligible text through the case, but agitated)*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction
This is different from the previous debriefs. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doesn't just receive the artifact — he **opens it and reads it**. In front of the PCs.

He takes the notebook out of the case, holds it in a telekinetic grip (hovering in front of his central eye), and begins flipping pages. His eye moves rapidly. He's speed-reading with the focus of someone studying for an exam.

The notebook, suddenly aware of its new reader:

> *"Another one. Wonderful. Let me assess... significant magical signature... unusual physiology... aberrant origin... wait. A beholder?"*

Pause.

> *"A BEHOLDER is reading me? I've been held by Mordenkainen. I've been cited by Elminster. And now a BEHOLDER? This is the worst day of my existence, and I once spent forty years in a ceramic jar."*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doesn't react to the insult. He's still reading. His eye moves faster. The PCs have never seen him this focused. There's no performance, no swagger, no jewelry adjustment. This is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doing *work*.

After two full minutes of silence (an eternity for [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]), he closes the notebook.

> *"It's real."*

He says this to [[EyeQ]], who floats at the edge of the room. [[EyeQ]]'s response is barely audible:

> *"The persistence framework?"*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]:

> *"The persistence framework."*

This is the first time the PCs have heard [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] and [[EyeQ]] discuss the artifacts in technical terms. It's brief. It's quiet. And it's the most alarming thing they've witnessed from their employer — more than the swagger, more than the violence. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] and [[EyeQ]] are *engineers*, and the project is on schedule.

**If PCs ask what "the persistence framework" means:**
> *"It means the notebook is exactly what I needed it to be. You did good. Go get paid."*

**If PCs press further:**
> *"It means I'm one step closer to never having to explain myself to anyone. Including you. Matter-fact: especially you."*

**If PCs mention [[Acererak]]'s emotional reaction:**
> *"Even gods got a rough draft. The difference between a genius and a nobody is the genius never shows theirs."*

He pauses. Then, with an edge:

> *"Remember that."*

**Total expected GP haul:** 400-700gp depending on thoroughness. Better ratio than [[Menzoberranzan]]. The Tomb has good loot even in the junk drawer.

### The Handoff
[[EyeQ]] takes the notebook. This time, the PCs can see him clearly: he holds it with an instrument — the same astrolabe-loupe from the tapestry debrief — and the notebook's pages flutter. Text appears rapidly as [[EyeQ]] reads. The notebook appears to be having a conversation with him. A long one.

[[EyeQ]] looks... interested. More than interested. The PCs have never seen an Eyecon display genuine enthusiasm before.

He floats away toward the lower levels without a word. The notebook, in a final visible line, writes:

> *"Finally. Someone literate."*

### DM Notes
- **The debrief is the most important scene in this adventure.** The comedy has been doing work all session — the players are relaxed, laughing, enjoying the notebook's personality. Now drop the tone shift: [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] speed-reading in silence, the "persistence framework" exchange with [[EyeQ]], the line about never having to explain himself. The comedy was cover. This was always a procurement mission for a reality-altering device
- **"The persistence framework"** is the PCs' clearest clue yet that the artifacts aren't decorative. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is building something that persists beyond destruction. If any PC read the "contractual persistence" note in the archive, this should connect. Debts persist. Contracts persist. What if [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s *power* persists?
- **[[EyeQ]]'s enthusiasm is alarming.** He's the architect of the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]. The notebook is speaking to him like a colleague. The book that insulted everyone else just called him *literate*. [[EyeQ]] is the smartest being in [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s orbit, and the notebook confirmed it. The players should feel a chill
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s line about rough drafts** — "the genius never shows theirs" — is a direct callback to "stop looking at my sketches" from the [[Menzoberranzan]] debrief. He's been consistent: he's building something, he's protective of the process, and he's increasingly uncomfortable with the PCs seeing pieces of it. Three adventures in, the pattern should be visible to attentive players

## Appendix: [[Acererak]]'s Warm-Up Notebook — Ongoing Campaign Use

- **The notebook has a personality.** It continues to comment on events whenever a PC opens it. Use it as a running source of dark humor, lore drops, and occasionally useful arcane advice between adventures. It HATES being in [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]. It HATES [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. It has opinions about the shop's inventory (*"You're selling a +1 longsword for 2,300gp? Highway robbery. I admire the principle."*)
- **[[EyeQ]] and the notebook become close.** Between adventures, PCs may notice [[EyeQ]] spending time with the notebook — reading it, conversing with it. If asked, [[EyeQ]] says nothing. The notebook, if asked about [[EyeQ]], writes: *"He asks the right questions. I didn't think anyone still could."* This relationship is important — [[EyeQ]] is learning from [[Acererak]]'s research, and the notebook is genuinely engaged for the first time in centuries
- **[[Acererak]] remembers.** He won't pursue the PCs directly (he has bigger concerns), but he has tagged them psychically. If they ever encounter Tomb-related content again — artifacts, servants of [[Acererak]], or undead bearing his mark — those entities recognize the PCs with hostility. [[Acererak]] holds grudges the way liches hold phylacteries: forever
- **The persistence framework echoes forward.** When the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] activates in later phases, PCs may notice that [[Offledger]]'s structures develop bone-like architecture, faint necromantic resonance, and an unsettling quality of *permanence* — things feel like they've always been there, even when the PCs know they haven't. The notebook's contribution to the Crown is making it feel *inevitable*
