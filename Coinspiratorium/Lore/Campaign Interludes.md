# Campaign Interludes
*What happens when you're not in a dungeon.*

> **DM EYES ONLY** — This document contains narrative beats between quests that pace the overarching plot.

---

## How to Use This Document

Each **Interlude** runs between two quests, during the [[Store Management|Store Phase]] downtime in [[Offledger]]. They aren't full sessions — they're **scenes you weave into the shopping, restocking, and tribute-paying**. Some take five minutes. Some take thirty. Let them breathe.

Each interlude contains:
1. **Shop Vignette** — A customer, sale, or economic moment that makes the store feel alive
2. **Offledger Shift** — An environmental detail showing the Crown's growing influence
3. **Faction Beat** — A subplot scene advancing O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E., the Gilt Edge, or an Eyecon arc
4. **Veezy Moment** — A scene tracking his arc from charming boss to consumed architect
5. **Thread Hook** — A detail connecting the last quest to the next one

---

## Phase Map

The interludes also mark the campaign's five tonal phases (see [[The Device#Campaign Plot Phases]]):

| Phase | Quests | Tone | The PCs Believe... |
|-------|--------|------|--------------------|
| **1 — Blissful Ignorance** | 1-3 | Comedy with edge | "We're just doing jobs" |
| **2 — Creeping Awareness** | 4-5 | Comedy darkening | "Something's off about this place" |
| **3 — The Glimpse** | 6-7 | Dread beneath comedy | "These artifacts are connected" |
| **4 — The Vice** | 8-9 | Tension, fractures | "We're trapped and everyone knows" |
| **5 — The Choice** | 10+ | Tragic clarity | "We built this. We have to stop it." |

# Phase 1: Blissful Ignorance

*The PCs are new. The jobs are weird. The boss is funny. Everything is fine.*

---

## Interlude 1: After the Mirror, Before the Tapestry
*Between [[01 - Strahd's Favorite Mirror]] and [[02 - The Underdark Art Acquisition]]*

### Shop Vignette: The First Real Customer

The PCs' first Store Phase. A **half-orc bounty hunter named Groth** comes in looking for something to track a mark across planes. He's gruff, impatient, and keeps sniffing the merchandise. If the PCs have anything remotely applicable from their Barovia haul (even the Barovian red wine), he'll buy it — but he haggles brutally.

**Purpose:** Teaches the Store Phase mechanics in a low-stakes interaction. Groth can become a recurring customer if the PCs treat him well. If they gouge him, he tells people. Word travels in Offledger.

### Offledger Shift: The Hum

The first night back, a PC on watch (or anyone with a passive Listen above 15) hears a low **hum** coming from beneath the Coinspiratorium's floor. It lasts about ten seconds, then stops. If they investigate, there's nothing — just stone. If they ask anyone in Offledger about it, nobody else heard it.

**What it is:** The Vanishing Mirror being installed in the Workshop below. The first breadcrumb. Don't explain it. Don't even confirm it's real.

### Faction Beat: Strahd's Signet Rings

If the PCs accepted Strahd's "gift" (the signet rings from Quest 1), they occasionally catch the rings **warming slightly** in the presence of mirrors or reflective surfaces. A DC 15 Knowledge (Arcana) check reveals a faint divination aura — Strahd is watching, casually, the way a cat watches a mouse it isn't hungry for yet. This isn't a threat. It's a thread. Strahd remembers debts.

If they didn't accept the rings, skip this — but have [[Iris Major]] mention offhandedly that "a Barovian courier delivered something to the shop" and hand them a small velvet box containing a single ring and a note: *"For the one who was honest. — S."* (Addressed to whichever PC was most straightforward with Strahd during the masquerade.)

### Veezy Moment: The Performance Review

Veezy summons the PCs for their **first performance review**. He's set up a small stage in the back room with a podium, a banner reading "Q1 RESULTS," and a crystal ball projecting pie charts that are clearly fabricated.

He rates each PC on:
- **Brand alignment** (did they dress well in Barovia?)
- **Revenue generation** (how much loot did they bring back?)
- **Vibe compliance** (did they represent the Coinspiratorium with appropriate swagger?)

This is absurd, corporate, and hilarious. But at the end, almost as an afterthought, he asks: *"The mirror — it didn't show you anything... gold, did it? Gold light, gold shapes, that kinda thing?"*

He's checking whether the mirror revealed anything about the Crown. His tone is casual. His central eye is not.

**If they mention gold-tinged visions:** He nods slowly. "Temporal bleed. Common with old glass. Don't worry about it." He worries about it.

**If they say no:** He relaxes visibly. "Good. Old mirrors lie. That's what makes 'em valuable."

### Thread Hook: "The Next Contract"

As the PCs leave the review, [[Iris Major]] intercepts them in the corridor. She hands them a dossier with a wax seal — the Underdark job.

Her demeanor is colder than Quest 1's briefing. She adds one line that wasn't in the briefing packet: *"This one has a no-rescue clause. Read it."*

She pauses. Then: *"The last crew that went to Menzoberranzan came back with three members instead of four. The fourth is still paying off his debt. From a cage."*

This reframes the tone. Quest 1 was a heist at a party. Quest 2 is a heist in a warzone.

## Interlude 2: After the Tapestry, Before the Spellbook
*Between [[02 - The Underdark Art Acquisition]] and [[03 - Tomb of Horrors Spellbook]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Collector

A **drow exile named Velexis** enters the shop two days after the PCs return from Menzoberranzan. She's been living in Offledger for decades, cut off from Lolth's web. She doesn't want to buy anything. She wants to *smell* the merchandise — specifically anything that still carries the scent of the Underdark.

If the PCs brought back any endemic items (fungal samples, drow-made goods, cavern minerals), she'll pay absurd prices for them — not for utility, but for nostalgia. She cries silently while holding a piece of faerzress-laced quartz, then pays triple market value and leaves without a word.

**Purpose:** Reinforces that everything in Offledger has a story. Exile, loss, and homesickness exist even in a criminal haven. Also: easy money, which makes the next tribute payment less painful.

### Offledger Shift: The Thread

A PC notices a single **silk thread** running along the baseboard of the Coinspiratorium's back wall. It wasn't there before. It's the color of Lolth's Unfinished Tapestry — an impossible, shifting iridescent that the eye can't quite pin down. It runs from the back wall, disappears into a hairline crack in the floor, and goes down.

If they pull it, it resists gently — like it's attached to something heavy below. If they cut it, it dissolves into nothing. The next morning, a new thread has appeared in a different location.

**What it is:** The Tapestry being woven into the Workshop's architecture. The divine weave interface connecting to the building's structure. Second breadcrumb. Still deniable.

### Faction Beat: Szordrin's Warning

The PCs receive a **sending** from Szordrin Hune, the drow smuggler from Quest 2. It's garbled — he's clearly casting it under duress or in secret:

> *"They know about the tapestry. Zhael'ra sent hunters. Not after you — after Offledger. She wants it back. Watch the portals."*

This does two things: establishes that the PCs' actions have consequences beyond the dungeon, and seeds a future complication. Zhael'ra's hunters won't arrive for several quests — but when strange drow appear in Offledger later, the PCs will remember this.

[[Iris Major]], if told, responds with professional calm: "Noted. I'll adjust portal security." She's unbothered. The Gold Standard has dealt with worse.

### Veezy Moment: The Sketchbook

The PCs catch Veezy in the shop's back room, alone, drawing in a leather-bound sketchbook. He's not performing. He's not monologuing. He's *concentrating* — brow furrowed (as much as a beholder can furrow), tongue slightly out, central eye half-closed.

If they peek at the sketches: geometric diagrams. Circles within circles. Lines connecting nodes. It looks like a circuit board designed by someone who thinks in orbits.

If he notices them looking, he snaps the book shut and immediately shifts into performance mode: *"Design work. Brand architecture. You wouldn't understand — it's visionary-level stuff. Above your pay grade. WAY above."*

But during Quest 2's debrief, he said *"My art. My sketches"* when talking about the tapestry. If a PC connects this, reward them with a Sense Motive check (DC 15). Success: Veezy wasn't lying — the sketches ARE the tapestry's function translated into something he can build.

### Thread Hook: "Failure Is Instructive"

Veezy's next briefing opens with an unusual tone. No stage. No charts. Just Veezy in the back room, sketchbook closed on the table beside him.

*"Next job's different. I need something... personal. A notebook. Written by someone who almost got it right. Almost beat death. Almost became eternal."* A beat. *"Almost."*

He looks at the PCs. His voice drops to something almost vulnerable: *"Failure is instructive. That's not a motto. That's a confession."*

Then the mask snaps back on: *"Anyway, it's in a death trap. Classic. You're gonna love it. Iris has the details."*

This is Veezy's most genuine moment yet. The Tomb of Horrors spellbook is about persistence through failure — and Veezy relates to that more than he wants to show.

## Interlude 3: After the Spellbook, Before the Raid
*Between [[03 - Tomb of Horrors Spellbook]] and [[04 - Blackstaff Tower Raid]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Cursed Return

A previous customer (Groth, Velexis, or someone new) returns with an item they bought — it's **malfunctioning**. A wand that shoots backwards. Boots of speed that only work in reverse. A cloak of protection that protects everyone EXCEPT the wearer.

The PCs must handle their first **customer complaint** in a shop with no return policy, no warranty, and no consumer protection laws. Whatever they decide sets a precedent for how the Coinspiratorium handles disputes. [[Major Look]] is watching and will have opinions about the brand implications.

**Purpose:** Shop management as character expression. The PCs' decisions here define what kind of business they're running — and whether they're becoming more like Veezy.

### Offledger Shift: The Writing on the Wall

Faint **golden symbols** have appeared on the Coinspiratorium's interior walls. They're almost invisible — you only see them when light hits at a certain angle, like watermarks. They look like a script nobody recognizes.

A DC 18 Knowledge (Arcana) check identifies them as **contractual notation** — the kind of binding language used in planar agreements. A DC 25 check (or a PC who reads Infernal/Celestial) recognizes fragments: *"...accrues to the holder..."* and *"...in perpetuity across all instantiations..."*

**What it is:** The warm-up notebook's persistence framework being tested. The contracts are literally being written into the walls. Third breadcrumb — this one is harder to dismiss.

### Faction Beat: The Gilt Edge Sighting

The PCs spot **[[Kess Darrowmere]]** in Offledger — the leader of [[The Gilt Edge]], whom they first encountered in the Tomb of Horrors. She's at a street vendor, buying rations. She looks tired.

If approached, she's guarded but not hostile. She won't reveal mission details, but she'll acknowledge what happened in the Tomb:

*"So you're the other crew. We figured Veezy had backups."* She pauses. *"How many contracts deep are you? We're on our fourth."*

She won't say more. But if a PC makes a DC 15 Sense Motive: she's exhausted. Not physically — existentially. The Gilt Edge is further along in their burnout arc than the PCs. They've been doing this longer.

If the PCs try to compare notes about Veezy, Kess shuts down: *"Don't. [[Snitch Ray]] has ears in the walls. Literally."*

**Purpose:** Establishes the Gilt Edge as parallel sufferers, not just rivals. Seeds future alliance or tragedy.

### Veezy Moment: The Silent Reading

During the debrief from Quest 3, Veezy read Acererak's Warm-Up Notebook in silence for two full minutes. Now, days later, the PCs notice the notebook is **open on Veezy's desk** every time they enter the back room. Different page each time. He's studying it obsessively.

If a PC asks about it, Veezy deflects with unusual speed: *"Research. Development. R&D. You don't ask Apple what's in the lab, do you? No. You wait for the keynote. Same principle."*

But [[EyeQ]] lingers after one of these exchanges. If a PC catches EyeQ alone, the architect offers a single unprompted comment: *"He hasn't read anything that carefully since the original contract. Your contract."*

Then EyeQ leaves. No elaboration. No follow-up. Just a data point, delivered like a gift the recipient doesn't know how to open yet.

### Thread Hook: The Accountant Disguises

[[Iris Major]] delivers the Quest 4 briefing with an unusual addition: a wardrobe rack. On it hang **perfectly tailored accountant disguises** — pressed robes, spectacles, quill cases, and name badges from a firm called "Vellum & Vice, Arcane Auditors."

Iris explains, without emotion: *"These identities were seeded into Waterdeep's municipal registries four months ago. The firm has a billing history, three fake references, and a mediocre Yelp rating. You'll be attending the annual Arcane Census at Blackstaff Tower."*

Four months ago. The PCs have been working for Veezy for less time than that. He planned this job before he met them. The operation thinks in quarters.

If a PC points this out, Iris allows a rare micro-expression — not quite a smile: *"Lord Vizzok plans ahead. That's why you work for him and not the other way around."*

# Phase 2: Creeping Awareness

*Something is off. The walls are humming. The boss is distracted. And someone from the government just handed you a business card.*

---

## Interlude 4: After the Raid, Before the Halo
*Between [[04 - Blackstaff Tower Raid]] and [[05 - Steal a Solar's Halo]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Debt Declaration

During the Store Phase, the **Black Ledger** (which the PCs just stole) is sitting in the back room awaiting transfer to Veezy. It's supposed to be sealed. It is not silent.

Every time a transaction occurs at the shop counter, the Ledger **booms a debt declaration** from the back room — audible to customers:

> *"GROTH, SON OF GRUTHAK: OWES 14 GOLD TO A WEAPONSMITH IN LUSKAN. COMPOUNDED QUARTERLY."*

> *"THE ENTITY KNOWN AS 'VELEXIS': DEFAULTED ON A TEMPLE TITHE IN THE YEAR OF THE DARK PROMISE. OUTSTANDING BALANCE: HER PEACE OF MIND."*

Customers are horrified, fascinated, or delighted depending on temperament. Sales may spike (people want to see the screaming book) or crash (people don't want their debts announced). Let the PCs figure out how to handle it.

**Purpose:** The Black Ledger is a comedic set piece AND a preview of Law II (Debt Becomes Destiny). When the Crown activates, this is what reality sounds like — every debt, announced, enforced, inescapable. The PCs are living in the prototype.

### Offledger Shift: The Gravity Stutter

For three seconds one afternoon, **gravity reverses** in a two-block radius around the Coinspiratorium. Everything floats — customers, merchandise, street vendors, a stray dog. Then it snaps back. Items crash. People yell. Someone's cart is ruined.

Nobody in authority addresses it. [[Snitch Ray]]'s gauths sweep the area and report "localized planar hiccup — nothing actionable." If the PCs press, Snitch Ray narrows his eyes: *"You documenting anomalies now? That's my job. Stay in your lane."*

**What it is:** The Black Ledger's debt-indexing protocols being stress-tested. The Crown is learning to exert force through obligation. Fourth breadcrumb — this one affects other people. It's getting harder to ignore.

### Faction Beat: Pentival's Card

If the PCs kept [[Auditor Pentival]]'s business card from Quest 4, it begins **vibrating faintly** whenever they're near the Coinspiratorium's back wall (where the Workshop is below). The card is made of enchanted vellum — it's resonating with the arcane energy bleeding through the floor.

This is Pentival's craftsmanship, not his intention. He builds things too well. The card is picking up what's beneath the shop the way a tuning fork picks up a nearby note.

If the PCs surrendered the card to Veezy: skip this. But have a PC find a **scrap of enchanted vellum** in the gutter outside the shop — torn corner, same material, same faint vibration. O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. has been here. They just didn't knock.

### Veezy Moment: The Almost-Announcement

Veezy is in the shop when the Black Ledger activates during a transaction. It cycles through several customer debts, then suddenly pivots:

> *"VIZZOK, CALLED 'THE OPULENT': OUTSTANDING OBLIG—"*

Veezy's central eye flares. The Ledger goes silent. A thin wisp of smoke rises from its cover.

The room is very quiet. Veezy's eyestalks are rigid. His voice, when it comes, is the flattest the PCs have ever heard it:

*"Integrate it with the framework. Test the debt-indexing protocols. And get it out of the shop before it says something else I have to correct."*

He leaves. No jokes. No performance. [[EyeQ]] collects the Ledger without comment.

**What happened:** The Ledger almost revealed Veezy's own outstanding debt — [[Veezy's IOU|the 49gp owed to Grindle]]. This is the first hint that the Master Creditor is also a debtor. The PCs don't know what it means yet. But they will.

### Thread Hook: A Different Kind of Handler

The Quest 5 briefing comes not from [[Iris Major]] but from **[[Major Look]]** — the Eyecon of image and PR. He arrives with fabric swatches, pilgrim costume concepts, and a mood board.

*"Celestia. The holy mountain. Festival of the First Dawn. You're going as redeemed sinners — pilgrims who've turned their lives around."* He looks each PC up and down. *"We'll need to workshop your backstories. Nobody on Celestia is going to believe* **you** *found redemption unless we* **sell** *it."*

Major Look handles logistics with flair and passive-aggression. But unlike Iris Major's clinical efficiency, his briefing reveals something: Veezy wants a **divine artifact**. He's reaching into heaven. The targets are escalating.

If a PC asks why Veezy wants an angel's halo, Major Look's smile tightens: *"Above your clearance, darling. Just bring it back pretty."*

## Interlude 5: After the Halo, Before the Vault
*Between [[05 - Steal a Solar's Halo]] and [[06 - Aboleth Info-Vault]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Light That Won't Leave

The Halo of the Dawn's First Judgement was in the shop for approximately four hours before transfer. In that time, its continuous zone of truth (Will DC 24) irradiated the entire building. The effect faded after the halo left — mostly.

For 1d4 days after, the shop has a **residual honesty field**. Customers can't lie about what they want. Sellers can't bluff about item quality. Negotiations become brutally sincere. A fence trying to sell stolen goods breaks down and confesses mid-transaction. A regular customer admits he only comes in because he's lonely.

Sales are actually **better** during this period. Turns out honest commerce works. The irony writes itself.

**Purpose:** A comedic inversion that's also thematically devastating. The halo's light shows what commerce *could* be — fair, honest, human. Then it fades, and the Coinspiratorium goes back to being what it is.

### Offledger Shift: The Golden Veins

Thin **veins of gold** have appeared in the stone walls of the Coinspiratorium's basement. Not gold ore — liquid gold, moving sluggishly through the rock like blood through capillaries. They pulse faintly. The pulse matches no heartbeat the PCs can identify.

If a PC touches one: warmth, and a flash of light behind their eyes — for a fraction of a second, they see a **circular shape** made of orbiting objects. Then it's gone.

**What it is:** The Crown's power conduits spreading through the architecture. The divine light capacitor (halo) supercharged the system. The Crown is becoming visible to anyone who looks. Fifth breadcrumb — this one shows them the shape of the thing.

### Faction Beat: The Blessing That Stayed

If the PCs participated in the Festival of the First Dawn, they received a sacred blessing (+1 to saves). It persists. It should not still persist — festival blessings are temporary. But this one won't leave.

An NPC cleric or divine caster in Offledger (if the PCs seek one out) is baffled: *"That's... Celestia's mark. Permanent. She blessed you. The mountain itself blessed you."* A pause. *"What did you DO up there?"*

The blessing is real. Celestia blessed thieves. The implications are uncomfortable in every direction — either Celestia makes mistakes, or it wasn't a mistake. [[Seraphiel]]'s words echo: *"Whatever chains you carry, they are not forever."*

### Veezy Moment: The Near-Confession

During the halo's residual honesty period, Veezy visits the shop. He doesn't know about the field. Mid-sentence during a routine check-in, his voice catches:

*"You know, I used to — "*

He stops. His central eye dilates. He feels it — the compulsion to speak truth. Every eyestalk goes rigid. For three full seconds, Lord Veezy, the most performative creature in the multiverse, **fights to keep his mouth shut**.

He wins. Barely.

*"— used to eat breakfast earlier. Anyway. Tribute's due. Iris has the numbers."*

He leaves faster than the PCs have ever seen him move.

If a PC makes a DC 25 Sense Motive: whatever he almost said, it terrified him more than anything in the campaign so far. Not because it was dangerous — because it was *true*.

### Thread Hook: The Deep Cut

The Quest 6 briefing is somber. Veezy delivers it himself, but without the usual energy. He's sitting — unusual for a creature that floats — resting on a custom chaise in the back room.

*"Next one's deep. Underwater. Old. I'm talking... primordial old. Before the gods had names. Before debt had language."*

He's quieter than they've ever heard him. Then:

*"There's a pearl down there. It remembers everything. Every thought any aboleth ever had. Every secret, every scheme, every long-game manipulation since before the sun existed."*

A long pause. His central eye drifts toward the floor — toward the Workshop below.

*"I need it to remember* **me.** *Not who I am now. Who I'm gonna be."*

Then, snapping back: *"Also there's a lobster-dragon-mindflayer thing guarding it and it has strong opinions about copyright law. Bring waterbreathing."*

# Phase 3: The Glimpse

*The PCs know something is wrong. The artifacts aren't random. The boss isn't just greedy. And the building is alive.*

---

## Interlude 6: After the Vault, Before Sigil
*Between [[06 - Aboleth Info-Vault]] and [[07 - Sigil Quick Pickup]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Memory Hangover

The Primordial Memory Pearl was in transit through the shop for less than an hour, but its psychic residue lingers. For 1d4 days, customers who enter the Coinspiratorium experience **involuntary memory flashes** — vivid recollections of their earliest childhood memory, their worst mistake, or (most disturbingly) a memory that isn't theirs.

A grizzled dwarf veteran suddenly remembers being a fish. A tiefling warlock recalls a mother she never had. A goblin fence speaks fluent Aboleth for thirty seconds, then forgets how.

Some customers find this therapeutic. Others are furious. One weeps and buys nothing but thanks the PCs. Business is unpredictable.

**Purpose:** The pearl's influence is bleeding outward. The artifacts aren't just sitting in a vault — they're *active*. The Crown's components affect reality by proximity.

### Offledger Shift: The Construction Below

The PCs can now **hear construction** beneath the Coinspiratorium. Not the subtle hum of Interlude 1 — actual sounds. Metal on stone. Energy discharge. Something heavy being moved.

If they try to access the lower levels, they find a door they've never noticed before — heavy iron, no handle, no keyhole. Warm to the touch. Warded. A DC 20 Search check reveals tiny text around the frame in Veezy's handwriting: *"AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY. THIS MEANS YOU. YES, YOU SPECIFICALLY."*

[[EyeQ]] is seen entering and exiting this door at odd hours. He doesn't acknowledge the PCs when he does.

### Faction Beat: Pentival's Letter

Between Quests 6 and 7, [[Auditor Pentival]] sends a formal letter to the PCs. It arrives by **extradimensional courier** — a small construct that materializes inside the shop, deposits an envelope, and vanishes.

The letter is stamped with O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.'s geometric seal:

> *To the current operators of the establishment known as "The Coinspiratorium":*
>
> *Our office has maintained a file on your employer for some time. Recent acquisitions — a mirror, a tapestry, a notebook, a ledger, a halo, and now a pearl — suggest a pattern we find deeply concerning.*
>
> *We don't want you. We want him.*
>
> *If you're interested in discussing your future — a future that doesn't end in multiversal destabilization or personal vaporization — please respond via the enclosed card.*
>
> *Yours in procedural diligence,*
> *Auditor Pentival, Forensic Analysis Division*
> *O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.*
>
> *P.S. — The gravity thing last month? That was him. You know that, right?*

The PCs now have a choice: respond, ignore, or report it to Veezy. This is the **official O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. recruitment pitch**. Whatever they decide, the pressure ratchets from here.

### Veezy Moment: The Pearl He Won't Touch

The pearl is in the Workshop now. But during its transfer, the PCs may have noticed: Veezy wouldn't touch it. He directed [[Snitch Ray]] to handle it with telekinesis.

If a PC asks why, Veezy's answer is uncharacteristically direct:

*"That thing remembers everything. I mean* **everything.** *Every aboleth, every thought, every scheme since before the world had water."*

A pause. Longer than it should be.

*"I don't need it remembering me from before. I need it remembering me from now. There's a difference."*

**What he means:** The pearl contains memories of a younger Veezy. Before the Crown. Before Offledger. Before he became Lord Veezy. The person he was — who borrowed 49gp from a goblin for a cape and a mixtape — still exists in the pearl's memory. He can't let the PCs see that version of him.

### Thread Hook: Veezy's Absence

For the first time in the campaign, **Veezy does not deliver the next briefing**. [[Iris Major]] and [[EyeQ]] handle Quest 7's briefing together. Iris is professional. EyeQ is precise. Neither explains where Veezy is.

If the PCs ask: *"Lord Vizzok is occupied with strategic initiatives,"* says Iris. *"He trusts us to manage the procurement pipeline."*

If a PC makes a DC 18 Sense Motive on Iris: she's not lying, but she's uneasy. Veezy has never been absent before. He's consumed by the Crown's construction.

EyeQ adds, almost as an afterthought: *"The device requires his attention. As it should. It is, after all, his life's work."*

This is the first time anyone has called it "the device" in front of the PCs.

## Interlude 7: After Sigil, Before the Egg
*Between [[07 - Sigil Quick Pickup]] and [[08 - Hoard of Tiamat Dragon Egg]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Key's Echo

The Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once enforced absolute honesty in a 10-foot radius. Like the halo, it left a mark — but different. For 1d4 days, every **lock in the Coinspiratorium opens itself**. Doors swing wide. The vault unseals. Display cases pop open. The cash drawer won't stay closed.

This is not theft — nothing is taken. It's the Key's echo: a memory of unlimited access, bleeding through. The shop is, temporarily, a place where nothing is hidden.

Customers find this either refreshing or terrifying. [[Major Look]] is apoplectic about the security implications. [[Snitch Ray]] stations himself inside the shop for the duration, which kills the vibe entirely.

**Purpose:** Comedic chaos that mirrors the Key's Crown function (universal access). Also forces the PCs to confront what their shop looks like when all the barriers come down.

### Offledger Shift: The Workshop Revealed

This is the big one. During Quest 7's debrief, the PCs are taken to **the Workshop** for the first time. What they see:

Six artifacts orbiting a central point in a vast underground chamber. The Vanishing Mirror. Lolth's Unfinished Tapestry. Acererak's Warm-Up Notebook. The Black Ledger. The Halo. The Primordial Memory Pearl. Each one suspended in golden light, connected by hair-thin chains of energy, slowly rotating like a cosmic mobile.

And in the center: an **empty frame**. A skeletal structure waiting to be filled. The shape of a crown.

Veezy's voice, from behind them: *"Seven down. Five to go."*

The PCs now know. Not everything — but enough. The artifacts they've been stealing aren't inventory. They're components. And Veezy is building something.

### Faction Beat: Agent Quill's Warning

If the PCs encountered [[Agent Quill]] in Sigil (Quest 7), she follows up between adventures. She appears in Offledger disguised as a customer — a halfling in a hooded cloak browsing the shop's budget section.

She speaks quickly and quietly: *"I know you saw the workshop. I know you're counting artifacts. We're counting too. He needs twelve. He has seven. That gives us — gives* **you** *— five more chances to decide whose side you're on."*

She buys a minor potion, pays in exact change, and leaves. If [[Snitch Ray]] is watching, she's gone before he processes her. If he isn't, she lingers an extra moment: *"Pentival says the math is almost done. When it is, you'll understand why we're scared."*

### Veezy Moment: Rue's Memory

After the PCs return from Sigil, Veezy asks about [[Rue Kethani]] — the Key's 312-year guardian. His tone is different. Softer.

*"Was she okay? The lady with the Key. Rue."*

If the PCs describe their interaction, Veezy listens. Actually listens. No performance, no interruptions.

*"She was nice. Honest. The Key does that to people — makes 'em so honest they can't function in the real world anymore."* A pause. *"She deserved better than 312 years in a one-bedroom in Sigil."*

Then, recovering: *"Anyway. The Key's installed. Seven components. Moving on."*

But if a PC makes a DC 20 Sense Motive: Veezy isn't performing grief. He actually feels something about Rue. She reminds him of who he was before — someone who valued honesty. Someone who borrowed 49gp and meant to pay it back.

### Thread Hook: EyeRate Deployment

The Quest 8 briefing comes with a surprise: **[[EyeRate]] will accompany the party**.

This has never happened before. EyeRate is the enforcer — the Tier 5 escalation. He doesn't go on missions. He hovers in the shop and scares people.

Veezy's explanation: *"Tiamat's hoard. Draconic aspects. Five chromatic chambers. This one's above your weight class. EyeRate evens the odds."*

But [[Iris Major]], in a rare moment of transparency, pulls a PC aside after the briefing: *"EyeRate hasn't been deployed since the founding of Offledger. Lord Vizzok sending him means one of two things: the target is extremely dangerous, or the target is extremely important."*

She pauses. *"Or both."*

# Phase 4: The Vice

*They know what Veezy is building. O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. knows they know. The Eyecons are cracking. And the Crown is almost finished.*

---

## Interlude 8: After the Egg, Before the Convention
*Between [[08 - Hoard of Tiamat Dragon Egg]] and [[09 - The Beholder Convention Job]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Screaming Inventory

The Platinum Dragon Egg screams when touched by living creatures. It was in the shop for twenty minutes during transit. In those twenty minutes, every item in the display cases **briefly screamed in sympathy**. Swords wailed. Potions bubbled and shrieked. A pair of boots ran in circles.

The screaming stopped when the egg was moved to the Workshop. But one item — a random piece of inventory, DM's choice — still occasionally whimpers at closing time.

Customers have started asking about "the screaming shop." Some avoid it. Others come specifically for the novelty. A bard offers to write a review for the Offledger equivalent of a newspaper: *"Four stars. Great selection. Emotionally devastating ambiance."*

**Purpose:** Comedy with an ache. The egg's scream is the voice of a dragon that will never be born — unfulfilled potential made audible. The shop is starting to feel like a haunted house. The things the PCs sell are beginning to have feelings about it.

### Offledger Shift: The Spatial Hiccups

Offledger's geography is becoming **unreliable**. Streets that connected yesterday don't today. A tavern the PCs visited last week is now three blocks further east. The Portal Hub takes an extra turn to reach. Nothing dramatic — just a persistent sense of wrongness, like the demiplane is being rewritten.

The locals notice but adapt. Offledger has always been weird. But a DC 15 Knowledge (The Planes) check tells the PCs: this is consistent with **paradox bleed** — the kind of spatial instability caused by introducing irreconcilable concepts into a demiplane's foundational logic. The Crown is straining the architecture of reality itself.

### Faction Beat: EyeQ's Offer

After Quest 8, [[EyeQ]] makes the most significant overture of the campaign. He approaches a PC — the one he considers most analytically capable — in private. His voice is quieter than usual:

*"Every Eyecon carries a segment of the Crown's architecture. We were built that way. From the beginning."*

He lets that settle.

*"If one segment were absent from the final assembly — removed, damaged, or simply... elsewhere — the Crown would function. But imperfectly. Imprecisely. Perhaps unstably."*

He looks at the PC with his central eye.

*"I have not yet formed an opinion on whether I agree with Lord Vizzok's project. But I am telling you that I* **have** *a segment. And that I am not yet certain what I wish to do with that information."*

Then he leaves. No instructions. No request. Just a door, left open.

**Purpose:** This is the first crack in the machine from the inside. EyeQ is offering the PCs an ally — or at least a wildcard. What they do with this information could reshape the endgame.

### Veezy Moment: The Egg Lullaby

Late one night, a PC passing the Workshop door hears something unexpected: **Veezy, singing to the egg**.

Not a performance. Not a monologue. A lullaby — low, tuneless, in a language that might be primordial Beholder. The egg is quieter than it's ever been. Not silent — but the scream has softened to a keen.

If the PC listens at the door (DC 15 Listen):

*"...you and me, we got something in common. We both got cheated out of what we shoulda been. But I'm gonna fix that. For both of us. I'm gonna make it so nobody ever gets cheated again."*

A pause. Then, barely audible: *"...starting with me."*

This is Veezy's thesis statement. The Crown is grief weaponized as cosmic tyranny. He's not just building a god-machine — he's trying to retroactively heal a wound that can't be healed. The egg is sterile. Its destiny was stolen. And Veezy sees himself in that.

### Thread Hook: The Invitation

Veezy is THRILLED about the next mission. More animated than he's been in weeks. The exhaustion drops away. The performance returns at full volume:

*"THE CONCLAVE OF EYEFELLOWS! The annual gathering of the most MAGNIFICENT beings in existence! Sixty beholders! Under one dome! AND I — "*

He spins. All eyestalks flare.

*"— am the KEYNOTE SPEAKER!"*

The PCs are going to a beholder convention. As Veezy's entourage. While secretly stealing the oldest artifact in beholder history.

[[Major Look]] is already measuring the PCs for matching uniforms. [[Snitch Ray]] is deployed with them for the first time. And Veezy, for the first time in the campaign, is going to be in the same room as the target.

## Interlude 9: After the Convention, Before the Mountain
*Between [[09 - The Beholder Convention Job]] and [[10 - White Plume Mountain Redux]]*

### Shop Vignette: The Quiet Week

After the chaos of the Beholder Convention, the shop has a **genuinely slow week**. Few customers. Low traffic. The Market Week Roll is irrelevant — nobody's buying.

But the quiet isn't peaceful. It's tense. Offledger feels like it's holding its breath. NPCs the PCs know are staying home. The streets are emptier than usual. Even the Portal Hub has less traffic.

Something is coming. Everyone can feel it. Nobody will say what.

**Purpose:** Silence as tension. After nine quests of escalating madness, a quiet week is more unsettling than a busy one. Let the PCs sit in the discomfort. The calm before the last act.

### Offledger Shift: The Crown's Pulse

Nine artifacts are in the Workshop. The PCs can feel it through the floor — not a hum anymore, but a **pulse**. Regular. Rhythmic. Like a heartbeat.

The golden veins in the walls are brighter. The contractual notation is legible now — anyone who reads Celestial, Infernal, or Abyssal can make out full clauses: *"...all obligations accrue interest at the rate of existence..." "...the debtor's identity is collateral..." "...in perpetuity, across all instantiations, without appeal..."*

The Coinspiratorium is being written into the Crown's contract. The shop itself is becoming part of the device.

At night, the building **glows faintly gold** from the outside. Passersby on the street look up at it and walk faster.

### Faction Beat: Snitch Ray's Silence

After Quest 9, [[Snitch Ray]] filed a report about the Beholder Convention heist. The PCs don't know this — but they should notice the aftermath.

Snitch Ray is **different**. He's quieter. Less combative. He doesn't snipe at the PCs when they pass. He doesn't hover near their conversations. He doesn't report their minor infractions.

If a PC asks what's wrong (DC 15 Diplomacy to get him talking): *"I filed my report. Standard procedure."* A pause. *"I may have... edited some sections. For clarity."*

He won't elaborate. But the PCs know Snitch Ray. He doesn't edit for clarity. He edits to protect. The surveillance Eyecon lied on an official report — the first time any Eyecon has put themselves at risk for the PCs.

If they press: *"Drop it. I didn't do it for you. I did it because the alternative was worse."*

He won't say what the alternative was. But if a PC recalls EyeQ's offer from Interlude 8 — that every Eyecon carries a Crown segment — then Snitch Ray's rebellion takes on a new meaning. The components are resisting.

### Veezy Moment: The Golden Eye

Veezy's central eye is now **fully gold**. Not the enchanted monocle — the eye itself. The iris has been replaced by something that looks minted, like a coin pressed into his face.

He doesn't acknowledge it. Nobody does. But it's impossible not to notice.

If a PC asks about it, Veezy touches his central eye with one stalk — gently, almost tenderly:

*"Optimization. The system needs a clear signal. I'm just... improving the antenna."*

But his voice cracks on "improving." Just for a syllable. Then it's gone.

If a PC makes a DC 22 Sense Motive: Veezy didn't choose this. The Crown is changing him. The device he's building is already building him back. And he's not sure he likes what it's making.

### Thread Hook: EyeQ Deploys

The Quest 10 briefing is delivered by **[[EyeQ]]** — in the field for the first time. No explanation from Veezy. No performance. EyeQ simply arrives at the shop with the briefing materials and says:

*"White Plume Mountain. The Keraptis Binder. I will accompany you."*

If the PCs ask why: *"Because the Crown is almost complete. And there are things you need to understand before it is."*

He means it. During Quest 10, EyeQ drops all careful hedging and gives the PCs the full Crown Infinite revelation. But that's for the quest itself.

What matters here, in the interlude, is the **silence between the lines**. EyeQ is deploying because he's made his decision. He doesn't say which way. But he's chosen.

# Phase 5: The Choice

*Ten artifacts orbit the Crown. Two remain. The IOU sits in someone's pocket like a bomb that hasn't gone off yet. Everything that happens now is endgame.*

## Interlude 10: After the Mountain, Before the End
*Between [[10 - White Plume Mountain Redux]] and the final quests (TBD)*

### Shop Vignette: Grindle's Receipt

If the PCs obtained [[Veezy's IOU]] from [[Grindle Gruk'tak]] at White Plume Mountain's base camp, they now possess the most dangerous document in the multiverse. A 49gp IOU for a sequined cape and ten demo copies of a mixtape.

It sits in someone's inventory. Unremarkable. A scrap of old parchment with a goblin's shaky handwriting and a younger Veezy's surprisingly neat signature.

During the Store Phase, if a PC handles the IOU near the shop counter, the **Coinspiratorium's ledger twitches**. The binding magic in the shop recognizes a debt instrument. The golden veins in the walls pulse brighter for a moment. The pulse from below — the Crown's heartbeat — stutters.

Just for a second. Then everything returns to normal.

If a PC notices (DC 15 Spot or Sense Motive): the shop *reacted* to the IOU. The building knows what it is, even if the PCs don't. Not yet.

### Offledger Shift: The New Laws

Offledger is changing in ways that can no longer be dismissed. Small, strange new rules are manifesting — not laws anyone passed, but **physics that simply... started**:

- A street vendor discovers he literally cannot sell an item for less than he paid for it. His hands won't release the merchandise. The concept of "loss" has become physically impossible within two blocks of the shop.
- A debtor who owes money to three different lenders finds himself physically unable to walk away from any of them. He's standing at an intersection, unable to move, pulled in three directions by obligations that have become gravitational.
- A bard who tells a story about a king who forgave a debt develops a **nosebleed** that won't stop until she changes the ending.

These are the **Crown's laws manifesting in prototype**. Law I (Reality Prioritizes Influence), Law II (Debt Becomes Destiny), and Law III (Gold = Power) are bleeding into Offledger's reality. The Crown isn't activated yet — but it's dreaming. And its dreams are becoming real.

### Faction Beat: The Convergence

Three things happen in quick succession:

**1. Pentival's Proof.** If the PCs have been in contact with O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E., [[Auditor Pentival]] sends his completed mathematical model of the Crown Infinite. It's dense — pages of equations, planar diagrams, contractual flow charts. But the conclusion is simple, delivered in Pentival's enthusiastic scrawl:

> *"The Crown creates a closed system of obligation. All debts flow upward to the Master Creditor. But — and this is the beautiful part — a closed system cannot contain a contradiction. If the Master Creditor OWES, the system experiences recursive failure. A creditor who is also a debtor creates an infinite loop. The engine cannot reconcile. It collapses."*
>
> *"Of course, this is purely theoretical. Lord Vizzok would never be foolish enough to have outstanding debts."*

The PCs look at the IOU.

**2. The Gilt Edge Breaks.** [[Kess Darrowmere]] appears at the Coinspiratorium. Alone. No Whisper, no Ashenveil, no Torque. She looks like she hasn't slept in days.

*"We're done. Torque's circuits are frying — same golden veins your building has. Ashenveil won't stop praying. Whisper..."* She falters. *"Whisper saw what the Crown does. She hasn't spoken since."*

She sets a small object on the counter: her Gold Standard contract. *"I'm breaking it. Tell Veezy I'm gone. Tell him whatever you want."*

If the PCs warn her about the consequences: *"I know. EyeRate. The enforcement ladder. The empty shop."* She looks at them. *"At least I chose."*

She leaves. The PCs never see the Gilt Edge together again.

**3. Director Casseel.** If the PCs have been cooperating with O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E., [[Director Casseel]] makes contact for the first time — not through agents, not through letters, but in person. A fallen angel in a perfectly pressed suit, standing in the Coinspiratorium like she was always there.

*"Two more artifacts. That's what he needs. We're out of time for subtlety."* Her voice is calm. Her eyes are not. *"When he sends you for the eleventh component, you need to decide: bring it back to him, or bring it to us. There is no third option."*

She's wrong about that. There is a third option. It's in someone's pocket. But Casseel doesn't know about the IOU — only Pentival's math points to it, and Pentival thinks it's theoretical.

### Veezy Moment: The Last Honest Conversation

Veezy finds the PCs after the Quest 10 debrief. Not in the back room. Not on stage. He's in the **shop itself**, floating between the display cases, looking at the merchandise like a customer.

His central eye — fully gold now — reflects every item on the shelves.

*"Ten. Two more. Then it's done."*

He's quiet. The PCs have never seen him like this — not performing grief, not performing menace. Just... present.

*"You wanna know why? Really why?"*

If the PCs say yes:

*"Nobody — not the gods, not the planes, not ANYBODY — gets to decide I don't matter. Ever. Again."*

He lets that sit. No follow-up joke. No mask.

*"I was nobody. A nothing beholder from a nothing clutch on a nothing plane. I borrowed money from a GOBLIN to buy a CAPE. That's who I was."*

His voice drops.

*"The Crown doesn't make me a god. It makes me* **undeniable.** *It writes me into the rules. Permanently. So nobody can ever look through me again."*

Then, almost as an afterthought: *"You ever feel like that? Like the world decided you don't count?"*

He's asking the PCs — his indentured employees, his unwilling operatives, the people he trapped in a contract and worked half to death — if they understand what it's like to be invisible.

And the horrible thing is: they probably do.

## Using the Interludes

### Pacing

Not every element needs to land in every interlude. If a session runs long, cut the Shop Vignette. If the PCs are deeply engaged in faction politics, expand the Faction Beat and trim the Veezy Moment. These are **ingredients, not scripts**.

### Escalation Rhythm

The interludes follow a pattern:
- **Phase 1** (Interludes 1-3): Artifacts leave subtle marks. Veezy is charming. Faction contacts are distant.
- **Phase 2** (Interludes 4-5): Artifacts affect the environment. Veezy cracks. Factions make contact.
- **Phase 3** (Interludes 6-7): The Workshop is revealed. Veezy withdraws. Factions pressure the PCs.
- **Phase 4** (Interludes 8-9): The Crown reshapes reality. Eyecons rebel. The PCs are squeezed.
- **Phase 5** (Interlude 10): All threads converge. The IOU activates. The choice arrives.

### Player Agency

The interludes present situations, not solutions. The PCs decide:
- Whether to investigate the hum, the threads, the golden veins
- Whether to respond to O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.'s overtures
- Whether to trust EyeQ's offer
- Whether to help Kess or let her go
- Whether to confront Veezy or play along
- What to do with the IOU

The narrative glue binds the quests together. It doesn't bind the players.

### The IOU Activation

The IOU's significance should crystallize **gradually across Interlude 10**:
1. The shop reacts to it (physical confirmation)
2. Pentival's math explains why (intellectual confirmation)
3. Veezy's confession reveals his debt history (emotional confirmation)

By the time the PCs place it on the counter, they should feel the weight of every interlude — every hum, every golden vein, every cracked mask — converging on a single scrap of paper worth 49 gold pieces.
