## Overview
The Gold Standard is the criminal enterprise headed by [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]], a beholder of considerable wealth and influence. The Gold Standard operates through a network of front businesses (including [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|The Coinspiratorium]]), contracted adventurers, enforcers, and intermediaries. Its interests span black market trade, dungeon exploitation, magical item trafficking, and money laundering on a grand scale.

## Goals
- Accumulate wealth (always more wealth)
- Expand territory and influence
- Acquire rare magical items and curios for Veezy's personal collection
- Maintain control over indentured operatives (that's you)
- Launder money through legitimate-seeming retail operations

## Methods
- **Front Businesses** — shops like the Coinspiratorium that move product and clean money
- **Contracted Dungeoneering** — sending adventurer-debtors into dungeons to recover loot
- **Enforcement** — agents and muscle to ensure debts are paid and loyalty maintained
- **Information Networks** — Veezy's many eyes (literal and figurative) see much

## Leadership
- **Leader:** [[Vizzok the Opulent]] — the beholder at the top
- **Inner Circle:** [[The Eyecons]] — 4-6 advanced beholder-kin (managers, enforcers, hype crew)
- **Key Members:** *To be developed — lieutenants, enforcers, accountants, other shopkeepers?*

## Resources
- Vast personal wealth
- Multiple front operations
- Network of indentured adventurers
- Magical items and enchantments
- Fear and reputation

## Relationship to the Coinspiratorium
The shop is one node in a larger network. The players are one crew among several. This means:
- Veezy has other priorities beyond just the players
- There may be rival crews competing for Veezy's favor
- The organization can provide resources... for a price
- Failure reflects poorly not just on the players but on Veezy's operation

## Rival Crews
### [[The Gilt Edge]]
Veezy doesn't keep one crew — he keeps several. **The Gilt Edge** is the PCs' primary rival: a four-person team of indentured adventurers who owe Veezy just as much as the PCs do, hate the PCs just as much as the PCs hate them, and are exactly competent enough to be a genuine threat without being so competent that Veezy prefers them.

**Members:**
- **Kess "the Fang" Darrowmere** — Human fighter 4 (scales with party). Leader. Former pit fighter from Calimshan who borrowed from The Gold Standard to buy her freedom from one arena and ended up owned by a worse one. Aggressive, direct, disdainful of the PCs' methods. Fights with paired short swords and a permanent scowl. She respects strength and nothing else
- **Whisper** — Changeling rogue 4 (scales with party). The infiltrator. Nobody knows Whisper's real face, real name, or real gender — they shift between identities the way most people shift between moods. They're the only Gilt Edge member who seems to genuinely enjoy working for Veezy. This is concerning. Specializes in locks, traps, and making the PCs feel watched
- **Brother Ashenveil** — Tiefling cleric 4 (scales with party, domain: Trickery). The face. A disgraced priest of Waukeen who embezzled temple funds, got caught, and ended up owing Veezy through a chain of increasingly bad decisions. Charming, amoral, and deeply conflicted about serving a beholder when he used to serve a goddess. Heals Gilt Edge, lies to everyone else
- **Torque** — Warforged artificer 4 (scales with party). The muscle and the mechanic. Torque doesn't owe Veezy money — Torque IS the collateral. Veezy purchased Torque's life-debt from a Karrnathi weapons manufacturer. Torque follows orders because Torque was literally built to follow orders. Quiet, efficient, and increasingly showing signs of independent thought that everyone pretends not to notice

**Role in the Campaign:**
- **Early quests (1-4):** The PCs hear about Gilt Edge through Veezy's casual comparisons — *"Gilt Edge brought back their last contract in half the time. Just sayin'."* Foreshadowing only. The PCs encounter evidence of Gilt Edge's work (cleared rooms, sprung traps, a smug note) but don't meet them face-to-face until Quest 3
- **Mid quests (5-7):** Direct encounters. Gilt Edge is running parallel contracts for Veezy — sometimes the same artifact. When both crews converge on the same target, the competition creates three-way encounters (PCs vs. dungeon vs. Gilt Edge). Veezy encourages the rivalry because it produces results
- **Late quests (8-10):** The rivalry matures. Gilt Edge starts showing signs of their own doubts about the Crown Infinite. Kess has nightmares about the egg's scream. Whisper has been spending time near the Workshop, watching. Ashenveil prays to Waukeen again for the first time in years. Torque asks a question nobody expected: *"What happens to us when the device activates?"*
- **Endgame:** Gilt Edge becomes either an ally or an enemy depending on the PCs' choices. If the PCs share what they know (the IOU, EyeQ's revelation), Gilt Edge joins them. If the PCs keep secrets, Gilt Edge remains loyal to Veezy — and stands between the PCs and the Crown

**Veezy's Management Style:**
He plays the crews against each other. Deliberately. He shares Gilt Edge's successes with the PCs and the PCs' successes with Gilt Edge. He adjusts tribute demands based on the other crew's performance. He has NEVER told either crew they're his favorite. He has told BOTH crews they're his backup plan if the other one fails.

This is not poor management. This is Veezy's management philosophy distilled: competition produces loyalty because neither crew can afford to be the one he replaces. The debt binds them. The rivalry motivates them. The fear of being second keeps them sharp.

**Gilt Edge's Debt:**
Collectively, Gilt Edge owes approximately the same amount as the PCs. Their tribute schedule is identical. Their freedom number is equally impossible. They are the PCs seen through a mirror — same trap, same boss, same impossible math. The difference is what they do about it.
