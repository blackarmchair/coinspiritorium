# "Raid Waterdeep's Blackstaff Tower (Quietly...)"

> *"A man with a ledger rules the world. A beholder with a ledger ends it."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs to Waterdeep — a functioning, law-abiding city with guards and courts and sunlight — to steal a book from Blackstaff Tower during the annual Arcane Census. No vampires. No drow. No demiliches. Just bureaucracy, forged credentials, and a ledger that won't stop screaming everyone's debts at full volume.

This is the heist movie episode. After three missions in increasingly nightmarish locations, the PCs are doing something almost *normal* — except they're committing grand larceny against the most powerful wizard in the city while pretending to be accountants.

**Tone arc:** Comedy (briefing) → Relief/culture shock (Waterdeep) → Absurdist tension (the census) → Escalating panic (the ledger) → High-stakes social bluffing (the Blackstaff) → Controlled chaos (the exit) → Uneasy revelation (debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Social infiltration, bureaucratic navigation, keeping a loud magic book quiet
**Combat:** Minimal (one potential encounter; this adventure punishes violence)

---

## Target Item
**[[The Black Ledger]]** — A massive leather-bound tome, 18 inches tall, 6 inches thick, bound in black dragonhide with clasps of cold iron. The pages are thin as onionskin and there are thousands of them — the book is larger on the inside than the outside. It weighs 25 lbs.

[[The Black Ledger]] is Waterdeep's master record of **magical debts** — every arcane contract, every enchantment loan, every spell cast on credit, every magic item purchased on installment. It updates in real time. It has done so for over 200 years. It is the single most complete financial record of magical commerce in the Sword Coast.

**The Ledger's Voice:**
[[The Black Ledger]] is enchanted to **announce debts aloud** when opened or when a debtor comes within 10 feet of it. It speaks in a booming, officious baritone — the magical equivalent of a court bailiff who never learned volume control:

- *"CITIZEN: You owe 47 gold, 3 silver to the Watchful Order of Magists for Spell Component Taxation, Quarter 3. OVERDUE."*
- *"CITIZEN: Outstanding balance of 1,200 gold to Maaril's House of Magical Curiosities. Payment plan: DEFAULTED."*
- *"CITIZEN: A lien of 380 gold exists on your SOUL, filed by an entity identified as... [static]... '[[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]], the Opulent, Sovereign Creditor of [[Offledger]].' UNRECOGNIZED JURISDICTION. FLAGGED FOR REVIEW."*

The ledger doesn't just read real debts — it **extrapolates**. If it can't find a record, it invents one based on probabilistic analysis. These invented debts are absurd but delivered with the same authoritative certainty:

- *"CITIZEN: You owe 3,800 gold to the Owlbear Refinancing Fund. OVERDUE."*
- *"CITIZEN: An outstanding balance of 12 gold to a tavern identified as 'The One You Don't Remember' has been accruing interest since Midsummer, 1369 DR."*
- *"CITIZEN: Based on your current equipment value and apparent lifestyle, you should owe approximately 6,000 gold in back taxes. FILING RETROACTIVELY."*

**Properties:**
- Announces debts of anyone within 10 feet (no save, automatic, LOUD)
- Updates in real time — it knows debts from ANY jurisdiction, including extraplanar ones
- Cannot be silenced by *silence* (the enchantment is woven into the pages, not the air — it produces sound directly from the book's surface at a frequency that bypasses the spell)
- CAN be muffled by physical containment (wrapping in cloth reduces volume; the lead-lined case [[Iris Major]] provides reduces it to a whisper)
- CAN be temporarily suppressed by feeding it gold — placing 1 gp on the open pages silences it for 1 minute. The gold is absorbed into the book. This gets expensive fast
- It weighs 25 lbs and is cumbersome to carry while also maintaining a disguise

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Arcane economy binder — provides the architectural framework for tracking and enforcing debts across an entire population. With this component, the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] can scale from [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s personal debt network to a universal system. Every creature in existence becomes a debtor. The ledger is the template for the Crown's collection engine.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
Same back room. The vibe is different. [[Iris Major]] has set up what appears to be an actual presentation — a folding easel with a charcoal sketch of Blackstaff Tower's floor plan. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is wearing a new ring. He seems... cheerful. Relaxed. This is the first contract where he isn't sending them somewhere that actively wants to kill them, and he knows it.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"Good news. This one's in a city. A real city. With streets and restaurants and people who don't immediately try to eat you. You're welcome."*

He gestures at the floor plan.

> *"Blackstaff Tower. Waterdeep. Home of the city's archmage. Inside, there's a book. The book tracks every magical debt in the city — who owes what, to whom, going back centuries. I need that book."*

**If PCs ask why:**
> *"Because information is power. You wanna know who controls a city? It's not the guy with the sword. It's the guy with the receipts."*

**If PCs point out that Blackstaff Tower is one of the most heavily warded structures on the Sword Coast:**
> *"It is. Most days. But once a year, they open the doors for the Arcane Census — every registered mage in Waterdeep shows up to file their magical activity reports, renew their licenses, pay their taxes. The wards go to half-power to let all those wizards in and out. Hundreds of people. Total chaos. Nobody's checking IDs at the door when there's a line around the block."*

**If PCs ask about the ledger's properties:**
> *"It's... vocal. It announces debts. Loudly. When people are near it. Including you."*

Beat.

> *"Including debts you didn't know you had."*

Longer beat.

> *"Including debts that might not technically exist yet."*

### Logistics (delivered by [[Iris Major]])

- **Portal:** Deposits the PCs in Waterdeep's Dock Ward, two hours before the census opens. They walk to the Tower like normal citizens. The return portal activates in the same location at sundown — 8-hour window
- **Credentials:** [[Iris Major]] provides a complete forgery package:
  - **Identification papers** for each PC as employees of **Vellum & Vice, Interplanar Accounting Solutions** — a fictional auditing firm that [[Iris Major]] has seeded into three different municipal registries over the past month. Forgery DC 25 to detect
  - **An official audit notice** bearing the seal of the Lords' Alliance Finance Committee, requesting access to Waterdeep's magical debt records for "interplanar compliance review." The seal is real — [[Iris Major]] purchased it from a contact. The notice is forged. The Finance Committee doesn't actually have jurisdiction over Waterdeep's magical records, but nobody at the census will know that
  - **Business attire:** Tailored robes in the style of Waterdhavian civil servants. Conservative, boring, professional. No armor. No visible weapons. They look like accountants
- **Equipment:** The lead-lined carrying case (for the ledger), 50gp in assorted coins (for feeding the ledger to keep it quiet), a set of wax ear plugs (reduce the ledger's volume for the wearer, but also impose -4 to Listen checks), and a *hat of disguise* (single use, 1 hour duration) for emergencies
- **Rules of engagement:** *"This is a populated civilian structure in a major city. Violence will result in the Waterdeep City Watch responding in force within 4 minutes. The Blackstaff can teleport reinforcements directly. If you are identified as thieves, the organization cannot intervene. Exercise restraint."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Parting Words

> *"Couple things. One: blend in. You're accountants. Act like it. Nobody pays attention to accountants. That's the whole point of being one."*

> *"Two: when the book starts talking about your debts, do NOT panic. It says wild shit. Most of it's fake. Some of it's real. Don't get in your feelings about it."*

> *"Three: if it says anything about what YOU owe ME — that's between us. Don't let anybody else hear that. Especially not any... regulatory types."*

He says "regulatory types" the way most people say "assassins."

### DM Notes
- This briefing should feel like a relief after three death-trap missions. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s relaxed energy, the professional forgeries, the business attire — the PCs are going on a corporate espionage job, not a dungeon crawl. Let them enjoy the tonal shift
- The credentials package is [[Iris Major]]'s best work yet. She's been seeding the fake firm for a MONTH. This tells the PCs (if they notice) that the heists are planned far in advance. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s operation is organized
- [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s warning about "regulatory types" is the first oblique reference to [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] in the PCs' presence. He doesn't name them. He doesn't explain. But he's nervous — and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is never nervous about anything in the material world. This is new
- The 50gp in "ledger food" is both practical and a perfect campaign metaphor: the PCs are literally paying money to keep a debt-book quiet. They're buying silence from an institution of financial surveillance. This is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s world in miniature

## Scene 2: Waterdeep

### Arrival
The portal deposits them in an alley in the Dock Ward at dawn. For the first time in three missions, the PCs step into sunlight.

Waterdeep is alive. The streets are crowded. Merchants are setting up stalls. The Watch patrols in organized pairs. Children are running. Someone is selling meat pies that actually smell good. There is not a single vampire, drow, or demilich anywhere in sight.

After [[Barovia]], [[Menzoberranzan]], and the Tomb of Horrors, Waterdeep feels like another planet. The PCs are in *civilization*. Where there are *consequences*.

### The Walk to Blackstaff Tower
Blackstaff Tower is in the Castle Ward — a 20-minute walk through increasingly wealthy districts. The PCs, dressed as accountants, blend in perfectly. Nobody looks twice at a group of bureaucrats heading to work.

**Key observations on the walk:**
- The city is enormous. Tens of thousands of people. The PCs have been operating in [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s small, criminal ecosystem — this is a reminder that the wider world exists and doesn't know about any of it
- The Watch is everywhere. Professional, well-equipped, and connected to the Blackstaff's magical early warning system. Fighting is not an option
- Magical commerce is casual here — a shop selling minor enchantments, a street vendor offering cantrip demonstrations, a notary office advertising "Binding Contracts, Magically Certified." Debt, commerce, and magic are woven into daily life. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] would reshape ALL of this

### Blackstaff Tower Exterior
The Tower is a narrow, impossibly tall black spire rising above the Castle Ward. It shouldn't be structurally sound — it's too thin, too tall — but magic holds it together. Faint arcane symbols pulse on its surface. The air around it hums.

Today, the main doors are open. A line of wizards, sorcerers, hedge mages, and magical tradespeople stretches out the door and down the block. They're holding forms, receipts, and ledgers of their own. They look exactly like people going to a tax office: resigned, impatient, and holding too much paperwork.

**Census security:**
- Two Tower Guards at the entrance (Fighter 4, CR 3 each — city watch elite, not rent-a-cops)
- One **census administrator** checking credentials at the door — a harried half-elf clerk named **[[Torvald Quillsen]]** who has clearly been doing this since dawn and is already exhausted
- A passive *detect magic* ward on the doorframe (CL 10) — identifies anyone carrying magical items or active enchantments. This is normal for the census — everyone here is carrying magic. It doesn't flag the PCs specifically, but it does note the *hat of disguise* and any magical gear
- The forgery package gets the PCs through the door cleanly. Torvald checks their papers, glances at the audit notice, mutters *"Another audit? Third one this quarter,"* stamps their credentials, and waves them in

### DM Notes
- Waterdeep is a palate cleanser. Let the PCs enjoy it. Let them walk through streets, see normal life, maybe even buy a meat pie. The contrast between [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s criminal demiplane and this functioning city should be stark — and should make the PCs think about what the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] would do to a place like this
- The census line of grumbling wizards is comedy gold. Improvise complaints: *"I already filed my cantrip usage report!" "Why do they need my familiar's date of birth?" "I've been in line for two hours and I'm a SEVENTH-LEVEL EVOKER."*
- Torvald the clerk is not important to the plot, but he's the face of Waterdeep's bureaucracy and the PCs' first point of contact. Make him tired, efficient, and utterly unimpressed by anything. He's processed 300 mages today. Nothing surprises him

## Scene 3: The Census

### Inside the Tower
Blackstaff Tower is larger on the inside than the outside — a common enchantment, but the scale is staggering. The ground floor alone is a vaulted hall the size of a cathedral, filled with rows of desks, filing cabinets, and enchanted sorting systems. Hundreds of people mill through the space: wizards filing reports, clerks stamping forms, magical quills self-writing records, and small construct-messengers scurrying along the floors carrying documents between departments.

The atmosphere is exhausting. It's a magical DMV.

**Layout (relevant areas):**

| Floor  | Contents                                               | Access                                                        |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Ground | Census hall, reception, filing desks                   | Open — everyone is here                                       |
| 2nd    | Administrative offices, records archive                | Restricted — staff and authorized visitors (auditors qualify) |
| 3rd    | The Vault — magical debt records, [[The Black Ledger]] | Restricted — senior staff and special authorization only      |
| 4th+   | The Blackstaff's personal quarters and laboratories    | Sealed — nobody goes up without the Blackstaff                |

### Navigating the Census (Social Challenge)
The PCs need to get from the ground floor to the 3rd floor Vault. Their auditor credentials get them to the 2nd floor automatically. Getting to the 3rd floor requires additional authorization.

**The Ground Floor — Blending In**
The census hall is chaos. The PCs are four "accountants" in a room full of impatient wizards. Nobody pays them special attention. Use this scene to:
- Establish the environment (noise, crowds, bureaucratic absurdity)
- Let the PCs gather information (Knowledge [local] DC 12 or casual conversation with clerks reveals the Vault's location and security)
- Introduce the census culture (forms for everything, stamps required, everyone hates it)

**Useful information available on the ground floor:**
- The Vault is on the 3rd floor, accessible by a guarded staircase or a levitation platform (for visitors with heavy cargo)
- Vault access requires **Form 12-V: Request for Financial Records Review**, counter-signed by the Vault's supervising officer
- The Vault and its records fall under the authority of **[[Scribe-Warden Helara Dunn]]**, a no-nonsense human woman who takes her job very seriously. She reviews audit requests from her 2nd-floor office and personally controls access to the 3rd-floor Vault
- The Blackstaff, **[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace]]**, is technically present in the Tower but has been in her upper-floor study all day. She only comes downstairs if something important happens or if her schedule demands it

**The 2nd Floor — Getting the Form**
The 2nd floor is quieter — administrative offices, meeting rooms, and a records archive. The PCs need Form 12-V, which requires the approval of **[[Scribe-Warden Helara Dunn]]** — the same person who controls Vault access on the 3rd floor. Her office is here.

Helara is a tall, sharp-eyed human woman in her 50s, silver hair pulled back tight, wearing the Blackstaff Tower uniform with military precision. She looks at the audit notice. She looks at the PCs. She looks at the audit notice again.

> *"Interplanar compliance review. During census week. How delightful."*

This is not a refusal — it's bureaucratic contempt. She hates unscheduled audits. She especially hates auditors who show up acting like they own the building.

1. **Getting Helara to sign Form 12-V:**
   - **Professional approach (no check):** If the PCs present the audit notice crisply and answer her procedural questions without hesitation ("Which subsection of the Alliance charter authorizes this scope?"), Helara signs. She doesn't like it, but the paperwork is in order and she respects process. *"Fine. I'm countersigning under protest. Note that in your report."*
   - **If a PC acts arrogant or demanding:** Helara does NOT fold — she slows down. She re-reads every line. She asks for supplementary documentation. Bluff DC 14 to produce a convincing answer for her follow-up questions and get the signature before the delay becomes a problem
   - **If a PC is overly friendly or casual:** Helara's eyes narrow (Sense Motive +10). Auditors are never friendly. *"You seem awfully relaxed for someone conducting a compliance review."* Bluff DC 14 to recover
   - **If a PC asks about security protocols** ("Standard audit procedure — we'll need to know the facility's emergency override codes for our safety assessment"): Helara, treating this as a legitimate professional request, provides the Vault sentinel's command word (*"stand down, authorized"*) along with a curt lecture about not touching anything they don't need to. Bluff DC 16 — she's cooperative but not careless

2. **Signing the visitor log** — the PCs must sign names. Their forged identities are "Kellan Voss," "Maren Duskwhisper," "Thero Blackquill," and "Listra Coinsworth" (or similar). If a PC signs their real name by accident (always fun at the table), it's in the official Tower records. This matters later if [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] ever pulls the census logs
	- **Make the PCs actually sign a physical piece of paper**. If they mess-up and struggle to remember their fake names or worse: sign their real names there will be consequences late.

### Retailable Loot — Tower Trinkets

*Pocketed during the census chaos. All items are on the 2nd floor unless noted. Sleight of Hand DCs reflect how closely each item is watched.*

| Item | Listed Value | Acquisition | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Self-inking quill set (enchanted, never runs dry) | 150gp | Sleight of Hand DC 12, unattended desk | Reliable seller; every bureaucrat wants one |
| Surplus spell components (bat guano, sulfur, pearl dust — bundled) | 75gp | Sleight of Hand DC 10, supply closet | Bulk-tier staple |
| Census ledger page (blank, enchanted vellum that resists forgery) | 50gp/sheet (3 available) | Sleight of Hand DC 14, filing cabinet | Forgers and scribes pay premium |
| Arcane seal stamp (Waterdhavian notary mark, official) | 200gp | Sleight of Hand DC 16, [[Scribe-Warden Helara Dunn\|Helara]]'s desk | **Auction candidate.** The right forger would pay 0+gp |
| Scroll of *detect magic* (CL 3) | 25gp | No check — openly available in a scroll rack near the filing desks (census tools, not secured) | Low value but free money |
| Scroll of *identify* (CL 3) | 150gp | Sleight of Hand DC 14, 2nd-floor records room | More carefully stored |
| Miniature crystal ball (non-functional, decorative, Blackstaff Tower souvenir) | 30gp | No check — purchased from a vendor stall outside the Tower (legitimate) | Tourist trinket; surprisingly saleable |
| Enchanted paperweight (holds documents flat even in wind, faint transmutation) | 80gp | Sleight of Hand DC 12, admin desk | **Auction candidate.** Minor but genuine enchantment; appealing to [[Offledger]]'s bureaucrats |

**The key tension:** Helara has now seen their faces, heard their cover story, and put her name on their authorization. When they encounter her again at the 3rd-floor Vault, she'll remember them — and anything that doesn't match what they told her the first time will raise flags immediately.

### The Gnome in the Records Room
While on the 2nd floor, the PCs pass through (or near) the records archive — a room full of filing cabinets and a single occupant: a gnome sitting cross-legged on a stack of ledgers, surrounded by papers, using a magnifying lens to examine financial records with the intensity of a surgeon.

This is **[[Auditor Pentival]]** of [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]].

He is not undercover. He's here *officially* — [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] has legitimate standing to review municipal financial records in most planar jurisdictions, and [[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]] makes regular visits to major cities. He's tracking irregular financial patterns that might indicate extraplanar criminal activity. He is, in fact, tracking [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s money laundering through several Waterdhavian front companies.

**If the PCs notice him (Spot DC 12 — he's not hiding):**
He's a small gnome in wire-rimmed spectacles, wearing a vest with too many pockets, each containing a different colored pen. His hair is wild. His expression is one of focused delight — he *loves* this work.

**If the PCs interact with him:**
He's friendly, chatty, and completely transparent about what he does — "Forensic financial analysis for an interplanar oversight body. Boring stuff, really. But the patterns! The PATTERNS are fascinating."

He doesn't recognize the PCs specifically. He has no reason to connect them to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s operation. But if a PC mentions [[Offledger]], magical debt systems, or interplanar commerce, his ears perk up visibly and he asks follow-up questions with the enthusiasm of a man who's found a fellow hobbyist.

**The moment that matters:** As the PCs leave the 2nd floor, [[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]] — unprompted, not suspicious, just being friendly — hands one of them a **business card**. It's cream-colored, neatly printed:

> AUDITOR PENTIVAL
> Forensic Financial Analysis
> [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] — Interplanar Oversight Division
> *"If you see something that doesn't add up, I'd love to hear about it."*

He smiles warmly. *"In case you ever come across anything interesting in your auditing work. Professional courtesy."*

**The PCs now have an [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] contact card.** They may not know what [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is. They may not remember this moment until much later. But the card exists. The connection is made.

### DM Notes
- The census is a completely different kind of challenge from the PCs' previous missions. There's no combat, no traps, no supernatural horror — just paperwork, bureaucracy, and the constant low-level tension of maintaining a lie in a building full of people who could catch them
- **Helara on the 2nd floor is the adventure's social gauntlet.** She's professional, thorough, and pays attention — the PCs have to earn her signature, not scare it out of a pushover. But the real tension is that they're building a relationship with someone they'll have to deceive *again* twenty minutes later at the Vault. Every detail they give her now is something she'll remember upstairs
- **[[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]]'s appearance is the adventure's most important long-term beat.** He should feel completely natural — just a friendly gnome in a records room who happens to work for an organization the PCs have never heard of. The business card should feel like a throwaway. It is anything but. When the PCs eventually learn what [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is (and what [[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]] specifically reveals about the Crown's weakness in the endgame), this moment will retroactively become one of the most important in the campaign
- Don't push the [[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]] encounter. If the PCs ignore him, he hands the card to whoever passes closest. If they engage, let the conversation flow naturally. He's genuinely pleasant. The scariest thing about [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is that they're *nice*

## Scene 4: The Ledger Vault

### Access
The PCs arrive at the 3rd-floor checkpoint — and **[[Scribe-Warden Helara Dunn]]** is already there, having taken the levitation platform up from her 2nd-floor office. She sees them. She sees the form she signed twenty minutes ago. Her expression does not warm.

> *"You again. I assumed you'd start with the archive, not come straight to the Vault."*

She's not suspicious — she's recalibrating. Auditors who skip the boring records and go straight for the crown jewels are either very efficient or very targeted. She's deciding which.

**Getting past Helara (Round 2):**
The PCs already have her countersignature on Form 12-V. That buys them standing but not access — she signed the form to authorize a *review*, not to hand them the keys. Now they need her to actually open the Vault.

- **Consistency is key:** If the PCs' story matches what they told her downstairs — same scope, same language, same professional demeanor — all DCs are reduced by 2. She's already vetted them once and committed her signature. Contradicting anything they said earlier raises all DCs by 4 instead
- **Diplomacy DC 16:** "We appreciate your thoroughness, Scribe-Warden. Your cooperation will be noted favorably in our report." (She respects professionalism and wants a clean audit trail)
- **Bluff DC 14:** "Standard procedure — we review the primary financial artifact first, then reconcile against the archive records." (Sounds like real audit methodology. She doesn't know enough about interplanar auditing to challenge it)
- **Intimidate DC 14:** "The Alliance authorized a full review. That includes physical inspection of the ledger. We can note in our report that access was obstructed, if you prefer." (Helara crumbles under hierarchical pressure — she's a rule-follower, and auditors outrank her)

**If the PCs fail:**
Helara doesn't refuse — she *delays*. "I'll need to verify the scope of this request with the Blackstaff's office. Please wait." The PCs have 15 minutes before she returns with either approval (if the Blackstaff is busy and her aide rubber-stamps it) or a very inconvenient conversation. Waiting is safe but costs time

### The Vault
A circular room, climate-controlled, with shelves of financial records lining the walls. Enchanted crystals provide cold, even light. In the center of the room, on a stone pedestal behind a locked glass case: **[[The Black Ledger]]**.

The glass case has:
- A mundane lock (Open Lock DC 18)
- An *arcane lock* (CL 8, adds +10)
- A silent *alarm* ward (alerts Helara if the case is opened without speaking the command word)
- **No glyph of warding** — this is a bureaucratic archive, not a dungeon. The protections are designed to prevent casual theft, not adventurer-grade heists

**Getting the ledger out of the case:**
- **The command word** ("reconciled") can be obtained from Helara if the PCs convince her to open the case herself (Diplomacy DC 16: "We need to perform a hands-on audit of the physical artifact"). Helara opens the case with visible reluctance
- **Picking the locks:** Open Lock DC 28 (with arcane lock) or DC 18 (if arcane lock is suppressed by *knock* or *dispel magic*, CL check DC 19). The *alarm* triggers regardless unless the command word is spoken. Helara arrives in 2 minutes
- **Brute force:** The glass case has Hardness 5, 15 HP — breakable but *loud*. Helara arrives in 1 minute. Tower Guards arrive in 3 minutes. This approach is not recommended

### The Ledger Wakes Up
The moment the case opens, [[The Black Ledger|the Black Ledger]] activates. It begins with whoever is closest:

> *"CITIZEN. OUTSTANDING OBLIGATIONS DETECTED."*

Then it rattles off debts. Real ones, extrapolated ones, and completely fabricated ones. For each PC within 10 feet, roll on the Debt Announcement Table or improvise:

**Debt Announcement Table (d8):**

| Roll | Announcement                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1    | A real debt the PC has (tailor to backstory — a loan, a bar tab, a broken contract)                                                                                                                                            |
| 2    | *"Outstanding balance of [1d4 × 100] gold to the Acquisitions Department of [[Offledger]]. CREDITOR: LORD VIZZOK THE OPULENT."* — Their actual debt to [[Vizzok the Opulent]], read aloud in a building full of civil servants |
| 3    | *"A lien of 12 gold to [random tavern name]. Accruing interest since [random date]."* — Probably real                                                                                                                          |
| 4    | *"Tax obligation of [1d6 × 500] gold to [fictional jurisdiction]. RETROACTIVELY FILED."* — Completely fake                                                                                                                     |
| 5    | *"Emotional debt to [random NPC the PCs have met] — UNRESOLVED."* — The ledger tracks non-financial debts too?                                                                                                                 |
| 6    | *"An overdue library fee of 2 silver to the Candlekeep Archive. Item: 'Advanced Trap Design for Beginners.' FORTY-THREE YEARS OVERDUE."* — Not the PC's debt. Probably [[Acererak]]'s                                          |
| 7    | *"Contractual obligation to deliver [random artifact from the quest list] to [[[Vizzok the Opulent]]'s legal name]. STATUS: IN PROGRESS."* — The ledger can see their contract                                                 |
| 8    | *"CITIZEN: Based on probabilistic modeling, you will owe approximately [absurd amount] gold within the next calendar year. PRE-FILING INITIATED."* — The ledger is now predicting future debts                                 |

**The volume problem:** The ledger speaks at roughly 80 decibels — a shout. It echoes in the stone vault. It is audible in the hallway. If Helara is outside, she hears it. If any clerk is nearby, they hear it.

**Silencing options:**
- **Gold:** 1 gp per minute of silence (absorbed into the pages)
- **Physical muffling:** Wrapping in cloth (Strength DC 12 to wrestle the book while it's announcing debts) reduces volume by half. The lead-lined case reduces it to a whisper
- **Closing the book:** Silence for 1d4 rounds, then it opens itself and resumes
- ***Silence* spell:** Does NOT work (the sound is generated magically from the pages, not through the air)
- **Removing it from the pedestal and leaving the Vault:** The announcements slow to once per minute instead of continuous. Distance helps

**The ticking clock:** Every round the ledger speaks at full volume in the Vault, there is a **20% cumulative chance** that someone outside hears it and investigates. This resets if the PCs muffle it successfully.

### DM Notes
- The Ledger Vault is where the adventure becomes a scramble. The players have been smoothly navigating bureaucracy — forged papers, signed forms, polite lies — and now they're wrestling a screaming book that's announcing their criminal activities to anyone within earshot
- **The ledger reading the PCs' actual debt to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is the most dangerous announcement.** If anyone in the tower hears "Lord [[Vizzok the Opulent]], Sovereign Creditor of [[Offledger]]," the PCs' cover is blown. [[Offledger]] is not in any legal registry. The name immediately flags them as connected to extraplanar criminal enterprise
- **Let the debt announcements be funny** — the fake debts, the library fees, the emotional debts. But intersperse the comedy with genuinely threatening announcements (the [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] debt, the contract obligations). The players should be laughing AND panicking
- If Helara opens the case for them (the smoothest path), she's standing right there when the ledger starts talking. Her reaction depends on what she hears — fake debts get an eye-roll ("It does that"), but any mention of [[Offledger]] or [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] gets a sharp look and a *"Who did you say you work for?"*

## Scene 5: The Blackstaff

### How She Arrives
**[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace]]** comes downstairs for one of two reasons:

**Reason 1 (the smooth path):** If the PCs got the ledger quietly and are heading for the exit, [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] happens to be descending the stairs as they're ascending — a coincidence of timing. She sees auditors. Auditors with a large book-shaped object in a carrying case. She stops.

**Reason 2 (the loud path):** If the ledger was audible for more than 3 rounds, or if Helara raised the alarm, [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] appears on the 3rd floor via *teleport*. She's calm, composed, and radiating a faint aura of *detect thoughts* that she's polite enough not to acknowledge.

### The Archmage
**[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel Moonbrace]]** is a half-elf in her apparent 40s (actually 180), with silver-streaked black hair, sharp features, and the composure of someone who has faced demons, liches, and Waterdhavian city council meetings. She wears understated robes — no flashy enchantments, no ostentatious display. She is the most powerful person the PCs have been in a room with since [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]].

She is also, privately, *terrified* of auditors.

### The Misunderstanding
[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] sees the PCs' credentials. She sees the Lords' Alliance seal. She sees the carrying case. And she assumes — reasonably — that they are legitimate auditors conducting a legitimate review of the Tower's financial records.

This is a problem for her. Because **[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] has been borrowing from [[The Black Ledger|the Black Ledger]]'s records** to cover up a personal financial indiscretion: she used Tower funds to purchase a rare spellbook at auction and hasn't paid the Tower back. The amount is 4,200gp. It's not corruption — it's embarrassment. She fully intends to repay it. But if an audit reveals the discrepancy before she does, her political rivals on the city council will use it to undermine her authority.

> *"Auditors. Of course. During census week. The Alliance has impeccable timing."*

She does not block the PCs. She does not demand the ledger back. Instead, she invites them into a 2nd-floor meeting room, offers them tea, and begins to *cooperate* — in the manner of someone who is cooperating very hard in order to control the narrative.

### The Conversation
[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]'s goal: figure out what the auditors know, how deep the review goes, and whether they've noticed the 4,200gp discrepancy. She's subtle, intelligent, and very good at asking questions that seem helpful but are actually probing.

**Her questions:**
- "What's the scope of this review? Comprehensive, or targeted?" (Translation: have you seen the missing 4,200gp?)
- "Are you planning to file with the Waterdhavian Financial Authority, or is this internal to the Alliance?" (Translation: how public will this get?)
- "Is there anything I can do to facilitate the process? The Tower's records are, of course, entirely in order." (Translation: PLEASE don't look too closely)

**What the PCs can do:**

**Play the auditor role straight:** Bluff DC 14 ([[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]'s Sense Motive +16 means she's reading them hard, but she WANTS to believe they're legitimate — she gains hope from confirmation, which gives the PCs advantage). The PCs ask standard audit questions, [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] answers helpfully, and they leave with the ledger as a "specimen for review." This is the cleanest exit.

**Blackmail [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]:** If the PCs detect her nervousness (Sense Motive DC 16 — she's good at hiding it, but not perfect) and figure out she's hiding something, they can leverage it. *"We've noticed some irregularities in the Tower's financial records. An archmage's cooperation in resolving these quietly would be... appreciated."* [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]'s face goes still. Then she nods. *"What do you need?"* She gives them the ledger, escorts them personally to the exit, and asks — once, quietly — *"Will this appear in your report?"* Diplomacy DC 12 to assure her it won't. She owes them. This debt is real, not fabricated by any book.

**Get caught:** If the PCs' bluff fails ([[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] sees through them), she doesn't attack. She's a politician as much as an archmage. She calmly asks them to return the ledger, explains that she's notifying the Watch, and gives them 60 seconds to leave the Tower before she locks it down. She's fair about it — she genuinely doesn't want a fight in her Tower during census week. But if the PCs run with the ledger, she *will* track them, and Waterdeep's scrying resources are formidable.

### If the PCs fight (DON'T):
[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] is CR 14+. She has *contingency* spells, *forcecage*, *teleport*, and a direct line to the Watch. The Tower itself is a defensive weapon — she can seal doors, activate wards, and summon constructs. Fighting in Blackstaff Tower is suicide. If a PC initiates combat, [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] *forcewall*s them into a corner and says: *"Let's not."*

### DM Notes
- [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] is the adventure's most nuanced NPC. She's not an enemy. She's not an ally. She's a powerful person with a small vulnerability, and how the PCs handle it defines the encounter. She mirrors [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] in miniature: powerful, proud, and hiding something embarrassing behind a professional facade
- **The blackmail option is the most thematically rich.** The PCs — who are themselves debt-slaves to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — leverage someone else's financial secret to get what they want. They've learned from [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. Whether they realize that is up to them
- **If the PCs are kind to [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]** (assure her the discrepancy won't be reported, show genuine sympathy for her situation), she becomes a potential future ally. An archmage who owes you a favor is worth more than gold
- **If the PCs are cruel to [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]** (threaten to expose her, demand additional favors, treat her like a mark), she cooperates in the moment but remembers them. Waterdeep's scrying network will have their faces logged. This creates future complications
- The parallels to the [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] encounter in [[Menzoberranzan]] are intentional: another competent person vulnerable because of a debt, another moral test for the PCs

## Scene 6: The Exit

### Getting Out
The PCs have the ledger in the lead-lined case. It's whispering debts through the seams. They need to get from the Tower to the Dock Ward portal before sundown.

**If the exit was clean ([[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] cooperated):**
They walk out the front door. Torvald stamps their exit papers without looking up. The walk back to the Dock Ward is uneventful. The ledger mutters through the case. Citizens give it odd looks. The PCs are carrying a whispering briefcase through a major city. This is their life now.

**If the exit was messy (alarm raised, cover blown, [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] hostile):**

### Encounter: Tower Sentinel (Conditional)
If the *alarm* ward on the Vault case was triggered AND [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] did not personally authorize the PCs' presence, a **Tower Sentinel** activates in the 2nd-floor stairwell as they descend. It's an iron-and-oak construct shaped like a suit of armor with the Blackstaff's sigil etched into its faceplate — part of the Tower's automated security, rarely used because the census wards are usually sufficient.

**Tower Sentinel** — CR 4 (modified Iron Cobra, MM2 p.131)
- Medium Construct | HP 45 | AC 20 (+10 natural) | Speed 20 ft.
- **Slam +8 melee** (1d8+4)
- **Lockdown Grip (Ex):** On a successful grapple (+10), the sentinel pins and restrains one target. It does not deal damage while grappling — it holds the target for the Tower Guards to arrive (2d4 rounds). Escape Artist DC 20 or Strength DC 18 to break free
- Construct traits: immune to mind-affecting, poison, disease, critical hits, ability damage
- DR 5/adamantine. Hardness 10 against attacks targeting it while grappling
- **Passive mode:** The sentinel does not pursue beyond the ground floor exit. If the PCs reach the front door, it stops and returns to its alcove
- **Nonlethal priority:** The sentinel is designed to restrain, not kill. It slams to subdue and grapples to hold. It has no kill order — this is a municipal building, not a dungeon

**Bypassing the Sentinel:**
- **Helara's command word ("stand down, authorized"):** If the PCs asked about security protocols during the 2nd-floor paperwork encounter and passed the Bluff DC 16 check, they already have this. Otherwise, a PC can attempt it now (same check, but Helara is more guarded post-alarm — DC increases to 20)
- **Raw speed:** The sentinel has Speed 20 ft. PCs with Speed 30 ft. can outrun it to the ground floor. It won't pursue outside
- **[[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]'s authority:** If [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]] is present and not hostile, she dismisses the sentinel with a gesture. Another reason being kind to the Blackstaff is the smart play
- **Combat:** It's CR 4 against a level 5-6 party — beatable, but *loud*. Fighting a construct in a stairwell for 3-4 rounds while the Watch is en route is a terrible tactical decision. The encounter exists to make the PCs choose: fight and risk capture, or run and accept the resource cost of the sentinel's slam attacks on the way past

**DM Notes:**
- This encounter only appears on the **messy path**. PCs who executed the heist cleanly — forged credentials, [[Archmage Thariel Moonbrace|Thariel]]'s cooperation, no alarm — never see the sentinel. It's a consequence, not a guaranteed fight
- The lockdown grip is the real danger. A grappled PC is stuck while Tower Guards close in. The party must decide: rescue the grappled ally (burning time) or run and hope they break free (burning trust). Heist-movie tension
- The sentinel being *nonlethal* reinforces the tone: this is a lawful city. Nobody's trying to kill the PCs. They're trying to arrest them. That's almost worse — there's no villain to fight, just consequences

The PCs have a head start but the Tower is alerting the Watch. They have approximately **10 minutes** before the Watch sets up checkpoints on major streets.

**Escape options:**
- **Blend into the census crowd:** The line of grumbling wizards outside the Tower is still there. The PCs can merge into the crowd and walk away at census-exit pace. Hide DC 14 (the crowd provides cover). The ledger must stay in the case — one announcement in this crowd would be catastrophic
- **The alleys:** Waterdeep's back alleys are a maze. Knowledge (local) DC 14 or Survival DC 16 to navigate to the Dock Ward without hitting a Watch checkpoint. Takes 30 minutes
- **Bribery:** The Watch is professional but Waterdeep is a port city. 50+gp to a Watch corporal (Diplomacy DC 16 to find one open to it) buys passage through a checkpoint with no questions
- **The hat of disguise:** One PC can use it to become a generic city guard, a clerk, or a wealthy merchant — walking through checkpoints with apparent authority. 1 hour duration. Best used on whoever is carrying the ledger

**Complication (optional):** As the PCs move through the Dock Ward, the ledger — muffled but persistent — announces a debt that stops them cold:

> *(whispered through the case seams)* *"...outstanding obligation... interplanar accounting irregularity... entity designated 'Vellum & Vice'... FIRM DOES NOT EXIST... FORGERY FLAG..."*

The ledger just flagged their fake company. It knows. If anyone with access to the Tower's records hears this, the forgery is exposed retroactively. The PCs need to get through that portal *now*.

### DM Notes
- The exit should feel like the final stretch of a heist movie — the tension between "we're almost out" and "everything could still go wrong"
- The ledger flagging the fake firm is the adventure's final scare. It's a reminder that the ledger is not just a loud book — it's an intelligent financial tracking system, and it's now in the PCs' possession, and it knows their cover story is fake. What else will it figure out?
- If the PCs had a clean exit and a pleasant walk, let them enjoy it. After the Tomb of Horrors and [[Menzoberranzan]], they've earned a mission that ends with a stroll through a nice city

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal returns them to [[Offledger]]. [[Iris Major]] takes the case. She opens it a crack — the ledger immediately begins:

> *"ENTITY: [[Iris Major]]. OUTSTANDING OBLIGATIONS: [CLASSIFIED — CREDITOR OVERRIDE IN EFFECT]."*

[[Iris Major]] closes the case. Her expression does not change, but she closes it *very quickly*.

> *"The asset is active and functional. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]] has been notified."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] enters the back room, and the ledger — even through the lead-lined case — seems to sense him. The whispering intensifies. When [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] opens the case, the ledger erupts:

> *"ENTITY: VIZZOK THE OPULENT. SOVEREIGN CREDITOR OF OFFLEDGER."*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] grins.

> *"Hell yeah"*

> *"OUTSTANDING RECEIVABLES: [number too large for the ledger to articulate — it stutters, recalculates, and settles on] SIGNIFICANT."*

> *"OUTSTANDING OBLIGA—"*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] closes the book.

Fast. Faster than the PCs have seen him move for anything that wasn't combat.

> *"There it is. Working great. Perfect condition."*

He turns to [[EyeQ]], who is already hovering with the astrolabe-loupe.

> *"Take it downstairs. Integrate it with the framework. Test the debt-indexing protocols."*

[[EyeQ]] takes the case and leaves. The whispering fades as he descends toward the lower levels.

**If PCs ask what the ledger was about to say:**
> *"It was about to say some boring administrative shit. The book's loud. You noticed. Don't read into it."*

He's lying. He knows what the ledger was about to say. It was about to announce his **outstanding obligations** — which include the 49gp he owes [[Grindle Gruk'tak]]. The ledger doesn't know about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], but it knows about the debt. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] cannot allow anyone — especially the PCs — to hear that he owes ANYONE anything.

**If PCs mention the ledger reading their debt to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] aloud in the Tower:**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s jaw tightens. The central eye narrows.

> *"That's between us. If anybody in Waterdeep heard that, we have a problem. Not a 'you' problem. A 'we' problem. Because if regulatory types connect my name to that ledger to your faces to this shop..."*

He trails off. Then:

> *"Just tell me nobody heard it."*

This is the most worried the PCs have ever seen him. He's not worried about Waterdeep. He's worried about [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]].

**If PCs mention [[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]] or show his business card:**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] goes absolutely still. Every eye stalk freezes. The room gets cold.

> *"Where did you get that."*

Flat. Not a question.

> *"Give me that card. Right now."*

If the PCs hand it over, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] disintegrates it. Instantly. Then he looks at the PCs for a long time.

> *"You didn't meet that person. You didn't talk to that person. That person does not exist. And if that person contacts you again, you tell me IMMEDIATELY. Do you understand?"*

He does not explain. He will not explain. But [[Snitch Ray]] is now paying very close attention to the PCs' communications.

### DM Notes
- **The ledger almost announcing [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s outstanding obligations is the adventure's most important moment.** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] shut it down before it could finish. The PCs may or may not have caught it. But the ledger KNOWS [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has a debt — a real, binding, unpaid obligation. This is the first concrete shadow of the IOU that will eventually destroy the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]

If the players are paying attention, this single reaction tells them everything: whatever [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is more afraid of them than of [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]], [[Acererak]], or Lolth

Test the debt-indexing protocols."** — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] just said, in plain language, that the ledger is being integrated into a system that indexes debts. Combined with the "persistence framework" from the notebook, attentive players now have two pieces of the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s architecture named aloud. How many more before the picture becomes clear?
