# GP is XP
*Advancement requires investment.*

## The Rule
Players do **not** earn experience points through combat, exploration, or roleplaying in the traditional sense. Instead, **gold pieces spent explicitly for the purpose of leveling up** are the sole means of advancement.

- Gold spent for leveling **does not** count toward any other transaction, upgrade, or service.
- You cannot level up "for free" by finding a sword — you level up by *burning gold into the universe*.
- Coins must jingle in the hands of fate, not fall into the coffers of convenience.

## Design Intent
This creates a three-way tension at the heart of the campaign:
1. **Level up** — spend gold to get stronger
2. **Stock the shop** — spend gold on inventory to sell for profit
3. **Pay the debt** — tribute to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is non-negotiable

Every gold piece is a choice. That's the point.

## Narrative Flavor
*How does leveling "feel" in-world?* Some possibilities:
- The gold is ritually sacrificed, burned, or dissolved into raw magical potential
- The Coinspiratorium's ledger records the "investment" and the universe... adjusts
- Veezy's contract literally siphons power from wealth — the more you spend, the more the contract empowers you
- It's a mystery even to the players — the gold vanishes and they just... get better
