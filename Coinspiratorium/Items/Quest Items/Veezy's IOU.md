# Veezy's IOU
*The Cape & The Mixtape*

> **DM EYES ONLY** — This item is the campaign's endgame MacGuffin.

---

## Description
A scrap of shimmering vellum, creased and faded, written in a hurried hand with cheap quill ink. It smells faintly of old rags and goblin cooking. It looks like junk.

It is the most valuable object in the multiverse.

## The Text

> *"I, Vizzok the Visionary, hereby promise to repay*
> *forty-nine gold pieces (49gp)*
> *lent to me by Grindle Gruk'tak*
> *for:*
> *(1) One Vision Cape*
> *(2) 10-record mixtape pressing ('Eyes on the Horizon')*
> *with interest,*
> *after my first big score.*
>
> *— V."*

Yes, the mixtape is titled *Eyes on the Horizon.*
Yes, it is terrible.

## Properties
- **Type:** Debt instrument / arcane contract
- **Apparent Value:** Worthless (a forgotten IOU for 49gp)
- **Actual Value:** Infinite — the highest-value debt instrument in existence
- **Aura:** Faint (undetectable without specific divination targeting debt magic)
- **Weight:** Negligible

## Why It Matters
Under the [[The Device|Crown Infinite's]] own metaphysical rules:

> *Any unpaid debt owed by the Crown's Master Creditor becomes the highest-value liability in the system.*

Because:
- A creditor cannot owe
- A debt cannot self-cancel
- Recursive interest has nowhere to flow
- The Universal Ledger prioritizes unresolved obligations
- The engine cannot reconcile a creditor being a debtor

Veezy's 49gp debt — with centuries of compounding interest and no recipient to pay — creates a **metaphysical paradox** that is worth more than every soul, nation, and plane combined.

## How It's Used
The PCs present the IOU at the Coinspiratorium counter. The store appraises it at full metaphysical value. The debt transfers from Veezy (debtor) to the PCs (creditors presenting it). The infinite value is applied to the PCs' own debt.

**Their contract is fulfilled in a single transaction.**

The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] instantly:
- Loses its keystone obligation
- Loses the PCs as original debtors
- Loses the cosmic anchor
- Loses the recursive economic structure
- Suffers a metaphysical null-pointer error
- **Collapses.**

## Where It Is
In a drawer in [[Grindle Gruk'tak]]'s shabby potion stall, wrapped in old rags. He gives it away casually. He has no idea what it is.

## Fine Print
This item has no magical properties in the traditional sense. It cannot be detected by *detect magic*, *identify*, or similar spells. Its power is entirely **contractual and metaphysical** — it only becomes relevant once the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is active and the PCs understand how the system works.

It is, in every measurable way, a worthless scrap of paper.
Until it isn't.

## Notes
- The IOU should be acquired in what feels like a throwaway side quest
- The PCs should have it in their possession for at least one or two sessions before they realize what it does
- The "aha" moment should come from an offhand comment by a sage or O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. auditor
- If the PCs try to *identify* or *detect magic* on it, nothing happens — it's not magical, it's *contractual*
