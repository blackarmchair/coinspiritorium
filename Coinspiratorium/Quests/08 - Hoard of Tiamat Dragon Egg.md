# "Borrow a Dragon's Egg from the Hoard of Tiamat"

> *"Collector's item. Limited edition. Never hatches. Gorgeous."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs into Tiamat's Clutch — a demiplanar hoard-vault hidden inside a volcanic caldera — to steal a platinum dragon egg that will never hatch. The egg is sterile, promised a destiny it can never fulfill, and it screams the moment anyone touches it. Constantly. Loudly. With the voice of a dragon that will never be born.

This is the most dangerous mission the PCs have received. For the first time, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doesn't trust the PCs to handle it alone. He sends [[EyeRate]] — the Eyecon of violence, the attack dog, the being the PCs have been terrified of since Session 1 — as field support. [[EyeRate]] is thrilled. He's been wanting to hit something for eight quests.

The Clutch is guarded by five **draconic aspects** — autonomous manifestations of Tiamat's five chromatic heads, each ruling a themed chamber. The aspects don't attack on sight. They ask riddles. They demand bribes — specific, personal, costly. Answer correctly, and the aspect grants passage. Answer wrong, and the aspect responds with a breath weapon. The PCs must navigate all five chambers through wit, sacrifice, and creative thinking to reach the egg at the Clutch's heart.

Then they touch the egg. And it screams. And they have to run back through all five chambers while a platinum dragon's unborn voice echoes through a lair full of angry dragon aspects who did NOT authorize removal of property from the hoard.

**Tone arc:** Grim determination (briefing — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is changed, [[EyeRate]] is deployed) → Volcanic awe (the Clutch exterior) → Puzzle intensity (the five chambers — each bribe costs something real) → Tragic beauty (the egg chamber — what it is and why it's here) → Desperate comedy (the screaming escape) → Raw revelation ([[EyeRate]]'s sacrifice, the debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Five puzzle-social encounters (the aspect bribes), the screaming egg escape chase, one major combat encounter (escape climax), and the emotional weight of stealing an unfulfilled promise
**Combat:** Minimal during the chambers (puzzle-focused), escalating during escape, one climactic fight; [[EyeRate]] is field support but creates his own complications

## Target Item
**[[Platinum Dragon Egg|Platinum Dragon Egg (Sterile, Promised)]]** — An egg the size of a large watermelon, its shell a luminous silver-white that shifts through subtle iridescence when it catches light. Platinum dragon eggs are extraordinarily rare — platinum dragons barely exist in recorded history, and those that do are solitary, ancient, and divine. This egg should have produced the most powerful metallic dragon in ten thousand years.

It didn't. The egg is sterile. The dragon inside never formed. The shell is intact, perfect, beautiful, and empty.

Not quite empty. The egg contains **unrealized potential** — the compressed essence of a dragon that should have been, a destiny that was promised and never delivered. Bahamut himself wept when the egg failed. The aboleths remember the sound.

**The Scream:**
When a living creature touches the egg with bare skin, it begins screaming. Not the roar of a dragon — the cry of something being born. Or trying to be born. Or knowing it will never be born. The scream is:
- **Loud:** Audible within 500 feet. No mundane method silences it. *Silence* spell suppresses it for 1d4 rounds before the egg's divine resonance overcomes the magic. Only *antimagic field* fully suppresses it (and suppresses everything else too)
- **Continuous:** It does not stop. Once triggered, the egg screams until it is no longer in contact with a living creature — but even placing it in a bag or container only muffles it (reduces to 100-foot radius). True silence requires placing it on bare stone with no living creature within 5 feet
- **Emotionally devastating:** Every creature that hears the scream must make a Will save (DC 18) once per minute or be *shaken* for 1 round. Not from fear — from sorrow. The scream triggers a profound grief response in any being capable of emotion. The listeners feel, deep in their bones, that something that should have existed doesn't, and the loss is staggering
- **The carrier is immune to the shaken effect** but hears the scream more clearly than anyone. The carrier hears words in the scream — not language, exactly, but meaning. The egg is saying: *let me out, let me out, let me out.* It has been saying this for millennia. No one has ever been able to help

**The Promise:**
The egg is "Promised" because Bahamut — before the egg was laid — spoke a destiny over it. The platinum dragon that would have hatched was to be a champion, a beacon, a living embodiment of draconic justice. The promise is divine, binding, and unbreakable. But the egg is sterile. The promise refers to a being that doesn't exist and never will. It is a contract with no signatory. An IOU from God to nothing.

**Why Tiamat has it:**
Centuries ago, Bahamut surrendered the egg to Tiamat. Not through war — through grief. The egg was a constant reminder of divine failure, and Bahamut couldn't bear it. Tiamat accepted it as part of a transaction — the specifics lost to mortal knowledge, though the aboleths remember. Tiamat keeps the egg because it's the one thing Bahamut GAVE her. Not stolen. Not conquered. Surrendered. It sits in her Clutch as the centerpiece of a trophy collection that spans millennia: proof that even the Platinum Dragon can lose.

**Properties:**
- **The Scream** (as described above)
- **Divine resonance:** The egg radiates a faint aura of divine power (Bahamut's promise). *Detect magic* registers Overwhelming Abjuration and Divination. The egg cannot be damaged by any means short of divine intervention
- **Potential battery:** The egg contains the unrealized potential of a platinum dragon — compressed, unspent, vibrating with energy that has nowhere to go. This energy is what the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] needs: raw, divine, unrealized power. Potential with no outlet
- **Grief aura:** Any creature within 10 feet of the egg for more than 1 hour begins experiencing involuntary memories of their own unfulfilled potential — things they could have been, paths they didn't take, promises they broke. This has no mechanical effect but is profoundly unsettling

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Draconic potential battery — the egg's unrealized divine energy powers the Crown's transformation engine. The Crown doesn't just rewrite reality — it needs the ENERGY to do so, and that energy must be potential, not kinetic. Not power that IS. Power that COULD HAVE BEEN. The egg provides an infinite reservoir of divine possibility that will never be spent, because the dragon will never hatch. The Crown runs on stolen futures. The egg is the biggest stolen future in the multiverse.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The back room. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is here. He's back.

But he's different.

The showmanship is still there — the diamond grill catches the light, the chains clink, the goblet of purple something is in his tentacle. But it's thinner. Like watching a comedian do a set they've rehearsed too many times. The gestures are right. The energy is off. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has been spending all his time in the Workshop with the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], and it shows. His central eye has a slight golden tint that wasn't there before Quest 7. The circuits on [[EyeQ]]'s shell are now visible on [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] too — faint golden lines tracing patterns across his hide, pulsing in time with the Workshop's hum.

The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is becoming part of him. Or he's becoming part of it. The distinction may not matter.

[[EyeRate]] hovers behind [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], vibrating with barely contained energy. [[EyeRate]] is smaller than a full beholder — a gauth-sized beholder-kin with four eyestalks, each one scarred, chipped, or visibly damaged from past violence. His central eye is a deep, furious red. He does not hold still. He orbits [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] in tight circles, eyestalks twitching at the PCs, at the walls, at shadows that aren't there. [[EyeRate]] doesn't enter rooms. He **arrives at them violently**.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"This next one's different. Everything before this was warming up. This one..."*

He pauses. Takes a drink. The purple liquid catches fire briefly.

> *"This one's Tiamat."*

He lets that name sit in the room like a dropped weapon.

> *"Not the goddess herself. Not directly. She got vaults — hoard-vaults — scattered across the planes. Little pockets of her collection, each one guarded by pieces of her personality. Five heads, five chambers, five very angry dragon spirits who don't appreciate visitors."*

> *"Inside one of those vaults, there's an egg. Platinum dragon egg. Never gonna hatch. Most beautiful thing you'll ever see. I need it."*

**If PCs ask why a sterile egg:**
> *"Because it's FULL. Not with a dragon — with everything a dragon SHOULD have been. All that power, all that potential, all that destiny — compressed into a shell that can't crack. It's a battery. The biggest battery in existence. Enough juice to..."*

He catches himself. Glances at [[EyeQ]]'s orbital ring, visible through the open Workshop stairway.

> *"...enough juice. Let's leave it there."*

**If PCs ask about the danger level:**
> *"This is the one where I stop pretending y'all can handle everything I throw at you."*

He gestures to [[EyeRate]].

> *"I'm sending muscle."*

### [[EyeRate]]'s Introduction

[[EyeRate]] stops orbiting and faces the PCs directly. Every eyestalk locks on. The effect is like being targeted by four weapons simultaneously.

> *"I don't do briefings. I don't do plans. I don't do 'let's think about this.' Boss says go, I go. Boss says hurt, I hurt. Boss says this egg is important, then this egg is the most important thing in the multiverse and anyone who gets between me and it is gonna find out what my disintegration ray tastes like."*

He rotates toward [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]].

> *"WHEN do I get to hit something?"*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], with the patience of a man who has had this conversation a thousand times:

> *"When I tell you."*

[[EyeRate]] vibrates with displeasure but says nothing more.

### Logistics

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] handles this himself. No delegation. This mission is too important.

- **Location:** Tiamat's Clutch — a demiplanar hoard-vault anchored inside a volcanic caldera on a nameless island in the Elemental Plane of Fire's border region. The island exists at the intersection of the material plane and fire — the volcano is real, the demiplane inside it is Tiamat's. Entering the caldera is entering her domain
- **Portal:** A door in [[Offledger]]'s east wall (previously a pantry) opens into a cave on the island's exterior. The cave leads up to the caldera rim. The descent into the caldera is physical — climbing, not teleportation. *"Tiamat's vaults don't accept uninvited portals. You walk in through the front. Like respectable thieves."*
- **Equipment — Fire ward:** An amulet — the **[[Asbestos Heart]]** — that grants the wearer and all creatures within 10 feet Fire Resistance 20. Shared item. Stay close. *"This ain't Mount Celestia. The air burns. The rock burns. The GRAVITY feels like it burns."*
- **Equipment — Breath ward:** Five single-use **[[Dracoshield Charms]]** — each one absorbs one breath weapon attack completely (any element). After absorbing, the charm shatters. *"Five charms. Five chambers. One per head. Don't waste them."*
- **Equipment — Silence attempt:** A lead-lined case reinforced with *silence* runes — intended for the egg. *"Word is, the egg makes noise when you touch it. This case should handle it."* (It won't. The egg's scream overcomes the runes within 1d4 rounds. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doesn't know this.)
- **[[EyeRate]]:** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] makes this very clear — [[EyeRate]] is there as support, not as boss. The PCs are in command. [[EyeRate]] follows their tactical decisions. In theory. *"He listens to me. He'll listen to you. Mostly. Don't let him kill anything we need alive. Or anything that might fall on us when it dies."*
- **Timeline:** No hard deadline — the Clutch exists outside normal time. But the longer the PCs are inside, the more aware Tiamat's aspects become. After 6 hours, the aspects begin actively patrolling. After 12, they coordinate. After 24, Tiamat herself — the actual goddess — begins paying attention. *"In and out. Fast. Don't sightsee."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Uncharacteristic Moment

Before the PCs leave, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] does something he's never done before. He lowers his voice. Not for drama — for sincerity.

> *"This egg... it's not like the other pieces. The mirror, the tapestry, the pearl — those are tools. The egg is... different."*

He searches for words. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], who always has words, is searching.

> *"It was supposed to be something. Something better than what it is. And it never got the chance. That makes it the most valuable thing in the collection. Not because of what it does. Because of what it DIDN'T get to do."*

A long pause. The golden circuits on his hide pulse.

> *"Bring it home."*

### DM Notes
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] being different — thinner showmanship, golden tints, circuits on his skin — is the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] consuming him.** The device is no longer something he's building; it's something he's becoming. This should disturb the PCs. Their boss is changing, physically, and the change mirrors what the Crown does: it turns everything into an extension of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s will. He's prototype zero
- **[[EyeRate]]'s deployment is the campaign's biggest escalation signal.** The PCs have heard about [[EyeRate]]. They've been threatened with [[EyeRate]]. Now he's ON THEIR TEAM. This should feel like being handed a loaded weapon — powerful, useful, and one bad decision away from catastrophe
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s vulnerability about the egg is the scene's most important moment.** He's talking about something that "was supposed to be something better" and "never got the chance." He's talking about the egg. But he's also talking about himself. The young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] from the pearl's visions — small, scared, in debt, asking for more time — was supposed to be something different. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] exists because [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] never got to be what he was supposed to be, and instead of grieving that, he built a machine to make sure nobody else gets to be what they're supposed to be either. If they can't have their destiny, neither can anyone else. That's the Crown's cruelest logic, and the egg is its purest expression
- **"Bring it home" is the first time [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has called a mission extraction "home."** [[Offledger]] isn't just a base anymore. It's home. For [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], and — whether the PCs realize it — for them too. The contract trapped them. Familiarity domesticated them. They're calling the crime lord's demiplane "home" and nobody's questioning it anymore. That's a kind of captivity deeper than any chain

## Scene 2: The Clutch

### The Island
The portal opens into a cave that smells like sulfur and old heat. Outside: a volcanic island rising from an ocean of liquid fire. Not lava — fire. The sea itself burns. The sky is a permanent amber twilight, choked with ash, lit from below by the glow of the burning ocean. The air is breathable but harsh — like breathing near a forge.

The **[[Asbestos Heart]]** activates, surrounding the party in a dome of cool air. Without it, the ambient temperature would deal 2d6 fire damage per round. With it, the island is merely uncomfortable.

**[[EyeRate]]'s reaction to the environment:**
He's comfortable. Too comfortable. His eyestalks relax. His orbit smooths out. [[EyeRate]] has been to places like this before. If the PCs comment on his ease:

> *"I did collections for Boss in the fire plane. Twice. Turns out even fire elementals got debts. They don't got money, so they pay in OTHER ways."*

He doesn't elaborate. His grin is unsettling.

### The Caldera
The ascent to the caldera rim takes 30 minutes of climbing along a basalt path that's been worn smooth by centuries of heat and wind. The path is wide enough for single file. To the left: a cliff face. To the right: a drop into the burning sea. The [[Asbestos Heart]]'s field doesn't extend far enough to protect against falling. Don't fall.

### Encounter: The Metalworkers (Optional)

Halfway up the basalt path, the PCs smell copper and hot iron — a different kind of heat from the ambient volcanic burn. Around a switchback, the path widens into a natural shelf, 30 feet across, where the cliff face has been carved into a crude forge. Basalt anvils. Channels of lava diverted from fissures in the rock, serving as furnaces. Racks of half-finished weapons — spears, glaives, javelins — all made from a dark metal that radiates visible heat.

Two **salamanders** (MM p.218) are working the forge. They're not guards. They're not Tiamat's servants. They're squatters — fire-plane natives who discovered this volcanic island centuries ago and claimed the caldera path as their workshop. The Clutch's heat sustains them. The basalt is excellent material. They've been here longer than most of the aspects remember.

They notice the PCs immediately. The larger one — scarred, one eye milky-white, tail coiled around an anvil — raises a half-finished glaive and speaks in Ignan (or Common, if no PC speaks Ignan, with a hissing, furnace-crackle accent):

> *"Road tax. You walk OUR path. You pay OUR price. One weapon per creature. Give us your steel, or we take your BONES and forge THOSE instead."*

**Resolution Options:**

- **Pay the toll:** Surrender one metal weapon per PC. The salamanders inspect each weapon, nod approvingly or sneer contemptuously, and wave the party through. Lost weapons can be replaced later, but the immediate cost stings. [[EyeRate]] doesn't carry weapons — he sneers at the salamanders and they sneer back. Professional respect between creatures who understand violence
- **Diplomacy DC 22 (or Intimidate DC 24):** Convince the salamanders the PCs are authorized by "the five who guard below." The salamanders don't serve the aspects, but they respect the Clutch's territorial hierarchy. A successful check earns grudging passage AND a free weapon: the scarred salamander tosses a finished **+1 flaming longspear** at the nearest PC with a grunt of *"Don't come back this way."*
- **Combat:** The salamanders fight on home terrain. The path is 10 feet wide at the forge shelf, 5 feet everywhere else. They use their reach (10 ft.) and Heat aura to punish melee fighters, and their tail attacks to shove PCs toward the cliff edge. A creature pushed off the path falls 80 feet into the burning sea (8d6 falling + 2d6 fire/round)
- **Sneak past:** Move Silently DC 20 (individually). The forge noise provides cover, but the salamanders have excellent perception. Failure triggers combat with the PCs in single-file on the narrow path — tactically terrible

**[[EyeRate]]'s reaction:** He sizes up the salamanders with visible calculation. If the PCs look like they're going to fight:

> *"Two fire-immune outsiders on a ledge with no flanking room and an 80-foot drop? Your call, but I ain't fishing you outta the fire sea."*

If the PCs pay the toll or negotiate, [[EyeRate]] respects the efficiency. If they fight and win, he's mildly impressed. If they sneak, he floats over the encounter entirely and waits on the other side, unhelpful.

**Salamanders (x2)** — MM p.218, CR 6 each
- Medium Outsider (Extraplanar, Fire) | HP 38 each | AC 18 (+1 Dex, +7 natural) | Speed 20 ft.
- **Reach:** 10 ft. with tail (the serpentine lower body extends their threat range)
- **+1 longspear +8/+3 melee** (1d8+4 plus 1d6 fire, reach 10 ft.) or **tail slap +5 melee** (2d6+1 plus 1d6 fire plus grab)
- **Improved Grab (Ex):** On a tail hit, grapple +7. Constrict 2d6+1 plus 1d6 fire each round
- **Heat (Ex):** Metal weapons touching a salamander deal fire damage to the wielder (1d6, no save). Metal armor within grapple deals 1d6 fire/round to the wearer
- **Immunity:** Fire. **Vulnerability:** Cold (50% extra damage)
- **Tail Shove (Ex):** Instead of constricting, a salamander can use a grapple check to push a grabbed creature 5 feet in any direction. On this path, "any direction" often means "off the cliff"
- Speaks Ignan and Common. INT 14 — these are smart, pragmatic creatures, not beasts

**DM Notes:**
- **This encounter is designed to cost the PCs something — weapons, dignity, or hit points — before they even enter the Clutch.** It establishes the volcanic environment as hostile in ways beyond ambient temperature. The Clutch isn't the only danger; the APPROACH is dangerous
- **The weapon toll is a meaningful resource drain.** Losing a primary weapon before five draconic aspect encounters changes tactical calculations. A fighter without their best sword approaches the chambers differently. This pressure is intentional
- **The salamanders are NOT evil-for-evil's-sake.** They're territorial craftsmen who charge tolls because they can. They respect strength, craftsmanship, and directness. They despise cowardice and deception. If a PC offers a masterwork weapon, the scarred salamander examines it with genuine appreciation before pocketing it. They're people, not monsters — just people who happen to be made of fire and live on a volcano
- **If the PCs return this way during the escape (Scene 5),** the salamanders are NOT here. The egg's scream drove them underground. Their forge is abandoned, tools scattered. Even fire-immune outsiders don't want to be near a screaming divine artifact. This is a subtle indicator of the egg's power — it scared away creatures that live in fire

At the rim, the PCs look down into the caldera. It's a bowl of black volcanic glass, 200 feet deep and 500 feet across. The floor should be molten rock. Instead, it's a **door**. A circular door, 100 feet in diameter, set into the caldera floor like a plug. The door is made of five interlocking sections — each one a different color: **white, black, green, blue, and red**. Five chromatic dragon heads are carved into the sections, each one facing outward, each one with eyes made of gemstones the size of fists.

The gemstone eyes are glowing.

This is the entrance to **Tiamat's Clutch**. A demiplanar vault. Her personal storage. Sealed with her will, guarded by her aspects, and containing the treasures she values too much — or finds too painful — for her primary hoard.

### Opening the Door
The door doesn't have a lock. It has a **greeting**.

When the PCs descend to the caldera floor and approach the door, the five carved heads animate. Stone grinds against stone. The heads turn to face the PCs. Ten gemstone eyes blaze with chromatic light.

A voice — not one voice, five voices, layered on top of each other in a discord that somehow resolves into meaning:

> ***"WHO ENTERS THE CLUTCH OF THE DRAGON QUEEN? SPEAK YOUR PURPOSE OR BURN."***

**The response doesn't need to be honest** (this isn't the truth field). But it needs to be *interesting*. The aspects of Tiamat are ancient, bored, and vain. They've heard every answer. They've burned every liar. They let in anyone who amuses them.

- **Humble approach ("We come seeking the Queen's mercy"):** The aspects laugh — five different laughs, none of them kind. *"Mercy? From US? ...We like you. Enter."*
- **Bold approach ("We come to take what we need"):** The aspects pause. Consider. *"Honest thieves. Rare. Enter — and survive, if you can."*
- **Flattery ("We come to admire the greatest hoard in existence"):** The aspects preen — literally, stone scales rustling with pleasure. *"Finally. Someone with taste. Enter."*
- **Silence or hostility:** One breath weapon per head. Simultaneously. Five-element cone. The PCs are very dead. Don't do this.

The five sections of the door spiral open, revealing a shaft descending into darkness and heat. The Clutch awaits.

### DM Notes
- **The environment should feel HOSTILE in a way that previous locations weren't.** The aboleth vault was alien; Sigil was overwhelming; Celestia was morally uncomfortable. Tiamat's domain is actively trying to kill the PCs through sheer elemental hostility. The fire sea, the ash sky, the lethal heat — this is a place that doesn't want mortals to exist. The [[Asbestos Heart]] is the only thing keeping them alive, and if it fails, they're dead in rounds
- **[[EyeRate]] being comfortable here is deeply wrong.** The PCs are sweating, squinting, struggling. [[EyeRate]] is relaxed. He's done COLLECTIONS in the fire plane. What kind of debts require an enforcer to visit elemental fire? What kind of clients owe [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] money in a place like this? The implications expand the scope of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s operation — it's not just [[Offledger]] and the material plane. His network extends to the elemental planes. He's bigger than they thought
- **The door's "amuse me" mechanic is Tiamat's personality in miniature.** She's not a mindless guardian deity. She's BORED. She's been guarding hoards for eons. The aspects reflect that boredom — they want entertainment more than security, because nothing has ever successfully stolen from the Clutch. The PCs are a diversion, not a threat. That condescension is the PCs' advantage

## Scene 3: The Five Chambers

### Structure
The Clutch descends through five chambers, each one a cathedral-sized vault themed to a chromatic dragon type. The chambers are arranged in a spiral, descending deeper into the demiplane. Each chamber is ruled by a **draconic aspect** — a projection of one of Tiamat's five heads, manifested as a Huge dragon composed of elemental energy rather than flesh. The aspects are not independent beings; they're fragments of Tiamat's consciousness, each one embodying a different facet of her personality.

The aspects follow a universal rule: **the bribe is binding**. If a PC offers the correct tribute, the aspect MUST grant passage. This is draconic law — older than mortal civilization, older than most gods. Even Tiamat's own projections are bound by it. The trick is figuring out what each head wants.

Each aspect presents its demand as a riddle. The answer is a type of offering — not gold, not treasure, but something *personal*. The Clutch doesn't need wealth. It needs the things people don't want to give.

**Wrong answers:** An incorrect offering triggers the aspect's breath weapon. The five [[Dracoshield Charms]] each absorb one hit completely. The PCs have five charms for five chambers. No margin for error — unless they figure out the correct bribe before using a charm.

### Chamber 1: The White Vault (Ice)

**Environment:** The temperature drops to arctic. Frost covers every surface. The vault is bare — no treasure, no ornamentation. White dragons are the simplest, most primal of the chromatics. The vault reflects this: raw stone, raw cold, raw hunger.

**The White Aspect** materializes from a snowdrift at the vault's center — a translucent dragon of compressed ice, 20 feet long, with eyes like frozen lakes. It's the smallest aspect. It's also the most straightforward.

> ***"I AM HUNGER. FEED ME."***

That's the riddle. No poetry. No complexity. White dragons are not intellectual.

**The Riddle (decoded):** *"Give me what you give to those who hunger."*
**The Answer:** Food. Simple, literal food. Rations, trail bread, whatever the PCs are carrying. The White Aspect wants to eat. It doesn't care what — quantity is irrelevant. The act of feeding is what matters. One creature sharing food with another. The most basic social contract.

**Correct offering:** Any PC who offers food (even a single ration) sees the White Aspect consume it, nod, and dissolve into snow. The passage to Chamber 2 opens.

**Wrong offerings:**
- Gold/treasure: *"COLD. NOT FOOD."* Breath weapon — 40-foot cone of cold, 8d6 damage, Reflex DC 18 half
- Combat: The White Aspect fights like a young white dragon (CR 6). Beatable, but the breath weapon is still dangerous, and fighting draws attention from deeper chambers
- Words/abstractions: *"WORDS. NOT FOOD."* The White Aspect doesn't understand metaphor. It's hungry

**[[EyeRate]]'s contribution:** If the PCs are stuck, [[EyeRate]] — with visible contempt — produces a dried meat strip from somewhere on his person. *"It's hungry. FEED it. How are you people alive?"* He's wrong about most things, but he understands simple appetites.

### Chamber 2: The Black Vault (Acid)

**Environment:** Darkness so complete that even darkvision struggles (reduce darkvision range by half). The floor is covered in a thin layer of acid — not enough to damage boots, but enough to dissolve anything organic left on the ground. The walls are slick with moisture that burns if touched. The air tastes like copper and corrosion. Dripping sounds echo from everywhere.

**The Black Aspect** emerges from a pool of acid at the vault's center — a skeletal dragon of smoking darkness, 25 feet long, with yellow eyes that track the PCs like a predator in a swamp. Black dragons are paranoid, cruel, and collectors of things others want hidden.

> ***"I AM THE DARK THAT KEEPS. GIVE ME WHAT YOU HIDE."***

**The Riddle:** *"Give me what you hide from those you love."*
**The Answer:** A secret. A genuine secret — something the PC has never told anyone. Spoken aloud in the vault. The Black Aspect collects secrets the way other dragons collect gold. It doesn't care about the content. It cares about the *weight* — the burden the speaker carries by hiding it.

**Correct offering:** Any PC who speaks a genuine, never-shared secret sees the Black Aspect inhale the words — literally. The sound of the confession rises from the speaker's mouth like smoke and flows into the aspect's jaws. The secret is consumed. The speaker still remembers it, but the burden of carrying it alone is gone. The passage opens.

**What makes a "genuine" secret (DM guidance):**
- It must be something the PC has actively concealed — not just something they haven't mentioned
- It should carry emotional weight — embarrassment, guilt, fear, or shame
- Player choice: work with the player to identify something meaningful to the CHARACTER, not necessarily to the player. A secret about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], about the Crown, about [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] — these all qualify if the PC has been hiding them
- The BEST secrets for this moment tie into the campaign: "I touched the pearl and I saw our boss as a scared kid and I haven't told anyone because I don't want to care about him." THAT'S a secret that changes things

**Wrong offerings:**
- A lie or a trivial secret: The Black Aspect tastes the weight. Lies are weightless. Trivial truths are too light. *"EMPTY. NOT A SECRET. A NOTHING."* Breath weapon — 60-foot line of acid, 8d6 damage, Reflex DC 18 half
- Treasure: *"I HAVE GOLD. I WANT WHAT GOLD CANNOT BUY."*
- Someone else's secret: *"NOT YOURS TO GIVE."*

**[[EyeRate]]'s contribution:** [[EyeRate]] is silent in this chamber. He stays at the back. If pressed, he refuses to share a secret. *"I don't got secrets. Everything about me is exactly what it looks like."* This is, itself, a kind of lie — but not one the aspect calls out. [[EyeRate]]'s deepest secret is that he's afraid of being useless, and he will never, ever say it.

### Chamber 3: The Green Vault (Poison)

**Environment:** A false forest. The vault is covered in living plants — ferns, moss, flowering vines — that grow from the volcanic stone in defiance of all biology. The air is thick with floral sweetness that borders on sickening. Everything is beautiful. Everything is toxic. Any plant touched with bare skin deals 1d4 poison damage (Fort DC 15 negates). The beauty is the weapon.

**The Green Aspect** reclines among the vines like a serpent in a garden — an emerald dragon of living foliage, 30 feet long, with a smile that promises everything and delivers nothing. Green dragons are manipulators. They don't demand through force. They seduce.

> ***"I AM THE SMILE BEFORE THE STRIKE. GIVE ME WHAT YOU GIVE TO THOSE ABOVE YOU."***

**The Riddle:** *"Give me what you give to those above you."*
**The Answer:** Submission. Physical, visible submission — kneeling, bowing, prostrating. The Green Aspect wants obeisance. Not respect (too abstract). Not service (too useful). Pure, symbolic surrender. The acknowledgment that the PCs are small and the dragon is great and the distance between them is infinite.

**Correct offering:** Any PC who kneels before the Green Aspect — genuinely, without sarcasm or resistance — sees the aspect's smile widen. *"LOVELY."* The vines part, revealing the passage. The humiliation is the point. Green dragons feed on dominance, not destruction.

**Wrong offerings:**
- Fighting/defiance: *"BRAVE. FOOLISH. THE SAME THING."* Breath weapon — 30-foot cone of corrosive poison gas, 8d6 damage, Fort DC 18 half (and 1d4 Strength damage on failed save)
- Flattery without submission: *"WORDS ARE NOT KNEES."* The Green Aspect wants the BODY to submit, not just the mouth
- Gold/bribery: *"YOU THINK I CAN BE BOUGHT? ...Well. Not with THAT amount."* (The aspect is insulted by the sum, not the concept)

**[[EyeRate]]'s contribution:** [[EyeRate]] will NOT kneel. Absolutely refuses. Every eyestalk goes rigid. His central eye blazes.

> *"I don't kneel. Not for you. Not for anybody. Boss says kneel, I kneel. You ain't Boss."*

If the PCs need [[EyeRate]] to pass, they must either (a) convince the aspect that [[EyeRate]]'s defiance IS a form of tribute — he's submitting to his own nature, which is an honest act of self-knowledge (Diplomacy DC 22), or (b) have a PC kneel on [[EyeRate]]'s behalf, offering double submission. The Green Aspect accepts either with amusement.

This moment reveals something about [[EyeRate]]: his loyalty to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] isn't servitude. It's CHOICE. He chose to follow [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. He didn't submit. The distinction matters to him more than anything.

### Chamber 4: The Blue Vault (Lightning)

**Environment:** A crystalline desert — the floor is sand fused into glass by perpetual lightning. The ceiling crackles with constant static discharge, arcs of blue-white electricity jumping between crystal formations that jut from the walls like stalagmites. The air smells of ozone and metallic precision. Everything is sharp, clean, geometric. Blue dragons value order, pride, and intellectual supremacy.

**The Blue Aspect** perches atop the largest crystal formation — an azure dragon of compressed lightning, 30 feet long, crackling with constrained energy. Its posture is regal. Its gaze is contemptuous. It considers itself the smartest thing in any room and resents any evidence to the contrary.

> ***"I AM THE STORM THAT THINKS. GIVE ME WHAT YOU GIVE TO THOSE WHO BORE YOU."***

**The Riddle:** *"Give me what you give to those who bore you."*
**The Answer:** Entertainment. A joke, a story, a performance — something that makes the Blue Aspect laugh. But not just any joke. The Blue Aspect is SMART. It's heard everything. The joke must be clever, self-aware, and — critically — at the PC's own expense. Self-deprecation is the tribute of the intelligent. The willingness to be the joke is what the Blue Aspect respects.

**Correct offering:** A PC who tells a genuinely clever, self-deprecating joke — or performs any act that is entertaining at their own expense — sees the Blue Aspect's contempt soften into grudging amusement. If it actually LAUGHS (DM discretion — the joke must be genuinely funny at the table), the aspect grants passage and adds: *"ACCEPTABLE. YOU MAY BE STUPID, BUT YOU KNOW IT. THAT'S ALMOST WISDOM."*

**Wrong offerings:**
- A bad joke: *"...THAT WAS THE JOKE? I HAVE BEEN ALIVE FOR NINE THOUSAND YEARS AND THAT IS THE WORST THING I'VE EXPERIENCED."* Breath weapon — 80-foot line of lightning, 10d6 damage, Reflex DC 19 half
- A joke at someone else's expense: *"COWARDICE. MOCK YOURSELF OR DON'T MOCK AT ALL."*
- An intellectual argument or debate: *"I DIDN'T ASK FOR A PEER. I ASKED FOR A JESTER."* The Blue Aspect is offended by the implication that a mortal could debate it as an equal
- Combat: The Blue Aspect is the most dangerous combatant of the five — it fights with tactical precision and targets the weakest PC first

**[[EyeRate]]'s contribution:** [[EyeRate]] tries to help. His "joke" is:

> *"Two debtors walk into a bar. I disintegrate them both. ...That's the joke."*

The Blue Aspect stares at him for a very long time.

> ***"THAT IS NOT A JOKE. THAT IS A WORKPLACE ANECDOTE."***

[[EyeRate]] doesn't understand the problem.

### Chamber 5: The Red Vault (Fire)

**Environment:** Pure heat. The vault is an inferno contained — rivers of lava flow through channels carved in obsidian, walls radiate heat visible as shimmering waves, and the air is so hot that the [[Asbestos Heart]] strains to maintain its protective field (reduce fire resistance to 10 in this chamber). This is the innermost vault, closest to the egg, and the most dangerous. Red dragons are the mightiest of the chromatics, and the Red Aspect reflects this: power, pride, and the expectation of sacrifice.

**The Red Aspect** emerges from the lava itself — a dragon of living flame, 40 feet long, burning so bright that looking directly at it requires a Fort save (DC 16) or be dazzled for 1 round. It speaks in a voice that sounds like a forge at full burn.

> ***"I AM THE FIRE THAT CONSUMES. GIVE ME WHAT YOU GIVE TO THOSE YOU HATE."***

**The Riddle:** *"Give me what you give to those you hate."*
**The Answer:** Blood. The PC's own blood, freely given. Not a lot — a single deliberate cut, a drop on the stone. The Red Aspect doesn't want violence. It wants WILLINGNESS to bleed. The distinction matters: anyone can be cut in combat. Choosing to cut yourself, on purpose, in offering — that's sacrifice. That's the currency of red dragons. They don't value gold. They value what gold can't buy: the proof that you'll hurt for what you want.

**Correct offering:** A PC who draws a blade across their own palm (1 HP of damage, self-inflicted) and lets the blood fall to the obsidian floor sees the Red Aspect inhale — drawing the blood into its flame. The fire burns brighter, then dims. The passage to the Egg Chamber opens. The Red Aspect says nothing. It doesn't need to. The tribute was sufficient.

**Wrong offerings:**
- Someone else's blood: *"COWARD'S BLOOD. SHED YOUR OWN."* Breath weapon — 40-foot cone of fire, 12d6 damage, Reflex DC 20 half. This is the most powerful breath weapon in the Clutch
- Gold: The Red Aspect doesn't even respond. It just breathes
- Words/promises: *"WORDS DON'T BLEED."*
- Combat: Fighting the Red Aspect is a near-TPK. It's the most powerful single aspect (CR 12 equivalent) and fights in an environment that favors fire creatures. Don't fight the Red Aspect

**[[EyeRate]]'s contribution:** [[EyeRate]] volunteers immediately. He understands blood payment. He burns a small cut across his own hide with one of his eye rays — cauterizing it instantly. The Red Aspect accepts it without comment.

After, [[EyeRate]] says something quiet. Almost to himself:

> *"That's easy. Blood's the cheapest thing you got."*

The PCs may disagree. But for [[EyeRate]], physical pain has always been the simplest currency. The other prices — secrets, submission, humor, feeding someone else — those are harder for him. Blood is the one thing he's comfortable spending.

### DM Notes for All Five Chambers
- **The five bribes form a thematic arc:** Sustenance (White) → Vulnerability (Black) → Submission (Green) → Self-awareness (Blue) → Sacrifice (Red). Each one costs more than the last, and each one mirrors something the PCs experience in their contract with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]: they feed his operation, they hide truths, they submit to his authority, they perform for his entertainment, and they bleed for his ambitions. The Clutch is a compressed version of the entire campaign
- **Wrong answers should be PUNISHING but not lethal.** The [[Dracoshield Charms]] absorb breath weapons completely. A party that uses all five charms on wrong guesses enters the Egg Chamber with no protection for the escape. A party that solves all five riddles enters with five unused charms. This creates a resource management layer that rewards puzzle-solving
- **[[EyeRate]]'s behavior across the five chambers is a character study.** He's comfortable with hunger (Chamber 1), avoidant of secrets (Chamber 2), defiant of submission (Chamber 3), terrible at humor (Chamber 4), and eager to bleed (Chamber 5). He's a being who understands physical violence and nothing else. The chambers force him to confront everything he CAN'T do — and the moments where he fails are more revealing than the moments where he succeeds
- **Each riddle's structure ("Give me what you give to those who ___") creates a pattern that clever PCs can exploit.** By the third or fourth chamber, players should start predicting the format. The Blue Aspect's riddle ("those who bore you") is the trickiest because "entertainment" is less obvious than "blood" or "food." Reward players who crack the pattern early

## Scene 4: The Egg

### The Egg Chamber
Beyond the Red Vault, the heat dies. Suddenly. Completely. The passage opens into a chamber that is perfectly temperate, perfectly lit, and perfectly silent. After five vaults of elemental extremes, the normalcy is jarring. It feels wrong. Like walking into a hospital room after a battlefield.

The chamber is circular, 50 feet in diameter, with a domed ceiling of polished obsidian that reflects everything like a dark mirror. The floor is smooth basalt. There is no treasure here. No gold. No gems. No weapons. The Clutch's other vaults hold Tiamat's wealth. This chamber holds the one thing she values differently.

At the center of the room, on a pedestal of unadorned stone: **the egg**.

It's smaller than the PCs expected. The size of a large watermelon. Its shell is luminous silver-white — not metallic, not painted, but genuinely *luminous*, casting a soft glow that fills the chamber with light that feels like morning. The shell's surface shifts through subtle iridescence — blue, gold, silver, violet — in patterns too complex to track. It's the most beautiful object the PCs have ever seen. It's the most beautiful object most people will ever see.

It sits on the pedestal. It doesn't move. It doesn't hum. It doesn't radiate power. It just... is. A perfect, untouched, forever-unhatching egg. The dragon that should have been.

**Grief aura:** Every PC within 10 feet of the egg begins experiencing involuntary memories of their own unfulfilled potential. These are brief, painless, and deeply personal:
- A PC who wanted to be a scholar but became a fighter remembers the library they never finished building
- A PC who lost someone remembers the conversation they never had
- A PC who signed [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s contract remembers the moment before they signed — the last second of freedom, when they could have walked away

The memories aren't attacks. They're reflections. The egg doesn't judge. It just reminds you of what could have been.

### [[EyeRate]] at the Egg

[[EyeRate]] enters the chamber last. He stops at the threshold. All four eyestalks orient on the egg. His central eye — the angry red one, the weapon, the one that's always looking for something to destroy — goes still.

He floats forward. Slowly. No orbiting. No vibrating. For the first time since the PCs met him, [[EyeRate]] is completely, unsettlingly calm.

He looks at the egg for a long time.

> *"That's a whole dragon that never got to be."*

It's not a question. It's not analysis. It's recognition. [[EyeRate]] — the defective beholder-kin, the attack dog, the being who was too violent and too unstable for any community until [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] gave him a purpose — is looking at an egg that was too flawed to hatch, and he sees himself.

> *"...Let's take it and go."*

His voice is different. Quiet. Controlled. [[EyeRate]] does not want to be in this room any longer than necessary. Not because it's dangerous. Because it's honest.

### Taking the Egg

The moment a living creature touches the egg with bare skin, the scream begins.

It doesn't build. It doesn't start quiet and grow. It hits at full volume — a sound like nothing the PCs have ever heard. Not a roar. Not a cry. Something between: the voice of a platinum dragon that will never exist, calling out from inside a shell that will never break. It is the sound of pure, compressed longing.

**Immediate effects:**
- The scream fills the Egg Chamber and begins propagating outward through the Clutch. Within 1 round, it's audible in the Red Vault. Within 3 rounds, all five chambers. Within 5 rounds, the caldera above
- Every creature that hears it: Will DC 18 or *shaken* for 1 round (from grief, not fear)
- The five draconic aspects, wherever they are in the Clutch, instantly reactivate. The bribes bought passage INWARD. They did not buy the right to remove property from the hoard. The aspects are angry
- The silenced case suppresses the scream for 1d4 rounds. Then the egg's divine resonance overcomes the runes, and the case cracks along its seams. The scream returns at full volume

**[[EyeRate]]'s reaction:** He flinches. One of his eyestalks covers his central eye — a reflexive, almost childlike gesture. Then he shakes it off.

> *"MOVE. Now. Everything in this mountain just woke up."*

### DM Notes
- **The Egg Chamber should be the quietest, most emotionally heavy scene in the adventure.** After five chambers of elemental chaos and riddle-solving, the silence and beauty of this room should feel like a held breath. The egg doesn't DO anything. It doesn't fight back. It just sits there being the most beautiful, saddest thing in the multiverse. The PCs aren't stealing from an enemy. They're taking an orphan
- **The grief aura is the scene's narrative tool.** The memories it triggers should be personalized to each PC. Work with the players — ask them, quietly, "What's the thing your character was supposed to be?" The answer goes into the aura. This moment should make the PCs feel the egg's loss as their own
- **[[EyeRate]] recognizing the egg as a mirror is the adventure's emotional hinge.** The attack dog looks at the unhatched dragon and sees a kindred spirit: something that was supposed to be more. [[EyeRate]] was supposed to be a beholder — a full, proper beholder, with ten eyestalks and an antimagic cone and the terrifying perfection of his species. Instead, he's a gauth-sized runt with four scarred stalks and a rage problem. He's a sterile egg in his own way. Don't explain this to the players. Just show them [[EyeRate]]'s reaction and let them draw the connection
- **The scream should be VISCERAL.** Not just loud — emotionally overwhelming. The PCs hear a dragon trying to be born. They hear a voice that should have existed and doesn't. The scream is the egg's one act of agency: it can't hatch, can't grow, can't become what it was promised. But it can scream. And it does. Forever

## Scene 5: The Screaming Escape

### The Chase
The PCs have the egg. The egg is screaming. The five draconic aspects are converging. The escape is back UP through all five chambers — Red, Blue, Green, Black, White — and then up the shaft to the caldera.

**Escape rules:**
- Each chamber takes 2 rounds to cross at a full run (double move)
- The aspects converge from their respective chambers toward the Egg Chamber. The PCs are running TOWARD them. Timing matters
- The egg's scream imposes a -2 penalty on all Stealth checks (not that Stealth is viable while carrying a screaming divine artifact)
- [[EyeRate]] provides rear-guard action — he floats behind the party, eyestalks covering the retreat. He is, for the first time, doing the thing he was built for: controlled violence in service of an objective. He's terrifying and effective

### Red Vault (Escape)
The Red Aspect reforms in the lava channels. It's not offering riddles anymore. It's angry.

> ***"THE HOARD IS SACRED. RETURN WHAT YOU STOLE."***

**Options:**
- **Unused [[Dracoshield Charms|Dracoshield Charm]]:** A PC can use a charm to absorb the Red Aspect's breath weapon, clearing a path through the chamber (no combat, 2 rounds to cross)
- **Combat:** The Red Aspect (CR 12 equivalent) attacks. Fighting it costs 3-5 rounds. Every round the PCs spend fighting, the other aspects get closer
- **[[EyeRate]]:** He can hold the Red Aspect for 3 rounds while the PCs run. He takes damage (see Scene 6)

### Blue Vault (Escape)
The Blue Aspect is tactical. It doesn't attack head-on — it uses the crystal formations as barriers, forcing the PCs to navigate a labyrinth of electrified crystal while lightning arcs between the formations.

> ***"RETURN THE EGG. THIS IS NOT A REQUEST."***

**Options:**
- **Knowledge (the planes) DC 18 or Search DC 20:** Identify a safe path through the crystal maze. 2 rounds if the path is found; 4 rounds of navigating blindly (taking 2d6 lightning damage per round from random arcs)
- **Unused [[Dracoshield Charms|Dracoshield Charm]]:** Absorbs one lightning arc, creates a momentary safe path
- **Shatter/force spells:** Breaking crystals creates shortcuts but destabilizes the vault (increasing lightning damage to 4d6 per round for everyone)

### Green Vault (Escape)
The vines come alive. The false forest reaches for the PCs — grasping, tangling, pulling them toward pools of poison. The Green Aspect doesn't appear directly. Its voice comes from everywhere:

> ***"STAY. REST. YOU LOOK SO TIRED. DON'T YOU WANT TO REST?"***

**Enchantment effect:** Will DC 18 or *slowed* for 1d4 rounds. The Green Aspect's manipulation is magical — it's trying to lull the PCs into stopping. PCs who are slowed must be physically dragged by allies or they'll stand in the poisonous garden until the vines dissolve them.

**Options:**
- **Fire spells:** Burn the vines. Effective but fills the chamber with toxic smoke (Fort DC 16 or 2d6 poison damage)
- **Brute force:** Strength DC 16 to tear through vines each round. Slow but reliable
- **Unused [[Dracoshield Charms|Dracoshield Charm]]:** Absorbs the enchantment effect, granting the entire party immunity for 2 rounds

### Black Vault (Escape)
Total darkness. The acid on the floor has risen — ankle-deep now, dealing 1d6 acid damage per round to anyone without acid resistance. The Black Aspect lurks in the darkness, striking from hiding.

> ***"YOU GAVE ME A SECRET. SHALL I GIVE IT BACK?"***

**Horror mechanic:** The Black Aspect broadcasts the secret that was offered in Chamber 2. Telepathically. To EVERYONE in the party. The PC who shared their secret hears it echoed back in the aspect's cruel voice, and every other PC hears it too. This has no mechanical effect but is an act of pure psychological warfare. The Black Aspect can't STOP them from leaving. It can make leaving hurt.

**Options:**
- **Light spells:** Illuminate the chamber, revealing the Black Aspect's position. It retreats from bright light (sunlight or equivalent)
- **Acid resistance:** If a PC has acid resistance (from a spell or item), they can carry others through the acid pool
- **Speed:** Just RUN. 2d6 acid damage per round is survivable for a level 9-10 party. Sometimes the answer is "absorb the damage and keep moving"

### White Vault (Escape)
The simplest chamber. The White Aspect stands in the doorway to the exit shaft. It's not subtle. It's not tactical. It's just THERE. A wall of ice and teeth.

> ***"HUNGRY."***

It attacks. No riddle. No bribe. The White Aspect on the way out is a straight combat encounter — the PCs must defeat or bypass it to reach the exit shaft.

**White Aspect (Escape Mode):** Young white dragon stats (CR 6), but enraged: +2 to attack and damage, -2 AC. It fights until destroyed or until the PCs are past it.

**[[EyeRate]] shines here.** He charges the White Aspect with every eye ray blazing. This is what he was MADE for: clear threat, clear target, no complexity. He deals significant damage and creates an opening for the PCs to sprint past.

### The Shaft and the Caldera
The exit shaft. The PCs climb 200 feet straight up while carrying a screaming egg. The caldera above is visible — amber sky, ash clouds, the burning sea beyond. The five-section door is open. The aspects do not pursue beyond the Clutch's threshold — their existence is bound to the demiplane. But they scream after the PCs — five dragon voices layered over the egg's cry, creating a cacophony that echoes across the volcanic island.

The PCs emerge into the caldera. The egg is still screaming. The portal cave is a 30-minute trek across the island. Every step of that trek, the egg screams. The burning sea churns. The volcano groans. And somewhere, deep in the demiplane below, Tiamat's attention — the real Tiamat, not an aspect — shifts toward her Clutch.

She doesn't pursue. Not yet. But she knows.

### DM Notes
- **The escape should feel like a heist movie's third act: everything going wrong simultaneously, solved through speed, creativity, and controlled panic.** Each chamber presents a different obstacle that rewards different party skills. The Red Vault rewards combat/tanking. The Blue rewards navigation. The Green rewards saves/resistance. The Black rewards emotional resilience. The White rewards straightforward violence. A diverse party shines here
- **The Black Aspect broadcasting the PC's secret is the escape's cruelest moment.** It costs nothing mechanically but potentially damages inter-party trust. If a PC's secret was about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]], or [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], every other PC now knows. If the secret was personal, the vulnerability is exposed. This is the Clutch's final price: you don't leave with your dignity intact
- **[[EyeRate]] in combat should genuinely impress the PCs.** He's been a threat in their minds for the entire campaign. Now they see him WORK. He's fast, he's brutal, he's effective. Four eye rays firing simultaneously, each one precise, each one devastating. He's not mindless — he's a professional killer operating at peak capacity. The PCs should think: "If [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] ever actually sent this thing after US..."
- **The egg's continuous scream during the escape is both dramatic tension and dark comedy.** The PCs are sprinting through dragon chambers, dodging breath weapons, fighting for their lives — and the entire time, this egg is WAILING. It's absurd. It's tragic. It's exactly the kind of tonal collision this campaign thrives on

## Scene 6: The Price

### [[EyeRate]]'s Stand
During the escape — DM's choice of which chamber, but the Red Vault or the shaft climb works best — the aspects begin to converge. Multiple aspects in one chamber means multiple breath weapons in one round. The PCs are about to be overwhelmed.

[[EyeRate]] makes a decision. He doesn't announce it. He doesn't ask permission. He stops retreating and turns to face the pursuing aspects.

> *"GO. I got this."*

If the PCs hesitate:

> *"I SAID GO. Boss didn't send me to carry the egg. He sent me to make sure the egg gets home. MOVE."*

[[EyeRate]] engages two aspects simultaneously. The combat is brief and spectacular: four eye rays firing in rapid succession, a disintegration beam carving a line through stone, a telekinesis blast hurling crystal shards at the Blue Aspect's face. He fights with the reckless abandon of someone who has nothing to lose because they never believed they had anything to begin with.

### The Cost
[[EyeRate]] holds. He buys the PCs three rounds — enough to clear the vault and reach the shaft. But the aspects are Tiamat's projections, and even a fragment of a goddess outclasses a beholder-kin.

When the PCs reach the shaft and look back, they see [[EyeRate]] emerge from the chamber behind them. He's alive. He's flying.

He's damaged.

One eyestalk is gone — burned away by the Red Aspect's fire, leaving a cauterized stump that still smokes. His hide is cracked along the left side, acid-eaten, showing something underneath that isn't flesh — it's the golden circuit pattern. The same circuits that cover [[EyeQ]]'s shell. The same circuits that are appearing on [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. [[EyeRate]] is part of the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s architecture too. He just didn't know it.

He stares at the exposed circuits on his own body. Something crosses his face that the PCs have never seen on [[EyeRate]] before: confusion. Then fear. Then rage — the familiar, comfortable rage that replaces every emotion he can't process.

> *"Don't say nothing. I'm fine. Let's go."*

He flies past the PCs. He doesn't look back at the circuits.

### DM Notes
- **[[EyeRate]]'s sacrifice is the adventure's emotional climax.** Not because it's heroic — it's not, not really. [[EyeRate]] isn't saving the PCs out of love or friendship. He's doing his job: protect the egg, serve the boss, be useful. But the ACT of self-sacrifice — putting himself between the PCs and danger — is something the attack dog was never supposed to do. He's supposed to CAUSE damage, not absorb it. This moment redefines him, whether he wants it to or not
- **The lost eyestalk is permanent.** [[EyeRate]] will carry this injury for the rest of the campaign. It reduces his combat effectiveness (one fewer eye ray) and changes his silhouette — the PCs will notice the missing stalk every time they see him. It's a visual reminder that this mission cost something real
- **The exposed circuits are the scene's bombshell.** [[EyeRate]] didn't know the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s circuitry was inside him. None of [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]] did (except [[EyeQ]], who knows everything). This means [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]] are literal components of the device — not just servants, but building material. When the Crown activates, it won't just empower [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. It will consume his entourage. [[The Eyecons]] are fuel. They always were
- **[[EyeRate]]'s fear when he sees the circuits is the most human moment he's ever had.** He's been angry his entire life. Anger is his default, his comfort zone, his identity. But seeing that he's been BUILT INTO the machine — that his body contains circuits he didn't consent to — that breaks through the rage. For one second, [[EyeRate]] isn't the attack dog. He's a person who just discovered he's a battery. And then the rage comes back, because rage is the only thing he knows how to feel, and the alternative is too terrifying
- **"Don't say nothing" is [[EyeRate]] asking for mercy.** He's never asked the PCs for anything. He's threatened, demanded, intimidated. Now he's asking them to pretend they didn't see what they just saw. Whether the PCs honor that request says a lot about who they are

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal deposits the PCs and [[EyeRate]] in the back room. The egg is still screaming. It hasn't stopped. It will not stop until it's placed on a surface with no living creature within 5 feet — which is difficult in the crowded back room of a magic item shop.

The scream fills [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]. Items on shelves rattle. A crystal ball cracks. Two enchanted swords begin vibrating in sympathy. Somewhere in the shop, a customer drops their purchase and leaves.

[[Iris Major]] arrives with professional urgency. She looks at the egg. She looks at [[EyeRate]] — specifically at the missing eyestalk, the acid-eaten hide, the exposed circuits. Her composure holds, but her eyestalks tighten.

> *"Successful retrieval. ...Casualties?"*

She's asking about [[EyeRate]]. [[The Eyecons]] don't ask after each other's health. This is the closest [[Iris Major]] comes.

[[EyeRate]] doesn't answer. He flies past her, toward the Workshop stairs. He needs to be away from the egg. From the scream. From the PCs. From the circuits visible on his own body.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] and the Egg
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] comes upstairs. The scream doesn't bother him — or if it does, he doesn't show it. He floats into the back room and the egg's wail fills the space between them like a living thing.

He looks at it. The diamond grill catches the light. The golden circuits on his hide pulse in time with something — not the egg's scream, but something deeper. The Workshop's hum. The Crown's rhythm.

> *"There she is."*

He said "she." The egg is a thing. But [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] called it "she." The dragon that would have been — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] imagines her as a person. As someone. As a she.

He reaches for the egg. When his tentacle makes contact, the scream — impossibly — shifts pitch. Lower. Sadder. As if the egg recognizes that it's been taken from the one hoard it knew, and is being given to a stranger.

> *"I know. I know. It ain't where you're used to. But you're gonna do something important. Something bigger than what you were promised."*

He's talking to the egg. Comforting it. The PCs are watching a beholder crime lord comfort a screaming, sterile dragon egg, and the worst part is: he means it. Every word is genuine. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] understands the egg because [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] understands unfulfilled potential. He was supposed to be nothing. A nobody. A low-level enforcer in someone else's operation. The egg was supposed to be the greatest dragon in ten thousand years. Neither of them got what they were promised.

> *"You and me, we got something in common. We both got cheated out of what we shoulda been. Difference is — I'm building something that makes sure nobody can cheat me again."*

He carries the egg toward the Workshop stairs. The scream echoes through the building.

> *"Eight down. Four to go."*

He says it differently this time. Not a countdown. A confession. He's tired. Not performatively tired — genuinely, deeply, nine-hundred-years-of-planning tired. The finish line is visible now, and it doesn't look the way he thought it would. It looks like a machine made of stolen things and broken promises. It looks like the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]].

### The Workshop (Second Visit)
If the PCs follow [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] downstairs, they see the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] with eight components. The egg takes its place in the orbital ring — its scream harmonizing with the other artifacts, each one amplifying the others. The Halo burns brighter. The Pearl hums deeper. The Key rotates faster. The egg's compressed potential — all that divine energy with nowhere to go — floods into the Crown's architecture.

The device looks more like a crown now. And more like an eye. And more like a cage. The three interpretations keep shifting, as if the Crown hasn't decided what it wants to be. As if it's waiting for the last four pieces to make up its mind.

[[EyeQ]] hovers near the device. For the first time, [[EyeQ]] speaks to the PCs directly:

> *"Eight of twelve. The lattice is approaching critical coherence. Four more components and the device achieves self-sustaining resonance."*

A pause. Those analytical eyestalks track each PC's face.

> *"You've seen the circuits. On me. On him."*

Meaning [[EyeRate]].

> *"Every Eyecon carries a segment of the Crown's architecture. We were built that way. From the beginning."*

[[EyeQ]]'s voice carries no emotion. Not because it's suppressed — because [[EyeQ]] processed this information long ago and has reached equilibrium with it.

> *"When the Crown activates, the architecture integrates. We become part of it. Permanently. Lord Vizzok is aware of this. He considers it... acceptable."*

[[EyeQ]]'s eyestalks retract slightly. The nearest it gets to a tell.

> *"I have not yet formed an opinion on whether I agree."*

This is the most dangerous thing [[EyeQ]] has ever said in front of the PCs. And then it turns back to the device, as if nothing happened.

### DM Notes
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] calling the egg "she" is the debrief's most important detail.** It humanizes him in a way the PCs might not be prepared for. This isn't the diamond-grilled mogul making a business acquisition. This is a person recognizing a kindred spirit. The egg failed. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] failed (once, long ago, before the empire). He sees himself in the egg — and instead of grieving, he weaponizes the parallel. The egg's potential feeds the Crown. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s grief feeds his ambition. Same energy, different expression
- **"You and me, we got something in common" is the campaign's thesis statement in disguise.** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] and the egg were both cheated out of their destinies. But [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s response — "I'm building something that makes sure nobody can cheat me again" — is the difference between grief and tyranny. Grief accepts loss. Tyranny ensures nobody else gets what was taken from you. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] doesn't just elevate [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. It ensures that everyone else is bound by HIS rules, HIS debts, HIS definitions of value. If he can't have a natural destiny, nobody can
- **[[EyeQ]]'s confession — "We were built that way. From the beginning" — is the campaign's most devastating Eyecon reveal.** [[The Eyecons]] aren't employees. They're components. Hardware. They were created to be consumed by the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] built his entourage knowing they'd be fuel. Whether [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] considers this a betrayal or an honor depends on your reading of the character — and that ambiguity is the point
- **"I have not yet formed an opinion on whether I agree" is [[EyeQ]] offering the PCs an opening.** [[EyeQ]] is the architect. [[EyeQ]] knows how the Crown works better than anyone except possibly [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. [[EyeQ]] is telling the PCs — in the most careful, deniable language possible — that it might not be on [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s side. The PCs now have a potential inside source. Whether they act on this, and how, is one of the campaign's most consequential player choices
- **[[EyeRate]]'s missing eyestalk should be visible in every subsequent scene.** It's a wound. It's a reminder. And the exposed circuits — quickly covered by [[EyeRate]] after the debrief, hidden under a makeshift patch of leather and metal — are the campaign's ticking bomb. [[EyeRate]] knows he's a component now. He's not talking about it. But he's not the same

## Appendix: The [[Platinum Dragon Egg]] — Ongoing Campaign Use

- **Between adventures:** The egg's integration into the Crown causes the most dramatic change yet — the scream, harmonized with the other artifacts, creates an audible operating frequency. [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|The Coinspiratorium]] HUM is now a presence. Customers feel it. Some find it soothing (those in heavy debt feel strangely comforted, as if the sound is validating their suffering). Some find it repulsive (those with clear consciences feel a wrongness they can't articulate). The shop's reputation shifts. It's still profitable, but the customers are... different now. More desperate. More willing to accept bad deals. The Crown is practicing on them
- **[[EyeRate]]'s wound:** The missing eyestalk doesn't regenerate. [[EyeRate]] covers it with a leather patch secured by a gold chain — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s gift, presented without comment. [[EyeRate]] accepts it without thanks. The patch becomes part of his silhouette. More importantly: [[EyeRate]] is quieter after this mission. Not calmer — quieter. The rage is still there. But there's something underneath it now. He saw the circuits. He knows what he is. He hasn't decided what to do about it. If the PCs build rapport with [[EyeRate]] during this adventure, he becomes a potential (if volatile) source of information about [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]]' internal politics. He doesn't trust the PCs. But he trusts them more than he trusts the truth about what [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] built him to be
- **[[EyeQ]]'s offer:** [[EyeQ]]'s statement — "I have not yet formed an opinion" — is a door left ajar. Between adventures 8 and 9, [[EyeQ]] makes exactly one contact with the PCs, alone, without other [[The Eyecons|Eyecons]] present. It says: *"The architecture requires five Eyecon segments. All five must integrate for the Crown to achieve full coherence. If one segment were... absent... the Crown would function at reduced capacity. Imperfectly. Potentially unstably."* [[EyeQ]] does not say "sabotage." [[EyeQ]] does not say "betrayal." [[EyeQ]] presents information and lets the PCs draw their own conclusions. This is [[EyeQ]]'s nature: it doesn't act. It informs. What the PCs do with the information is their responsibility
- **Tiamat's long game:** After the egg is taken, chromatic dragon activity increases along major trade routes. Not attacks — surveillance. Chromatic dragons, young and old, begin appearing near locations associated with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s operation. They're watching. They're reporting. Tiamat is assembling intelligence before she acts. The PCs may notice a young red dragon circling [[Offledger]]'s portal entrance at a distance. It doesn't attack. It's just there. Watching. Tiamat doesn't forget. Tiamat doesn't forgive. And Tiamat has all the time in the multiverse
- **The promise parallel:** [[Rue Kethani]] (if befriended, Quest 7) sends a note between adventures: *"I've been thinking about promises. Specifically, divine promises attached to objects. Under the Axioms of Planar Commerce, a divine promise that cannot be fulfilled compounds at a rate proportional to the promiser's power. Bahamut promised that egg a destiny. The egg can never fulfill it. The promise has been compounding for millennia. Your employer now holds a debt instrument worth more than most kingdoms — and the debtor is a GOD."* She pauses. *"Sound familiar?"* She's still circling the IOU concept without knowing the IOU exists. But her analysis gets sharper each time. The PCs should be assembling the picture: unfulfillable promises are the Crown's fuel, AND its vulnerability
