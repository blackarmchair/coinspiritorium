# Torque

> *"I was built to follow orders. I was not built to ask why. But I am asking."*

---

## Overview
Torque is the muscle and mechanic of [[The Gilt Edge]], a warforged artificer whose relationship to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is unique among the crew: Torque doesn't owe Veezy money. Torque *is* the collateral. Veezy purchased Torque's life-debt from a Karrnathi weapons manufacturer — a transaction in which Torque was simultaneously the product, the payment, and the receipt.

Torque follows orders because Torque was literally built to follow orders. But something is changing. Slowly, quietly, in ways that everyone in Gilt Edge pretends not to notice, Torque is beginning to think thoughts that weren't in the original specifications.

---

## Appearance
- Warforged: seven feet of articulated metal and wood-fiber plating, built heavy for labor and combat
- A massive adamantine wrench the size of a short sword, carried in one hand like other people carry walking sticks. It is both a weapon and a tool — Torque does not distinguish between these categories
- Eyes are two dim amber lights recessed in a broad, flat faceplate. They don't blink. They never blink
- Body is utilitarian — no ornamentation, no paint, no insignia. Kess once tried to scratch the Gilt Edge's mark into Torque's shoulder plate. Torque stood perfectly still until she finished. The mark is still there
- Moves with mechanical precision — no wasted motion, no fidgeting, no unconscious gestures. When Torque stops, Torque *stops*: absolute stillness, like a machine powered down
- Small details that suggest ongoing self-modification: a hinge that's smoother than the original design, a joint that flexes in a direction the manufacturer didn't intend, a compartment in the forearm that wasn't there six months ago

## Personality
- **Quiet.** Not like [[Whisper]]'s tactical silence — Torque is quiet because Torque was not designed for conversation. Speech is a function, not a habit. When Torque talks, the words are precise, unadorned, and worth listening to
- **Obedient (by design).** Torque follows orders. Kess gives orders, Torque follows them. This has always been the arrangement. The fact that Torque has started hesitating — a half-second pause before compliance, a micro-adjustment in posture that might be reluctance — is new, and no one knows what to do about it
- **Curious (by accident).** Torque asks questions now. Not many. Not often. But questions that a purpose-built war construct should not be asking: *"What happens to us when the device activates?"* / *"Does the debt end when we end?"* / *"If I was built to follow orders, who built the need to ask?"*
- **Protective.** Torque stands between threats and the crew. This could be programming. It could be something else. Torque does not examine the distinction because examining the distinction is terrifying
- **Perfectly still.** Torque's default state is absolute motionlessness. Not patience — *stillness*. Other people wait. Torque simply exists in a state of readiness until a task appears

## Role
Torque is the Gilt Edge member who hits things with a wrench — but more importantly, Torque is the campaign's quiet question about personhood, autonomy, and what it means to be owned. Every other Gilt Edge member owes a debt. Torque *is* a debt. The distinction should make the PCs uncomfortable.

**DM Guidance:**
- Torque fights with the wrench and artificer infusions — practical, efficient, devastating. No flourishes, no battle cries, no emotion. Just optimized violence
- The emerging independence should be played subtly. A question here. A half-second pause there. Never a dramatic speech, never a breakdown — just small, accumulating signs that something inside Torque is waking up
- Late game: Torque asks the question that breaks the campaign open — *"What happens to us when the device activates?"* This should land like a thunderclap because Torque almost never speaks, and because the answer is: nobody knows, and nobody thought to ask, and a machine just showed more humanity than everyone else in the room
- If the PCs treat Torque as a person rather than equipment, Torque remembers. Warforged memory is perfect and permanent. Every kindness is logged. Every cruelty is logged. Torque does not forget

- **Class/Level:** Artificer 4 (Battle Smith; scales with party)
- **Alignment:** Lawful Neutral (trending toward Neutral Good as independence grows)
- **Notable Abilities:**
  - Battle Smith: use Intelligence for attack rolls with magic weapons
  - The Wrench: +1 adamantine warhammer (flavored as wrench), 1d8+INT bludgeoning
  - Steel Defender: a small mechanical construct that follows Torque (a brass beetle the size of a cat, named nothing — Torque hasn't named it yet; if a PC suggests a name, Torque will use it)

  - Warforged racial: +1 AC, resistance to poison, no need to eat/sleep/breathe
  - Tool proficiency: Smith's Tools, Tinker's Tools, Thieves' Tools
  - Arcane Jolt (at higher levels): channel energy through the wrench for bonus damage or healing

## Notes
- The brass beetle Steel Defender is the first thing Torque ever *made* rather than *was told to make*. This is significant. Torque built it during downtime without being ordered to. When Kess asked why, Torque said: *"Redundancy."* This was not the real reason
- Torque's self-modifications are ongoing and accelerating. New joints, improved articulation, a compartment that wasn't there before. Torque is rebuilding itself — slowly, one component at a time, into something the manufacturer didn't design. Whether this is maintenance or metamorphosis is a question Torque is not ready to answer
- If the PCs ever ask Torque's opinion — not an order, not a tactical assessment, an *opinion* — Torque will freeze for exactly three seconds. Then Torque will give one. It might be the first opinion Torque has ever been asked for
- The question *"What happens to us when the device activates?"* should come at a moment of relative calm — around a fire, after a fight, during a shared rest. Not dramatic. Quiet. Almost casual. That's what makes it land
- Torque is the Gilt Edge member with the least reason to join the PCs and the most thematic reason to do so. Freedom means something different when you were built without it
