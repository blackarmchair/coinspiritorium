# Revised Treasure Values

> *Based on Table 3-3 (Treasure Values per Encounter) and Table 3-5 (Treasure Type Breakdown) from the DMG. EL calculated using the campaign formula: **EL = (highest CR) + (2 × additional same-CR enemies)**. For mixed-CR encounters, the combined encounter is treated as a single EL based on total threat.*

The 10 campaign quests were paying out roughly **5-30% of expected treasure** per Table 3-3. This document corrects that. All encounters receive full treasure — optional or bypassable encounters have equivalent loot placed in the environment nearby.

### Wealth Verification

The per-character totals deliberately fall below 100% of expected wealth gain for each level range. This is intentional for three reasons:

1. **The Coinspiratorium has other income sources.** Shop sales, side jobs, and found treasure supplement quest payouts. The quests are the primary income but not the only income.

2. **Veezy takes his cut.** Tribute to [[Vizzok the Opulent\|Veezy]] is a mechanical drain on wealth — the players will never keep 100% of what they earn. Building in a gap between "earned" and "expected" means the tribute system reduces wealth to appropriate levels rather than below them.

3. **The debt is the design.** The core tension of the campaign is that there's never quite enough gold. If the quests paid 100% of expected wealth, the debt would feel manageable. It should feel like a real obligation — the PCs are always slightly behind, always scrambling, always choosing between power, profit, and the boss.

**Target range:** Each quest should provide **20-65% of expected per-character wealth** for its level range. The remaining 35-80% comes from shop income, side jobs, and the DM's discretion. If the PCs are falling too far behind, increase shop traffic or drop a windfall. If they're getting comfortable, Veezy's tribute gets ambitious.

## DM Notes

- **Rounding:** All values are approximate. Table 3-3 gives exact per-EL values, but actual treasure placement should feel organic, not mathematical. Round generously — an extra 50gp in a coin pile won't break the economy, but a missing 2,000gp item will be felt.
- **Item adjustments:** The specific items listed above are suggestions. Swap freely to match party needs — a party without a divine caster doesn't need a *pearl of power*, and a party of all-melee fighters needs more potions and fewer scrolls.
- **Damaged/limited items:** Several high-value items are listed at reduced value (cracked rings, warped swords, time-limited effects). This is intentional — it gives the PCs access to powerful effects without permanently inflating their wealth above the curve. The DM controls item durability; if the party needs a permanent *ring of protection +1*, let the cracked one hold together.
- **The Gilt Edge treasure:** In Quests 03 and 06, significant treasure comes from the rival crew. This only works if the encounter happens and the PCs win or the rivals drop gear during retreat. If the DM removes [[The Gilt Edge]] encounters, redistribute their treasure value into the dungeon environment.
- **Environmental treasure for Q09/Q10:** These values are softer than encounter treasure. The ~20,000gp represents maximum possible haul with thorough looting. Rushed parties or parties that prioritize mission objectives over treasure hunting will get less. DM discretion on how much environmental loot is accessible given the party's actions.
