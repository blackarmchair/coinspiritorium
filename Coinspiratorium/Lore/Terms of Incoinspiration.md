# Terms of Incoinspiration
*A Binding, Glimmering Accord of Trade, Power, and Possibly Damning Consequences.*

> By entering into commerce with **The Coinspiratorium**, herein referred to as "the Establishment", you, the Customer/Adventurer/Indentured Servant, hereby acknowledge, accept, and are held irrevocably responsible to the following terms:

---

## Article I: Purpose of Incorporation
The Establishment exists for the open pursuit of:
- Personal Power
- Magical Wealth
- Coin-Fueled Self-Actualization
- Retail Opportunities of a Dubious Moral Nature
- Money Laundering for a vicious criminal enterprise

To this end, the Establishment offers magical items, obscure curiosities, minor cataclysms, and promotional hexes for sale at exorbitant, but undeniably tempting, prices.

---

## Article II: Liability & Curse Limitations
1. The Establishment is not responsible for:
    - Explosive wand misfires
    - Sentient merchandise
    - Items that whisper insults during combat
    - Enchantments that implode above 3,000 ft altitude
2. All sales are final.
3. All warranties are theoretical.
4. Any cursed items must be identified post-sale and may be returned for store credit in another dimension.

---

## Article III: Debt Acknowledgement
You owe money. A lot of money.

Specifically, you're beholden to a beholder: **[[Vizzok the Opulent]]** (AKA Lord Veezy), an aberration of considerable wealth, significant influence, and wholly unreasonable expectations.

Veezy is:
- The flamboyant, floating, gold-dripping crime lord who bankrolled your misadventure.
- A collector of debts, magical curios, and bespoke enchanted grills.
- Never seen without at least one eye stalk wearing a solid gold signet ring the size of a teacup.

You now owe him more gold than the local economy technically contains. He expects:
- Regular tribute payments
- Respectful groveling
- Returns on investment (in the form of dungeon loot and blood)

Failure to comply may result in:
- Interest (financial and corporal)
- Repossession of spell slots
- Unsolicited and extemporaneous vaporization

---

## Article IV: On Dungeoneering & Inventory
Your adventures into the unknown are not heroic. They are contractual.

Each dungeon you enter is a mob contract assigned (or "encouraged") by Veezy and his agents. You are not freelancers — you are on the payroll.
- The loot you recover? That's your payment.
- The artifacts you find? That's your inventory.
- The dragons you slay? Please ensure their hoards are itemized appropriately.

Dungeon outings are:
- Mandatory for inventory replenishment
- The only reliable way to stay ahead of your debt
- Occasionally lethal, frequently cursed, and always profitable (for someone)

---

## Article V: The Premise of This Fool's Errand
You control The Coinspiratorium.
- You must run, stock, defend, and profit from the store.
- You are adventurers, debtors, involuntary agents of a gold-obsessed psychopath, and wholly unlicensed and unregulated retailers.
- You must rise in power — but only as fast as you can afford to.
- You must repay your debt — but only by risking life and limb.
- And someday, perhaps, you might even buy your freedom.

---

## Article VI: Final Clauses
- Glory is temporary. Gold is glorious.
- Heroism is negotiable.
- All contracts are magically binding.
- See store for tragic details.
