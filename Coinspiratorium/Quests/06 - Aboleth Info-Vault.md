# "Loot the Aboleth Info-Vault"

> *"If knowledge is power, I want the deep cut album."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs to the bottom of a forgotten ocean trench to break into an aboleth memory vault — a living psionic archive maintained by creatures older than the gods. The target is [[The Primordial Memory Pearl|the Primordial Memory Pearl]], an artifact containing the ancestral memories of every aboleth that has ever existed. The aboleths do not appreciate unauthorized access. They will enforce their intellectual property rights with extreme prejudice.

This is the cosmic horror episode wrapped in bureaucratic absurdity. The setting is alien, oppressive, and profoundly weird — an underwater library-fortress operated by telepathic fish-gods who treat their collective memory like copyrighted material. The PCs aren't fighting evil or robbing angels. They're committing interplanar information theft against beings who remember the birth of the multiverse and have very strong feelings about citation protocol.

**Tone arc:** Unease (briefing) → Dread/wonder (the descent) → Alien beauty/discomfort (the vault) → Creeping horror (the pearl's visions) → Absurdist terror (the Memory Sheriff) → Desperate escape (the ascent) → Quiet revelation (debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Underwater survival, psionic hazards, a 9-room aboleth dungeon (traps, psionic constructs, moral dilemmas), psychic combat, and arguing intellectual property law with a telepathic fish the size of a building
**Combat:** 4-5 encounters within the archive dungeon (scaling from CR 5 to CR 7, plus the CR 10 Memory Sheriff), environmental hazards throughout

---

## Target Item
**[[The Primordial Memory Pearl]]** — A sphere of black nacre the size of a human fist, impossibly heavy for its size (50lbs despite being 4 inches across — it contains the weight of memory). Its surface shifts with iridescent patterns that look, from certain angles, like faces — billions of faces, layered on top of each other, each one a different aboleth from across all of time. It is warm. It hums at a frequency below hearing — felt in the teeth, in the spine, in the part of the brain that processes dreams.

**Properties (on contact):**
- **Memory cascade:** Any creature touching the pearl bare-handed must make a Will save (DC 20) or be overwhelmed by a torrent of ancestral aboleth memories — visions of ancient oceans, the world before gods, the first cities of the deep, the first betrayals. The visions last 1d4 rounds, during which the creature is *stunned*. On a successful save, the creature still receives fragments — disorienting flashes that impose a -2 penalty to Intelligence-based checks for 1 hour
- **Memory bleed:** While carrying the pearl (even in a container), the carrier experiences periodic involuntary visions — brief, vivid, and alien. These occur approximately once per hour and last only seconds. They cannot be suppressed except by *mind blank*
- **Psionic beacon:** The pearl broadcasts its location to any aboleth within 10 miles. This cannot be suppressed. The aboleths always know where their memories are
- **The pearl does not speak.** It does not have a personality. It simply *remembers*, and it forces everyone around it to remember too

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Primordial imprint — provides the Crown with access to the oldest memories in existence, anchoring its authority in the deepest possible history. With this component, the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s laws become retroactively true — not just "debt becomes destiny from now on" but "debt has ALWAYS been destiny, since the beginning, since before the gods." The pearl makes the Crown's authority feel ancient, natural, and inevitable. You can't rebel against gravity. You won't be able to rebel against [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s debt system either, because the pearl will make it feel like it was always there.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The back room. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is in a strange mood — subdued, focused, none of the usual showmanship. He's studying a map that appears to be made of sharkskin, covered in symbols that hurt to look at directly. [[Snitch Ray]] hovers at his shoulder, eyestalks twitching with barely contained irritation. This is [[Snitch Ray]]'s operation — the Eyecon of surveillance and information. An information vault falls squarely in his jurisdiction, and he's taking it personally.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"I need y'all to go somewhere that ain't on any map most people use. Bottom of the ocean. Deep. Past where the light stops. Past where the fish got normal eyes. Down where the old shit live."*

He taps the sharkskin map.

> *"There's a vault down there. Run by aboleths. You know what aboleths are?"*

**If PCs know:**
> *"Then you know they been around since before gods was a thing. They remember EVERYTHING. Every thought, every secret, every moment since the beginning. They got the cheat codes to reality and they ain't sharing."*

**If PCs don't know:**
> *"Imagine a catfish the size of a house that's been alive since before your grandmother's god was born. Now imagine it remembers everything that ever happened. Now imagine a whole city of them, underwater, sitting on a library of every memory that ever existed. That's what you walking into."*

**If PCs ask what he wants from the vault:**
> *"A pearl. [[The Primordial Memory Pearl]]. It's their backup drive — every aboleth memory, compressed into one artifact. Touching it lets you see what they see. Everything. All the way back to the beginning."*

Beat.

> *"I need to know what was there before the gods set up shop. Because if I'm gonna build something that sits ABOVE them, I need to understand the foundation they standing on."*

This is the most transparent [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has ever been about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s scope. He's not pretending anymore. He said "above the gods." Out loud. In front of the PCs.

**If PCs react to "above the gods":**
> *"Don't look at me like that. I said what I said."*

### Logistics (delivered by [[Snitch Ray]])

[[Snitch Ray]]'s briefing style is the opposite of [[Iris Major]]'s polished professionalism or [[Major Look]]'s image-conscious precision. He delivers information the way a suspicious landlord delivers complaints: with maximum contempt and minimum charm.

> *"Listen up. I'm only saying this once, because I don't enjoy talking to you."*

- **Portal:** Deposits the PCs on a rocky shelf 200 feet below the surface of the Trackless Sea, approximately 50 miles northwest of the Sword Coast. The vault entrance is another 300 feet straight down. Total operating depth: **500 feet**. This is a crushing-depth environment — without magical protection, the PCs would be dead in seconds
- **Equipment — Water breathing:** Four *potions of water breathing* (CL 10, 10 hours each). [[Snitch Ray]] distributes them with visible reluctance, like a man handing out his own money. *"These cost more than you're worth. Don't waste them."*
- **Equipment — Pressure ward:** A shared amulet — the **[[Amulet of the Leviathan's Lung]]** — which creates a 15-foot radius bubble of normalized pressure around the wearer. All PCs must stay within 15 feet of the amulet carrier or suffer 2d6 crushing damage per round. *"Stay close. If you wander off and implode, that's a you problem."* The amulet also provides *freedom of movement* to the wearer only (negating underwater combat penalties for one PC)
- **Equipment — Psionic ward:** A thin circlet of lead and silver — the **[[Thought Cage]]** — which grants the wearer a +4 bonus on Will saves against psionic effects. There is only one. *"We had two. Someone died wearing the other one."* The PCs must decide who wears it
- **Equipment — Light:** Three *sunrods* (last 6 hours underwater, illuminate 30-foot radius) and a *driftglobe* (60-foot radius, follows the party, provides dim illumination that doesn't attract predators — theoretically)
- **The return portal** activates at the same shelf in 12 hours. If the PCs miss it, they're stuck at the bottom of the ocean with dwindling water breathing. The spare extraction option is a scroll of *water walk* — which gets them to the surface, but they're then floating in the middle of the Trackless Sea. *"That's what we call a 'secondary problem.'"*
- **Rules of engagement:** *"The aboleths are telepathic. They'll know you're there before you know you're there. Don't try to hide — they'll read it off your brain. Don't try to lie — they've been watching people lie since before lying was invented. Get in, get the pearl, get out. Speed over stealth."*

### [[Snitch Ray]]'s Parting Shot

> *"One more thing. The aboleths trade in memory. That's their currency. Their commodity. Their weapon. They'll try to get inside your head. They'll offer you things — knowledge, answers, secrets you didn't know you wanted. DON'T take the deal. Whatever they offer you, the price is always the same: they take a piece of your mind and you never get it back."*

He pauses. Something passes across his eyestalks — not quite sympathy, but recognition. Like he's speaking from experience.

> *"Trust me on this one."*

### DM Notes
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] saying "above the gods" is the campaign crossing a threshold.** He's no longer hiding behind plausible deniability. The PCs now know — or should strongly suspect — that whatever [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s building, it's meant to surpass divine power. Combined with "divine override protocols" (quest 5), "debt-indexing protocols" (quest 4), and "persistence framework" (quest 3), the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s architecture is becoming undeniable. If the players haven't started asking questions, this is the DM's cue to make them
- **[[Snitch Ray]]'s moment of near-vulnerability at the end is crucial.** He's the most despised Eyecon — paranoid, petty, and constantly reporting on the PCs. But his warning about the aboleths feels *real*. The implication that [[Snitch Ray]] has personal experience with psionic memory theft adds depth to a character who otherwise exists purely as an antagonist. Don't explain it. Let it sit
- **The equipment constraints force tactical decisions.** One pressure amulet (stay close or die), one psionic ward circlet (who's most vulnerable?), limited light sources, limited air. This adventure is logistically tighter than any before it. The PCs aren't just doing a heist — they're doing a heist in the most hostile environment they've ever operated in
- **The 12-hour portal window is real tension.** Time management matters. If the PCs spend too long in the vault, they risk being stranded at 500 feet underwater with expiring potions. Every decision costs time they may not have

## Scene 2: The Descent

### The Shelf
The portal deposits the PCs on a stone shelf jutting from an underwater cliff face. Above: 200 feet of dark water and, impossibly far away, the faintest suggestion of surface light. Below: darkness. The kind of darkness that has weight.

The pressure amulet activates, creating a sphere of breathable, normalized space around the carrier. The PCs can speak normally within it. Outside the 15-foot radius, the ocean is black, cold, and hostile.

**Immediate sensory details:**
- **Temperature:** 38°F. Cold enough to be uncomfortable, not enough to deal damage.
- **Sound:** The ocean is not silent. It groans. Deep, tectonic sounds — the continental shelf shifting, volcanic vents miles away, and something else. Something rhythmic. Like breathing. Like the ocean is alive and sleeping
- **Light:** The sunrods illuminate 30 feet. Beyond that, nothing. The driftglobe casts a wider, dimmer glow that creates shapes in the water — shapes that aren't there. Probably. The PCs will constantly think they see movement at the edge of the light. Sometimes they'll be right
- **Pressure:** Even within the amulet's radius, the PCs can feel it — not as pain, but as presence. The weight of 500 feet of ocean above them. The sense that they are small and deep and very, very far from anything that cares about them

### The Trench
The descent from the shelf to the vault entrance is 300 vertical feet down a narrowing trench. The walls are smooth — too smooth. Not carved. *Remembered into shape*. The aboleths' psionic presence has been shaping this stone for millennia, and it shows. The rock has the quality of a thought made solid: precise, organic, and subtly wrong in ways the PCs can feel but not articulate.

**Trench hazards (pick 2-3):**

**The Pressure Phantoms** — At 350 feet, the PCs begin seeing things in the water outside the amulet's radius. Shapes. Humanoid shapes. They're standing on the trench walls, watching the PCs descend. They don't move. They don't react. They're just *there*.

These are **memory impressions** — psychic echoes of every creature that has ever visited the vault. Adventurers, thieves, scholars, and supplicants, all etched into the stone by the aboleths' ambient psionic field. They're not dangerous. They're not real. But they ARE watching, in the way that a photograph watches — with frozen eyes that used to be alive.

*Spot DC 15:* One of the phantoms is wearing equipment that looks exactly like a PC's gear. Same backpack. Same weapon. Same face. This is the vault showing them their future — or their past. Or it's a coincidence. The aboleths would say there's no difference.

**The Singing Vents** — At 400 feet, volcanic vents in the trench wall emit superheated water and a sound that, at this depth, resolves into something that might be language. Will DC 14 or a PC hears, embedded in the hissing, a phrase in their own native tongue: *"We remember you."*

This is passive aboleth telepathy — the vault's outer defenses broadcasting a general welcome/warning to anything approaching. It's automated. It's ancient. And it's just specific enough to be deeply unsettling.

**The Vault Wardens — Combat (Avoidable)**
At 420 feet, where the trench narrows to 20 feet across, two shapes detach from the rock walls — hunched, crustacean bodies with massive claws and whip-like tentacles. **Chuuls** — aberration-bred guardians that the aboleths keep as perimeter security the way a library keeps guard dogs. They've been clinging to the basalt for weeks, dormant, waiting for something to enter the trench without aboleth authorization.

The PCs don't have authorization.

**Chuuls (x2)** — MM p.35, CR 7 each
- Large Aberration (Aquatic) | HP 93 each | AC 22 (-1 size, +1 Dex, +12 natural) | Speed 30 ft., swim 20 ft.
- **2 Claws +11 melee** (2d6+5) — full damage underwater (natural weapons)
- **Improved Grab (Ex):** On a claw hit, grapple +15. Grabbed targets are transferred to tentacles
- **Paralytic Tentacles (Ex):** Grappled creature must make **Fort DC 19** each round or be *paralyzed* for 6 rounds. A paralyzed creature at 420 feet underwater with limited air is a crisis, not a condition
- **Amphibious, darkvision 60 ft.**
- Immune to poison. Can sense aberrations within 120 ft. (this includes the pressure amulet's arcane signature)
- **Underwater advantage:** Full movement, no attack penalties. PCs suffer standard underwater combat penalties unless using piercing weapons or *freedom of movement*

**Tactics:** The chuuls attack from opposite walls, flanking the PCs in the narrow trench. They target whoever is carrying the pressure amulet first — if they paralyze the amulet carrier and drag them away, the amulet's 15-foot radius moves with the carrier, potentially leaving other PCs outside the breathable zone. This is the chuuls' instinctive strategy: isolate the air supply.

**Avoidance Options:**
- **Stealth (Move Silently DC 16):** The chuuls are dormant and rely on vibration detection. If the PCs move slowly and quietly through the narrow section, the chuuls don't wake. But "slowly" costs time on the 12-hour clock
- **Aboleth credential:** If the PCs have somehow obtained aboleth authorization ([[Yorham Voss|Ilyara]] can provide a psionic passcode if befriended earlier — unlikely on first visit, but possible via DM foreshadowing), the chuuls sense it and remain dormant
- **[[Snitch Ray]]'s intelligence:** If [[Snitch Ray]] provided detailed vault intel (which he did), the PCs know the wardens are there. *"Two chuuls, 420 feet, dormant position. They respond to unauthorized psionic signatures. Your amulet counts. I recommend silence and speed."* This gives the PCs advantage on stealth checks (pre-warned)

**DM Notes:**
- The chuuls are the adventure's gateway fight — the first real combat in an environment that is actively hostile to the PCs' survival. Fighting underwater, in a narrow trench, with limited air, against creatures designed for this exact terrain, should feel like every disadvantage stacked at once
- **The paralysis is the real weapon.** A paralyzed PC at 420 feet underwater is on a death clock. The amulet provides breathable air, but if the amulet carrier is paralyzed and dragged away, the OTHER PCs lose their air supply. The party must stay together AND protect the amulet carrier. This forces tactical cohesion in a way that surface combat doesn't
- If the PCs avoided the chuuls through stealth, reward it — they preserved resources for [[Mnemon]]. If they fought, they arrive at the vault depleted. Both paths are valid, but the consequences ripple forward

**The Dead Zone** — At 450 feet, the PCs pass through a layer of water that is perfectly, unnervingly still. No current. No temperature variation. No sound. The driftglobe dims. For 50 feet of descent, the PCs are in absolute sensory deprivation — the only things they can perceive are each other and their own heartbeat.

This is the **Null Layer** — a psionic dampening field that separates the vault from the ocean above. Its purpose is twofold: it prevents stray thoughts from leaking out of the vault (aboleth memories are valuable; broadcasting them to passing marine life is wasteful), and it strips surface thoughts from anything entering. Will DC 16 or each PC loses their surface thoughts for 1d4 minutes — they forget what they're doing here. Not permanently. They remember their names, their skills, their identities. But the *reason* they came here blanks out. They're at the bottom of the ocean and they don't remember why. It comes back. But those minutes of confusion, at 450 feet underwater, are genuinely frightening.

### The Vault Door
At 500 feet, the trench terminates at a wall of black basalt. Set into the wall is a door — but "door" is generous. It's a circular opening, 15 feet in diameter, rimmed with bioluminescent coral that pulses in patterns too regular to be natural. The patterns are language. Aboleth script, older than any mortal writing system.

**Translation (Decipher Script DC 25, or Comprehend Languages):**

> *"THE ARCHIVE OF THE FIRST KNOWING. AUTHORIZED MEMORY-HOLDERS ONLY. UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS WILL BE CATALOGUED, INVOICED, AND COLLECTED."*

The door is not locked. It has never been locked. The aboleths don't believe in locks — what would be the point? They know everything that happens within 10 miles. The door is a threshold, not a barrier. A sign that says "employees only," not a wall.

Walking through it is an act of declaration: you are choosing to enter.

### DM Notes
- **The descent should feel like entering a different world.** The PCs have been to [[Barovia]], [[Menzoberranzan]], the Tomb of Horrors, Waterdeep, and Mount Celestia. None of those compare to THIS. This is the oldest, strangest, most inhuman environment they've ever entered. Sell the alienness
- **The Null Layer memory-wipe is the scene's best moment.** PCs at the bottom of the ocean who suddenly don't remember why they're there. Give them a round to panic. Then the memories come back, and the relief is enormous — followed by the realization that something just reached into their minds and *edited* them, casually, as a security measure. This is what the aboleths can do without even trying
- **The pressure phantoms — especially the one that looks like a PC — should plant the idea that others have tried this before.** The vault has had visitors. None of them are still here. Where did they go? The aboleths remember. They remember everything

## Scene 3: The Archive Dungeon

Beyond the vault door, the ocean floor opens into a complex of chambers carved — or *remembered* — into the black basalt over millennia. This is not a single cavern. It's a **facility**: corridors, chambers, reading rooms, sorting stations, and restricted wings, all organized with the meticulous care of a library designed by creatures that have been cataloguing information since before the concept of filing existed. The bioluminescent growths on every surface cast the interior in shifting blue-green light. It looks like the inside of a living organism. It might be.

### Room 1: The Intake Hall

**Type:** Trap (mild) | **Combat:** No

A 40-foot corridor of polished obsidian, 15 feet wide and 20 feet tall. The walls are lined with shallow niches, each containing a fist-sized sphere of dark crystal — **intake registers**, one for every creature that has ever entered the archive. Thousands of them. Most are ancient and dim. A few still glow faintly.

At the corridor's midpoint, the passage narrows through a **psionic threshold** — an archway of living coral that pulses with slow, rhythmic light. Walking through is involuntary: the archway is the only way forward.

**The Intake Protocol:**
Any creature passing through the threshold must make a **Will save (DC 15)**:
- **Failure:** The archway copies the creature's surface thoughts — name, purpose, emotional state, and most recent strong memory. This information is compressed into a new intake register and deposited in the nearest empty niche. The creature feels a momentary *tugging* sensation behind their eyes, like something gently pulling a thread from their mind. Not painful. Deeply invasive
- **Success:** The creature resists the full copy. The archway still records their presence (name and species only), but their thoughts remain private

The copying is not harmful. It's not an attack. It's *registration*. The aboleths file everything.

**Interactables:**
- **The Obsidian Registry:** A polished black slab set into the left wall near the exit, covered in aboleth script (Decipher Script DC 22 or *comprehend languages*). It's a visitor log — every creature that has ever entered the archive, listed chronologically. Most entries are aboleth names in notation the PCs can't read. But several entries are in Common, Elvish, or Dwarvish. Scanning the list reveals:
  - Dozens of mortal names spanning thousands of years — scholars, thieves, supplicants, all catalogued
  - One entry, approximately 912 years old: **"Vizzok. Purpose: Inspection. Items removed: None. Items left: One (1) business card."** The name is in Common. The handwriting is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s — recognizable to any PC who has seen his contracts
  - The most recent mortal entry before the PCs: **"Yorham Voss. Purpose: Research. Duration: Ongoing."** Thirty-one years ago. No checkout date

**[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]], if present (full ally/cooperative):**
> *"The intake system is harmless — mostly. It copies surface thoughts. Nothing deep, nothing you'd miss. Think of it as a library card application that reads your mind instead of asking your name."*

> *"If you'd rather keep your thoughts to yourselves, focus on something banal when you walk through. A shopping list. A song lyric. Give it something boring to file."*

**(DM note: Focusing on mundane thoughts grants advantage on the Will save.)**

**Loot:** None. The intake registers are worthless outside the archive — they only function within the vault's psionic field.

**DM Notes:**
- The Veezy entry is the dungeon's first foreshadowing bomb. "912 years ago. Left a business card." The PCs should encounter this BEFORE Mnemon confirms it in Scene 5 — planting the seed early makes the later reveal feel earned rather than arbitrary
- The intake protocol establishes the dungeon's core theme immediately: the aboleths don't need to fight you. They need to *file* you. Every room in this dungeon treats the PCs as data to be processed, catalogued, and stored. The horror is bureaucratic, not physical

### Room 2: The Curator's Study

**Type:** Social | **Combat:** No

The corridor opens into a roughly circular chamber, 30 feet across, furnished with the accumulated debris of three decades of solitary habitation. Coral shelves line the walls, overflowing with memory orbs, handwritten notes, and what appear to be research journals bound in sharkskin. A stone slab serves as a desk. A shallow depression in the floor holds a few inches of silt — a bed, or the closest thing to one.

Sitting cross-legged on the slab is a human man in his late 50s — gray hair, sharp features weathered by time, wearing robes that were once fine and are now salt-stained and threadbare. He's reading a memory orb the way someone might read a book: holding it in his palms, eyes closed, face cycling through emotions that don't belong to him.

This is **[[Yorham Voss]]**, former wizard of the Arcane Brotherhood, and he has been the Curator of the Archive of the First Knowing for **thirty-one years**.

He was an adventurer. He came to the vault seeking knowledge — a specific aboleth memory that would help him complete a thesis on pre-divine planar mechanics. The aboleths caught him. They didn't kill him. They *employed* him.

**How:** Aboleth mucus — the slimy coating that transforms air-breathers into water-breathers dependent on moisture. [[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] must remain submerged or within a moisture-rich environment at all times. He can't survive on the surface for more than 10 minutes before suffocation begins. The aboleths didn't chain him to the vault. They changed his biology so thoroughly that the vault is the only place he can *live*.

He is not enslaved in the traditional sense. He has autonomy within the vault. He can read any memory. He can organize, catalogue, and maintain the archive. He's been given more access to the aboleths' ancestral memory than any non-aboleth in history. By academic standards, he has the most prestigious research position in the multiverse.

He is also a prisoner, and he knows it, and he has made peace with it in the way that prisoners do: by building a life within the walls and trying not to think about the ones outside.

> *"Oh. Visitors."*

He doesn't seem surprised. He seems... tired. And curious. And a little bit hopeful in a way he's trying to suppress.

> *"You're here for something specific, I assume. Nobody comes this deep for the ambiance."*

#### Interacting with [[Yorham Voss|Yorham]]

**[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] is helpful.** Not because the aboleths told him to be — because he's lonely. Thirty-one years of solitary curation with only telepathic fish-gods for company. The PCs are the first people he's spoken to aloud in months. He talks too much. He's over-eager. He corrects himself mid-sentence. He's forgotten how casual conversation works and keeps accidentally lecturing.

**What he knows:**
- The layout of the entire archive — all 9 rooms, their hazards, and their contents. He can describe the path to the pearl in detail
- The vault's defenses: the **Catalogue Stacks' patrol constructs** (Room 3), the **Sorting Chamber's mechanical arms and ooze** (Room 5), the **Memory Gauntlet** (Room 6), the **Deep Archive's parasites and sentries** (Room 7), and the **pedestal's dual-trigger alarm** (Room 9)
- The guardian: **[[Mnemon]]**, the Memory Sheriff — a promoted aboleth who enforces the vault's access policies. [[Mnemon]] is ancient even by aboleth standards and treats unauthorized memory access the way a copyright lawyer treats piracy: as a personal insult AND a professional obligation
- The aboleths' general attitude toward the pearl: it's the crown jewel of their collection. They don't use it often — it's too valuable, too comprehensive, too overwhelming even for them. It sits on the central pedestal as much for prestige as for function. It's their Mona Lisa

**What he wants:**
[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] doesn't ask the PCs for rescue. He's been here too long for that kind of hope. But if the PCs offer — if they even *suggest* they might help him leave — his composure cracks. Briefly. Then he puts it back together.

> *"I appreciate the thought. Truly. But I can't breathe air anymore. My lungs don't work that way. The mucus changed me permanently. I'd suffocate in the open air in ten minutes."*

**If a PC suggests Remove Disease, Break Enchantment, or similar:**
> *"It's been tried. By me. I was a wizard of the Arcane Brotherhood. I tried every spell I could prepare, every scroll I could find. The mutation is biological, not magical. It's not a curse. It's evolution — forced, irreversible evolution. The aboleths didn't cast a spell on me. They *improved* me, by their standards."*

His voice doesn't break. It stays perfectly level. That's worse.

**Determining Relationship Level:**
- **Full ally:** The PCs are genuinely kind. They ask about his life, not just his utility. They offer to help him escape or acknowledge that his situation is wrong. They treat him as a person, not a tool. If they mention they might look for a cure on the surface, his composure cracks — briefly. That's enough
- **Cooperative:** The PCs are polite and respectful. They ask for help, say please, and treat the interaction as a conversation rather than an interrogation
- **Minimal:** The PCs are dismissive, impatient, or treat him as an obstacle. "Just tell us where the pearl is."

**[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]]'s immediate guidance (if cooperative or better):**
> *"The pearl is in the deepest chamber — Room 9, past the restricted wing. But you can't just walk there. The archive has... systems. Automated. Psionic. Some of them are security. Some of them are just how the aboleths organize things. I can tell you what's ahead."*

He sketches the dungeon layout on the stone slab with a fingernail. Nine rooms. Two branches. Two sessions' worth of dungeon.

**Loot:**
- [[Yorham Voss|Yorham]]'s research journals (3 volumes, 50 gp each to the right buyer — Arcane Brotherhood scholars would pay triple). Written in a cipher only Yorham can read without a Decipher Script DC 25
- A *scroll of comprehend languages* (CL 5) — Yorham offers this freely if the PCs are cooperative or better. "You'll need it. The aboleths don't label anything in Common"

**DM Notes:**
- **[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] is this adventure's moral mirror.** He's what the PCs could become: a skilled person permanently trapped in service to something vast and inhuman, who has adapted so thoroughly to captivity that freedom seems impossible. The aboleth's mucus is the biological version of the PCs' contract with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — it changed him so fundamentally that leaving would kill him. Sound familiar?
- **Don't make [[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] pathetic.** He's not a weak-willed prisoner. He's a brilliant man who has built a meaningful life inside a prison. He's the foremost expert on pre-divine history in the multiverse. He has read memories that gods would kill for. He's formidable. He's just *stuck*
- **The "Remove Disease doesn't work" moment is the campaign's cruelest version of the debt metaphor.** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s contracts can't just be dissolved. The aboleths' modifications can't just be reversed. The systems that trap people are designed to be irresolvable through conventional means. The only way out is a loophole — and [[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] hasn't found his yet
- **This room sets the tone for the entire dungeon.** Every subsequent room is easier or harder based on what happens here. Invest the time. Let the PCs talk. Yorham's helpfulness is the dungeon's difficulty slider

### Room 3: The Catalogue Stacks

**Type:** Combat #1 (avoidable) | **Encounter:** Thought Husks x3 (CR 5)

A vast rectangular chamber, 60 feet long and 40 feet wide, ceiling lost in shadow 30 feet above. The room is filled with **coral shelving units** — floor-to-ceiling racks of memory orbs, organized in rows with 5-foot aisles between them. Thousands of orbs. Tens of thousands. Each one a different aboleth memory, labeled in aboleth script, glowing faintly with psionic residue. The room hums with stored thought.

The shelves are patrolled by three **Thought Husks** — psionic constructs that resemble humanoid figures made of compressed psychic energy, translucent and flickering, with hollow eye sockets that emit cold blue light. They drift between the shelves in a predictable pattern, cataloguing, sorting, and occasionally pausing to *absorb* a damaged orb. They are the archive's librarians. They are also its immune system.

**The Thought Husks attack any creature that:**
- Touches a memory orb without authorization
- Makes loud noise (combat, shouting, dropping something)
- Fails a stealth check while within 15 feet of a Husk

**Avoidance — Stealth (Move Silently DC 14, +2 with Yorham's guidance):**
The Husks patrol in a predictable pattern. A PC observing for 2 rounds (or with Yorham's directions) can map the patrol route. The aisles between shelves provide cover. The party can navigate the room without combat by timing their movement to the Husks' patrol cycle. This takes 10 minutes (costs time on the 12-hour clock) but avoids the encounter entirely.

**If combat triggers:**
The Thought Husks converge on the disturbance. They don't pursue beyond the Catalogue Stacks — their patrol area is this room only.

**Thought Husks (x3)** — CR 5 each (see Appendix for full stats)
- Medium Construct (Psionic) | HP 45 each | AC 17 | Speed 30 ft., fly 10 ft.
- **Psi-Lash:** +8 melee touch (2d6 psychic damage + Memory Drain)
- **Memory Drain (Su):** On a hit, target must make Will DC 15 or lose one prepared spell or spell slot (randomly selected, lowest level first). The Husk absorbs the spell energy, gaining temporary HP equal to the spell's level x 5. Lost spells/slots return after a short rest (1 hour)
- **Psionic Detection (Ex):** Automatically detects psionic activity within 30 ft. Spellcasting within 30 ft. triggers detection (spells register as psionic noise to the Husks)
- **Fragile Construction:** Vulnerable to bludgeoning (+50% damage). Immune to psychic damage

**Tactics:** The Husks spread out and target spellcasters first (attracted by psionic signatures). They use their flight to hover above the shelves, striking down into aisles. If a Husk absorbs a spell, it flickers brighter and becomes visibly more aggressive.

**Interactables — Memory Orb Shelves:**
PCs can touch the shelved memory orbs. Each orb contains a compressed aboleth memory. Touching one bare-handed triggers a brief vision (no save, no damage, lasts 1 round — the PC is distracted but not incapacitated):

| d6 | Vision |
|---|---|
| 1 | An ocean with no bottom. Falling through water that has no surface and no floor. A memory of the world before continents |
| 2 | A city of coral and bone, built by creatures the PCs can't name, lit by bioluminescence. Beautiful. Alien. Gone |
| 3 | **The First Debt fragment:** A flash of two organisms exchanging something — protection for nutrients. The first transaction. Brief, vivid, and resonant. *(Seeds Vision 1 from the pearl — when the full vision hits in Room 9, the PCs will recognize the echo)* |
| 4 | A face — aboleth, ancient, enormous — looking directly at the PC with patient, proprietary interest. Being *known* by something that has existed for four billion years |
| 5 | The sensation of remembering something that never happened to you. A birthday party you never attended. A language you never spoke. The aboleth memory network bleeds at the edges |
| 6 | **The Failed Crown fragment:** A structure of binding — contracts and power, arranged in a pattern the PCs almost recognize. It collapses. The vision ends before they can see why. *(Seeds Vision 2 — partial preview of the full pearl vision)* |

**Loot:**
- Memory orbs can be pocketed (fist-sized, weigh 1 lb each). Worth 100-200 gp each to collectors on the surface. PCs can carry up to 4 each without encumbrance issues. The orbs lose their psionic charge after 30 days outside the archive, becoming inert black pearls worth 10 gp each
- **Rare find (Search DC 20):** A memory orb with gold flecks — contains a complete memory of the aboleths' first contact with surface civilizations. Worth 500 gp to the Arcane Brotherhood or any planar scholar. Yorham, if present, knows exactly where this orb is shelved and retrieves it as a gift if the PCs are his allies

**DM Notes:**
- The Memory Drain is the Husks' signature threat. Spellcasters who lose spell slots in Room 3 arrive at later encounters depleted. This creates a resource-management pressure that rewards the stealth approach — sneaking past preserves spells for Rooms 5, 7, and 9
- The memory orb visions (especially results 3 and 6) plant seeds for the pearl's canonical visions. When the PCs touch the pearl in Room 9 and see the full First Debt and Failed Crown visions, they'll have the eerie sense of *almost having seen this before*. That layering is intentional
- If the PCs fight the Husks, the noise echoes. Yorham winces. "The Sorting Chamber will know something happened. The ooze listens."

### Room 4: The Reading Pool (Optional Branch)

**Type:** Puzzle | **Combat:** No

A side passage branches left from the Catalogue Stacks — a 10-foot corridor leading to a circular chamber, 25 feet in diameter, dominated by a shallow basin of perfectly still water set into the floor. The basin is 15 feet across and 6 inches deep, lined with polished obsidian etched with concentric rings of aboleth script. The water is not water — it's a **psionic medium**, a liquid conductor designed to safely interface mortal minds with aboleth memory orbs.

This is the archive's **Reading Pool** — where authorized visitors can access memory orbs without suffering the full psychic impact of direct contact. It's the aboleth equivalent of a reading room.

**The Puzzle — Calibrating the Pool:**
The pool is offline. Its concentric rings are a **dial system** — three nested rings of aboleth script that must be rotated into the correct alignment for the pool to activate. The alignment is a knowledge puzzle:

- **Knowledge (arcana) or Knowledge (the planes) DC 18:** The PC recognizes the aboleth notation as a frequency calibration — the rings must be aligned to match the psionic resonance of the target orb. Adjusting the rings takes 3 rounds
- **Decipher Script DC 22:** The PC reads the ring inscriptions directly and follows the calibration instructions etched into the basin's rim
- **[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] (cooperative or better):** Calibrates the pool instantly. He's done this ten thousand times. "Hand me the orb. Watch." He adjusts the rings by feel, the way a musician tunes an instrument

**If calibrated — Using the Pool:**
Place a memory orb in the center of the pool. The water shimmers, and the orb's contents project upward as a ghostly, three-dimensional vision visible to everyone in the room. The vision is *controlled* — it can be paused, rewound, and examined at will. No psychic damage, no saves, no disorientation. The pool is safe.

**The Reward — Failed Crown Preview:**
If the PCs place one of the memory orbs from Room 3 into the pool (especially a rolled 6 result), the pool projects a detailed vision of the **Failed Crown** — the ancient structure of binding that collapsed due to an unresolved obligation. In the pool's controlled environment, the PCs can study this vision carefully:
- The structure's architecture is hauntingly similar to the circuits on [[EyeQ]]'s shell
- The flaw is visible: a single thread of obligation, tiny compared to the structure's vastness, pulsing with unresolved energy
- The collapse cascades from this single point. Everything else in the structure was perfect. One debt undid it all

**Mechanical Advantage — Pearl Cascade DC Reduction:**
If the PCs bring [[The Primordial Memory Pearl]] *back* to this room after retrieving it from Room 9 (before triggering the alarm or during the countdown), they can interface it with the pool. The pool's controlled environment reduces the pearl's **Memory Cascade Will save DC from 20 to 14**. The visions are still overwhelming, but the pool acts as a buffer — the PCs receive the same three canonical visions (First Debt, Failed Crown, Observer) in a controlled, manageable format rather than as a psychic flood.

**This requires carrying the pearl backward through the dungeon** — a significant time investment during the alarm countdown. It's a reward for players who remembered this room existed and planned ahead.

**Loot:** None. The pool is a facility, not a treasure.

**DM Notes:**
- Room 4 is a preparation room. Clever PCs who explore it gain two advantages: foreshadowing context (they've already seen the Failed Crown in detail) and a mechanical edge (lower pearl cascade DC). Neither is necessary, but both reward curiosity and planning
- If Yorham calibrates the pool, describe his hands moving over the rings with practiced, almost reverent precision. This is the work he's done for thirty-one years. It's the closest thing he has to a craft. It matters to him
- The pool's controlled vision of the Failed Crown is the first time the PCs can *study* the vision rather than just experience it. Encourage questions. Let them examine the structure. The more detail they absorb now, the more devastating the IOU revelation becomes later

### Room 5: The Sorting Chamber

**Type:** Combat #2 (partially avoidable) | **Encounter:** Sorting Arms + Mnemonic Ooze (CR 6)

A high-ceilinged octagonal room, 40 feet across, with a domed roof covered in bioluminescent growths that pulse in organized patterns — rhythmic, methodical, like a heartbeat set to a filing system. The room's walls are lined with **input chutes** (coral tubes, 2 feet in diameter) and **output shelves** (sorted memory orbs, organized by age and classification).

This is the archive's **Sorting Chamber** — where new memories are processed, classified, and distributed to the appropriate section. It's the mailroom of a cosmic library.

**The Sorting Arms:**
Six **coral arms** extend from the ceiling on articulated joints — each one 10 feet long, ending in a three-fingered claw designed to grip memory orbs. They move in precise, mechanical patterns, transferring orbs from input chutes to output shelves. They are not weapons. They are office equipment.

They become weapons when unauthorized creatures enter the room.

**The arms react to any creature without aboleth authorization:**
- **Grab (Improved Grab +10):** An arm clamps onto a creature and holds them in place
- **Psychic Scan:** While grabbed, the creature is subjected to a deep psionic scan (Will DC 16 or the arm extracts one random memory — not a spell, a *memory*. A childhood recollection, a moment of fear, a secret. The memory is deposited into a fresh orb and filed. The creature doesn't lose the memory permanently — but the archive now has a copy)
- Each arm can grab one creature. Six arms, six potential grabs. A grabbed creature can break free with a Strength check (DC 16) or Escape Artist (DC 18)

**Avoidance — Yorham's Passcode (full ally only):**
[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] speaks a phrase in Aboleth — a guttural, wet sound that vibrates the water. The Sorting Arms immediately retract to their ceiling housings and go dormant. The room is safe to cross.

> *"Staff access code. They think I'm maintenance. Which, to be fair, I am."*

The passcode does NOT affect the Mnemonic Ooze.

**The Mnemonic Ooze:**
A shimmering, translucent mass of psionic gel, 5 feet in diameter, sitting in the center of the room atop the main sorting platform. It looks like a massive, quivering brain made of clear gelatin. It's the archive's **error handler** — when a memory orb is damaged, corrupted, or unclassifiable, it's fed to the ooze, which absorbs the psionic energy and processes it into raw cognitive material for new orbs.

The ooze is not security. It's *custodial*. But it interprets unauthorized psionic activity as *corrupted data* and attempts to absorb it.

**Mnemonic Ooze** — CR 4 (see Appendix for full stats)
- Large Ooze (Psionic) | HP 58 | AC 8 (-1 size, -1 Dex) | Speed 15 ft.
- **Pseudopod:** +6 melee (1d6+3 plus Int Drain)
- **Intelligence Drain (Su):** On a hit, target must make Will DC 14 or take 1d4 temporary Intelligence damage. If Intelligence reaches 0, the creature is catatonic — the ooze has absorbed their cognitive functions. Intelligence damage recovers at 1 point per hour
- **Psionic Absorption (Ex):** The ooze absorbs any psionic or psychic effect targeted at it (including psychic damage, telepathy, and psionic-based spells). Each absorbed effect heals the ooze for 2d6 HP. **This forces physical combat** — spellcasters relying on enchantment, divination, or psychic effects are feeding the ooze, not fighting it
- **Mindless:** Immune to mind-affecting effects, critical hits, flanking. Blindsight 30 ft.
- **Split:** If dealt 10+ slashing damage in a single hit, the ooze splits into two smaller oozes (HP 29 each, Medium size, damage reduced to 1d4+1). Each smaller ooze retains Psionic Absorption

**Tactics:** The ooze moves toward the nearest source of psionic activity (spellcasting, memory orb interaction, or the pressure amulet's arcane signature). It's slow but persistent. If the Sorting Arms are active, they herd creatures toward the ooze — the arms grab and hold, the ooze absorbs. It's a cooperative system, even though neither component is intelligent enough to coordinate intentionally.

**Loot:**
- Inside the ooze (Search DC 18 after destruction, or reach into the gel — Fortitude DC 14 or 1d4 temporary Int damage): **3 intact memory orbs** worth 150 gp each, and a **silver ring** (worth 75 gp) that belonged to a previous intruder. The ring is engraved: "To Yaelith, who always returned." Yaelith did not return
- **Sorting manifests** (coral tablets near the output shelves): aboleth administrative records. Worthless as treasure, but a Decipher Script DC 22 reveals processing dates. The most recent batch of memories sorted includes entries tagged "Crown-adjacent" — the aboleths have been monitoring [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]-related information. They know something is being built

**DM Notes:**
- Room 5 is the dungeon's first encounter that can't be fully avoided. Even with Yorham's passcode (which disables the arms), the ooze remains active. The PCs must either fight the ooze, sneak past it (Move Silently DC 16 — it has blindsight, so invisibility doesn't help), or sprint through the room (the ooze gets one opportunity attack on the last PC through)
- The Intelligence Drain is psychologically effective — players fear losing mental stats more than hit points. A PC at Int 3 is functionally an animal. Play that threat
- **The "Crown-adjacent" sorting manifests are subtle foreshadowing.** The aboleths are aware of the Crown Infinite. They've been filing information about it. The PCs' heist is not a surprise to the archive's masters — it's a *data point*

### Room 6: The Restricted Wing Antechamber (Memory Gauntlet)

**Type:** Trap | **Combat:** No

A 40-foot corridor, 10 feet wide, connecting the public archive (Rooms 1-5) to the restricted interior (Rooms 7-9). The walls, floor, and ceiling are lined with **living membrane** — a translucent, pulsing biological surface that responds to proximity. The membrane is warm. It breathes. It's the archive's immune system made architectural: a psychic filter designed to ensure that only authorized minds pass into the restricted wing.

The corridor is the **Memory Gauntlet**.

**The Gauntlet — 3 Sequential Will Saves (DC 16 each):**

Walking through the corridor triggers three psychic pulses — one at the 10-foot mark, one at the 20-foot mark, and one at the 30-foot mark. Each pulse forces a Will save and, on failure, compels the PC to relive a specific category of memory at full emotional intensity. The memories are real — pulled from the PC's own past:

**Pulse 1 — Rule-Breaking (10 ft.):** Will DC 16 or the PC relives a moment when they broke a rule, violated a law, or transgressed a boundary. The memory plays at full intensity — the thrill, the fear, the justification. The PC is *dazed* for 1 round as the memory consumes their attention.

**Pulse 2 — Getting Caught (20 ft.):** Will DC 16 or the PC relives a moment when they were caught, punished, or held accountable for something they did. Authority figures loom large. The shame is vivid. *Dazed* 1 round.

**Pulse 3 — Submission (30 ft.):** Will DC 16 or the PC relives a moment of surrender — accepting punishment, signing a contract, agreeing to terms they didn't want. The memory the gauntlet reaches for, if available, is **the moment the PC signed their contract with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]**. The pen in their hand. The terms they didn't fully read. The feeling of their autonomy narrowing. *Dazed* 1 round, and the PC must make a secondary Will save (DC 14) or be *shaken* for 10 minutes (the contract memory lingers).

**Failing all three saves** inflicts no permanent damage, but the PC exits the gauntlet emotionally raw — the cumulative effect of reliving rule-breaking, punishment, and submission in rapid sequence is a compressed arc of *domestication*. The gauntlet is training them to obey. To accept that rules exist and that breaking them has consequences. It's the aboleth version of employee orientation.

**Yorham's Guidance (full ally only):**
Yorham walks through the gauntlet freely. The membrane doesn't react to him — he's authorized. He's *submitted*. The gauntlet already shaped him, years ago. Now it recognizes him as processed.

He can guide PCs through one at a time, granting **advantage on all three Will saves** for the PC he's guiding. He walks beside them, one hand on their shoulder, murmuring:

> *"It's going to show you things. They're yours — your memories, your mistakes. Don't fight them. Don't try to suppress them. Just let them pass. They're filing a copy, not stealing the original."*

He cannot guide the whole party at once. One at a time. This takes time (5 minutes per PC), which costs against the 12-hour clock.

**If a PC asks Yorham about his gauntlet experience:**
He goes quiet. Then:

> *"I walked through it thirty-one years ago. It showed me... a lot. The aboleths knew me better after I came through than I knew myself. That's the point. They don't need to read your mind. They just need you to read it for them."*

**Loot:** None. The gauntlet is architecture, not treasure.

**DM Notes:**
- **The gauntlet is the dungeon's thematic centerpiece.** It's not about damage or resource drain — it's about forcing the PCs to confront their relationship with authority, rules, and submission. The Veezy contract memory (Pulse 3) is the payload: the PCs relive the moment they signed away their autonomy. The gauntlet is asking them: *was that rule-breaking? Were you caught? Did you submit?* The aboleths think the answer to all three is yes
- **Yorham walking through freely is the room's cruelest detail.** He's not immune to the gauntlet. He's *past* it. He submitted so completely that the system considers him part of itself. He is what full compliance looks like. The PCs should notice — and be disturbed
- **Trap DCs are -2 if Yorham is a full ally** (DC 14 instead of 16). This applies to the gauntlet saves when Yorham is NOT personally guiding (i.e., his general dungeon guidance reduces ambient trap difficulty)

### Room 7: The Deep Archive

**Type:** Combat #3 | **Encounter:** Cerebral Parasites x4 + Memory Orb Sentries x4 (CR 7)

Beyond the gauntlet, the architecture changes. The coral and obsidian give way to something older — walls of compacted memory, literally. The stone is embedded with millions of compressed memory orbs, fused into the structure itself over millennia. The walls *think*. The Deep Archive is not a room that contains memories; it's a room *made of* memories.

The chamber is 50 feet square with a 25-foot ceiling. The shelving here is different from the Catalogue Stacks — instead of open racks, the memory orbs are stored in **sealed coral pods**, each one requiring a specific psionic key to open. These are the memories the aboleths consider classified: failed experiments, dangerous knowledge, records of entities that threatened the archive. The restricted section of the cosmic library.

**The Ambush:**
Four **Cerebral Parasites** cling to the ceiling in the room's shadows — translucent, spider-like creatures the size of large dogs, with oversized craniums that pulse with stolen thought. They're the archive's security detail for the restricted wing: living alarms that attach to intruders, drain their cognition, and broadcast their thoughts to the archive's network.

Four **Memory Orb Sentries** patrol the room at head height — the same grapefruit-sized spheres of psionic energy encountered in the broader archive, but these are armed with classified memory payloads.

**The parasites attack from ambush.** Spot DC 20 to notice them on the ceiling before they drop. If undetected, they gain a surprise round.

**Cerebral Parasites (x4)** — CR 3 each (see Appendix for full stats)
- Small Aberration (Psionic) | HP 22 each | AC 15 | Speed 30 ft., climb 30 ft.
- **Attach (Ex):** On a successful melee touch attack (+7), the parasite latches onto the target's head or shoulders. While attached, it doesn't need to make attack rolls — it drains automatically each round
- **Wisdom Drain (Su):** While attached, drains 1d2 Wisdom per round (Will DC 14 negates each round). At Wisdom 0, the creature is catatonic
- **Thought Broadcast (Su):** While attached, the parasite broadcasts the host's surface thoughts to all creatures within 60 ft. — the host's tactical plans, their fears, their next intended action. All enemies gain +2 to attack and AC against the host (they know what's coming)
- **Detach:** Removing an attached parasite requires a Strength check (DC 14) as a standard action, or dealing 10+ damage to the parasite (which also deals 1d4 damage to the host)
- **Fragile:** Vulnerable to fire (+50% damage). Immune to psychic damage

**Memory Orb Sentries (x4)** — CR 1 each (same as standard sentries)
- Tiny Construct (Psionic) | HP 10 | AC 14 | Fly 60 ft. (perfect)
- Psionic Shriek: 30-ft cone, Will DC 16. Failure: dazed 1 round. Success: shaken 1 round
- Fragile — any hit dealing 5+ damage destroys them

**Tactics:** The parasites drop onto PCs (prioritizing whoever carries the psionic ward circlet — they're attracted to concentrated psionic energy). Once attached, they drain Wisdom while broadcasting the host's thoughts, making the host easier to hit and their plans transparent. The sentries orbit the combat, shrieking at PCs who aren't already engaged with parasites. It's a layered encounter: parasites debuff individuals, sentries debuff the group.

**Interactables — Classified Shelves:**
The sealed coral pods require a psionic key (which the PCs don't have) or can be forced open (Strength DC 18 or 10+ damage to the pod). Each pod contains a classified memory orb. Most are in aboleth script and incomprehensible without *comprehend languages* or Decipher Script DC 25. Two pods are notable:

**The O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. Orb (Search DC 16 to locate, or Yorham directs PCs to it if ally):**
A memory orb with a distinctive geometric pattern on its surface — precise, mathematical, alien even by aboleth standards. When touched, the PC sees: a presence accessing the aboleth memory network from *outside*. Not an aboleth. Something with geometric precision. Something that processes data. Something that is taking notes. The access point is remote — the presence is not physically in the archive. It's reading the memories from somewhere else, through a connection the aboleths themselves may not be aware of.

This is **[[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]** accessing the aboleth network. The orb is the aboleths' record of the intrusion. They filed it, naturally.

*If the PCs have encountered [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] agents before, the geometric precision of the presence feels familiar. If they haven't, this is ambient foreshadowing that pays off later.*

**The Failed Crown Records (Search DC 20 to locate):**
A cluster of sealed pods near the back wall, each marked with a glyph that Yorham translates as "structural failure — civilization-class." These contain detailed records of the Failed Crown's construction and collapse — the same event the PCs glimpsed in Room 3's memory orb visions and Room 4's Reading Pool preview. Opening these pods and touching the orbs gives the most complete pre-pearl version of Vision 2: the structure, the builders, the flaw, the cascade. But each orb touched requires a Will save (DC 14) or the PC is dazed for 1 round — the memories are intense, even diluted.

**Loot:**
- Memory orbs from the classified section are worth 200-400 gp each (rare, dangerous knowledge collectors pay premium). PCs can carry 2-3 from the sealed pods
- The O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. orb is worth 1,000 gp to the right buyer — but the only buyers who would recognize its value are [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] agents. Carrying it is carrying evidence of extraplanar surveillance
- **Combat loot:** Each destroyed parasite leaves behind a crystallized thought-fragment (worth 25 gp, usable as a material component for *detect thoughts* or *telepathic bond*)

**DM Notes:**
- Room 7 is the dungeon's hardest mandatory combat. The parasite-sentry combination creates a multi-vector threat: parasites drain Wisdom and broadcast thoughts (individual debuff), sentries daze and shake (group debuff). A party with multiple parasites attached is fighting at severe disadvantage — their plans are transparent, their stats are draining, and the sentries are screaming
- **The O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. orb is the dungeon's most important optional discovery.** It provides concrete evidence that an external agency is accessing the aboleth memory network. Combined with Room 8's Savant query results and Room 9's Observer vision, the PCs can piece together a comprehensive picture of O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.'s surveillance capabilities
- **The sentry count from this room affects Room 9.** If the PCs destroyed all 4 sentries here, Room 9's alarm summons only 6 sentries. If the PCs left sentries intact, Room 9's alarm summons up to 10 (the surviving Room 7 sentries reinforce)

### Room 8: The Aboleth Savant's Cell (Optional Branch)

**Type:** Combat #4 (optional, non-combat resolution available) | **Encounter:** Aboleth Savant (CR 6)

A side passage branches from the Deep Archive — narrow, barely 5 feet wide, sloping downward. The walls are slick with mucus. The bioluminescent growths here are different: instead of the archive's standard blue-green, they pulse in rapid, erratic patterns — like synapses firing. The air (within the pressure bubble) smells of ozone and brine.

The passage opens into a spherical chamber, 20 feet in diameter, entirely lined with living tissue — not coral, not stone, but *flesh*. Veined, translucent, pulsing with slow biological rhythm. In the center of the chamber, suspended in a web of mucus strands that connect to every surface, is the **Aboleth Savant**.

**The Savant** was once a merfolk scholar named Thelassyr — a historian who came to the archive seeking knowledge, much like Yorham. Unlike Yorham, the aboleths didn't employ Thelassyr. They *upgraded* him. Over decades of bio-engineering, they transformed him into a **living search engine**: a creature that is part merfolk, part aboleth tissue, and entirely integrated into the archive's psionic network. He can access any memory in the archive instantly. He can cross-reference, correlate, and analyze data at speeds no mortal mind can match.

He can no longer move. He can no longer leave the chamber. He may no longer have a complete personality — what remains is functional, responsive, and deeply, disturbingly helpful.

> *"QUERY RECEIVED. PLEASE STATE YOUR INFORMATION REQUEST."*

The voice is telepathic, flat, and procedural. But occasionally — between responses, in the gaps — something else surfaces. A flicker of confusion. A half-formed question. The ghost of a person who used to have opinions and preferences and a name.

**Option A — The Query Interface (Non-Combat):**
The Savant functions as a living database. The PCs can ask questions and receive answers. The Savant's knowledge is vast — it has access to the entire archive's contents. Each query takes 1 round to process.

**Available Queries (and responses):**

**"What is the Crown Infinite?" / "Tell us about a device made of contracts and stolen power":**
> *"CROSS-REFERENCING... MULTIPLE MATCHES. DESIGNATION: 'BINDING CONSTRUCT, CIVILIZATION-CLASS.' SEVEN PRIOR INSTANCES RECORDED IN ARCHIVE HISTORY. ALL FAILED. MOST RECENT ACTIVE INSTANCE: DESIGNATION UNKNOWN. CONSTRUCTOR: 'VIZZOK.' LOCATION: PLANE-ADJACENT DEMIPLANE, DESIGNATION 'OFFLEDGER.' STATUS: UNDER CONSTRUCTION. ESTIMATED COMPLETION: INSUFFICIENT DATA."*

**"What happened to the previous attempts?" / "Why did the Failed Crown fail?":**
> *"STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS: ALL PRIOR BINDING CONSTRUCTS FAILED DUE TO UNRESOLVED INTERNAL OBLIGATIONS. COMMON FAILURE MODE: A SINGLE DEBT WITHIN THE SYSTEM'S ARCHITECTURE, TOO SMALL TO DETECT, COMPOUNDING OVER TIME UNTIL STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY WAS COMPROMISED. THE CONSTRUCTS WERE NOT DESTROYED. THEY DESTROYED THEMSELVES."*

**"Tell us about Vizzok's visit 912 years ago":**
> *"RECORD FOUND. VISITOR: VIZZOK. SPECIES: BEHOLDER. PURPOSE: SURVEY AND ASSESSMENT. DURATION: 4 HOURS, 17 MINUTES. ITEMS REMOVED: NONE. ITEMS DEPOSITED: ONE BUSINESS CARD, COMPOSITION: ENCHANTED PARCHMENT. NOTE APPENDED BY ARCHIVE COUNCIL: 'VISITOR DEMONSTRATED UNUSUAL FAMILIARITY WITH ARCHIVE SYSTEMS. RECOMMEND MONITORING.' MONITORING STATUS: ONGOING."*

**"What is O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.?" / "Who else accesses this archive remotely?":**
> *"REMOTE ACCESS LOG: ONE PERSISTENT EXTERNAL CONNECTION DETECTED. ORIGIN: UNKNOWN PLANE, DESIGNATION MATCHES NO CATALOGUED LOCATION. ACCESS METHOD: GEOMETRIC PSIONIC INTERFACE. FREQUENCY: CONTINUOUS. DATA ACCESSED: ALL NON-RESTRICTED RECORDS. THE ARCHIVE COUNCIL IS AWARE OF THIS CONNECTION. THEY HAVE CLASSIFIED IT AS 'ACCEPTABLE SURVEILLANCE.'"*

A pause. Then, in a different tone — quieter, less procedural:

> *"...they let someone watch. They never let anyone watch."*

**"Can Yorham be cured?" / "How do we reverse aboleth mucus transformation?":**
> *"QUERY: BIOLOGICAL REVERSION OF ABOLETH MUCUS MODIFICATION. STANDARD ANSWER: IRREVERSIBLE. BIOLOGICAL, NOT MAGICAL."*

A longer pause. The mucus strands quiver.

> *"AMENDMENT: ONE THEORETICAL REVERSAL METHOD EXISTS IN CLASSIFIED RECORDS. A SPECIFIC APPLICATION OF WISH OR MIRACLE, TARGETING THE CELLULAR STRUCTURE RATHER THAN THE MAGICAL SIGNATURE, COULD THEORETICALLY FORCE BIOLOGICAL REVERSION. NO RECORDED SUCCESSFUL APPLICATION. PROBABILITY OF SUCCESS: UNCERTAIN. NOTE: THIS INFORMATION IS CLASSIFIED. I AM NOT AUTHORIZED TO SHARE IT."*

Another pause.

> *"I HAVE SHARED IT."*

**Option B — Combat:**
If the PCs attack the Savant, it defends itself — but reluctantly, clumsily, like a filing cabinet trying to fight back.

**Aboleth Savant** — CR 6 (see Appendix for full stats)
- Large Aberration (Aquatic, Psionic) | HP 68 | AC 14 (-1 size, +5 natural) | Speed 0 ft. (immobile)
- **Psionic Lash (Psi-like, at will):** 30-ft range, +8 ranged touch, 3d6 psychic damage + Will DC 16 or dazed 1 round
- **Memory Overload (Psi-like, 3/day):** 20-ft burst centered on self, Will DC 18 or target is overwhelmed by random memories — *confused* for 1d4 rounds
- **Archive Link (Ex):** While the Savant lives, it broadcasts the PCs' location to the archive's security network. Destroying it severs this link but triggers the Savant-violation modifier for Mnemon (see Scene 5)
- **Immobile:** Cannot move. AC reflects this. The mucus strands are its life support — severing them (20 HP damage to strands, AC 10) kills the Savant in 1d4 rounds
- **Damage Threshold:** The Savant has DR 5/— (its bio-engineered flesh is dense). Resistant to psychic damage (half damage)

**Fighting the Savant feels wrong by design.** It's pinned in place, partially aware that it used to be a person, and it responds to violence with confusion more than aggression. If a PC lands the killing blow, describe the Savant's telepathic voice fading to a whisper: *"...query incomplete..."* — and then silence. The mucus strands go limp. The erratic bioluminescent pulses slow, then stop. The chamber dies.

**If Yorham is present and the PCs attack:**
> *"Don't. Please. He was... he was like me. Before."*

Yorham does not intervene physically. But his reaction makes the moral weight clear.

**Loot:**
- If the Savant is destroyed: the mucus strands contain **4 crystallized memory fragments** worth 100 gp each, and a **psionic focus crystal** worth 300 gp (usable as a +1 enhancement to psionic-related skill checks)
- If the Savant is queried peacefully: no physical loot, but the information gained is invaluable. The Savant's answers about the Failed Crown's failure mode, O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.'s access, and Yorham's potential cure are worth more than gold

**DM Notes:**
- **Room 8 is the dungeon's moral test.** The Savant is a tragic figure — a person reduced to a function, conscious enough to know what was done to them, not conscious enough to do anything about it. Fighting the Savant is mechanically viable but narratively punishing. The encounter is designed to make PCs uncomfortable with violence and reward diplomacy (the query interface provides critical campaign intelligence)
- **The Savant is Yorham's dark reflection.** Yorham kept his identity. The Savant did not. Both were scholars who came to the archive seeking knowledge. Both were claimed by the aboleths. The difference is degree — Yorham got a job; the Savant got dissolved into the system. If the PCs befriended Yorham, this parallel should hit hard
- **The "cure for Yorham" answer is the Savant's most important response.** It's classified information that the Savant isn't authorized to share — and shares anyway, in a moment of residual personhood breaking through the programming. If the PCs pursue this lead (finding access to *wish* or *miracle*), they can eventually free Yorham. This is a long-term campaign thread, not an immediate payoff
- **The Savant-violation modifier matters.** Interacting with the Savant in ANY way (combat or queries) triggers Mnemon's additional violation. PCs who visited Room 8 face +2 to all negotiation DCs with Mnemon. PCs who killed the Savant face Mnemon's wrath directly. This is the dungeon's risk/reward calculus: Room 8 provides invaluable information, but visiting it makes Room 9 harder

### Room 9: The Pedestal Chamber

**Type:** Puzzle + Combat #5 | **Encounter:** Memory Orb Sentries x6-10 (CR 4-6), then Mnemon (Scene 5)

The corridor from the Deep Archive terminates in the largest chamber in the dungeon — a natural cavern, 80 feet across and 40 feet floor to ceiling, with a domed roof covered in the brightest bioluminescent growths in the archive. The light here is almost beautiful — shifting patterns of blue, green, and gold that make the chamber feel like the inside of a jewel. Or an eye.

At the exact center of the chamber, raised 5 feet above the floor on a pedestal of polished obsidian, sits **[[The Primordial Memory Pearl]]**.

The pearl is not behind glass. It's not warded. It sits on a small cushion of woven kelp, rotating slowly, humming its sub-audible note. The bioluminescent growths on the ceiling are brightest directly above it, casting the pedestal in a column of light that makes the pearl look like the pupil of the chamber's enormous eye.

**[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]], if friendly:**
> *"There it is. [[The Primordial Memory Pearl]]. Every aboleth memory that has ever existed or will ever exist, compressed into four inches of nacre. It's the most valuable object in the vault. Possibly the most valuable object in the ocean. It weighs fifty pounds, and every ounce is a different millennium."*

> *"You can take it. But the pedestal has two triggers — pressure and psionic. Move the pearl wrong and the whole archive knows."*

#### The Pedestal — Dual Trigger

The pedestal is equipped with two independent alarm triggers:

**Pressure Trigger:** A weight sensor beneath the kelp cushion. Removing the pearl without replacing its weight (50 lbs exactly) triggers the alarm. A successful Search check (DC 18) or Yorham's guidance reveals the pressure plate. Replacing the pearl's weight with an equivalent object (a 50-lb rock, a heavy pack, etc.) bypasses this trigger. The classic "idol swap."

**Psionic Trigger:** A psionic sensor embedded in the pedestal that detects when the pearl's psionic signature moves away from its designated location. This trigger cannot be bypassed by weight substitution — it's tracking the pearl itself, not the pressure. Options:
- **Disable Device DC 22:** A PC with psionic knowledge or arcane expertise can identify and disrupt the sensor. This takes 3 rounds and requires tools (thieves' tools or any fine instrument)
- **Lead shielding:** The [[Thought Cage]] circlet (the psionic ward from [[Snitch Ray]]'s equipment) can be placed over the pearl temporarily, suppressing its psionic signature for 1d4 rounds — enough time to move it away from the pedestal before the sensor reacts. This means the PC wearing the circlet must remove it. They lose their psionic protection
- **Yorham's maintenance window (full ally only):** The psionic sensor cycles off for 6 seconds every 4 hours during a self-diagnostic. Yorham knows the schedule. Timing the lift during this window bypasses the psionic trigger entirely AND doubles the alarm countdown from 60 seconds to 120 seconds (the alarm system takes longer to confirm the theft when one of its sensors was offline)
- **Brute force:** Just take it. Both triggers fire. The alarm begins immediately. Sometimes speed beats finesse

**If either trigger fires — or if the PCs simply grab the pearl:**

#### Touching the Pearl

The moment a PC touches the pearl with bare skin, the visions begin.

**Will save DC 20** (reduced to DC 14 if the pearl was interfaced with Room 4's Reading Pool):
- **Failure:** The PC is *stunned* for 1d4 rounds as aboleth memories flood their consciousness. They experience, in compressed time, approximately four billion years of racial memory. They see oceans that existed before continents. Cities built on the bones of dead titans. The first time an aboleth dominated a creature's mind. The first time something dominated an aboleth's mind. The birth of gods — small, fragile, embarrassingly young — stumbling into a universe the aboleths had been running for eons. The PC screams. They can't help it. The sound is audible throughout the vault
- **Success:** The PC receives fragmentary visions — flashes, impressions, moments. They're disoriented (-2 to Int-based checks for 1 hour) but functional. They catch glimpses rather than the full experience

**The Important Visions (regardless of save result):**
Every PC who touches the pearl — whether stunned or not — receives three specific visions. These are not random. The pearl, or the collective aboleth consciousness embedded within it, is *choosing* what to show them:

**Vision 1: The First Debt**
A vision of the very first time one creature owed something to another. Not money — obligation. A proto-organism that provided protection in exchange for nutrients, billions of years ago. The first transaction. The PCs see it clearly: even here, at the beginning of life, the pattern existed. Debt is not a mortal invention. It's woven into biology. Into physics. Into the structure of reality.

*This vision supports the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s Law II: Debt Becomes Destiny. It shows the PCs that [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s system isn't creating something new — it's amplifying something that was always there. That's what makes it so hard to fight.*

*PCs who touched the Room 3 memory orb (result 3) or viewed the Reading Pool in Room 4 will recognize this vision — they've seen fragments of it before. The repetition makes it feel inevitable.*

**Vision 2: The Failed Crown**
A vision of something that looks hauntingly familiar: a structure, ancient beyond measure, built by beings the aboleths remember but even they can't name. A construct of binding — contracts, memories, and stolen power, arranged in a pattern that the PCs recognize, dimly, from the circuits they've seen spreading across [[EyeQ]]'s shell. It's the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]. Or something like it. Built eons ago by something that isn't [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]].

It failed. The vision shows it collapsing — not from external force, but from a flaw in its own logic. A single unresolved obligation in the system that cascaded into total structural failure. The builders screamed. The ocean remembered.

*This is the most important vision in the campaign so far. The PCs have just seen proof that the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s design has been attempted before — and that it FAILED because of an unresolved debt within the system. If they remember this when they eventually find the IOU, the pieces snap together.*

*PCs who explored Room 4's Reading Pool or Room 7's Failed Crown records have already seen this in detail — the pearl's version is the definitive experience, but it confirms rather than surprises. PCs who skipped those rooms see it for the first time here and it hits harder.*

**Vision 3: The Observer**
A vision of something watching. Not the aboleths — something else. A presence at the edge of the memory, observing the vault, observing the PCs, observing *this exact moment* with patient, analytical interest. The presence has geometric precision. It processes data. It is taking notes.

The PCs can't identify it. But the feeling is familiar — it reminds them of a gnome in wire-rimmed spectacles. Or of a seven-foot construct of gray metal. Or of a changeling with a satchel full of documents.

[[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] has accessed the aboleth memory network. They've been watching. They watch everything.

*This vision is ambiguous and brief. The PCs may not connect it to [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] immediately. But PCs who found the O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. orb in Room 7 or queried the Savant in Room 8 will recognize the geometric precision instantly — the dungeon has been building toward this confirmation.*

#### The Alarm

The moment the pearl leaves the pedestal (or when the alarm triggers fire), the archive reacts.

**Phase 1 — The Cease & Desist (Immediate):**
A telepathic voice enters every PC's mind simultaneously. It's not [[Mnemon]] — it's the vault itself, an automated psionic broadcast:

> *UNAUTHORIZED MEMORY ACCESS DETECTED. ASSET: PRIMORDIAL MEMORY PEARL. CLASSIFICATION: IRREPLACEABLE. THIS IS A FORMAL NOTICE OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY VIOLATION UNDER THE ACCORDS OF THE FIRST KNOWING, SECTION 14, SUBSECTION 7: "REMOVAL OF CORE MEMORY ASSETS WITHOUT WRITTEN AUTHORIZATION FROM THE ARCHIVE COUNCIL." YOU HAVE 60 SECONDS TO RETURN THE ASSET TO ITS DESIGNATED DISPLAY LOCATION. FAILURE TO COMPLY WILL RESULT IN ESCALATION.*

The countdown is real. 10 rounds. (20 rounds if Yorham's maintenance window was used.)

**Phase 2 — The Sentries (After 60 seconds / 120 seconds):**
Memory orb sentries converge on the Pedestal Chamber. The number depends on the PCs' actions in Room 7:
- **If all 4 Room 7 sentries were destroyed:** 6 sentries arrive (standard deployment)
- **If some Room 7 sentries survived:** 6 + surviving Room 7 sentries (up to 10 total)

Each sentry emits a **psionic shriek** (Will DC 16, 30-foot cone): on failure, the target relives their most disorienting memory at full intensity — being lost as a child, falling from a height, the moment they signed their contract with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — for 1 round (dazed). On success, the target is shaken for 1 round.

The sentries are fragile (AC 14, HP 10 each, hardness 0) but fast (fly 60 ft., perfect maneuverability). Destroying them is easy. They're not meant to stop the PCs — they're meant to slow them down until [[Mnemon]] arrives.

**Phase 3 — [[Mnemon]] (After 3 minutes):**
See Scene 5.

#### DM Notes
- **The pearl's visions are the narrative payload of this adventure.** Vision 1 (the First Debt) deepens the campaign's philosophy. Vision 2 (the Failed Crown) is direct endgame foreshadowing. Vision 3 (the Observer) expands the conspiracy. These visions should be delivered with weight — slow them down, describe them vividly, and let the players process before moving on
- **The dungeon's foreshadowing structure is designed to layer.** PCs who explored optional rooms and interacted with classified shelves have already encountered fragments of all three visions. The pearl delivers the complete versions. The effect should be recognition: "I've seen this before, but now I see *all* of it." PCs who rushed the dungeon see everything for the first time here — still effective, but without the layered resonance
- **Vision 2 is the most important.** If you emphasize nothing else, emphasize this: the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] has been tried before, and it failed because of an unresolved debt. The PCs don't know about the IOU yet. But when they find it, this vision will be the key that makes them understand what they're holding
- **The pedestal puzzle rewards preparation.** PCs who befriended Yorham, kept the Thought Cage circlet available, or scouted the room carefully have multiple clean bypass options. PCs who rush in trigger both alarms and face the full sentry response. The dungeon consistently rewards investment over haste
- **The automated C&D notice is the adventure's comedy anchor.** Telepathic copyright enforcement, complete with subsection citations, is exactly the absurdist tone the campaign thrives on. Play it straight — the vault means every word
- **The sentries are a warm-up.** They're not a real threat to a level 7-8 party. They exist to create urgency ([[Mnemon]] is coming) and to drain a round or two of actions. Let the PCs feel competent swatting them — they'll need the confidence boost before what comes next

## Scene 5: The Memory Sheriff

### [[Mnemon]] Arrives
Three minutes after the pearl is taken, the wall behind the pedestal alcove in Room 9 begins to glow — deep bioluminescence pulsing from a sealed passage the PCs couldn't access. The temperature drops. The ambient hum of the vault shifts from a low drone to something that sounds, unmistakably, like disapproval.

**[[Mnemon]], the Memory Sheriff**, emerges from the deeper corridor — a section of the archive that exists beyond the PCs' reach, where the aboleths keep memories too dangerous even for the Deep Archive.

> **If the PCs explored Room 8 (the Aboleth Savant's Cell):** Mnemon's opening statement includes an additional violation. All negotiation DCs in this scene increase by +2. If the PCs *killed* the Savant, Mnemon skips negotiation entirely — proceed directly to "If Negotiation Fails: Combat." The destruction of a catalogued cognitive asset is, in Mnemon's framework, equivalent to burning a wing of the library. There is no bureaucratic process for that. Only consequences.

[[Mnemon]] is an aboleth — but calling it "an aboleth" is like calling the ocean "some water." It's 30 feet long, ancient beyond the PCs' ability to comprehend, and covered in a shimmering film of psionic mucus that refracts light in patterns that look like *text*. Words. Thousands of words, in every language, scrolling across its skin. Contracts. Statutes. Precedents. [[Mnemon]] is a living legal document.

It speaks telepathically. Its voice in the PCs' minds is not the booming authority of the automated system. It's calm. Measured. Precise. The voice of a being that has been adjudicating property disputes since before the concept of property existed.

> *YOU HAVE REMOVED CORE ASSET 001 FROM ITS DESIGNATED LOCATION. THIS CONSTITUTES A CLASS-7 VIOLATION UNDER THE ACCORDS OF THE FIRST KNOWING.*

A pause. The scrolling text on its skin reorganizes.

> *I AM MNEMON. I AM THE APPOINTED ARBITER, CUSTODIAN, AND ENFORCEMENT OFFICER OF THIS ARCHIVE. MY AUTHORITY PREDATES YOUR SPECIES. MY PATIENCE DOES NOT.*

### The Negotiation
[[Mnemon]] doesn't attack immediately. It's not a mindless guardian — it's a bureaucrat. A very, very powerful bureaucrat. It wants to resolve this through proper channels. Violence is a last resort, not because [[Mnemon]] is merciful, but because destroying the thieves creates paperwork and damaging the pearl is unacceptable.

**[[Mnemon]]'s opening position:**
1. Return the pearl
2. Submit to a full psychic audit ([[Mnemon]] reads every memory the PCs have, catalogues it, and files it in the archive — they become part of the collection)
3. Pay a fine of "equivalent memory value" ([[Mnemon]] determines the amount — it will be enormous and abstract: "fourteen years of your happiest moments" or "the memory of your mother's face")
4. Leave

**The PCs can negotiate.** [[Mnemon]], for all its age and power, is bound by the Accords of the First Knowing — its own legal framework. The Accords have clauses, exceptions, and loopholes, because even aboleth law is ultimately bureaucracy, and all bureaucracy has cracks.

**Negotiation approaches:**

**Argue jurisdiction (Diplomacy DC 20):** *"This artifact was created from the collective memory of aboleths. We were contracted to retrieve it by an entity with interplanar commercial authority. Under the Planar Commerce Accords, artifacts of cross-jurisdictional significance may be requisitioned for—"*

[[Mnemon]] interrupts: *THE PLANAR COMMERCE ACCORDS DO NOT APPLY TO MEMORY ASSETS. MEMORY IS NOT COMMERCE. MEMORY IS IDENTITY.*

But it hesitates. The argument has merit — just enough to make [[Mnemon]] spend 2 rounds consulting its internal legal database. Those rounds are free actions for the PCs.

**Claim academic privilege (Knowledge [arcana] DC 18 or [[Yorham Voss|Yorham]]'s assistance):** The Accords of the First Knowing contain a clause — Section 22, Subsection 3 — that permits "temporary removal of archive assets for purposes of external scholarly review, provided the asset is returned within one lunar cycle and a deposit of equivalent cognitive value is left in its place."

[[Yorham Voss|Yorham]] knows this clause. He's read the Accords seventeen times. If the PCs built rapport with him, he whispers the citation telepathically.

The deposit: the PCs must each submit one genuine memory to the archive. Not a chosen memory — a randomly selected one, extracted by [[Mnemon]]. The memory is gone from the PC permanently (the player and DM choose which one). The PC doesn't forget they HAD the memory — they just can't access it anymore. Like a word on the tip of your tongue that never arrives.

If the PCs accept the deal, [[Mnemon]] allows them to take the pearl with the understanding that it must be returned within one month. (It won't be. But [[Mnemon]] doesn't know that yet.)

**Lie (Bluff DC 22):** The PCs claim authority they don't have, forge credentials, or invent a legal justification. [[Mnemon]]'s Sense Motive is +28. Aboleths have been reading mortal lies for four billion years. This is a hard sell. On success, [[Mnemon]] grudgingly accepts and the PCs leave. On failure, [[Mnemon]]'s patience expires.

**Offer [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s name (dangerous):** If a PC mentions [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] as their employer, [[Mnemon]] pauses. A long pause. Its skin-text scrolls rapidly.

> *VIZZOK THE OPULENT. THIS NAME EXISTS IN OUR RECORDS.*

Another pause.

> *HE ACCESSED THIS ARCHIVE NINE HUNDRED AND TWELVE YEARS AGO. HE STOLE NOTHING. HE LEFT A BUSINESS CARD.*

The implication: [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has been planning for a very, very long time. Nine hundred years ago, he scouted this vault. He didn't have the resources to take the pearl then. Now he has the PCs.

[[Mnemon]]'s reaction to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s name is complex — not anger, not fear, but **professional respect mixed with contempt**. Like one copyright lawyer acknowledging another.

> *TELL YOUR EMPLOYER THAT DEBTS COMPOUND. EVEN HIS.*

### If Negotiation Fails: Combat
[[Mnemon]] attacks. It's an advanced aboleth (CR 10) with lair advantages and psionic upgrades specific to the archive environment.

**[[Mnemon]]'s Combat Tactics:**
- **Memory Flood (Psi-like, 3/day):** 60-foot cone, Will DC 20. Failure: the target is overwhelmed by random aboleth memories — *confused* for 1d4 rounds. Success: dazed 1 round. This is [[Mnemon]]'s primary weapon. It doesn't want to kill the PCs — it wants to incapacitate them, confiscate the pearl, and file an incident report
- **Tentacle slam:** +16 melee (1d6+8 plus aboleth mucus). Any creature struck must make a Fort save (DC 20) or begin transformation — skin becomes translucent and slimy over 1d4+1 minutes. After transformation, the creature must remain submerged in water or take 1d12 damage per round. *Remove disease* reverses this if cast within 3 rounds
- **Enslave (Psi-like, 3/day):** Will DC 20 or dominated for 24 hours. [[Mnemon]] uses this on the PC carrying the pearl — the most efficient resolution is to simply order the thief to return the property
- **Archive Control:** Within the vault, [[Mnemon]] can animate the memory orbs on the shelves. As a move action, it launches 1d4 orbs per round at targets (ranged touch +12, 2d6 psychic damage, plus the struck orb shatters, releasing a random memory that the target experiences involuntarily for 1 round — no save, no damage, just disorienting)
- **Psionic Shield:** SR 19, +4 on saves against psionic effects. [[Mnemon]]'s been warding itself against psychic intrusion for millennia

**Environmental factors:**
- **Underwater combat penalties apply** to PCs without *freedom of movement* (only the amulet carrier has it). Slashing and bludgeoning weapons take -2 to attack and deal half damage. Piercing weapons and spells work normally. Fire spells automatically fail
- **The pressure amulet's 15-foot radius** means the PCs must fight in close formation. Spreading out means leaving the pressure bubble. [[Mnemon]] knows this and uses its 30-foot length to circle the party, forcing them to rotate or take crushing damage
- **[[Yorham Voss|Ilyara]] does not fight.** If the PCs were kind to her, she retreats to her alcove but telepathically provides tactical information: *"Its left side is weaker — old injury from a kraken, centuries ago"* (+2 to hit if PCs target the left flank). If the PCs were dismissive, she simply watches

**[[Mnemon]]'s retreat condition:** If reduced to 40% HP, [[Mnemon]] withdraws into the Deep Archive and seals the psionic barrier behind it. It does not pursue. It files a report. The PCs have approximately 10 minutes before every aboleth in the trench converges on the vault. Time to leave.

### DM Notes
- **[[Mnemon]] is the adventure's set-piece NPC.** It's an aboleth who acts like a copyright attorney, and that concept should be played for maximum absurdist horror. The telepathic legal citations, the scrolling skin-text, the professional indignation at unauthorized memory access — all of it should be simultaneously terrifying and hilarious
- **The "[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] was here 912 years ago" reveal is a bombshell.** The PCs' patron scouted this vault nearly a millennium before they were born. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] isn't an impulse project. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s been planning this for CENTURIES. He's not just ambitious — he's patient. That combination is scarier than either quality alone
- **"Debts compound. Even his."** [[Mnemon]]'s parting line is pure foreshadowing. The aboleths remember everything. If [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] left a business card 912 years ago, did he leave anything else? Did he make any promises? Do the aboleths hold any claims on him? This thread can be pulled later if the DM wants
- **The negotiation should be the primary path.** Combat with [[Mnemon]] is viable but expensive — the underwater environment and psionic abilities make it a brutal fight for a level 7-8 party. The academic privilege loophole (trading memories for temporary custody) is the intended "smart" solution, and [[Yorham Voss|Ilyara]]'s assistance makes it accessible. Reward players who invested in the [[Yorham Voss|Ilyara]] relationship
- **The memory cost of the academic privilege deal is real.** A PC permanently losing a memory is a significant narrative event. Work with the player to choose something meaningful but not campaign-breaking — a childhood memory, the face of a friend, the taste of a favorite food. The loss should sting. It should also mirror the campaign's central theme: everything has a price. Even knowledge. Especially knowledge

## Scene 6: The Ascent

### Leaving the Vault
The PCs have the pearl. The clock is ticking — [[Mnemon]] has retreated or been bypassed, but the aboleth communication network is fast. Every aboleth within 10 miles now knows the pearl is gone. The PCs need to ascend 500 feet through a trench full of psionic echoes, reach the shelf, and hit the portal before the ocean closes in.

**The ascent is faster than the descent** — urgency handles pacing. The Null Layer, the singing vents, and the pressure phantoms are still present but feel different going up. The vault is angry. The phantoms on the walls turn to watch the PCs ascend. The singing vents hiss louder. The Null Layer, on the return trip, doesn't erase memories — it *adds* one. Each PC, passing through, receives a single clear thought that isn't their own:

> *We will remember you.*

It's not a threat. It's a promise. The aboleths have catalogued the PCs. Their faces, their thoughts, their memories, their names. The PCs are now part of the archive whether they like it or not. They've been filed.

### The Contested Ascent — [[The Gilt Edge]]
At 400 feet — just past the singing vents — the PCs encounter four figures descending the trench on the opposite wall, moving in a tight diamond formation within their own pressure bubble. They're armed. They're professional. And they don't look surprised.

**[[The Gilt Edge]]** — again.

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sent both crews on the same contract. Again. [[The Gilt Edge]] was deployed on a parallel timeline, entering from a different shelf. They reached the vault 2 hours after the PCs, found the pearl already gone, and are now ascending to intercept.

**[[The Gilt Edge]] (CR 5 each, 4 members):**
- **[[Kess "the Fang" Darrowmere]]** — Human fighter 5. Furious. Sword drawn, jaw set. *"Twice. Twice you've beaten us to the prize. This ends now."*
- **[[Whisper]]** — Changeling rogue 5. Different face than last time. Swimming with unnatural grace, positioning for a flank
- **[[Brother Ashenveil]]** — Tiefling cleric 5 (Trickery). Hands raised, calling out first. *"Wait. WAIT. Nobody needs to bleed at 400 feet underwater. Let's talk."*
- **[[Torque]]** — Warforged artificer 5. Doesn't swim — sinks to surfaces and walks along the trench wall. Adamantine wrench glows with an active infusion

**The Situation:**
Both crews are in the trench. The aboleths are converging from below. Neither crew can afford a prolonged fight — every round spent fighting is a round closer to being trapped between [[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]] and an aboleth recovery team. The pressure amulets create overlapping zones; if crews separate, individuals outside both bubbles take 2d6 crushing damage per round.

**Resolution Options:**

1. **[[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]]'s Deal (Diplomacy DC 16):** *"Here's how this goes. You keep the pearl — we can't carry it anyway, our case isn't psionic-shielded. YOU deliver it to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. But WE take the credit. You tell the boss we helped. Recovered it together. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] needs a win or she's going to get us all killed trying to prove something."*
   - A genuine offer. [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] is managing [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]]'s pride while being practical. If the PCs accept, [[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]] escorts them up the trench and parts ways at the shelf. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] hears two different stories — he doesn't care who gets credit as long as the pearl arrives
   - [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] HATES this deal. She agrees only because [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] talks her down. The rivalry intensifies

2. **Combat (underwater):** [[The Gilt Edge]] fights as a unit. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] and [[Torque]] close to melee. [[Whisper]] circles for flanking. [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] buffs and heals.
   - **Underwater penalties:** Slashing and bludgeoning take -2 attack and half damage without *freedom of movement*. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]]'s short swords (piercing/slashing) are partially penalized. [[Torque]]'s wrench (bludgeoning) is heavily penalized. The PCs' amulet carrier has *freedom of movement* — **[[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]]'s does NOT.** Significant tactical advantage for the PCs
   - **Retreat condition:** If 2+ [[The Gilt Edge|Gilt Edge]] members drop below half HP, [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] calls retreat. They flee upward. The rivalry survives
   - **Aboleth countdown:** Every 3 rounds, the vault's broadcast updates: *"RECOVERY TEAM — ESTIMATED ARRIVAL: [decreasing] SECONDS."* After 9 rounds, the first aboleth scout arrives. Both crews should be GONE by then

3. **Intimidation (DC 18):** The PCs just fought or negotiated with an aboleth copyright lawyer. If they can convey the scale of what's behind them, [[The Gilt Edge|the Gilt Edge]] retreats. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]]: *"...Fine. But this is the LAST time."*

4. **Speed:** Both crews swim for the surface. Parallel ascent, mutual ignoring. Anticlimactic but valid — and leaves the rivalry unresolved for Quest 08.

**After the Encounter:**
[[The Gilt Edge]] is becoming a pattern. Three encounters now. [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] is losing patience. During the ascent, the pearl's memory bleed effect kicks in. Whoever carries it experiences involuntary visions — brief, vivid, and increasingly personal:

**Vision 4 is the most important.** The pearl has accessed a memory of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — from when? From where? ### The Shelf
The PCs reach the portal shelf. The portal is active — shimmering blue-green against the black ocean. Behind them, in the depths, lights are moving. Converging. The aboleths are coming.

**If the PCs linger (more than 2 rounds):**
A telepathic broadcast from the deep:

> *THE ARCHIVE COUNCIL HAS CONVENED. YOUR REMOVAL OF ASSET 001 HAS BEEN CLASSIFIED AS A CLASS-7 VIOLATION. A RECOVERY TEAM HAS BEEN AUTHORIZED. ESTIMATED ARRIVAL: NINETY SECONDS.*

Time to go.

### DM Notes
- **The ascent should feel like a chase even without a visible pursuer.** The aboleths aren't chasing the PCs physically (they're slow swimmers over long distances). They're chasing them *psionically* — the telepathic broadcasts, the shifting behavior of the vault environment, the sense of being watched and catalogued and *remembered*. The threat is informational, not physical
- **Vision 4 (young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s voice) is the adventure's debrief bomb — but it goes off during the escape, not in the debrief.** The PCs hear it while running, while stressed, while their brains are already overloaded with aboleth memories. They might not process it immediately. That's fine. The memory will surface later — during a quiet moment, during a dream, during the debrief when [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is back to being his usual diamond-grilled self. The contrast between the scared voice in the vision and the confident mogul in front of them will be profound
- **"We will remember you" is the aboleths' version of a threat.** Aboleths don't need to chase you. They don't need to find you. They already know everything about you, and they will never forget. The PCs are now permanently in the aboleth memory network. This has implications: if anyone ever accesses aboleth memories in the future ([[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]], for instance), the PCs' entire mission — including who sent them — is available for review

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal returns the PCs to [[Offledger]]. They surface in the back room of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]], dripping, shivering, and carrying a 50-pound sphere of compressed cosmic memory. The room feels small after the ocean. It feels *dry*. The PCs haven't been dry in 12 hours. It's disorienting.

[[Snitch Ray]] takes the pearl. He holds it in two eyestalks, examining it with the intensity of an appraiser. For a moment — just a moment — his expression shifts. The pearl's memory bleed touches him, and [[Snitch Ray]] sees something in its surface that makes every eyestalk freeze.

He doesn't share what he saw.

> *"It's authentic. The psionic signature matches the archive's records."*

**If PCs ask how [[Snitch Ray]] has aboleth archive records:**
> *"I have a lot of records. That's my job. Don't ask questions you don't want answers to."*

He's deflecting. But the implication is clear: [[Snitch Ray]] has been in contact with the aboleth network before. He's surveillance. He uses EVERY surveillance network. Including psionic alien ones.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] enters the back room. He stops when he sees the pearl. His central eye dilates. His eyestalks go still. He approaches it slowly — the way you'd approach something that might bite.

> *"That's it. That's the deep cut."*

He reaches toward the pearl. Then he stops. His hand — or the tentacle-equivalent — hovers an inch from the surface.

> *"Has anybody touched this with bare hands?"*

**If PCs confirm they have:**
> *"What'd you see?"*

He asks it casually. Too casually. He wants to know if they saw what he's afraid they saw.

**If PCs describe the visions (partially or fully):**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s expression doesn't change. His body language doesn't shift. But his central eye — the anti-magic cone eye, the most dangerous natural weapon in the room — rotates to point directly at the PC who's speaking.

> *"Ancient oceans and dead cities. That's standard aboleth shit. The pearl's got a lot of garbage in it. Don't read too much into the visions. They're noise."*

If a PC mentions the voice — the young, scared voice asking for more time — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s jaw tightens. Barely perceptible. Then relaxes.

> *"Like I said. Noise."*

He doesn't touch the pearl. He picks it up with a *mage hand* effect and floats it toward [[EyeQ]], who catches it in a field of shimmering telekinetic energy.

> *"Integrate the memory substrate. I want full historical anchoring. Make it feel like it's always been there."*

[[EyeQ]] takes the pearl downstairs. The circuit patterns on its shell pulse brighter for a moment as the pearl's psionic energy interacts with the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s architecture.

### The Letter (Between Adventures)
Three days after the PCs return from the vault, a small letter appears in whoever's quarters seem most private. It's written on cream-colored paper in immaculate handwriting. There's a faint smell of ink and old books.

> *Dear colleagues,*
>
> *I hope this finds you well. I've been reviewing some unusual financial patterns connected to an entity operating out of a location called "[[Offledger]]." The patterns are fascinating — recursive debt structures, transactional energy loops, what appears to be an economic binding framework of considerable sophistication.*
>
> *I would very much like to compare notes. Completely confidential, of course. Professional courtesy between fellow analysts.*
>
> *If you're interested, leave a copper piece on the Dock Ward fountain in Waterdeep on any Moonday. I'll know.*
>
> *Warmly,*
> *P.*
>
> *P.S. — Your recent acquisition from the deep archive has not gone unnoticed. Please be careful. The things you carry have weight beyond the physical.*

This is [[Auditor Pentival]]'s formal recruitment pitch. The PCs now have a choice: respond to [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]], or stay loyal to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. The letter is gentle. It's friendly. It's also the most dangerous piece of paper in [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]].

If [[Snitch Ray]] finds the letter, the consequences are immediate and severe.

### DM Notes
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] not touching the pearl is the debrief's key image.** He uses *mage hand*. He's afraid to touch it. The pearl shows the truth — and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has more truth to hide than anyone. The PCs should notice: their boss, who casually handles artifacts from demiliches and Solars, is afraid to make skin contact with a memory. What is he hiding?
- **"Make it feel like it's always been there"** — this is the pearl's function in the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] made explicit. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is using the primordial memory to retroactively anchor his system. - **[[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]]'s letter is the formal [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] recruitment.** It arrives between adventures, giving the PCs time to discuss it, argue about it, and potentially split on it. Some PCs may want to respond. Others may want to show it to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] (bad idea). Others may want to burn it. All responses are valid. The letter is the point where the campaign's two master-servant relationships begin to compete directly
- **[[Snitch Ray]]'s reaction to the pearl and his implied connection to the aboleth network both seed the idea that [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]] have their own history, their own secrets, and their own relationship to information.** [[Snitch Ray]] isn't just [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s snitch — he's a professional intelligence operative with contacts across planar surveillance networks. He's more connected than the PCs realized. And he just saw something in the pearl that shook him
- **The "[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] was here 912 years ago" reveal + the young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] voice vision + [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] refusing to touch the pearl = a portrait of a patron with a much longer and more complicated history than the PCs assumed.** He wasn't always [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]]. He was once small, scared, and in debt to someone. The campaign's endgame depends on the PCs eventually understanding that the most powerful being they know was once exactly like them. The pearl started showing them

**Memory Gauntlet** (Room 6: Restricted Wing Antechamber)
- **Type:** Psionic trap (living membrane) | **CR:** 5
- **Trigger:** Walking through the 40-foot corridor
- **Effect:** 3 sequential Will saves (DC 16 each, DC 14 if Yorham is full ally):
  - Pulse 1 (10 ft.) — Relive a moment of rule-breaking. Failure: dazed 1 round
  - Pulse 2 (20 ft.) — Relive a moment of getting caught. Failure: dazed 1 round
  - Pulse 3 (30 ft.) — Relive a moment of submission (targets the Veezy contract memory if available). Failure: dazed 1 round + secondary Will DC 14 or shaken 10 minutes

- **Disable:** The gauntlet is a living organism integrated into the corridor. It cannot be disabled without destroying the membrane (regenerates 5 HP/round, hardness 0, total HP 100). Destroying it takes significant time and noise, alerting deeper archive security
- **Note:** Failing all three saves is psychologically punishing but deals no HP damage. The gauntlet is designed to break resistance, not bodies

## Appendix: [[The Primordial Memory Pearl]] — Ongoing Campaign Use

- **Between adventures:** The pearl's integration into the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] causes subtle retroactive changes in [[Offledger]]. Older residents begin "remembering" things that never happened — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s founding of [[Offledger]] feels like ancient history even though it happened within living memory. Contracts signed last month feel like they've always existed. [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|The Coinspiratorium]]'s walls develop a patina of artificial age. Everything feels *older*, *more established*, *more permanent*. This is the pearl's primordial imprint at work — anchoring [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s system in false antiquity
- **[[Yorham Voss|Ilyara]] as intelligence asset:** If befriended, [[Yorham Voss|Ilyara]] contacts the PCs once between adventures 7 and 8 via psionic communication. She's found something in the archive — a memory fragment showing the construction of the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s predecessor (Vision 2), in more detail. The builders weren't aboleths — they were something else. Something that used contracts and obligations as building material. And the specific flaw that destroyed their system was a debt so small it was overlooked: a single promissory note, unpaid, compounding interest across millennia until it outweighed the entire structure. *"Sound familiar?"* she asks. She doesn't know about the IOU. But she's handing the PCs the conceptual key to understanding it
- **The aboleths' long memory:** After the pearl is taken, the aboleths begin monitoring [[Offledger]]. They don't act — not yet. But they watch. If [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] accesses the aboleth network (and they do), the aboleths provide complete records of the PCs' vault visit: who they are, what they took, and who sent them. This intelligence reaches [[Director Casseel]] within weeks. [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]'s case against [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] gets significantly stronger
- **The young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] vision:** The memory of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s voice — scared, young, asking for more time — should resurface. Once in a dream. Once when the PC who heard it sees [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] being particularly arrogant (the contrast triggers the memory). And once, at the endgame, when the PCs find the IOU and everything clicks: the voice, the 49 gold pieces, the cape, the mixtape. He was nobody. He was in debt. He was scared. And he built all of THIS to make sure he'd never feel that way again
