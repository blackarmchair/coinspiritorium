# "Steal a Solar's Halo"

> *"Diamonds? Gold? I'm past all that. I need holiness...as an accessory."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs to Mount Celestia — the holiest plane in the multiverse — to steal a Solar angel's literal halo during the Festival of the First Dawn. No monsters. No traps. No hostile terrain. Just radiant beauty, overwhelming kindness, and the slow-burning realization that the PCs are the worst people in the room by a margin so vast it curves.

This is the moral gut-punch episode. After four missions in hostile environments full of vampires, drow, demiliches, and bureaucrats, the PCs are sent somewhere *good*. Genuinely, painfully good. And they have to rob it.

**Tone arc:** Swagger (briefing) → Awe/discomfort (arrival) → Excruciating social comedy (the festival) → Tense precision (the theft) → Emotional devastation (the discovery) → Chaotic confessions (the escape) → Moral reckoning (debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Social deception under zone of truth, guilt management, stealing from beings with +30 Sense Motive
**Combat:** One potential encounter (escape only); Celestia punishes aggression, not with violence, but with disappointment so profound it deals Wisdom damage

---

## Target Item
**[[Halo of the Dawn's First Judgement]]** — The literal halo of a Solar angel. A ring of concentrated divine light, 18 inches in diameter, hovering and slowly rotating. It weighs nothing. It radiates warmth. Looking at it directly is like looking at a sunrise compressed into a circle. It hums a single sustained note — different for every listener, always the most beautiful sound they've ever heard.

**Properties (when within 10 feet):**
- Continuous *zone of truth* (Will DC 24 — Solar-grade; affects a 20-foot radius centered on the halo)
- +10 Diplomacy to the wearer/carrier
- **Compels confession:** Every minute spent within the halo's radius, each creature must make a Will save (DC 18) or involuntarily speak a humiliating personal secret aloud. Not dark secrets — *embarrassing* ones. The halo judges shame, not sin. Examples:
  - "I once cried because a horse looked at me funny."
  - "I stole a copper piece from a temple collection plate when I was nine and I still think about it."
  - "I think [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s chains are actually kind of cool and I hate that I think that."
  - "I'm afraid of geese."
- The confessions are compulsive but not mind-reading — the halo draws out what the creature *already* feels ashamed of. The more someone has to hide, the more frequent the confessions become
- The halo cannot be dimmed, muffled, or suppressed by mundane means. *Antimagic field* suppresses it. *Nondetection* grants advantage on the Will saves. Nothing short of divine intervention turns it off entirely

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Divine light capacitor — stores and redirects divine energy, allowing the Crown to interface with (and override) celestial authority. With this component, the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] gains the ability to rewrite divine mandates. Blessings, curses, prayers, and divine interventions would all route through [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s system first. He'd become a middleman between mortals and their gods.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The back room of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is in an unusual mood — reflective, almost philosophical. He's wearing a new accessory: a thin platinum circlet floating an inch above his central eye, held in place by minor telekinesis. It doesn't do anything. It's a fashion choice. He's been staring at himself in a hand mirror for the last twenty minutes.

[[Major Look]] is present, standing at attention. This is his operation — image, presentation, and making sure the PCs don't embarrass the brand in front of *literal angels*.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"You know what I ain't got? Holiness. I got gold. I got influence. I got the vision. But I don't got that... glow. That divine 'I'm better than you' energy that the celestials got."*

He puts the mirror down.

> *"There's a Solar up on Mount Celestia. Top shelf angel. Got a halo that's been sitting on her head since before most gods figured out how to make a sunset. I want it."*

**If PCs point out this is Mount Celestia, literally heaven:**
> *"Yeah. And? I been to the Abyss. I been to [[Barovia]]. I sent y'all to the Tomb of Horrors. Celestia ain't nothing but a gated community with better lighting."*

**If PCs point out Solars are CR 23:**
> *"You ain't fighting her. You STEALING from her. While she's busy being holy and shit. There's a festival. She takes the halo off for some purification ritual. That's your window."*

**If PCs ask why he needs an angel's halo:**
> *"Because divinity is the last market I ain't cornered. Gold, fear, influence — I got all that. But divine authority? That's the one thing nobody sells. You can't buy a blessing. You can't franchise a miracle. It's the ultimate monopoly, and I want IN."*

Beat.

> *"Also it would look incredible on me. Be honest."*

### Logistics (delivered by [[Major Look]])

[[Major Look]]'s briefing is precise and image-conscious. He's clearly been to Celestia before — or at least studied it obsessively.

- **Portal:** Deposits the PCs on Lunia, the first layer of Mount Celestia, at the base of the Silver Staircase. The Festival of the First Dawn takes place on the second layer, Mercuria, in a cathedral-garden called the Solarium. The PCs must ascend the Staircase and present themselves at the festival gates
- **Cover story:** The PCs are **redeemed criminals on pilgrimage** — former sinners who've turned their lives around and are making a holy journey to seek celestial blessing. [[Major Look]] has prepared backstories for each PC: reformed thieves, repentant mercenaries, ex-cultists who saw the light. *"The best lies are the ones you almost wish were true."*
- **Dress code:** White pilgrim robes. Simple. Humble. No armor, no visible weapons, no bling. [[Major Look]] is visibly pained by this. *"I know. I know. But celestials can smell vanity. Literally. They have a sense for it."*
- **Equipment:** A lead-lined reliquary case (for transporting the halo — divine energy contained, confession effect suppressed to 5-foot radius), a scroll of *nondetection* (CL 10, 10 hours — helps resist the halo's truth effects), a pouch of 200gp worth of incense and sacred oils (traditional pilgrim offerings, also serves as a bribe for temple functionaries — even angels have bureaucracy), and a scroll of *plane shift* (emergency extraction — the return portal activates at sunset, but if things go wrong, this gets them home immediately with no waiting)
- **Rules of engagement:** *"Violence is not an option. I don't mean tactically. I mean METAPHYSICALLY. You draw a weapon on Mount Celestia, the plane itself responds. Hostile acts suffer a -4 penalty to attacks and saves. Evil-aligned creatures take 1d6 radiant damage per round from ambient divine energy. You don't fight in heaven. You smile, you bow, you steal the shit when nobody's looking."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Parting Words

> *"Couple things. One: the halo makes people tell the truth. Not just regular truth — embarrassing truth. The kind of shit you think about at 3 AM. Don't let it break you. Everybody got shameful shit. Own it and move on."*

> *"Two: these celestials? They ain't like [[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]. They ain't like Lolth. They're NICE. That's the weapon. They're so goddamn nice you'll start feeling bad about what you're doing. DO NOT feel bad. Feelings are not currency."*

> *"Three: if anybody asks what you used to be before you 'reformed'..."*

He gestures at the PCs with casual disdain.

> *"...just tell 'em the truth. Nobody's gonna believe criminals this bad are still doing it."*

### DM Notes
- This briefing should feel different from the previous four. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] isn't sending them somewhere dangerous — he's sending them somewhere GOOD, and the danger is entirely moral. The PCs are about to walk into a place where everyone is kind, everything is beautiful, and they're the villains
- [[Major Look]]'s involvement signals this is an "image" operation. He's the PR Eyecon — he cares about how things look. If the PCs embarrass the organization in front of angels, [[Major Look]] takes it personally
- [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s line about "divinity is the last market I ain't cornered" is the most direct he's been about his ambitions. He's not even pretending this is about the shop anymore. He's talking about monopolizing divine authority. Attentive players should be disturbed
- The *plane shift* scroll as emergency extraction tells the PCs this job is risky enough to warrant an escape hatch. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doesn't usually provide those

## Scene 2: The Silver Staircase

### Arrival
The portal deposits the PCs on a beach of silver sand at the base of Mount Celestia. Lunia, the first layer, stretches before them — a vast, calm ocean under a sky of permanent twilight, stars reflected in water so still it looks like glass. The air smells like rain on clean stone. Everything is quiet in a way that isn't silence — it's *peace*. The absence of threat. The PCs have not experienced this since before they signed their contract with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]].

The Silver Staircase rises from the beach into the clouds — a wide, winding path of white marble ascending the mountain. Other pilgrims are visible ahead: a group of elderly aasimar, a halfling family carrying prayer flags, two dwarves in traveling cloaks who look like they've been walking for weeks. Everyone is heading up.

### The Ascent
The staircase is a 3-hour walk. It's not physically demanding — the steps adjust to the walker's pace, widening for the weary, flattening for the old. Celestia *helps*. The mountain wants you to reach the top.

**What the PCs experience on the staircase:**
- **Warmth.** Not heat — comfort. The exact temperature of a fireplace in winter.
- **Clarity.** Foggy thoughts sharpen. Headaches fade. Minor wounds knit closed. The plane radiates ambient *cure light wounds* — anyone on the staircase heals 1 HP per hour passively
- **Memory.** Each PC experiences, briefly, a vivid flash of their happiest memory. Not a vision — a *feeling*. The memory of safety. Of being loved. It lasts only a moment, but it's real, and it's been so long since any of them felt it
- **Guilt.** Wisdom DC 14 or each PC feels a creeping awareness of their own sins. Not the big ones — the small ones. The white lies. The shortcuts. The moments they chose convenience over kindness. This is Mount Celestia's ambient effect on impure souls. It's not an attack. It's the mountain showing them who they are

**Pilgrim interactions (optional):**
- The elven grandmother smiles at the PCs and offers them trail bread. *"First pilgrimage? You'll weep. Everyone does."*
- The halfling children ask the PCs to carry their prayer flags for a stretch of the staircase. If the PCs do, the children bless them with a traditional pilgrim's prayer. The blessing is real — +1 sacred bonus to saves for 24 hours. The PCs receive this blessing while on their way to commit grand theft
- One of the dwarves, a retired soldier named **[[Bram]]**, falls into step with the PCs and tells them about his pilgrimage. He's coming to Celestia to apologize to a friend he wronged forty years ago. The friend died in battle. [[Bram]] heard his spirit found peace on the second layer. *"Took me four decades to work up the nerve. Figured better late than never."* This interaction has no mechanical purpose. It exists to make the PCs feel like garbage

### The Festival Gates
The path opens onto Mercuria, the second layer — a landscape of golden fields under a sky of perpetual dawn. In the distance, a structure rises from the fields: **the Solarium**, an open-air cathedral made of living crystal and climbing roses. Music drifts from it — not instruments, but voices. Hundreds of voices.

The Festival of the First Dawn is a celestial celebration of redemption — a yearly gathering where beings from across the planes come to celebrate second chances, transformation, and the possibility of grace. Pilgrims, petitioners, celestials, and reformed creatures of all kinds gather to pray, feast, and affirm that goodness is always possible.

The PCs — dressed as redeemed criminals — are exactly the kind of person this festival was designed to celebrate.

At the gate, an **archon lantern** — a hovering sphere of warm light with a gentle, androgynous voice — greets them:

> *"Welcome, travelers. Are you here to celebrate the Dawn?"*

If the PCs confirm their pilgrim cover:

> *"Redeemed souls. How wonderful. The festival honors your courage. Please — enter, and know that you are cherished."*

It means it. Completely.

### DM Notes
- **Celestia should feel real.** Not as a game location — as a *place*. The beauty should be specific and overwhelming. The kindness should be genuine and disarming. If the players aren't uncomfortable by the end of this scene, you haven't sold it hard enough
- **[[Bram]] the dwarf is the emotional knife.** A man who walked across a plane to apologize for something he did forty years ago, and the PCs are walking the same path to steal from the beings who gave his friend peace. Don't push the comparison. Let the players make it themselves
- **The ambient guilt effect (Wisdom DC 14) is not a punishment — it's worldbuilding.** Celestia doesn't attack impure creatures. It shows them themselves. The PCs aren't being harmed. They're being *seen*. That's worse
- **The healing, the warmth, the happy memories — these are all genuine.** Celestia isn't trying to trick them or soften them up for judgment. The plane is simply good. It does good things for people who walk on it. Including thieves. Especially thieves. That's the whole point of grace, and it's going to make the PCs feel terrible

## Scene 3: The Festival of the First Dawn

### The Solarium
The cathedral-garden is immense — open to the sky, with crystal pillars rising like trees and vines of living light winding between them. Hundreds of attendees mill through the space: pilgrims, celestials, redeemed fiends, reformed undead (now living again), and beings the PCs have no names for. Everyone is happy. Not manic, not forced — *genuinely* happy.

Long tables are laden with food — simple, beautiful, and seemingly infinite. The wine never runs out. The bread is warm. Everything tastes like the best version of itself.

**Key festival areas:**

| Area                  | Description                                                              | Relevance                                             |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| The Welcome Hall      | Open courtyard where pilgrims gather and are blessed                     | PCs are greeted and assigned a celestial guide        |
| The Testimony Ring    | Circular amphitheater where redeemed souls share their stories           | The PCs are invited to speak here                     |
| The Purification Pool | A shallow basin of liquid light where sacred items are ritually cleansed | WHERE THE SOLAR REMOVES HER HALO — the theft window   |
| The Feast of Grace    | Communal dining beneath the open sky                                     | Social mingling, information gathering                |
| The Inner Sanctum     | Private meditation chambers for celestial VIPs                           | Where the halo is kept before the purification ritual |

### Retailable Loot — Festival Gifts

*Given freely to pilgrims during the festival. Taking them is technically not theft, which makes it worse.*

| Item | Listed Value | Acquisition | Notes |

*Requires active theft during or after the festival. Sleight of Hand DCs provided.*

| Item | Listed Value | Acquisition | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Archon feather (shed during blessing ceremony, radiates faint good aura) | 200gp | Sleight of Hand DC 14, available during the Welcome | **Auction candidate.** Divine material components are rare in [[Offledger]]'s market |
| Celestial incense (unused portion of the PCs' own supply — [[Iris Major]] won't audit exact quantities) | 30gp/stick (up to 3) | No check — it was already theirs, technically | The pettiest possible theft |

> **DM note:** Every item the PCs take from Celestia should be narrated. The festival gave them these things freely, with love. Selling them in [[Offledger]] for profit is a small, persistent cruelty. The Store Phase turns grace into gold.

### The Welcome (Oh No)
Upon entering, the PCs are immediately noticed. Not because they're suspicious — because they're *pilgrims*. The festival has an official welcome protocol for first-time visitors, especially redeemed souls. A trumpet archon named **[[Valanthir]]** — a tall, silver-armored celestial with a face like a kind teacher — approaches them with genuine warmth:

> *"Brothers and sisters! Welcome to the Festival of the First Dawn. I am [[Valanthir]], and I have the honor of serving as your guide today."*

He clasps their hands. His grip is warm. His eyes are full of uncomplicated joy.

> *"We've had so many pilgrims this year — redeemed souls from across the planes, all gathered to celebrate the light within them. You must be so proud of how far you've come."*

**He means it.** This is not a test. He genuinely believes these are reformed criminals who have made a sacred journey. He is *proud* of them.

He assigns himself as their guide for the festival. He will:
- Introduce them to other celestials and pilgrims
- Bring them food and drink
- Ask about their "journey of redemption" (requires Bluff checks — see below)
- Invite them to testify in the Testimony Ring
- Escort them to meet the Solar

**[[Valanthir]]'s Sense Motive:** +18. He's perceptive but trusting. His trust actually makes him *easier* to deceive — he wants to believe the PCs are good people. This gives the PCs a +2 circumstance bonus on Bluff checks against him, because his desire to believe is stronger than his ability to doubt.

### Maintaining the Cover (Social Challenge)
The PCs must navigate the festival for approximately 4 hours before the purification ritual creates their theft window. During this time, they're under constant social pressure from beings who are overwhelmingly kind, perceptive, and sincerely interested in their stories.

**The Bluff Gauntlet:**
Throughout the festival, various celestials and pilgrims will ask the PCs about their redemption journey. Each conversation requires a Bluff check:

| Conversation                                                   | Bluff DC | Consequence of Failure                                                                                                                                                                     |
| -------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| [[Valanthir]] asks about their "former lives"                  | 14       | He gets a flicker of doubt but rationalizes it away — "The journey is different for everyone"                                                                                              |
| A redeemed tiefling asks what sin they're atoning for          | 16       | Awkward pause. The tiefling shares her own story (she was an assassin) and cries. The PCs have to comfort a crying ex-assassin who thinks they understand her                              |
| An astral deva casually uses *detect evil* (standard greeting) | —        | If any PC is evil-aligned, the deva pauses. Then smiles. *"The Dawn's light reaches even the deepest shadow. Welcome, friend."* They think the evil is residual. They are SO understanding |
| A group of pilgrims invites the PCs to pray together           | 12       | Failure means the PC accidentally says something sincere during the prayer. Which is worse                                                                                                 |
| [[Valanthir]] asks the PCs to testify                          | 18       | See The Testimony Ring below                                                                                                                                                               |

**Failure mode:** The PCs are never *caught*. Celestia doesn't work that way. When the PCs lie badly or behave oddly, the celestials assume they're struggling with their redemption — that the shame is still fresh, that the old habits haven't fully died. They respond with MORE kindness. More patience. More gentle encouragement. Failure doesn't blow the cover. It makes the guilt worse.

### The Testimony Ring
The worst moment. [[Valanthir]] brings the PCs to a circular amphitheater where redeemed souls share their stories of transformation. There are maybe fifty beings in the audience — pilgrims, celestials, a few children of petitioners. It's intimate. It's honest. A woman is currently speaking about how she used to steal from temples, and how a priest's forgiveness changed her life. She's weeping. The audience weeps with her.

Then [[Valanthir]] turns to the PCs.

> *"Would any of you like to share? There's no pressure. But your journey brought you here, and that means something."*

**If a PC agrees to testify:**
They must stand before fifty celestial beings and either:
1. **Lie.** Bluff DC 18 (the audience's collective Sense Motive is high, but again, they WANT to believe). A successful lie gets a standing ovation, tearful embraces, and at least one celestial saying *"I knew goodness lived in you."* A failed lie gets a sympathetic murmur and someone offering to pray with them. Either way, the PC wants to die
2. **Tell the truth.** This is actually an option. A PC could stand up there and say: "I'm a thief. I work for a monster. I'm here to steal something." The audience would assume it's a metaphor. They'd applaud the raw honesty. They might even cry harder. The truth, in this context, reads as the deepest possible expression of redemption — someone so committed to their new path that they can name their sins without flinching. *The truth is the best lie available*

**If no PC agrees to testify:**
[[Valanthir]] nods understandingly. *"The bravest step is often the one you're not ready to take. The Dawn sees you regardless."*

The guilt damage is the same either way.

### DM Notes
- **This scene should be genuinely funny AND genuinely awful.** The comedy comes from the gap between what the PCs are doing and how everyone's treating them. The awfulness comes from the fact that the celestials are RIGHT — not about the PCs being redeemed, but about the possibility of redemption. The PCs COULD be good people. They're choosing not to be. That's the knife
- **[[Valanthir]] is the adventure's emotional center.** He's not naive — he's hopeful. He's met genuinely redeemed souls. He's seen the worst people become the best. He believes in second chances because he's witnessed them. The PCs are going to betray that belief. Make [[Valanthir]] someone the players genuinely like before that happens
- **The "truth is the best lie" moment in the Testimony Ring is the adventure's signature beat.** A PC standing before celestials and saying "I'm a thief who works for a beholder crime lord" and receiving a standing ovation because they think it's a metaphor — that's peak Coinspiratorium. Comedy and horror in the same breath
- **Don't overplay the guilt.** Let the players arrive at it naturally. The festival is genuinely wonderful. The beings are genuinely kind. The PCs are genuinely rotten. You don't need to point it out. They'll feel it

## Scene 4: The Purification Ritual

### The Solar
**[[Seraphiel]], Solar of the First Dawn**, arrives at the Purification Pool two hours before sunset. The festival falls quiet. Not from fear — from *reverence*.

[[Seraphiel]] is 15 feet tall, with four wings of white fire and a face that shifts between every beautiful thing the viewer has ever seen — a sunset, a mother's smile, a field of wildflowers in rain. Her eyes are solid gold. Her voice sounds like a chord played on every instrument at once, dialed down to a volume that won't shatter mortal eardrums.

Above her head: the halo. The [[Halo of the Dawn's First Judgement]]. It rotates slowly, shedding light that casts no shadows. It's the most beautiful object the PCs have ever seen. It's also — at this distance — activating its zone of truth.

> *"Children of the Dawn. I see you. All of you."*

She looks at the crowd. She looks at the PCs. Her gaze lingers for exactly one second longer than comfortable.

> *"I see what you were. I see what you are. And I see what you might yet become. That is enough. That has always been enough."*

**Sense Motive +38.** She knows they're lying. Of course she knows. She's a Solar. She can see souls the way the PCs see furniture.

She says nothing. Because either they'll prove her hopes right, or they'll prove them wrong, and she'll deal with whatever comes. That's grace.

### The Ritual
The Purification Ritual is a once-yearly ceremony where sacred objects are ritually cleansed in the Purification Pool — a shallow basin filled with liquid that looks like captured moonlight. The ritual requires that each sacred object be separated from its bearer and submerged in the pool for exactly **10 minutes** while prayers are spoken.

[[Seraphiel]] removes her halo.

The moment she lifts it from her head, the zone of truth collapses to just the halo's immediate area (5-foot radius around the pool). [[Seraphiel]]'s own divine presence is what amplified it to 20 feet. Without her wearing it, it's just a powerful magic item — not a divine broadcast tower.

She places the halo in the pool. It floats, spinning slowly, shedding prismatic light through the liquid. The assembled celestials begin to sing — a hymn without words, a frequency that vibrates in the chest.

**The window:** For 10 minutes, the halo is separated from the Solar, sitting in a pool of holy water, guarded only by the sacredness of the moment and the assumption that nobody would POSSIBLY steal from a purification ceremony in the holiest place in the multiverse.

They would be wrong.

### The Theft (Skill Challenge)
The Purification Pool is in the open courtyard, surrounded by singing celestials. The halo is 30 feet from the nearest cover. Every celestial in attendance has Spot bonuses ranging from +12 to +25. The Solar herself is standing 15 feet from the pool with her eyes closed in prayer.

**The PCs need:**
1. A distraction that draws celestial attention away from the pool (2-3 rounds)
2. A way to reach the pool undetected
3. A way to extract the halo and get it into the lead-lined reliquary case
4. An exit before the 10-minute ritual ends and [[Seraphiel]] reaches for her halo

**Approach options:**

**The Diversion:**
- A PC creates a "spiritual crisis" — falling to their knees, weeping, calling out for guidance. The celestials flock to help. Bluff DC 16 to sell the performance, but Celestia's ambient grace means the DC is actually *lower* here — the celestials are predisposed to believe someone is having a genuine moment. (If the PC is ACTUALLY having a genuine spiritual crisis from the guilt of all this, no check needed)
- A PC uses *ghost sound*, *minor image*, or similar magic to create a phenomenon at the far end of the courtyard — a "miraculous" vision, a burst of radiant light, a celestial portent. The celestials investigate. Spellcraft DC to avoid detection is normal, but *divination* magic is commonplace here, so the illusion is identified within 3 rounds
- A PC starts a hymn. A really loud one. Off-key, emotional, and involving a lot of arm gestures. The other pilgrims join in. The energy shifts to the singers. This is Mount Celestia — spontaneous worship is not only normal, it's *encouraged*. No check required. The singing provides auditory cover and draws visual attention

**The Approach:**
- Move Silently DC 20 to cross the 30 feet to the pool (open ground, but the singing and prayer create auditory cover — reduce to DC 16 during a hymn)
- Invisibility: the *nondetection* scroll suppresses magical detection. *Invisibility* works against the crowd, but [[Seraphiel]] has *true seeing*. However, she's in prayer with her eyes closed. If she opens her eyes, the invisible PC is instantly visible to her — 10% chance per round, rolled secretly by the DM
- A PC who participated in the festival and built rapport with [[Valanthir]] can claim they want to "pray closer to the light" — [[Valanthir]] escorts them to within 10 feet of the pool. No stealth check needed. But now they need to grab the halo with a celestial guide standing right there

**The Extraction:**
- The halo sits in the pool. Reaching into the water deals no damage (it's holy water, not acid), but the water is *warm* — and the halo's confession effect activates on contact. The PC touching the halo must make a Will save (DC 18) or speak a humiliating secret at full volume. This is the worst possible time for that
- Sleight of Hand DC 22 to palm the halo and replace it with... something. The PCs probably don't have a fake halo. Unless they're very creative. A *minor image* of a halo spinning in the pool buys 1d4+1 rounds before someone touches it and the illusion breaks
- The halo fits in the reliquary case. Closing the case reduces the confession radius to 5 feet and the zone of truth to the case's interior. The case latches are stiff — one round to close and lock

**Timing:** If the PCs haven't extracted the halo within 8 rounds of the ritual starting, [[Seraphiel]] opens her eyes and reaches for the pool. The window closes.

### DM Notes
- **The skill challenge should feel like a heist set-piece.** The clock is ticking (8 rounds). The stakes are clear (a Solar notices = game over). Each PC should have a role: distraction, approach, extraction, overwatch
- **The confession-on-contact moment is the adventure's mechanical centerpiece.** A PC reaches into holy water, touches a divine artifact, and their mouth opens against their will to announce their most embarrassing secret to a courtyard full of angels. The table will lose it. Let them
- **[[Seraphiel]] knowing and doing nothing is the hardest part to run.** She senses their deception. She allows them to act. Because grace means allowing people the freedom to choose — even badly. If the DM wants to make this explicit, [[Seraphiel]] can murmur during her prayer: *"Even now. Even here. I hope."* The PCs may or may not hear it. The weight is the same either way
- **Creative solutions should be rewarded.** If a PC comes up with something not listed — mage hand from behind a pillar, a summoned creature as a distraction, a pilgrim "fainting" into the pool and grabbing the halo in the chaos — let it work if the idea is sound. The festival setting is rich with improvisation potential

## Scene 5: The Discovery

### The Moment
One of two triggers:

**Clean theft:** The ritual ends. [[Seraphiel]] reaches into the pool. The halo is gone. She stands perfectly still for three seconds. The singing stops. The silence is the loudest thing the PCs have ever heard.

**Sloppy theft:** The confession effect goes off, or the illusion breaks, or [[Seraphiel]] opens her eyes early. She sees the empty pool (or the PC with their hand in the water). Same silence. Same stillness.

Either way, [[Seraphiel]] turns to face the crowd. Her expression is not anger. It's not rage. It's not even surprise.

It's *sorrow*.

> *"Someone has taken the halo."*

She says it the way you'd say "someone is hurt." Not an accusation — a diagnosis.

The festival goes silent. The celestials look at each other. The pilgrims look at their feet. [[Valanthir]]'s face cycles through confusion, denial, and dawning understanding in about four seconds.

### [[Seraphiel]]'s Response
The Solar does not attack. She does not accuse the PCs directly. She does not lock down the festival. Instead, she addresses the crowd:

> *"The halo will be missed. Not for its power — power can be rebuilt. But for what it represents. The Dawn's First Judgement was not a weapon. It was a promise: that truth, even painful truth, is an act of love."*

She pauses. Her golden eyes sweep the crowd and settle — briefly, unmistakably — on the PCs.

> *"If the one who took it hears me: I am not angry. I am not even disappointed. I am asking. Bring it back. Whatever drove you to this — whatever debt, whatever fear, whatever master — it is not worth what you will lose by keeping it."*

**She knows.** She's offering them an out. A genuine, no-consequences return. The halo back in the pool, no questions asked, the PCs walk away with their pilgrim cover intact and their souls slightly less damaged.

**If the PCs return the halo:**
[[Seraphiel]] takes it. She places it back on her head. She looks at the PCs for a long time. Then:

> *"Thank you. The Dawn sees the war inside you. Whatever chains you carry... they are not forever."*

The PCs leave Celestia empty-handed. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] will be *furious*. But they've been seen — truly seen — by a being of pure goodness, and they were found worthy of mercy. This is a valid campaign outcome. The PCs have made a moral choice that costs them materially but earns them something money can't buy. (It also means they need to figure out another way to get the halo, or [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] gets creative about punishing failure — see Scaling Notes)

**If the PCs keep the halo and run:**
[[Seraphiel]] watches them go. She does not pursue. She does not send the archons. She lets them leave.

[[Valanthir]] does not let them leave.

### [[Valanthir]]'s Pursuit
The trumpet archon who guided them, fed them, believed in them, and welcomed them as redeemed souls — he's standing at the Solarium gate. He's drawn his sword. Not in anger. His hands are shaking.

> *"I vouched for you. I brought you to her. I told the others you were proof that anyone could change."*

His voice breaks.

> *"Was any of it true?"*

**This is not a combat encounter.** [[Valanthir]] is CR 14, but he's not fighting to kill. He's fighting because he doesn't know what else to do. His worldview just shattered. He believed in redemption. He believed in the PCs. And they spat in his face.

**Resolution options:**

**Bluff DC 22:** *"We didn't take it. Someone else must have."* [[Valanthir]] hesitates. He wants to believe. He's programmed to believe. But his Sense Motive is +18, and this time, his trust is broken. On success, he lowers his sword and lets them pass — but his eyes follow them, and the PCs can feel the moment his hope dies. On failure, he doesn't attack, but he raises the alarm (see Escape)

**Diplomacy DC 20:** *"We had no choice. We're enslaved. The one who sent us will destroy us if we return empty-handed."* This is, essentially, the truth. [[Valanthir]]'s sword lowers. His expression shifts from betrayal to pity. *"Then I will pray for you. Because the thing that sent you here is worse than any sin."* He steps aside. He's crying. He lets them go because he understands they're victims too, and that understanding is worse than anything he could do with the sword

**Fight:** [[Valanthir]] is CR 14 (trumpet archon — see stat appendix). On Mount Celestia, he has the plane's support: +2 to all saves, fast healing 5, and hostile acts against him trigger the plane's -4 penalty against the aggressors. Fighting is possible but brutal, wasteful, and leaves the PCs as confirmed enemies of Celestia. Not recommended. Not even a little

**Run:** The PCs bolt past [[Valanthir]]. He pursues for 3 rounds, then stops. He doesn't have it in him. He stands at the gate and watches them go.

### DM Notes
- **This scene is the emotional climax of not just this adventure but the first half of the campaign.** Everything before this was stealing from monsters ([[Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]], Lolth, [[Acererak]]) or systems (Waterdeep bureaucracy). This is stealing from someone who loved them. The tonal shift should be seismic
- **[[Seraphiel]]'s "I am not angry" speech should make the table go quiet.** Anger would be easy to deal with. Combat would be easy to deal with. Genuine, unconditional compassion from someone you've just robbed? That's the real trap
- **The "return the halo" option must be real.** If the PCs choose to give it back, don't punish them narratively. This is a valid moral choice and should have genuine consequences — both the wrath of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] and the respect of the table. Adventures can fail. Characters can grow. Let it happen
- **[[Valanthir]] crying is the hardest moment to run.** It should feel earned, not manipulative. He's not performing grief — he's experiencing the death of something he believed in. If it lands right, at least one player will be genuinely upset. That's the adventure working as intended

## Scene 6: The Descent

### Running Down the Mountain
The PCs descend the Silver Staircase with the halo in the reliquary case. Even through the lead lining, the confession effect bleeds out in a 5-foot radius. Whoever carries the case is in the zone.

### The Silver Staircase Patrol — Hound Archons
Halfway down the staircase, three shapes emerge from behind a crystalline wayshrine. **Hound archons** — celestial guardians in hybrid form, standing upright, armored in white-gold plate, greatswords drawn. They're not angry. They're *resolute*.

The lead archon — a scarred veteran with silver-streaked fur and kind, tired eyes — steps forward:

> *"Pilgrims. The Solarium has reported a theft. A sacred relic is missing. We've been asked to ensure no one leaves the second layer carrying stolen property."*

He gestures at the reliquary case.

> *"I can hear it. The halo sings. Even in a lead box."*

**3 Hound Archons** — MM p.16, CR 4 each
- Medium Outsider (Archon, Good, Lawful) | HP 22 | AC 19 | Speed 40 ft.
- **Bite +7 melee** (1d8+3) or **greatsword +7 melee** (2d6+3)
- Spell-like abilities: *detect evil* at will, *aid* 3/day
- **Aura of Menace (Su):** Will DC 16 or -2 to AC, attacks, and saves for 24 hours
- On Mount Celestia: +2 to all saves, DR 5/evil
- **Fight to subdue:** All attacks deal **nonlethal damage** unless the PCs strike first with lethal force. Even then, the archons switch to lethal only after 2 rounds of being attacked lethally

**Resolution Options:**

1. **Bluff DC 20:** *"We carry sacred oils from the Solarium, blessed during the ritual."* Difficult — the archon can hear the halo humming. But the staircase is noisy with pilgrims and hymns, and the archon's reluctance to accuse pilgrims of sacrilege creates hesitation.

2. **Diplomacy DC 18:** Appeal to the archons' compassion. *"We serve a master who will destroy us if we return empty-handed. We didn't choose this."* If partially true — and on Mount Celestia, the ambient guilt makes honesty easier — the lead archon's conviction wavers. He may step aside with: *"Then I will pray for you. Go quickly. Do not come back."*

3. **Speed:** The PCs run. The archons pursue for 5 rounds at 40 ft. (PCs likely move at 30 ft.), but the staircase is crowded with pilgrims and the archons won't barrel through innocents. Tumble DC 15 or Diplomacy DC 12 to navigate the crowd faster than the archons can politely push through.

4. **Combat:** The archons fight to subdue with nonlethal damage and grapples. **Killing a hound archon on Mount Celestia triggers the plane's full hostile response:** -4 to attacks and saves for the rest of the descent, 1d6 radiant damage per round for evil-aligned PCs, and [[Valanthir]] is alerted and arrives in 10 rounds. This should be a LAST resort.

5. **Surrender the halo:** The archons return it to the Solarium. The PCs leave empty-handed. Merges with the "failure path."

**DM Guidance:** The archons are the adventure's mechanical teeth — the first real consequence for the theft. They should feel like honorable opponents: they don't want to fight pilgrims. But they WILL stop the PCs if no other option presents itself. The encounter taxes resources and makes the confession gauntlet below feel more precarious.

### Encounter: The Grieving Bralani (Optional)
If the PCs fought or fled past the Hound Archons, a second encounter occurs further down the staircase — at a bridge where the path narrows between two waterfalls of liquid starlight. A figure stands in the bridge's center: a **bralani eladrin** in her wind form — a woman-shaped column of glowing air, crackling with emotion, blocking passage.

Her name is **[[Aelindra]]**. She was at the festival. She saw the ritual. She saw [[Seraphiel]]'s halo go dark. And she followed the PCs down the mountain — not out of duty (bralani are chaotic good; they don't take orders from archons), but out of *grief*. She loved that festival. She loved what it meant. And now someone has stolen the light from it.

> *"I don't know why you did this. I don't want to know. I just want you to feel what that room felt like when the light went out."*

**[[Aelindra]], Bralani Eladrin** — MM p.93, CR 6
- Medium Outsider (Chaotic, Extraplanar, Good) | HP 33 | AC 20 (+4 Dex, +6 natural) or whirlwind form AC 23 | Speed 40 ft., fly 100 ft. (wind form)
- **+1 holy scimitar +8/+3 melee** (1d6+2 plus 2d6 vs. evil) or **+1 holy composite longbow +9/+4 ranged** (1d8+1 plus 2d6 vs. evil)
- **Wind Form (Su):** +3 AC, fly 100 ft. (perfect), deals 3d6 wind damage to adjacent creatures, immune to physical attacks. Cannot attack with weapons in this form
- **Spell-like abilities (CL 6):** At will — *blur*, *charm person* (DC 13), *gust of wind* (DC 14), *mirror image*; 2/day — *lightning bolt* (DC 15), *cure serious wounds*
- On Mount Celestia: +2 saves, fast healing 2
- DR 10/cold iron or evil
- SR 17

**[[Aelindra]]'s Tactics:**
She doesn't want to kill the PCs. She wants them to *stop*. She opens in wind form, trying to blast the reliquary case out of the carrier's hands (gust of wind, opposed Strength +3 vs. the carrier). If the case is dropped, she dives for it and flees upward — returning it to the Solarium. The PCs must catch a creature with 100 ft. fly speed on a mountain staircase.

If the PCs refuse to drop the case, she shifts to physical form and fights — scimitar drawn, tears in her eyes. She deals lethal damage but pulls her strikes on downed PCs (she won't coup de grace). If reduced below 10 HP, she shifts to wind form and retreats, shouting over her shoulder:

> *"Keep it, then. Keep the light. I hope it burns you every time you look at it."*

**Resolution Options:**
- **Combat:** Beatable for level 6-7, but the +2d6 holy damage on her weapon punishes evil-aligned PCs hard. The DR 10/cold iron means most parties chip away slowly. The real cost is time — every round of fighting is another round on the confession clock
- **Diplomacy DC 18:** *"We have no choice. We're bound by contract."* [[Aelindra]], being chaotic good, UNDERSTANDS servitude and HATES it. She won't help the PCs — but she steps aside, fury and pity warring on her face: *"Break your chains or they'll break you. I've seen it happen to better souls than yours."*
- **Bluff DC 20:** Nearly impossible — the halo's truth field bleeds through the case, and bralani have +12 Sense Motive. But if the PCs claim they're taking the halo to *protect* it from something worse... the lie is close enough to truth (the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] IS worse) that [[Aelindra]] hesitates. She demands to know what. If the PCs describe [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] or the Crown in the truth field's radius, they can't lie about it. The truth horrifies [[Aelindra]]. She lets them pass — and remembers everything they said
- **Run:** [[Aelindra]] has fly 100 ft. in wind form. She can pursue the length of the staircase. But she can't carry the case in wind form (no hands), so if the PCs hold it tight and sprint, she can only harass them with gust of wind attempts. Each round: Reflex DC 14 for the carrier or drop the case. [[Aelindra]] gives up after 5 rounds of failed attempts — her grief isn't infinite

**DM Notes:**
- [[Aelindra]] is the emotional mirror of the Hound Archons. They were DUTY. She is GRIEF. The archons stopped the PCs because the law required it. [[Aelindra]] stops the PCs because the theft *hurt* her. The two encounters bracket the spectrum of Celestia's response to evil: law and love, both wounded
- If the PCs kill [[Aelindra]] on Mount Celestia, the plane responds as described (hostile response, -4 penalties, radiant damage). But worse: a bralani is chaotic good. She's not a soldier. She's a poet, a wind spirit, someone who came to a festival to celebrate second chances. The PCs just killed someone who was crying while she fought them. Let that sit
- If the PCs befriend or spare [[Aelindra]], she becomes a minor ally — a voice in the celestial network who knows what the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is. She contacts them once, much later, with a warning from the upper planes: *"The thing your employer is building — the angels can feel it. All of them. It tastes like a prayer someone said backwards."*

### Encounter: The Pilgrim Who Knows Your Name ([[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]])

Somewhere on the Silver Staircase — between the archons and the beach, during the press of pilgrims and hymns — one of the PCs notices a halfling pilgrim who has been walking near them for a while. White robes. Humble expression. Bare feet on the silver steps. Completely unremarkable.

Except he called one of the PCs by name.

Not their cover name. Their *real* name.

The halfling falls into step beside the party. He moves through the pilgrim crowd with the ease of someone who belongs here — or someone trained to look like they belong everywhere.

> *"Don't react. Keep walking. You're doing fine."*

A warm smile. Kind eyes. The exact face you'd trust at a moment like this.

> *"My name is Quill. My colleague — the gnome with the spectacles — said he gave you a card in Waterdeep. You kept it, I hope? Good. You're smarter than your employer thinks you are."*

This is **[[Agent Quill]]** — changeling, [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] handler, and the warmest knife in the agency's drawer. He's been on Mount Celestia since before the PCs arrived. He attended the festival. He watched the theft. He watched the PCs steal a Solar's halo, carry it through a celestial patrol, and fight a weeping eladrin on a bridge of starlight. He watched all of it.

He didn't intervene.

> *"I'm not here to stop you. I'm not here to arrest you. I'm here because my superiors think you're worth talking to, and I agree with them."*

**The Halo's Effect on Quill:**

Here's the problem: the reliquary case bleeds. The halo's confession aura extends in a 5-foot radius, and Quill is walking right beside the carrier. The zone of truth (Will DC 24) and the confession compulsion (Will DC 18) affect everyone in range — including Quill.

Quill is good. His Will save is +11. He passes the zone of truth easily — changeling training, decades of practice.

The confession compulsion gets him.

Mid-sentence — between one smooth, professional statement and the next — Quill's composure cracks. His face flickers. For one instant, the halfling features blur, revealing something featureless underneath. And then he says, involuntarily, in a voice that is not the halfling's:

> *"I'm afraid of what happens when your boss finishes building it. Not professionally. Personally. I'm afraid."*

One second. Two seconds. The features reset. The halfling smile returns. Quill's composure reassembles with practiced speed, but there's a hairline fracture in it now. He heard himself. The PCs heard him.

> *"...That wasn't planned."*

A beat.

> *"But it's true. For whatever that's worth."*

**What Quill Offers:**

He doesn't hard-sell. He plants a seed and walks away.

> *"You work for Vizzok. I understand the position you're in — better than you think. My agency has been studying your employer for a very long time. We know what the artifacts are for. We know what he's building. We don't know how to stop it yet."*

He glances at the reliquary case.

> *"But we think you might. Eventually. When you're ready."*

**The Contact Method:**

> *"If you ever want to talk — and you don't have to, not today, not tomorrow, take your time — speak the following phrase into any mirror in [[Offledger]]: 'I'd like to make a withdrawal.' Someone will hear it. Someone will come."*

He lets that sit.

> *"Don't use it unless you mean it. And don't use it where [[Snitch Ray]] can see you. He watches mirrors. It's a whole thing."*

**Quill's Exit:**

A group of pilgrims passes between the PCs and Quill — singing, radiant, completely oblivious to the espionage happening in their midst. When the group passes, the halfling is gone. In his place, a different pilgrim — human, female, older — is walking at the same pace, in the same position. She doesn't look at the PCs. She might be Quill. She might be an actual pilgrim.

The PCs will never be sure.

**DM Notes:**
- **This scene escalates [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] from "a friendly gnome with a business card" to "an agency that can find you on Mount Celestia."** The progression: Quest 4, [[Auditor Pentival|Pentival]] was a chance encounter (or seemed like one). Quest 5, Quill is a deliberate contact. The message is clear — [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] has the resources and the will to track the PCs across planes
- **The confession beat is the scene's payload.** Quill is a professional. He controls every word, every expression, every interaction. The halo strips that away for one sentence. "I'm afraid" — not "my agency assesses risk" or "the threat level is elevated." *Afraid*. Personally. The PCs have now seen behind two masks: the halfling mask (professional) and the changeling mask (biological). What they glimpsed underneath both is a person who is genuinely scared of the Crown Infinite. That fear is more persuasive than any recruitment pitch
- **The mirror contact method — "I'd like to make a withdrawal" — is a loaded phrase.** In [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]], a "withdrawal" means taking gold out of the operation. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] would hear it as a business term. [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] would hear it as a code phrase. The double meaning is Quill's design — it can be spoken in public, in the shop, even in front of other people, without sounding suspicious. Unless [[Snitch Ray]] has been told what it means
- **Don't overplay this scene.** Quill appears, delivers his lines, gets hit by one confession, and vanishes. The whole encounter should take 5-7 minutes of real time. It's a whisper on a staircase, not a negotiation. The PCs should be left with questions, not answers — and with a mirror phrase they can use whenever they're ready

---

**The Confession Gauntlet:**
Every 10 minutes of the descent, the carrier must make a Will save (DC 18) or blurt a secret. On the Silver Staircase, surrounded by pilgrims, this creates a rolling disaster:

**Sample confessions (d6 per failed save):**

| Roll | Confession                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ---- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | A mundane embarrassment: "I named my sword after someone I had a crush on in school." Pilgrims nearby chuckle sympathetically                                                                                     |
| 2    | A professional secret: "I didn't reform. I'm still a criminal. I work for a beholder." Pilgrims assume it's a metaphor for addiction. They offer support                                                          |
| 3    | A [[Vizzok the Opulent]]-specific confession: "I think about murdering my boss every single day." Pilgrims nod. Relatable                                                                                         |
| 4    | A campaign-relevant secret: "We just stole an angel's halo and it's in this box." Pilgrims look uncomfortable. One laughs nervously. Nobody believes it because it's insane                                       |
| 5    | A deeply personal truth: "I'm scared. I'm always scared. I don't know who I am anymore." The nearest pilgrim takes the PC's hand and holds it for the rest of the staircase. In silence                           |
| 6    | DM's choice: a confession about another PC, a secret crush on an NPC, a childhood fear, or an opinion about [[Vizzok the Opulent]]'s music taste that would get them disintegrated if said aloud in [[Offledger]] |

**Pass the case:** PCs can trade the reliquary between them. Each new carrier resets the 10-minute timer. Smart rotation means fewer confessions. But every PC who carries it risks exposure.

### The Beach
The PCs reach the silver beach. The portal back to [[Offledger]] shimmers at the waterline. The sun is setting over the celestial ocean. It's the most beautiful sunset any of them have ever seen.

[[Bram]] the dwarf is on the beach, sitting on a rock, looking at the water. He sees the PCs. He waves. He looks happy. He looks like a man who finally apologized.

> *"How was the festival? Everything you hoped?"*

### DM Notes
- **The confession gauntlet is pure comedy after the emotional devastation of Scene 5.** Let it be funny. The PCs need the release. But thread the needle — some confessions should be genuinely vulnerable. A PC blurting "I'm scared" to a pilgrim who responds with silent kindness is comedy and heartbreak in the same breath
- **[[Bram]]'s presence on the beach is the final emotional note.** He went to Celestia to make things right. The PCs went to Celestia to make things wrong. He's at peace. They're carrying stolen holiness in a lead box while involuntarily confessing their sins. The juxtaposition is the point
- **If the PCs returned the halo (failure path):** The descent is peaceful. No confessions, no guilt, no lead box. Just four people walking down a holy mountain knowing they chose right and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s going to make them pay for it. This walk should feel like the calm before a very specific storm

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]

**If the PCs return with the halo:**

[[Iris Major]] takes the reliquary case. She opens it. The halo's light floods the back room of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]. For one moment, the dingy, gold-encrusted walls of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s operation are illuminated by pure divine radiance. Every scratch, every stain, every smudge of corruption is visible. It's the most unflattering light [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] has ever been in.

[[Iris Major]] closes the case. She looks at the walls.

> *"...The asset is secured."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] enters. He stops.

> *"You got it?"*

He opens the case. The halo's light hits him. For one instant — just one — the PCs see him clearly in that divine light. He's beautiful and terrible: every golden chain, every enchanted ring, every bespoke accessory illuminated alongside every alien angle, every tooth, every inhuman surface of a creature that was never meant to exist in proximity to holiness.

The halo's confession effect activates. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s jaw twitches. His eyestalks ripple. Something inside him pushes against the compulsion and *wins* — Will save versus DC 18, and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] has a +15 Will save. He beats it. But the PCs can see the effort. They can see him *almost* say something.

> *"...It's perfect. It's exactly what I need."*

He closes the case.

> *"[[EyeQ]]. Take this. Carefully. I want the divine override protocols integrated by the end of the week."*

[[EyeQ]] hovers forward. As they take the case, the halo's light leaks through the seams and briefly illuminates [[EyeQ]]'s surface — and the PCs notice something new. [[EyeQ]]'s shell has faint lines etched into it that weren't there before. Circuit-like patterns. Geometric. Spreading. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s architecture is being *installed* — not just in [[Offledger]], but in [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]] themselves.

[[EyeQ]] leaves without comment.

**If PCs ask what "divine override protocols" means:**
> *"It means I'm upgrading from local to universal. Don't worry about it."*

**If PCs ask about the halo's effect on [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — what was he about to confess:**
> *"Nothing. The thing's defective. Made me sneeze. Allergic to piety."*

He's lying. The confession the halo almost pulled from him would have been devastating — not a mundane embarrassment, but something real. Something about what the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] will actually cost. Or something about how he's afraid it won't work. Or something about [[Grindle Gruk'tak|Grindle]]. The DM should decide what [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] almost said and file it away. It will matter later.

**If PCs mention [[Seraphiel]]'s speech, or that she offered to let them return it:**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is quiet for a moment. Then:

> *"She offered you a way out. And you said no. You came back here. To me. With the goods."*

> *"That tells me everything. Either you loyal... or you scared. Either way, I fuck with that."*

He says it without cruelty. Almost with respect. Almost.

**If the PCs returned empty-handed (failure path):**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] stares at them for a long, terrible moment.

> *"You went to heaven. You were RIGHT THERE. And you gave it back because... what? A angel asked nice?"*

His central eye narrows.

> *"I don't want soldiers who break under niceness. Niceness ain't even a real force. It's a VIBE. You let a vibe beat you."*

He doesn't punish them immediately. That's worse. He leaves. [[Iris Major]] informs the PCs that their next tribute payment has been doubled. And that they will be returning to Celestia — with [[EyeRate]] as escort. The halo will be obtained. The PCs just lost their chance to do it without collateral damage.

### DM Notes
- **The divine light illuminating [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] is the visual thesis of this adventure.** Heaven's light in a criminal's den. Everything ugly made visible. The shop looks *wrong* under that glow. Let the players sit with that image
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] almost confessing is the adventure's most important narrative beat.** The halo nearly broke him. The most powerful, charismatic, unflappable NPC in the campaign was one failed Will save from revealing something that could unravel everything. The PCs should notice how hard he fought it. That tells them the halo isn't just powerful — it's powerful enough to threaten *[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]*. What is he hiding?
- **"Divine override protocols"** — combined with "persistence framework" (quest 3), "debt-indexing protocols" (quest 4), and the circuit patterns appearing on [[EyeQ]], the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s architecture is becoming visible to attentive players. The system is: undeath bypass + debt indexing + divine override. Whatever [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s building, it controls death, money, AND the gods. The picture is almost complete
- **[[EyeQ]]'s shell patterns are new.** The Crown isn't just being assembled in a room somewhere — it's being distributed across [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s entire network. [[The Eyecons]] ARE part of the machine. This has implications for the endgame: when the Crown collapses, [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]] collapse too
- **If the PCs failed and returned empty-handed:** This is a GREAT campaign outcome. The PCs made a moral choice. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s threat to send [[EyeRate]] creates genuine dread — the PCs know what [[EyeRate]] is. Sending him to Mount Celestia is like sending a wrecking ball to a nursery. The PCs have to live with knowing that their mercy created a worse outcome for Celestia

## Appendix: Scaling Notes

**For weaker parties (level 5-6, 3 players):**
- [[Seraphiel]] is replaced by a Planetar (CR 16) — still overwhelming, but the emotional impact is equally valid. The halo's DCs drop by 2
- [[Valanthir]] is replaced by a Hound Archon (CR 4) — less powerful but equally loyal and equally heartbroken
- The confession save DC drops to 15
- The theft window is 12 rounds instead of 8
- Celestial Spot bonuses are reduced to +8 to +18

**For stronger parties (level 7-8, 5+ players):**
- [[Seraphiel]] is present AND a second Solar observes the ritual from the Inner Sanctum. Two sets of *true seeing* eyes
- The confession save DC increases to 20 and occurs every 5 minutes instead of 10
- [[Valanthir]] is accompanied by two Hound Archons who DO pursue the PCs down the staircase
- **[[Agent Quill]]'s staircase encounter is more aggressive.** Instead of planting a seed and leaving, Quill directly asks the PCs to share intelligence: *"I need to know what the artifacts do when he puts them together. Anything you've seen. Anything you've heard."* He produces a thin dossier — a summary of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s known artifact acquisitions, with four of the five components documented and annotated. He offers it freely. *"Take it. Read it. When you see how the pieces fit, you'll understand why we're scared."* The dossier is dangerous to carry — if [[Snitch Ray]] or [[Iris Major]] finds it, the consequences are immediate. But the intelligence it contains is the most complete external picture of the Crown Infinite the PCs have ever seen

**If the PCs returned the halo (failure consequence quest):**
- [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends [[EyeRate]] to Mount Celestia three weeks later. [[EyeRate]] does not attend festivals. [[EyeRate]] does not wear pilgrim robes. [[EyeRate]] takes the halo by force. The PCs hear about it secondhand — [[Valanthir]] was injured defending the Solarium. [[Seraphiel]] surrendered the halo to prevent further violence. The PCs' mercy led to a worse outcome
- Alternatively: [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] gives the PCs ONE more chance, with [[EyeRate]] as "motivation." The PCs must return to Celestia and complete the theft, with [[EyeRate]] hovering behind them radiating menace. This version preserves player agency but adds the horror of bringing a volatile enforcer to the gentlest place in existence

## Appendix: [[Halo of the Dawn's First Judgement]] — Ongoing Campaign Use

- **Between adventures:** The halo is integrated into the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s framework. Its effects bleed into [[Offledger]]. The PCs may notice that [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s speeches become harder to doubt — not because he's more persuasive, but because the air around him now carries a faint echo of the zone of truth, *inverted*. Lies spoken near the Crown's assembly point feel MORE true, not less. The divine light capacitor is being used to create a "zone of mandatory belief." [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] isn't stealing truth — he's manufacturing it
- **Environmental changes in [[Offledger]]:** After the halo's integration, certain areas of [[Offledger]] glow faintly in the dark. Not the warm gold of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s decor — a cold, white radiance. Divine energy leaking through the seams. Customers report feeling "judged" near the back of the shop. Petty criminals refuse to enter. [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|The Coinspiratorium]] is developing an accidental holiness, and it's repelling the wrong clientele
- **The sacred blessing:** The +1 sacred bonus to saves from the festival blessing is a constant, quiet reminder. It fades after 7 days (or 30 if the PCs returned the halo). But while it lasts, every time a PC makes a save, they're reminded: Celestia blessed them. The angels loved them. And they stole the halo anyway
- **[[Seraphiel]] as distant presence:** If the PCs kept the halo, [[Seraphiel]] does not pursue them — but she watches. Once, between adventures 7 and 8, a PC has a dream: they're standing on the silver beach. [[Seraphiel]] is there. She says nothing. She just looks at them with those golden eyes. Then she turns and walks into the ocean. The dream ends. It's not a sending or a spell — it's just a dream. Probably. The PC wakes up with the feeling that someone, somewhere, hasn't given up on them
