# The Instrument of Contested Debt
*The gavel of the multiverse.*

---

## Description
A scroll case made of bone and tarnished silver, sealed with a wax stamp shaped like a closed fist. The case is old — not worn, but *aged*, the way stone ages. It doesn't decay; it accrues. Every year it exists, it becomes more itself.

Inside: a single sheet of legal vellum bearing a contract so ancient that the ink has fused with the material — not written on the page but part of it, like veins in marble. The text is in a dead language that predates [[Offledger]]'s founding. It smells like old coins and regret.

The Instrument is not a debt. It is the rules by which debts are settled.

## Properties
- **Type:** Meta-contract / adjudication framework
- **Rarity:** Artifact (unique)
- **Price:** Cannot be priced — it is the system by which prices are determined
- **Aura:** Faint transmutation and divination (nearly undetectable; *detect magic* reads it as "bureaucratic")
- **Caster Level:** N/A — the Instrument predates the distinction between arcane and divine
- **Weight:** 2 lbs.

## Mechanical Effects

### Custodial Binding
The Instrument binds to its custodian's legal personhood. While the custodian lives and is recognized under debt law, the Instrument cannot be transferred by any means — sale, theft, gift, teleportation, or divine intervention. It simply refuses. Attempts to forcibly move it while bound cause the thief's hands to pass through it as if it weren't there.

### Contestation State
Upon the custodian's death, the Instrument enters contested ownership for 30 days (or 3 hours if death occurs during the custodial renewal anniversary). During this period:
- Any entity with a legitimate debt-claim against the deceased or the Instrument may assert their claim
- Claims are ranked by age, depth, and contractual proximity
- The Instrument vibrates in the presence of the strongest claimant
- The Instrument goes still in the presence of pretenders

### Adjudication Field (30-ft. radius)
While in contested state, the Instrument emanates a field of contractual honesty:
- Creatures within 30 feet cannot misrepresent debts, obligations, or financial claims
- Lies about money cause the speaker's voice to fade to silence
- This is not *zone of truth* — lies about non-financial matters are unaffected
- No save. No spell resistance. *Antimagic field* does not suppress it
- Duration: active only during contestation

### Debt Jurisdiction (when installed in [[The Device|Crown Infinite]])
Grants the Crown authority to adjudicate, enforce, and settle debts across all planes. Transforms Law II (Debt Becomes Destiny) from a theoretical principle into an enforceable system.

## Fine Print
The Instrument does not create debts. It does not forgive them. It settles them — fairly, impartially, and permanently. The Instrument has no opinion about justice. It has opinions about *accuracy*.

In Veezy's hands, this impartiality becomes a weapon. The Crown doesn't need the Instrument to be biased. It needs the Instrument to be *thorough*.

## Source
Custodianship held by [[Aldenmere Voss]] for thirty years prior to his death at the Recession. Acquired by the Coinspiratorium through legitimate contestation claim. See [[11 - The Debt Collector's Wake]].

## [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose
Contractual jurisdiction — the gavel. Without it, the Crown can create universal debt but cannot enforce it. With it, every contract in the multiverse becomes subject to adjudication. Every debt becomes collectible. Every promise becomes binding.

The Crown needs teeth. The Instrument is teeth.

## Notes
- The Instrument should feel *heavy* in a way that isn't physical. Carrying it makes the PCs aware of every obligation they've ever made — promises, contracts, handshake agreements, childhood oaths. It doesn't enforce them. It just... knows
- When installed, Offledger's economy shifts noticeably: contracts self-enforce, debts activate, the Coinspiratorium's own transactions become frictionless. The PCs' debt to Veezy feels heavier
- Complements [[Veezy's IOU]] thematically: the IOU is a single forgotten debt that can destroy the Crown. The Instrument is the system that makes all debts inescapable. They are the same theme viewed from opposite ends
