# "The Underdark Art Acquisition Program"

> *"It's couture, it's cursed, and it's crocheted by a goddess. I need that energy."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs into [[Menzoberranzan]] — the drow capital, a theocratic slave-city in the deepest Underdark — to steal a half-finished divine tapestry from the Temple of Lolth. There is no masquerade to hide behind. There is no charming vampire lord to negotiate with. There are drow, spiders, and a priestess who interprets the PCs' presence as an act of holy war.

This is the adventure where the PCs realize these contracts aren't jokes.

**Tone arc:** Comedy (briefing) → Claustrophobic dread (descent) → Discomfort (the city) → Tension (temple infiltration) → Chaos (the duel) → Desperation (escape) → Quiet unease (debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Stealth, survival, a unique ritual-combat hybrid, a chase
**Combat:** Moderate-to-heavy (temple guards, the Weaving Duel, pursuit encounters)

---

## Target Item
**[[Lolth's Unfinished Tapestry]]** — A six-foot panel of divine silk, half-woven, stretched on a frame of polished spider-bone. The completed portion depicts events from the drow mythic past — the Descent, the founding of [[Menzoberranzan]], the Spider Queen's ascension. The unfinished portion writhes. Threads move on their own, weaving and unweaving, as though the tapestry is dreaming about what comes next.

It is not an object. It is an ongoing act of divine creation, interrupted.

**Properties:**
- The unfinished threads constantly reweave, showing visions of **possible futures** — usually futures where the viewer fails, suffers, or dies. These aren't prophecy; they're Lolth's editorial commentary. The tapestry has *opinions*
- **Touching the tapestry** with bare skin deals **1 Con damage** (no save) and leaves a faint, web-like scar on the hand that lasts 1d4 days. Gloves or cloth wrapping prevent this
- If held for more than 10 minutes continuously, the tapestry begins weaving a **portrait of the holder** into its unfinished section — incorporating them into Lolth's narrative. This is metaphysically bad and should be avoided. The portrait unravels if the tapestry is put down
- The tapestry weighs 15lbs (frame included) and hums faintly, a sound like many small legs moving on silk
- It radiates strong divination and conjuration magic. Anything with detect magic knows it's there from 120 feet

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Divine weave interface — connects the Crown to the fabric of fate and prophecy. With this component, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] can insert himself into destiny's pattern, ensuring the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] isn't just powerful but *inevitable* — woven into the narrative structure of reality itself.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
Same back room. [[Iris Major]] has added a second folding table covered in maps of tunnel systems. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is present but slightly less performative than the Ravenloft briefing — he's floating lower, his central eye is fixed on the maps, and he doesn't crack a joke for the first thirty seconds. For [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], that's the equivalent of arriving in funeral attire.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"I need a tapestry. It's in [[Menzoberranzan]]."*

He lets that hang.

> *"Before you start — yes, I know. Drow city. Spider goddess temple. Kill-on-sight for surface types. I know all that. That's why I'm sending professionals."*

Beat.

> *"That's you, by the way. You're the professionals. Act like it."*

He describes the tapestry in aesthetic terms — *"It's divine silk, hand-woven by priestesses of the Spider Queen. Unfinished. Still growing. It's basically a living couture piece and I NEED it for the collection."*

**If PCs ask why this specific tapestry:**
> *"Because it's one of a kind. Because it's divine-grade craftsmanship. Because I fuckin' said so. Pick whichever reason makes you walk faster."*

**If PCs protest the danger level:**
> *"First contract was a vampire's castle and y'all came back with wine. You're ready for this. Probably."*

**If PCs ask what the tapestry does:**
> *"It weaves. It's a tapestry. What you want it to do, taxes?"*

### Logistics (delivered by [[Iris Major]])
[[Iris Major]] is more detailed than usual — a sign that even she considers this one risky:

- **Portal:** Deposits the PCs in a natural cavern system approximately 6 hours' travel from [[Menzoberranzan]]. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] does not have portal access directly into the city — even his reach has limits in the Underdark
- **Extraction:** Same cavern. The return portal activates 48 hours after departure and stays open for 30 minutes. This is a two-day job, minimum
- **Darkvision:** If any PC lacks darkvision, [[Iris Major]] provides *goggles of minute seeing* modified for darkvision (60 ft., 48-hour duration, look like oversized jeweler's lenses — not subtle)
- **Contact:** A drow smuggler named **[[Szordrin Hune]]** operates in [[Menzoberranzan]]'s Bazaar district. He owes [[The Gold Standard]] a significant debt and has been "activated" for this operation. A bronze token shaped like an eye ([[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s sigil) will identify the PCs to him. [[Iris Major]]'s assessment: *"[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] is reliable in the way that a cornered animal is reliable. He will help you because the alternative is worse."*
- **Cover story:** [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] will handle cover within the city. The PCs should follow his lead and not improvise unless absolutely necessary
- **Contingency:** None. *"If you are captured by drow, the organization will not mount a rescue. This is stated in Article IX, Section 4: 'Operational losses in high-risk jurisdictions are the Debtor's responsibility.' I recommend not being captured."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Parting Words

> *"One more thing. The tapestry's a little... temperamental. Don't look at what it's showing you. Don't touch it without gloves. And definitely don't let it get your name."*

Pause.

> *"Actually, now that I say that out loud, maybe wear name tags that say something else. Something fake. Something sexy."*

He's half-joking. The advice about gloves is real.

### DM Notes
- This briefing should feel different from Ravenloft. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s slightly less performative energy signals that even he takes this one seriously — which means the PCs should too
- The "no rescue" clause from [[Iris Major]] is cold water. Let it land. The PCs are on their own in a way they weren't in [[Barovia]]
- If the PCs push back hard enough to refuse the contract, [[EyeRate]] drifts into the doorway and stares at them. Just stares. The contract is not optional
- The 48-hour window and 6-hour journey mean the PCs need to plan their time: ~6 hours in, ~6 hours out, leaves 36 hours to infiltrate, steal, and escape. In practice this means one sleep cycle in the Underdark. They should plan for that

## Scene 2: The Descent

### The Tunnels
The portal drops the PCs into a lightless cavern system. The air is cool, damp, and utterly still. Sound behaves differently down here — footsteps carry farther, voices echo wrong, and silence has texture.

**Key sensory details:**
- Total darkness for PCs without darkvision. Even with it, the tunnels feel *enclosed* — low ceilings, narrow passages, the constant sense of millions of tons of rock above
- Bioluminescent fungi provide occasional sickly green-blue light in larger caverns
- The tunnels are not empty: centipedes the size of dogs crawl along walls, blind cave fish drift in underground streams, and something large occasionally shifts in the dark ahead — then moves away
- The temperature drops steadily. By the time they're three hours in, breath mists (for living creatures)

### Travel Encounters (choose 1-2)

**The Patrol — Avoidable**
A drow scouting party (4 drow warriors + 1 drow sergeant, see Appendix) on standard perimeter patrol, 2 hours from [[Menzoberranzan]]. They're bored, alert enough to be dangerous, and traveling with two riding lizards.

- **Detection:** The patrol carries no light but the riding lizards' claws click on stone. Listen DC 14 to hear them 200 ft. away
- **Avoidance:** Hide DC 15 (per PC) to press into a side passage and let them pass. The tunnels offer plenty of cover — stalactite clusters, fungal groves, narrow fissures
- **If detected:** The drow challenge in Undercommon. PCs who don't speak Undercommon are immediately hostile targets. PCs who do speak it get one chance to bluff: "slaves on delivery for House Hune" (Bluff DC 16, using [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s house name). Failure means combat
- **Combat:** This fight is winnable but *loud*. If it lasts more than 5 rounds, a second patrol is alerted and arrives in 10 minutes. The PCs should be motivated to win fast or run

**The Clinging Dark — Combat**
In a larger cavern where the bioluminescent fungi grows thick, the ceiling rises to 30 feet — and three **darkmantles** (MM p.38) cling to the stalactites above, perfectly mimicking the stone formations. They've been feeding on the cave fauna for years. They are very hungry and very patient.

**Darkmantles (x3)** — MM p.38, CR 1 each
- Small Magical Beast | HP 6 each | AC 17 (+1 size, +6 natural) | Speed 20 ft., fly 30 ft. (poor)
- **Slam +5 melee** (1d4+4)
- **Improved Grab (Ex):** On a hit, automatic grapple (+5). On a successful grapple, the darkmantle wraps around the victim's head, blinding them and dealing 1d4+4 damage each round
- **Darkness (Su):** 1/day, 20-ft. radius *darkness* for 10 rounds. All three fire simultaneously — the cavern goes absolutely black. Darkvision does not penetrate magical darkness
- Hide +10 in stone environments. Spot DC 20 to notice them before they drop
- Blindsight 90 ft.

**Tactics:** All three drop *darkness* simultaneously, then dive. In pitch blackness, they target whoever's carrying a light source first (their blindsight gives them perfect accuracy; the PCs are fighting blind). Each darkmantle wraps one target's head. PCs must grapple (opposed check) or attack the darkmantle on their own head at -4 (firing into melee + blinded).

**Why this encounter matters:**
- It establishes the Underdark's baseline danger — this wasn't a drow ambush or a temple guardian. This was the TUNNEL. The things that live between the places that are actually dangerous
- Fighting while blinded primes the PCs for [[Menzoberranzan]], where most of the population can see in the dark and they can't
- The encounter is fast and scary but not lethal (18 total HP across all three). A level 3-4 party handles it in 2-3 rounds if they stay calm. If they panic in the dark, it takes longer. The Underdark rewards composure

**The Chasm — Navigation**
A 60-foot chasm splits the main tunnel. A natural bridge of rock spans it — 3 feet wide, no railing, slick with moisture. Below is blackness that swallows sound.

- **Crossing:** Balance DC 12 (DC 15 if hurrying). Failure by 5+ means a fall — 60 feet down (6d6 damage) to a ledge, not the true bottom. Rope and pitons reduce DC to 8
- **The shortcut:** A PC with Climb DC 18 can find a hand-and-foot route along the chasm wall that's actually faster and more stable. Reward creative traversal
- **Atmosphere:** Something moves at the bottom of the chasm. The PCs hear it — heavy, wet, dragging. It doesn't come up. This time

### Arrival
After roughly 6 hours, the tunnels open onto a vast cavern shelf overlooking **[[Menzoberranzan]]**.

**The view:**
The city fills a cavern so enormous the ceiling disappears into darkness. Towers of carved obsidian and living rock rise in clusters connected by bridges of spider-silk and stone. Soft light emanates from enchanted fungi, faerie fire, and the cold glow of innumerable magical wards. Everything is purple, black, and silver. It is alien and staggeringly beautiful.

And it is full of creatures that will enslave the PCs on sight.

The meeting point with [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] is a dead-drop alcove on the cavern shelf, marked with a scratched eye symbol. He'll be there at "first bell" — a time measurement the PCs don't understand (roughly 2 hours from their arrival). They wait.

### DM Notes
- The Descent is a pressure builder. No comedy here. The Underdark should feel hostile, alien, and *big* in a way that makes the PCs feel small
- The patrol encounter calibrates expectations. If the PCs fight drow and find them tough, they'll understand why sneaking through an entire city of them matters
- Don't rush to [[Menzoberranzan]]. Let the journey breathe. The longer the PCs spend in darkness, the more the city feels like arriving at a destination they can't leave
- If the PCs make no noise and avoid the patrol cleanly, reward that — [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] is impressed. *"You're quieter than most surface-dwellers. Most of them die singing."*

## Scene 3: [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] and the City

### The Contact
**[[Szordrin Hune]]** is a male drow in his middle years (by elven standards — he looks 30, he's 200). Lean, sharp-featured, with white hair cropped short and a thin scar across his throat from a punishment he doesn't discuss. He dresses in practical dark leathers, not the ornate robes of the priestess caste, and carries himself like a man who's been looking over his shoulder for so long it's become posture.

He arrives at the dead-drop on time. He examines the eye token without touching it, then looks the PCs over with visible disdain.

> *"So. Vizzok's errand-runners. I expected... more."*

**[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s deal with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]:** He borrowed capital from [[The Gold Standard]] to fund a smuggling operation that went wrong. He now owes more than he can repay and works off the debt through "favors." This mission clears a significant portion of his balance. He is not doing this willingly. He is doing this because [[EyeRate]] told him what happens to debtors who refuse.

**Personality:** Bitter, competent, darkly funny. He despises surface-dwellers on principle but respects anyone who can keep their mouth shut and follow instructions. He especially despises [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — *"Your boss is a floating ego with a jewelry addiction. But he pays on time and kills people who don't. I respect the system, not the man."*

**What [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] knows:**
- The Temple of Lolth's general layout (he's delivered "special merchandise" to the servant entrance before)
- The temple's security schedule: priestess rotations change at each bell (every 4 hours). The gap between rotations is approximately 15 minutes
- The Weaving Chamber is in the temple's inner sanctum, third level, accessible through the Hall of Silk
- The tapestry is attended by three acolyte-priestesses at all times, supervised by **[[Priestess Zhael'ra]]** on a rotating basis
- The temple is guarded by drow warriors, phase spiders, and at least one drider

**What [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] doesn't know:**
- The tapestry's magical properties (he's never seen it up close)
- The temple's inner ward layout
- What [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] wants with it

### Getting Into the City
[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] presents the options with the enthusiasm of a man choosing between poisons:

**Option A: The Slave Caravan ([[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s recommendation)**
[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] has a contact — a minor drow merchant — who runs a surface-slave trade. The PCs enter the city disguised as newly captured slaves, collared with fake enchanted manacles (they're real manacles but the lock is rigged). [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] walks them in as "merchandise for House Hune."

- **Advantage:** Unquestioned. Nobody looks twice at slaves in [[Menzoberranzan]]. The PCs walk right through the gates
- **Disadvantage:** The PCs must submit to being collared, chained, and led through the city in public. They cannot carry visible weapons or wear armor. Their gear is transported separately in [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s "cargo"
- **The discomfort is the point.** The PCs — who are already indentured to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — now must *physically* perform bondage to complete his mission. The thematic resonance should make the players squirm
- PCs who refuse this option: [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] shrugs. *"Your dignity or your life. I know which one I chose."*

**Option B: The Fungal Tunnels**
A network of maintenance passages used by slaves to transport fungal harvests from the city's farms. [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] knows an entrance that's lightly guarded.

- **Advantage:** PCs keep their gear. Less psychologically awful
- **Disadvantage:** Tighter tunnels, more chances for detection. Requires Move Silently DC 14 at three checkpoints (slave overseers). One failure means a combat encounter with 2 drow warriors in tight quarters
- The tunnels exit into the Bazaar district near [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s stall

**Option C: The PCs propose something creative.**
[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] is skeptical but will assess any plan on its merits. *"If it's stupid and it works, it's still stupid. But you're alive."*

### The Bazaar
However the PCs enter, they end up in [[Menzoberranzan]]'s Bazaar — the city's commercial district, where surface goods are traded, slaves are sold, and information flows like poison.

**Key details:**
- The Bazaar is the closest thing to "neutral ground" in the city. Violence is bad for business, so merchant houses enforce a fragile peace
- Surface-dwellers are present — as slaves, as curiosities, occasionally as tolerated traders (rare). The PCs aren't automatically attacked here, but they draw attention
- [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s stall is a covered alcove selling "exotic surface goods" — mostly junk he marks up 300%. It's a front for his smuggling and a staging area for the PCs
- PCs can acquire supplies here: drow poison (injury, Fort DC 13 or unconscious 1 minute, 50 gp per dose), darkvision-enhancing drops (extend darkvision to 120 ft. for 4 hours, 30 gp), spider-silk rope (50 ft., half weight of normal rope, 20 gp)

**Bazaar encounters (optional, choose 0-2 to flesh out the city):**

**The Auctioneer:** A drow merchant is publicly auctioning a surface elf — alive, terrified, and calling for help in Common. The PCs can hear every word. Intervening would blow their cover catastrophically. Not intervening costs nothing except whatever they can live with.

**The Curiosity Seeker:** A young drow noble (female, armed, bored) takes an interest in the PCs. She's never seen their particular race up close and wants to "examine" them — like a child with a new insect. [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] intervenes smoothly (*"My merchandise, honored lady, not yet catalogued"*), but if the PCs have already blown their cover, this becomes a tense negotiation.

**The Warning:** An old blind drow beggar (rarity — drow don't tolerate weakness) grabs a PC's sleeve and whispers in broken Common: *"The spider is always watching. The web is always growing. Don't touch the silk."* He then pretends it never happened. This is either genuine prophecy, madness, or both.

### DM Notes
- [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] is a mirror of the PCs' own situation — a competent person trapped by debt to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], doing terrible things to survive. If the PCs treat him well, he becomes a genuinely useful ally. If they treat him like a servant, he does the minimum required
- The slave disguise option should be offered first and presented as the smart choice. The discomfort is intentional — this campaign is about debt as bondage, and this is the scene where the metaphor becomes literal. Don't force it, but don't soften it either. If the players choose it, let the silence at the table do the work
- The Bazaar encounters are optional but powerful. The auctioneer scene in particular forces the PCs to confront the reality of the world they're operating in — and the fact that they're complicit by being here
- [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] should feel like a real person, not a plot device. He has opinions about the PCs, about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], about his own situation. Let him talk. Players will like him or hate him, and both are useful

## Scene 4: The Temple of Lolth

### The Approach
The Temple of Lolth is visible from anywhere in the city — a massive structure carved from a natural stalactite formation, shaped to resemble a spider descending from the cavern ceiling. Eight "legs" of carved stone and hardened webbing form bridges to the surrounding district. The temple glows faintly with purple-black light that seems to absorb illumination rather than produce it.

**Security overview (from [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s intelligence):**
- **Outer perimeter:** Drow warrior patrols (4-6 soldiers per patrol, rotating every 2 hours)
- **Inner temple:** Priestess-acolytes, phase spiders, magical wards (alarm, glyph of warding at key doors)
- **Inner sanctum (including the Weaving Chamber):** [[Priestess Zhael'ra]]'s domain. Higher-level guards, at least one drider, and the tapestry itself — which may have its own defenses
- **The rotation gap:** 15 minutes between shifts at each bell. This is the window

### Entry Routes

**Route A: The Servant Entrance ([[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s Plan)**
[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] has a standing delivery arrangement — he provides "exotic materials" (rare dyes, surface fabrics) to the temple's weavers. He can get one delivery in during the rotation gap.

- The PCs are hidden inside oversized delivery crates (uncomfortable but effective). [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] wheels the cart to the servant entrance, the slave workers unload it into a storage room, and the PCs emerge inside the temple
- **Risk:** Low on entry. The storage room is checked by a slave overseer once per hour. The PCs have approximately 45 minutes before the next check
- **Limitation:** Weapons and armor must fit in the crates. Heavy armor won't fit. Large weapons are borderline (DM discretion)

**Route B: The Web Shafts**
Ventilation shafts running through the temple's structure, threaded with old spider webbing. Accessible from the building's exterior via a Climb check.

- Climb DC 16 to ascend the exterior to a shaft opening on the second level
- Navigate the shafts: Escape Artist DC 14 to squeeze through tight sections (Medium creatures; Small creatures have no issue). The webbing is sticky but old — Strength DC 12 to push through without getting tangled
- **Risk:** A phase spider nests in one of the shafts (see Combat Encounters). It attacks anyone who enters its territory
- **Advantage:** Deposits PCs on the third level, very close to the Weaving Chamber

**Route C: The Forgotten Passage**
[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] has heard rumors of a passage beneath the temple — an old escape route from a previous era, sealed after a failed coup. If the PCs investigate (Gather Information DC 18 in the Bazaar, or Knowledge [dungeoneering] DC 16), they find the entrance: a cracked section of sewer tunnel behind a collapsed wall.

- The passage is unstable. Two sections require careful movement (Move Silently DC 12 to avoid triggering a minor cave-in, which alerts the temple)
- It emerges in a disused sub-basement beneath the inner sanctum. A locked trapdoor (Open Lock DC 18) opens into a storage closet on the third level
- **Risk:** Medium. The passage is unguarded but structurally dangerous
- **Advantage:** Completely bypasses all outer security

### Temple Interior — Key Areas

**Third Level: Hall of Silk**
A long corridor with walls draped in lesser tapestries — each depicting scenes from Lolth's mythology. The silk is beautiful and faintly warm, as though alive. Walking through feels like moving through a web.

- Two acolyte-priestesses patrol this hall on a 20-minute rotation. They carry no weapons but have access to *hold person* (DC 14), *spiritual weapon*, and *silence*
- The tapestries themselves function as an **alarm system**: if a creature passes without bearing Lolth's holy symbol, the tapestries rustle audibly. Not a magical alarm — a physical, intentional sound, like a congregation clearing its throat
- **Bypass:** A stolen holy symbol (available from [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] for 40 gp — *"Don't ask where I got it"*), or Move Silently DC 16 to pass during the patrol gap without triggering the tapestries (they respond to vibration, not magic)

**Third Level: The Antechamber**
A round room with a domed ceiling painted with spider constellations. This is the waiting room for audiences with [[Priestess Zhael'ra]]. Currently empty between rotation bells.

- One drider stands guard here at all times — **[[Kael'voth]]**, a former warrior of House Despana, transformed as punishment for ambition. He is bitter, watchful, and follows orders with mechanical precision
- [[Kael'voth]] can be avoided (Hide DC 18 — the room is open with limited cover) or engaged
- **Engaging [[Kael'voth]]:** He won't fight the PCs if given a reason not to. He hates his existence and specifically hates [[Priestess Zhael'ra]], who ordered his transformation. If the PCs offer to "cause problems for Zhael'ra," he will let them pass and look the other way. He doesn't need much convincing — Diplomacy DC 14, or just the implied promise that the priestess's day is about to get worse
- **If [[Kael'voth]] fights:** CR 7 (see Appendix). This is a hard encounter for level 3-4 PCs. Avoid if possible
- [[Kael'voth]] does not raise alarm either way. He doesn't care enough

### DM Notes
- The temple should feel sacred and *wrong* — a place of genuine divine power perverted by cruelty. The tapestries on the walls aren't decoration; they're offerings. The spiders aren't pests; they're congregation members
- Entry route choice matters for pacing. Route A is safest but slowest (PCs start on the first level and must navigate up). Route B is fastest but has a combat encounter. Route C rewards investigation and skill
- [[Kael'voth]] is a roleplaying opportunity disguised as a combat encounter. Rewarding the PCs for talking instead of fighting is on-brand for this campaign — debts, deals, and leveraging other people's resentment are the tools of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s world
- If the PCs have been quiet and careful up to this point, let them feel good about it. They've earned the Weaving Chamber

## Scene 5: The Weaving Chamber

### The Room
The heart of the temple's artistic and prophetic operations. A circular chamber, 40 feet across, with a domed ceiling covered in living web that pulses faintly with each heartbeat of the city's collective devotion. The floor is smooth obsidian, mirror-polished, reflecting the web above.

At the center: **[[Lolth's Unfinished Tapestry]]**, stretched on its bone frame, six feet tall, the completed half gorgeous and terrible in the dim light. The unfinished half writhes constantly — threads weaving and unweaving in patterns that almost resolve into images before dissolving.

Three **acolyte-priestesses** attend the tapestry, weaving new threads with silver needles. They chant softly in Abyssal. They are focused on their work and not expecting intruders.

**[[Priestess Zhael'ra]]** is not currently present — she visits the chamber at each bell. The PCs have arrived during the gap. If they act quickly, they have approximately **10 minutes** before she arrives.

### Taking the Tapestry (The Fast Way)
If the PCs act immediately:
- Neutralize or bypass the three acolytes (CR 2 each — *hold person* DC 14, *spiritual weapon*; 18 HP each). Surprise round is available if the PCs are stealthy
- Remove the tapestry from its frame: DC 12 Strength check to snap the bone mounting pegs, or 1 minute of careful work to unbind it
- **The tapestry reacts to being removed:** It flares with purple-black light, sends a pulse of divination magic outward (detectable by any spellcaster in the temple within 200 feet), and begins weaving a new image — the PCs, in this room, failing. Dying. The threads show them cut down by drow blades, trapped in webs, strangled by silk
- This vision is demoralizing but not mechanically debilitating. **Will save DC 14** or be shaken for 1d4 rounds (seeing your own death is unsettling)
- If the PCs grab the tapestry and run in under 10 minutes, they skip directly to **Scene 6: The Escape**. Zhael'ra is furious but behind them. This is the clean version — reward players who are efficient

### The Weaving Duel (The Dramatic Way)
If the PCs are still in the chamber when [[Priestess Zhael'ra]] arrives — either because they took too long, triggered an alarm, or deliberately waited — the encounter escalates into the adventure's signature set piece.

**[[Priestess Zhael'ra]]** enters the Weaving Chamber and stops. She is tall, imperious, draped in robs of spider-silk so fine they seem woven from shadow. Her eyes are cold and intelligent. She looks at the PCs with the expression of a woman who has found rats in her cathedral.

> *"Thieves. In the Weaving Chamber."*

She does not call for guards. She does not attack. She studies them.

> *"Who sent you?"*

This question matters. Her reaction depends on the answer:

**If the PCs name [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] (or she identifies them through the tapestry):**
Zhael'ra doesn't know [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] personally, but the tapestry *reacts* to his name. The unfinished threads lurch, weaving frantically, and for three seconds the tapestry shows a vision that fills the chamber: **a crown of orbiting artifacts, golden light rewriting the dark, and a single enormous eye at the center of everything.** The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], incomplete but recognizable.

Zhael'ra sees this. Her expression shifts from contempt to something colder — recognition of a genuine threat.

> *"Your master presumes to weave his own fate. Lolth weaves ALL fates."*

She interprets the PCs' mission as a **divine challenge** — an affront to Lolth's sovereignty over destiny. This triggers the Weaving Duel.

**If the PCs lie or say nothing:**
Zhael'ra touches the tapestry. It shows her who they are — their faces, [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]], [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s golden eye. She gets the same vision. Same result.

### The Weaving Duel — Mechanics

Zhael'ra invokes a rite called the **Unraveling** — a ritual combat sanctioned by Lolth's doctrine, where disputes over divine artifacts are settled through a contest of weaving and destruction. The tapestry is both the prize and the arena.

**Setup:**
- The chamber seals. Spider-silk threads extrude from the walls, floor, and ceiling, filling the room with a three-dimensional web grid (difficult terrain for non-spider creatures)
- Zhael'ra stands at the tapestry, drawing threads of divine silk from the air. The acolytes take positions at three points around the room, channeling energy
- Two **large monstrous spiders** (CR 2 each, see Appendix) descend from the ceiling as Zhael'ra's "attendants"

**The contest runs 5 rounds.** Each round has three phases:

**Phase 1: The Weaving (Skill Challenge)**
Zhael'ra weaves a pattern of silver-black silk that represents Lolth's dominion. The PCs must counter-weave — using whatever divine, arcane, or mundane skill they can muster.

*PC options each round (each PC picks one):*
- **Counter-Weave:** Spellcraft DC 15 or Knowledge (religion) DC 16 or Craft (weaving/any) DC 14 — actively disrupting Zhael'ra's pattern. Each success counts as 1 point toward the PCs' total
- **Channel Power:** Cast any spell of 1st level or higher into the tapestry. The spell is consumed but counts as 1 point (2 points if the spell is 3rd level or higher). The tapestry absorbs the magic and weaves it into a counter-pattern. Describe it — a fireball becomes a thread of flame woven into a defiant image; a cure spell becomes a thread of golden warmth
- **Cut the Threads:** Attack Zhael'ra's silk constructs (AC 14, 5 HP per strand, 3 strands per round). Destroying all 3 strands in a round counts as 1 point and disrupts her weaving
- **Invoke a Name:** Declare allegiance to a deity, patron, or power. This is a Charisma check (DC 14). Success counts as 1 point. If a PC invokes [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — *"We serve [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]], the Opulent, the Visionary, the—"* — the tapestry goes haywire, weaving gold threads into Lolth's pattern, and it counts as 2 points. But Zhael'ra becomes enraged and her next round's challenge DC increases by 2

**Phase 2: The Strikes (Combat)**
Zhael'ra's silk threads lash out, and her spider attendants attack.

- **Silk Lash:** Each PC must make a Reflex save (DC 15) or take 1d6+2 slashing damage from razor-silk threads and be entangled (Escape Artist DC 16 or Strength DC 18 to break free). Entangled PCs take -2 to skill checks in Phase 1
- The two monstrous spiders attack normally (bite +4, 1d6+1 plus poison Fort DC 12)
- The three acolytes channel: if all three are conscious, Zhael'ra gains +2 to her weaving checks. Knocking out acolytes (18 HP each, AC 14) weakens her. Each downed acolyte removes 1 from her bonus

**Phase 3: The Tapestry Speaks (Narrative)**
At the end of each round, the tapestry shows a vision. These are DM tools — use them for foreshadowing, misdirection, or emotional impact:

- **Round 1:** The PCs see themselves returning to [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]], triumphant. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] takes the tapestry without thanks. Normal. Then the vision shifts — the tapestry is being installed into something large and mechanical, threaded with golden light. A machine. The vision ends
- **Round 2:** The PCs see [[Menzoberranzan]] burning. Gold fire. The cavern ceiling cracked open. Sunlight — or something that looks like sunlight but *wrong* — pouring in. Drow screaming. This hasn't happened. This is a possible future under the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s Laws
- **Round 3:** The PCs see [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], alone, in a dark room, staring at something the vision doesn't show. He looks... uncertain. This is the first time the PCs have ever seen him look uncertain. The vision ends before they can see what he's looking at
- **Round 4:** The PCs see themselves, older, standing in a place they don't recognize. They look tired and hard. One of them is holding something that glows — the vision flickers before they can identify it. This is the endgame. Or a possible one
- **Round 5:** The tapestry shows nothing. The unfinished threads go still. For the first time since its creation, the tapestry has nothing to say about what comes next. This should be deeply unsettling

### Resolution

**PCs win (7+ points across 5 rounds):**
The tapestry rejects Zhael'ra. Her threads unravel. The silver-black silk dissolves. The tapestry folds itself off its frame — *voluntarily* — and drops into the nearest PC's arms (1 Con damage if bare-handed). Zhael'ra is staggered (stunned 1 round) and furious. The acolytes panic.

The PCs have approximately **3 rounds** to exit the chamber before Zhael'ra recovers and calls for reinforcements.

> Zhael'ra, on her knees, threads dissolving around her: *"The web remembers. Lolth REMEMBERS. Your master's golden thread will be cut."*

**PCs lose (6 or fewer points):**
Zhael'ra's weaving dominates. The silk constructs wrap the tapestry protectively and the PCs are pushed back — each takes 2d6 damage from a final silk lash. However, the tapestry does something unexpected: it shows one final vision, directly to the PCs, that Zhael'ra cannot see.

The vision: the tapestry, installed in the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], *reaching back* and unweaving Zhael'ra's victory from the timeline. As if it already belongs to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]].

Then the bone frame cracks. The tapestry falls free. In the confusion, the PCs have **1 round** to grab it and run before Zhael'ra realizes what happened.

This means **the PCs get the tapestry regardless** — but losing the duel means they take more damage, have less time, and the escape is harder (Zhael'ra recovers faster and the temple goes on full alert immediately).

### DM Notes
- The Weaving Duel is the adventure's set piece. It should feel like nothing the PCs have done before — part skill challenge, part combat, part narrative event. The tapestry's visions are the DM's chance to foreshadow the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] in vivid, visceral imagery
- The point system is forgiving by design. 7 points across 5 rounds with 4 PCs means roughly 1.5 successes per round — achievable if the PCs engage with the mechanics creatively. Spellcasters should feel useful (channeling spells counts). Martial characters should feel useful (cutting threads counts). Everyone should feel useful (invoking names counts)
- **Invoking [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is the funniest and most dangerous option.** If a PC does it with genuine bravado — shouting [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s titles in a temple of Lolth — reward it with 2 points AND make Zhael'ra's fury palpable. The tapestry's reaction to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s name is the clearest foreshadowing yet that the artifacts are connected to something enormous
- The "PCs lose but get the tapestry anyway" safeguard prevents the adventure from stalling. The tapestry *wants* to leave — not because it likes the PCs, but because it can feel the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s gravity pulling on it. It belongs to the device now, whether anyone involved knows it or not
- After the duel, do not linger. The PCs have the tapestry and the temple is waking up. Go directly to Scene 6

## Scene 6: The Escape

### The Situation
The PCs have the tapestry. The temple knows it. [[Priestess Zhael'ra]] is screaming for guards. Every phase spider, drider, and warrior in the building is converging on the Weaving Chamber. The PCs need to get out of the temple, out of the city, and into the Underdark tunnels before [[Menzoberranzan]] swallows them whole.

This is a chase, not a dungeon crawl. Keep it moving.

### Phase 1: The Temple (3-4 rounds of action)

The PCs' exit depends on their entry route:
- **Servant entrance:** Back through the storage rooms. Two drow warriors now guard the corridor (they were alerted by the divination pulse). CR 1 each, quick fight
- **Web shafts:** Accessible but now a phase spider is hunting them actively. It phases through walls to cut them off. One combat round against the spider (CR 5, see Appendix) or Tumble/Climb DC 16 to evade it in the shafts
- **Forgotten passage:** Unguarded, still the safest exit. The structural instability is worse now (the temple's magic is agitated) — Move Silently DC 14 or a minor collapse blocks the tunnel for 1 round (Strength DC 16 to clear)

**Complication (all routes):** The tapestry is radiating magic like a bonfire. Any drow with *detect magic* can track it. The PCs are dragging a homing beacon through enemy territory.
- **Mitigation:** Wrapping the tapestry in lead-lined cloth (available from [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] if they asked) suppresses the signal. Otherwise, pursuers gain +4 to tracking checks

### Phase 2: The City (narrated chase)

Once outside the temple, the PCs have two choices:

**The Streets (fast, risky):**
Sprint through [[Menzoberranzan]] toward the Bazaar. The city is now on soft alert — bells are ringing at the temple but the alarm hasn't reached all districts yet.

- 3 checks, in order:
  1. **Navigate the streets:** Knowledge (local/dungeoneering) DC 14 or Survival DC 16 or [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] guides them automatically (no check)
  2. **Avoid a patrol:** Hide DC 15 (entire party) or Bluff DC 16 (if they still have their slave disguises: *"Emergency delivery! Temple orders!"*)
  3. **The Bazaar exit:** The tunnel entrance is in [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s stall. Reaching it requires crossing the Bazaar, which is now buzzing with rumor. No check if [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] is with them — he knows every back alley. Without him: Move Silently DC 14 or Bluff DC 14

**The Beneath (slow, safer):**
Drop into the sewer system and navigate beneath the city. [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] knows the route.

- Takes twice as long but avoids all street encounters
- One hazard: a section of sewer is home to a **carrion crawler** (CR 4) that attacks anything that enters its territory
- The sewers exit into natural caverns near the city's perimeter

### Phase 3: [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s Choice

At the exit point — whether the Bazaar tunnel or the sewer opening — [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] makes a decision based on how the PCs treated him throughout the adventure.

**If the PCs treated [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] well** (respected him, didn't pull rank, acknowledged his situation):
He's waiting with their gear packed and a decoy prepared — a slave wearing PCs' discarded clothing, sent running in the opposite direction. *"You have ten minutes before they figure it out. Don't waste them. And tell [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] we're square."* He nods once and disappears into the city. He means it — he considers the debt paid.

**If the PCs treated [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] poorly** (ordered him around, reminded him of his debt, treated him like a tool):
He's waiting, but the gear is hastily packed and there's no decoy. He hands them their things and says: *"Debt's paid. Don't come back. And don't send anyone back."* He means it — he'll actively avoid [[The Gold Standard]] after this, possibly becoming a complication in future adventures.

**If the PCs were cruel to [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]** (threatened him, endangered him carelessly, treated him as expendable):
He's gone. Their gear is at the tunnel entrance in a pile, with a note in Undercommon: *"I told them you went north."* He told the drow patrol which way the PCs are actually going. The PCs have a 5-minute head start instead of 10, and a pursuit encounter in the tunnels (4 drow warriors + 1 drow priestess, CR 6 encounter).

### Phase 4: The Tunnels

Six hours back to the extraction portal. The PCs are tired, hurt, and carrying a cursed divine artifact that keeps showing them how they die.

**Pursuit (if applicable):**
If the PCs triggered a full city alert or [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] betrayed them, a drow hunting party is tracking them. Run this as a series of 3 checks at 2-hour intervals:
- **Check 1:** The trail is fresh. Survival DC 14 to obscure tracks, or Hide DC 16 at a chokepoint to let the hunters pass
- **Check 2:** The hunters are closer. Same DCs, but failure means a skirmish with the advance scout (1 drow warrior, CR 1)
- **Check 3:** The hunters are right behind them. Same DCs, but failure means full combat with the hunting party (4 drow warriors, CR 1 each)

If the PCs passed cleanly ([[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] gave them a decoy, no major alarms), the journey is tense but uneventful. Let them rest. The tapestry keeps weaving visions in the dark.

**The tapestry during travel:**
Every time a PC looks at the tapestry during the return journey, it shows something different:
- Their own death (vivid, personalized, unsettling — but a different death each time, suggesting none of them are fixed)
- [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|The Coinspiratorium]], empty, shelves bare, ledger open to a page that's solid red
- [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s central eye, enormous, filling the entire tapestry, looking directly at them
- Nothing — the threads go still, the weaving stops, and for one horrible moment the tapestry is just cloth. Then it starts again

### DM Notes
- The escape should feel breathless. Short descriptions, quick decisions, consequences that stack. Don't let the PCs rest until they're in the tunnels
- [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s loyalty check is the adventure's moral feedback loop. PCs who treated an indentured person with decency get a better outcome. PCs who exploited someone in the same situation they're in learn what that feels like from the other side. This mirrors the campaign's central theme without preaching about it
- The tapestry's travel visions are pure DM fun. Use them to foreshadow, mislead, unsettle, or give the PCs cryptic information they won't understand until later. The tapestry isn't trying to help or hinder — it's just *showing*
- If the PCs are badly hurt and low on resources, let the tunnel journey be quiet. Sometimes the scariest thing is walking for six hours in the dark with a cursed artifact and your own thoughts

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The extraction portal deposits them in [[Offledger]]. [[Iris Major]] is waiting. She does not inspect the tapestry — she stands three feet from it and reads her clipboard.

> *"Acquisition confirmed. Condition: active. Containment protocol required. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]] has been notified."*

She hands them a pair of lead-lined gloves and a silk-wrapped containment box. The tapestry goes in the box. The box goes to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]].

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction
This time, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] receives the artifact personally. He's in the back room with [[EyeQ]], who stands — floats — at a respectful distance with an instrument that looks like a jeweler's loupe crossed with an astrolabe.

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] opens the containment box and looks at the tapestry for a long time. Longer than he looked at the mirror. His expression is difficult to read — he has a lot of face, most of it eyes, and right now all of them are focused on the unfinished threads.

The tapestry weaves a vision for him. The PCs can't see what it shows. But [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] smiles — slowly, genuinely, like a man watching a building he designed come together.

> *"There it is. That's the thread count I was looking for."*

He closes the box.

**If PCs describe the danger they faced:**
> *"The Underdark is dark and underground. I'm aware. You're back. You're breathing. Sounds like it went great."*

**If PCs mention the tapestry's visions — especially the Crown-related ones:**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s smile doesn't move, but something behind it does. His central eye contracts.

> *"The tapestry shows whatever it wants. It's divine-grade silk — it's dramatic by nature. Don't read into it."*

Beat.

> *"What exactly did it show you?"*

He asks this casually. He is not casual about the answer. If the PCs describe the crown vision, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] nods slowly and says nothing for three seconds. Then:

> *"Art. It showed you art. My art. Now stop looking at my sketches before they're done."*

**If PCs mention [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] or his situation:**
> *"[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]? He still alive? Good. He's useful. Useful people stay alive. That's motivational advice, by the way — write that down."*

### Payout

| Reward | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Contract completion bonus | 350 gp (split among party) | Higher payout than Ravenloft — harder mission |
| Drow equipment (looted) | Variable | Drow arms/armor degrade in sunlight; sell fast |
| Bazaar purchases (resale) | Variable | Spider-silk rope and drow poison have markup value in [[Offledger]] |
| [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s goodwill | 0 gp | If earned: a Underdark contact for future jobs. Not nothing |
| Tapestry-related trauma | 0 gp | The visions don't stop immediately. PCs dream of threads for 1d4 nights |

**Total expected GP haul:** 400-650 gp depending on how much they looted and traded. The GP-to-danger ratio is worse than Ravenloft. This is intentional. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] doesn't pay hazard rates.

### The Handoff
[[EyeQ]] takes the containment box with visible fascination. He examines it with his instrument, mutters something the PCs can't hear, and floats away without a word.

If a PC specifically watches [[EyeQ]] leave, they notice him heading toward the **lower levels of [[Offledger]]** — a section of the settlement the PCs haven't been to. A section that's been under construction. A section that hums.

### DM Notes
- [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s *"What exactly did it show you?"* is the most important moment in this debrief. He's not asking out of curiosity — he's checking for security leaks. If the PCs saw the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] (even a fragment), [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] needs to know how much they know. His recovery is smooth but imperfect. The facade cracks a little wider than it did with the mirror
- The "my art / my sketches" line is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] telling the truth for the first time, disguised as a deflection. He IS building something. He IS creating. And he doesn't want anyone seeing it until it's finished. The PCs may not realize this now, but they will later
- [[EyeQ]] heading to the lower levels is a breadcrumb. Don't force the PCs to follow up. Just drop it. If they ask about the lower levels later, [[Iris Major]] says: *"Restricted. Infrastructure maintenance. Not your concern."*
- The 1d4 nights of tapestry dreams are a free DM tool. Use them between adventures to drop images, foreshadow future heists, or just unsettle the PCs at the table. *"You dream of threads. Silver and gold, weaving together. You hear something — not a voice, but a pattern. It sounds like counting."*

## Appendix: Scaling Notes

**For weaker parties (level 2-3, 3 players):**
- Remove the phase spider from the web shaft route
- Reduce the Weaving Duel to 4 rounds, PCs need 5 points to win
- Zhael'ra does not arrive if the PCs are fast (no duel — the fast-grab option is easier)
- Pursuit encounters use 2 drow warriors instead of 4
- [[Kael'voth]] is absent from the antechamber

**For stronger parties (level 4-5, 5+ players):**
- Add a second drider to the antechamber ([[Kael'voth]] has a partner who IS loyal)
- Zhael'ra arrives 2 rounds earlier (shrinking the fast-grab window)
- The Weaving Duel runs 6 rounds, PCs need 9 points
- Pursuit includes a drow priestess (cleric 4) who can track the tapestry's magic
- [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]]'s "betrayal" path (if PCs were cruel) includes him selling information to *multiple* parties, creating complications in future adventures

## Appendix: [[Lolth's Unfinished Tapestry]] — Ongoing Campaign Use

- **Post-mission dreams:** 1d4 nights of thread-dreams. Use these to foreshadow the next adventure, hint at [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] progress, or just make players uncomfortable at the table. The dreams fade but never fully stop — at moments of high stress, PCs who touched the tapestry feel phantom threads on their hands
- **Zhael'ra's grudge:** Lolth's clergy do not forget theft. Zhael'ra becomes an ongoing threat — she dispatches agents to track the tapestry. These agents may show up in unexpected places ([[Offledger]] is supposed to be secure, but drow are resourceful). This is a slow-burn complication, not an immediate one
- **[[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] as contact:** If the PCs earned his respect, [[Szordrin Hune|Szordrin]] becomes a valuable Underdark contact. He can provide information, supplies, and warnings about drow activities. He never returns to [[Offledger]] — he's done with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s world — but he'll work with the PCs directly
- **The tapestry as Crown component:** As more components are gathered, the PCs may notice [[Offledger]]'s architecture developing subtle web-like patterns — silk threads appearing in the walls, the "sky" occasionally showing woven scenes. The tapestry's influence on the Crown is aesthetic as well as functional — it's weaving [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] into the fabric of fate, literally
