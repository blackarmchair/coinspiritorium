# "The Beholder Convention Job"

> *"Gotta flex on my haters."*
> *"Eye-conic."*
> *"I'm stealing it from myself, spiritually."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is attending the annual Conclave of Eyefellows — the only beholder summit in the multiverse — and he's the keynote speaker. He's bringing the PCs as his "mortal portfolio," living proof of his business acumen. While [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] addresses a room full of beings who each believe they're the center of the universe, the PCs must steal the Conclave's most prized artifact: [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]], a floating gemstone of crystallized beholder ego that every beholder in attendance believes they already own.

This is the funniest mission in the campaign. It is also the one that reveals who [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] really is.

The Conclave takes place in the **Panopticon** — a demiplanar sphere designed by beholders, for beholders, where every surface is a viewing gallery and [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] floats at the dead center like a miniature sun. The PCs must navigate a trade show of incomprehensible ego (beholder panels, merch booths, eye-ray demonstrations, competitive narcissism), case [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s security, and execute a heist timed to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s keynote speech — because the speech isn't just showmanship. It's the distraction. Every dramatic pause, every grandiose gesture, every moment of peak performance is calculated to redirect every beholder's attention (and antimagic cones) toward the stage.

Then [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] vanishes. Every beholder immediately assumes another beholder stole it, because no beholder can conceive of a universe where they don't own it. A civil war erupts. Eye rays fly in every direction. And the PCs have to escape through the crossfire while their boss watches from the stage, goblet of purple fire in tentacle, enjoying the show.

**Tone arc:** Excitement and ego (briefing — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is THRILLED) → Absurdist comedy (the convention — beholder trade show insanity) → Genuine revelation (the keynote — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] speaks to his peers and says things he'd never say to the PCs) → Tension (the heist — surgical precision in a room full of paranoid aberrations) → Glorious chaos (the civil war — beholders disintegrating each other) → Quiet unease (the debrief — nine down, three to go)

**Primary challenges:** Social infiltration as non-beholders in a beholder space, antimagic-zone navigation puzzle, timed heist during the keynote, escape through beholder civil war
**Combat:** Minimal during infiltration, environmental chaos during the civil war (hazard-based, not targeted combat), one potential direct engagement during escape

## Target Item
**[[The Iris Prime]]** — A gemstone the size of a human fist, shaped like a vertical iris — the pupil of an eye, cut from a mineral that doesn't exist in normal geology. It's not quite crystal, not quite metal, not quite light. It shifts between states depending on the angle: from one direction it's a deep amethyst, from another it's molten gold, from a third it's the absolute black of a pupil dilating. It floats without magic, without mechanism, without explanation. It simply hangs in the air because it believes it should, and reality has chosen not to argue.

[[The Iris Prime]] is the oldest artifact in beholder history. No beholder remembers who created it. No beholder admits that anyone else could have. Every beholder who sees it experiences an immediate, bone-deep conviction: *that's mine. I have always owned that. It was taken from me, and now I'm seeing it again.* This conviction is not magical. It's psychological — or rather, it's **ontological**. [[The Iris Prime]] is a crystallized fragment of the belief structure that defines beholder consciousness: the absolute, unshakeable certainty that the self is the only real thing in the universe.

**Properties:**
- **Ego resonance (300-ft. radius):** Every beholder within 300 feet of [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] experiences amplified self-certainty. Their conviction that they are the most important being in existence intensifies from personality trait to physiological imperative. Minor disputes become existential conflicts. Disagreements become wars. [[The Iris Prime]] doesn't create ego — it *concentrates* it. This is why the Conclave both venerates and fears it: the Prime makes beholders MORE of what they already are
- **Possessive pulse:** When [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] is moved from its resting position, every beholder within 300 feet experiences a visceral sensation of LOSS — like a phantom limb, like a stolen heartbeat. They don't know what was taken. They just know something that belonged to them is gone. Within 1d4 rounds, the sensation resolves into certainty: someone stole the Prime. The beholder's natural paranoia does the rest
- **Identity anchor:** A creature that holds [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] for more than 1 hour begins to feel their sense of self intensify. Not ego, exactly — *identity*. The holder becomes more themselves. More certain. More defined. Quirks become compulsions. Beliefs become convictions. The holder's personality calcifies into something diamond-hard and unshakeable. For a beholder, this is bliss. For a mortal, it's deeply uncomfortable — imagine every doubt you've ever had vanishing, replaced by absolute certainty about everything. It sounds good until you realize certainty is a cage
- **Cannot be claimed:** No creature can "own" [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]. Any attempt to claim ownership — verbal, contractual, magical — is met with the Prime's passive resistance: the claimant's sense of self frays slightly, as if the Prime is saying *you're not enough to contain me.* Only a being with sufficient ego-mass can integrate the Prime. There may be one such being

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Beholder reality anchor — [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] provides the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] with the raw ego-mass needed to sustain [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s self-image at cosmic scale. The Crown doesn't just rewrite reality; it centers reality around a single identity. That requires an identity strong enough to serve as the axis. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s ego is formidable. But sustaining it at the scale of the entire multiverse — making every soul, every plane, every law of physics orient toward a single beholder — requires more self-certainty than any mortal mind can generate. [[The Iris Prime]] is supplemental narcissism. It's the booster rocket for the soul. With it, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s self-image becomes the gravitational center of all existence. Without it, the Crown has an identity crisis at the moment of activation. The device needs to know who it serves. The Prime makes the answer permanent: *him. Always him. Only him.*

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The back room. All surviving [[The Eyecons|Eyecons]] are present — [[Iris Major]], [[Major Look]], [[Snitch Ray]], [[EyeQ]]. The circuits on the walls pulse brighter than ever. The egg's muffled scream throbs through the floor in a steady rhythm that has become [[Offledger]]'s heartbeat. The Workshop hum has a new harmonic — richer, more complex, almost musical. Eight artifacts are orbiting down there. The architecture is approaching what [[EyeQ]] calls "critical coherence."

And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is in a MOOD. The best mood. The kind of mood where his central eye is practically glowing and his chains catch the light with every excited rotation.

He's going to a party.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Pitch

> *"Alright. Cancel everything. Push the schedule. This is the one I've been WAITING for."*

He's beaming. The diamond grill is polished to a mirror shine. New chains — heavier, more elaborate, with links shaped like tiny eyes.

> *"Every year, the greatest minds in beholder society — which is ALL beholder society, because every beholder is the greatest mind in beholder society — gather for the Conclave of Eyefellows. The most exclusive, most prestigious, most EYE-CONIC event in the multiverse."*

He savors the pun. Nobody laughs. He doesn't care.

> *"And this year, YOURS TRULY is the keynote speaker."*

[[Major Look]]'s eyestalks arrange into an expression that could be pride, could be nausea.

> *"I've been working on the speech for MONTHS. It's got everything. Vision. Metaphor. A section where I address my haters. A STANDING OVATION SECTION. It's art."*

He catches himself. Refocuses.

> *"But that's not why you're coming."*

### The Real Mission

> *"At the center of the Conclave — literally at the center, floating in the air like a beautiful little 'you're welcome' from the universe — there's [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]. Oldest beholder artifact in existence. Every beholder who sees it thinks it's theirs. Every single one. It's like...our collective ambition made into a rock. And I need it."*

**If PCs ask why:**

> *"Because the Crown needs a SPINE. It needs something at the center that says 'this is who this is for' and means it HARD ENOUGH that reality believes it. My ego's good. It's great. Top tier. But cosmic-scale? Universal? I need reinforcement. The Prime is... it's like looking in a mirror and the mirror agrees with you."*

**If PCs ask about security:**

> *"Here's the thing about beholder security: every beholder IS security. You know how our central eye works? Antimagic cone. Every beholder at the convention is projecting one. [[The Iris Prime]] sits in the overlap of DOZENS of antimagic fields. No spell touches it. No enchantment works near it. It's the most magically dead zone in the multiverse — which is why it's also the safest place to keep something priceless."*

He lets that implication sink in.

> *"Which means y'all can't use magic to steal it. You gotta use your HANDS. And your TIMING. And my beautiful, beautiful keynote speech."*

### The Plan

> *"When I'm on stage, every eye in the room — and we're talking HUNDREDS of eyes — will be on ME. That's not ego. That's tactical reality. My speech is designed to redirect every antimagic cone in the hall toward the stage. For approximately four minutes during the third act — I call it 'The Reckoning With Greatness' — [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s defensive perimeter drops. The antimagic cone overlap thins to a sliver. That's your window."*

He's not bragging. He's briefing. The showmanship is STRATEGY. Every pun, every pause, every dramatic gesture is a calculated weapon. The PCs are watching their boss reveal that the clown was always the smartest person in the room.

> *"[[Snitch Ray]] handles surveillance. He maps the cone patterns. He tells you when the window opens. You go in, you take the Prime, you put it in the case, and you walk out."*

**The case:** A lead-lined, ego-dampening container that suppresses [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s possessive pulse for approximately 5 minutes before the Prime's resonance overcomes it. After that, every beholder in the Conclave feels the loss simultaneously.

> *"Five minutes. That's how long you have between grabbing the Prime and every beholder in the building losing their collective minds. Use them wisely."*

### The Eyecon Deployment

Each Eyecon has a role:

- **[[Iris Major]]:** Coordinates with convention logistics. She's [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s "chief of staff" — the professional face that handles scheduling, security liaisons, and the hundred small details of attending a multi-day beholder summit. She will not be available for heist support. She's the alibi
- **[[Snitch Ray]]:** Field handler. He maps antimagic cone patterns from the rafters, feeds real-time intelligence to the PCs, and opens [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s display case with his eye ray at the critical moment. His surveillance skills are, for once, exactly what's needed. He is quietly, bitterly proud of this. He will never admit it
- **[[Major Look]]:** Image management. He dresses the PCs, coaches their behavior, and ensures they look like convincing "mortal portfolio" — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s living status symbols. Among beholders, having mortal servants is a dominance display. The PCs are accessories. [[Major Look]] intends them to be *convincing* accessories
- **[[EyeQ]]:** Present but peripheral. Studying the Conclave's architecture, the Panopticon's dimensional structure, [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s energy signature. [[EyeQ]] does not participate in the heist. [[EyeQ]] observes. If the PCs look closely, [[EyeQ]]'s circuits are brighter than ever — pulsing in time with the Crown's rhythm even here, hundreds of planes from [[Offledger]]

**[[EyeRate]] is absent.** He's recovering in [[Offledger]]. The missing eyestalk. The exposed circuits. The knowledge of what he is. [[Major Look]] argued against bringing him — *"The optics of a damaged Eyecon at the Conclave are unacceptable"* — and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] agreed without discussion. [[EyeRate]] didn't argue.

If the PCs ask about him:

> *"He's holding down the fort. It's fine. He's fine."*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] says this too quickly.

### Mortal Portfolio Preparation

[[Major Look]] takes over. This is his domain.

> *"You are attending the most prestigious event in beholder society as Lord Vizzok's mortal entourage. Do you understand what that MEANS? Every beholder there will look at you and form opinions about LORD VEEZY based on how you PRESENT."*

He circles the PCs.

> *"You will wear the provided uniforms. You will not speak unless addressed. You will not make eye contact with any beholder — their eyes are everywhere, they WILL notice. You will stand at parade rest behind Lord Vizzok during official functions. You will refer to him as 'the Visionary' in public. You will not laugh at the panels. You will not buy the merch. You will be FLAWLESS extensions of the brand."*

**The uniforms:** Black tunics with gold trim, embroidered with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s sigil (an eye surrounded by coins). Surprisingly well-made. Deeply humiliating.

### DM Notes
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s excitement is genuine and infectious — use it.** This is the first time the PCs see their boss truly, unreservedly happy. Not performing. Not intimidating. EXCITED. He's going to a party where he's the star, and he's been looking forward to it. This should make the PCs feel something complicated — their tyrant boss has the same giddy energy as anyone invited to give a speech at their industry's biggest event. He's a monster. He's also a person who gets excited about keynotes
- **The reveal that the showmanship is strategy is the scene's most important beat.** Everything the PCs have ever assumed about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — that he's vain, that the puns are ego, that the drama is self-indulgence — gets reframed. The keynote isn't showing off. It's a surgical distraction timed to the second. The clown was always the tactician. This should genuinely surprise the PCs, and it should make them reconsider every previous interaction
- **[[Major Look]]'s "mortal portfolio" preparation should be both funny and degrading.** The PCs are level 10-11 adventurers who've robbed the Hoard of Tiamat. Now they're being dressed in matching uniforms and told not to make eye contact. Among beholders, mortals are status symbols, not people. The PCs are props at a trade show. Let them feel it
- **[[EyeRate]]'s absence should be noted but not dwelt on.** The PCs know why he's not here. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s too-quick dismissal tells them it's not fine. [[EyeRate]], alone in [[Offledger]] with the screaming egg and the exposed circuits on his body, is having the worst day of his life. But the mission continues. The show goes on. That's how [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s operation works: broken things get left behind

## Scene 2: The Conclave

### The Panopticon
The portal opens into... everything.

The Panopticon is a sphere. Not a room shaped like a sphere — a **sphere**. The PCs step through a shimmering membrane and find themselves standing on a platform of force that hovers inside a hollow globe half a mile in diameter. There is no up. There is no down. Every surface of the sphere's interior is covered in architecture: viewing galleries, exhibition halls, vendor booths, panel stages — all of them arranged on the walls, the ceiling, the floor, every possible surface. Beholders float freely through the open center of the sphere, drifting between platforms and galleries at every angle. A beholder "walking" along what the PCs perceive as the ceiling is, from its perspective, right-side up. Gravity is local. Orientation is subjective. The entire space is designed for beings that fly and see in all directions.

For mortals, it is deeply, physically wrong. The PCs' inner ears revolt. Vertigo hits like a wave. The walkways of force that connect mortal-accessible platforms are translucent, narrow, and have no railings. Looking down reveals beholders floating below — and above, and to the left. The geometry is non-Euclidean in ways that are technically possible but emotionally unacceptable.

**And at the center of the sphere: [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]].** Floating at the exact geometric center of the Panopticon, casting light in every direction. It's the sun of this tiny universe. Every gallery, every platform, every walkway is oriented toward it. The entire Conclave is built around [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] the way a cathedral is built around an altar.

It's beautiful. It's also guarded by the most paranoid species in the multiverse, all of whom are looking at it RIGHT NOW because they each believe it's theirs.

### The Attendees
The Conclave of Eyefellows draws approximately **sixty beholders** and twice that many beholder-kin (gauths, spectators, directors, and lesser variants). The PCs have never seen this many beholders in one place. Nobody has. The effect is overwhelming: hundreds of eyes tracking every movement, dozens of antimagic cones sweeping the space in casual arcs, the air thick with the psychic static of beings that each consider themselves the universe's main character.

**[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] among his peers:** The moment [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] enters the Panopticon, he changes. Not his appearance — his *presence*. Among mortals, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is the biggest thing in the room by default. Among beholders, he's one of sixty. He has to WORK for attention. And he does: the chains catch the light, the diamond grill flashes, the entrance is perfectly timed to a moment when a spotlight-equivalent passes over the arrival platform. Every detail is calculated. The other beholders turn. They see him. They form opinions. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] drinks it in — the attention of beings who are, for once, his equals.

The PCs have never seen [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] need an audience's approval before. He's always had it by default (because he could disintegrate anyone who disagreed). Here, among peers, he EARNS it. It's a different kind of performance, and it's more vulnerable than anything he's ever done in front of the PCs.

**The mortal servants:** The PCs are not the only mortals here. Other beholders have brought entourages — humanoid servants, mind-controlled thralls, and a few voluntary employees (there are, apparently, mortals who CHOOSE to work for beholders — the pay is good and the life insurance is comprehensive because it needs to be). The mortals stick to the force-walkways, eyes forward, speaking only when addressed. The PCs blend in. It feels terrible.

### DM Notes
- **The Panopticon should feel ALIEN in a way that previous locations haven't.** The aboleth vault was dark and wet. Tiamat's Clutch was hot and hostile. The Panopticon is bright, social, and geometrically wrong. It's a space designed for beings with 360-degree vision and no relationship to gravity. The PCs can function here, but they're visitors in a space that was never designed for them. That feeling — of being an afterthought in someone else's architecture — is the beholder experience of mortals in reverse
- **Sixty beholders in one space should be TERRIFYING even though no one is attacking.** Each beholder is a CR 13 encounter. The room contains enough combined firepower to disintegrate a small army. And they're all just... chatting. Browsing merch. Arguing about who invented the disintegration ray. The casual power on display is the real horror: these beings could destroy the PCs without noticing, and the PCs are here wearing matching uniforms and pretending to be furniture

## Scene 3: The Convention Floor

### The Experience
The Conclave is, functionally, a trade show. And trade shows are the same across all species and all planes: fluorescent-adjacent lighting, uncomfortable seating, vendor booths staffed by beings who are too enthusiastic about niche products, and panels that could have been pamphlets.

**Notable convention features:**

**The Exhibition Hall:** The main gallery, curving along the Panopticon's interior surface. Vendor booths arranged in concentric rings around [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s central position. Every booth has a clear sightline to the Prime. Every beholder browsing the stalls is, at all times, able to see *their* gemstone.

**Merch booths:**
- "Original Eye Polish — Since Before You Were Spawned" — a beholder selling a suspicious amber fluid in crystal vials. It is olive oil. Extremely expensive olive oil
- "Antimagic Field Accessories" — decorative rings, chains, and monocles designed to be worn while one's central eye is active. The beholder equivalent of sunglasses
- "Beholder Art Collective" — abstract paintings that are, without exception, eyes. Eyes looking at eyes looking at eyes. The beholders consider this avant-garde. One painting is titled "Self-Portrait (The Only Valid Perspective)"
- "Trophy Mounts (Mortal Species)" — a taxidermist. The PCs should walk past this one quickly

**Panels:**
- **"Ten Eyes, Ten Perspectives: An Intra-Species Dialogue"** — A panel discussion that devolves into arguments within four minutes. The moderator is disintegrated by the third panelist. The audience considers this a success
- **"Antimagic Field Theory: New Frontiers"** — Academic. Dense. One beholder presents a 47-slide illusion deck. The audience physically cannot leave because the presenter's antimagic cone is blocking the exit
- **"Are Gauths Really Beholders? A Taxonomy Debate"** — The PCs should NOT attend this panel. The implications for [[The Eyecons|the Eyecons]] — beholder-kin standing in this room, hearing their existence debated as a taxonomic curiosity — are savage. If [[Snitch Ray]] is monitoring this panel's attendance, his telepathic commentary becomes notably acidic
- **"The Art of the Disintegration: Style, Form, and Follow-Through"** — A practical demonstration. The target dummies are wearing hats for some reason. One beholder insists on narrating each shot. Standing ovation

**The Eye-Ray Range:** A designated space where beholders demonstrate their eye rays on reinforced targets. Think: a shooting range, but every lane fires a different magical effect. The range is loud, flashy, and draws a crowd. **This is tactically useful:** during peak demonstration hours, the antimagic cone coverage around [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] shifts as beholders turn to watch the range. [[Snitch Ray]] notes this pattern.

#### The Gnome with the Notepad

In the mortal servant gallery — a railed observation platform 40 feet above the convention floor, accessible by a service staircase the PCs were pointedly told not to use — a gnome in a hand-stitched apron reading "EYE-SCREAM CATERING" is taking notes.

He is not catering.

**Spot DC 18** to notice him. The PCs have seen this gnome before: wire-rimmed spectacles, ink-stained fingers, the posture of a man who has forgotten that his body exists because his brain is too busy. This is **[[Auditor Pentival]]**, and his disguise is the worst disguise any of the PCs have ever seen. The apron is on backwards. He's holding a tray of canapes that he hasn't offered to anyone. He's staring at [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] through a monocle that is very obviously a *detect magic* focus, and he's muttering.

> *"Crystallized ego-mass... if the ego resonance is quantifiable — and it IS, I can SEE the Fibonacci-adjacent resonance spirals, identity as the independent variable — then the Crown's architecture requires a fixed-point attractor. A reference identity. Something that doesn't change. Something that CAN'T change..."*

He trails off. Scribbles something. Eats a canape from his own tray without noticing.

> *"Oh, this is just BEAUTIFUL math."*

**If the PCs approach:**

Pentival looks up. Blinks. Breaks into a genuine smile — the smile of a man who has been doing math alone for months and finally has someone to talk to.

> *"Oh! Oh, it's YOU. Hello! You look — well, you look like people who've been doing dangerous work for an unreasonable employer, which — I mean, of course you do. How are you? Are you eating? These canapes are terrible. I've been holding this tray for three hours."*

He sets the tray down on the railing. A canape slides off and falls 40 feet into the convention crowd below. Neither Pentival nor the beholder it lands on notices.

**What Pentival shares (unprompted, because he cannot help himself):**

He's been studying the [[The Iris Prime|Iris Prime]] for two hours. His observations are accidentally critical:

> *"The Prime is a crystallized ego construct — pure, self-referential identity compressed into mineral form. It's extraordinary. It's also exactly what your employer needs for the... the THING he's building."*

He catches himself. Barely.

> *"I shouldn't say 'thing.' I should say 'reality-restructuring binding construct of civilization-altering significance.' But 'thing' is faster."*

**If PCs ask what he knows about the Crown:**

> *"More than I did six months ago. Less than I will in six months. The mathematics are... converging. I can see the shape of what he's building. I can see most of the components. I can see the structural logic. What I CAN'T see is the flaw."*

He leans forward. The academic excitement drops for exactly one sentence.

> *"Every construct like this has a flaw. Every single one in recorded history. The flaw is always the same category: an unresolved obligation inside the system. A debt the builder forgot about — or thinks doesn't matter."*

Then the excitement returns:

> *"The math says it's there. I just can't find it yet. When I do — oh, when I do — the whole thing becomes so SIMPLE. One number. One obligation. That's all it takes."*

He has no idea he's describing [[Veezy's IOU]]. He has no idea the answer is 49 gold pieces and a goblin. He's going to find it eventually. The math is converging.

**If PCs ask about [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]'s plans:**

> *"Plans? Oh — I'm not really the plans person. [[Agent Quill|Quill]] handles the plans. [[Director Casseel|The Director]] handles the decisions. I handle the numbers. The numbers are very clear: if the device activates at full capacity, the relationship between debt and reality across all planes of existence changes permanently. Every contract becomes law. Every obligation becomes physics. Every debt becomes — well."*

He gestures at the room full of beholders.

> *"Imagine every creature in the multiverse owing something to one being, and the universe itself enforcing collection. That's the projection. I've run it fourteen times. I keep hoping the math is wrong."*

He picks up another canape. Puts it down.

> *"The math is not wrong."*

**If PCs warn him he'll be noticed:**

> *"Noticed? I'm a caterer. I'm holding canapes."*

He gestures at his backwards apron with complete confidence.

> *"Nobody notices the caterer."*

**Pentival's exit:** When the keynote begins, Pentival produces a second, smaller notebook and starts sketching [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s speech patterns in some kind of linguistic analysis shorthand. He's fully absorbed. If the PCs try to talk to him during the keynote, he shushes them. *"I'm WORKING."*

He vanishes from the gallery at some point during the civil war. The PCs don't see him leave. The tray of canapes remains on the railing, untouched except by Pentival himself.

**DM Notes:**
- **Pentival at the Conclave serves two functions:** he's a recurring, recognizable face (the PCs have his card, his letter, and now his personality in full bloom), and he independently confirms [[EyeQ]]'s identity architecture conclusions from an external analytical framework. Two different geniuses, working separately, arriving at the same math. The Crown's architecture is knowable. Its flaw is findable. Pentival just hasn't found it yet
- **The "unresolved obligation" speech is the closest anyone has come to naming the IOU directly.** Pentival is describing the exact mechanism without knowing the specific instance. If the PCs have been paying attention to the Failed Crown vision (Quest 6), to [[Rue Kethani]]'s analysis (Quest 7-8), and now to Pentival's math — the pattern is screaming. One debt. One flaw. One number. 49 gold pieces
- **Pentival's social obliviousness is genuine, not performed.** He is the worst undercover operative in [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]'s history. [[Agent Quill|Quill]] would never let him come alone to something this dangerous. The fact that he's here, alone, with a backwards apron and a tray of canapes, means either [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is desperate or Pentival went without authorization. Both options are concerning

### Casing [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]

**The display:** [[The Iris Prime]] floats at the Panopticon's center, approximately 200 feet from the nearest surface in any direction. It's surrounded by a sphere of overlapping antimagic cones — at any given moment, between 8 and 15 beholders have their central eyes partly oriented toward it. The overlap creates a zone roughly 30 feet in diameter where no magic functions. Within this zone, a transparent case of force (mundane, not magical — it persists inside antimagic) holds the Prime on a pedestal that rotates slowly.

**The case:** Locked with an **Ego Lock** — a beholder-designed mechanism that opens only when three specific types of eye ray are fired at it simultaneously. The combination changes yearly and is known only to the Conclave's chair, **[[Dazrathuur]] "Visionary D."** [[Snitch Ray]] has, through means he refuses to describe, obtained this year's combination: a telekinesis ray, a charm ray, and a fear ray, fired simultaneously from three different angles.

**[[Snitch Ray]]'s assessment:**

> *"The antimagic coverage is consistent but not uniform. When the keynote starts, beholders rotate to face the stage. The overlap shifts. For approximately four minutes during what Lord Vizzok calls 'The Reckoning With Greatness' — eyestalks on him, central eyes on him, every cone in the building pointed at the stage — the Prime's coverage drops to two, maybe three cones. A gap opens on the southern approach. Thirty seconds of clear access."*

He pauses. The bitterness softens into something almost like professional satisfaction.

> *"I mapped every cone. Every rotation. Every blink pattern for thirty-six beholders. I know when they look away. I know when they look back. I know their NAMES. I have been watching them watch the Prime for six hours and I know them better than they know themselves."*

He refocuses.

> *"You'll have thirty seconds. Don't waste them."*

### Visionary D

The PCs encounter **[[Dazrathuur]] "Visionary D"** during the convention floor exploration. He is impossible to miss.

[[Dazrathuur]] is an **elder beholder** — one of the oldest living beholders in the multiverse. He is enormous. Where [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is Huge, [[Dazrathuur]] is pushing the upper limits of Huge and flirting with Gargantuan. His hide is ancient, scarred, and covered in a patina of crystallized magical residue that glitters like embedded gemstones. His eyestalks are long, gnarled, and move with the careful deliberation of extreme old age. His central eye is the color of a dying star — pale gold, enormous, and radiating an antimagic cone so powerful that magic items dim within 50 feet of him.

He founded the Conclave of Eyefellows four hundred years ago as proof that beholders could cooperate. It was the most audacious bet in beholder history. It has almost worked. Almost. Every year, half the attendees try to disintegrate the other half. Every year, [[Dazrathuur]] mediates through raw power, ancient prestige, and the one thing beholders respect: an ego larger than their own.

He approaches [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s entourage directly. His eyestalks inspect the PCs with the casual thoroughness of a customs official.

> ***"Vizzok brought MORTALS. Again. Still with the mortals."***

His voice is deep. Each word sounds like it was considered for several centuries before being spoken.

> ***"In MY day, mortal servants were kept in sensory deprivation spheres until needed. This 'entourage' display is gauche. But I suppose it's... current."***

**If the PCs speak (and they're allowed to, barely):**

[[Dazrathuur]] regards them with the attention one gives to furniture that has started talking.

> ***"You speak. Vizzok taught his mortals to speak. ...Charming. In a novelty sense."***

**If the PCs ask about [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]:**

> ***"It's MINE. Obviously. I am the Conclave's founder. I am its custodian. The Prime recognizes my claim above all others."***

Every beholder in earshot turns. Every one of them has a different opinion on this statement. None of them agree. All of them are right (in their own minds).

> ***"But I display it for the Conclave because leadership requires sacrifice. My personal ownership is temporarily expressed as communal stewardship. This is what LESSER beings fail to understand about hierarchy."***

**If the PCs ask about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s keynote:**

> ***"Vizzok is... an anomaly. A young beholder with an old beholder's ambition and a mortal's talent for spectacle. I invited him to keynote because his story is instructive: a minor enforcer who built an empire through sheer force of self-regard. Every beholder in this room believes they're the most important being in existence. Vizzok is the only one building INFRASTRUCTURE to prove it."***

[[Dazrathuur]]'s central eye narrows.

> ***"I find this either inspiring or concerning. I have not decided which."***

### DM Notes
- **The convention floor should be FUNNY.** This is the comedy mission. Lean into the absurdity: beholder merch, terrible panels, eye puns everywhere, beholders arguing about who invented what. The PCs are level 10-11 adventurers in matching uniforms surrounded by entities that could each individually destroy them. The tonal collision between "deadly aberrations" and "trade show attendees" is the joke, and it should land repeatedly
- **[[Snitch Ray]]'s professional excellence should genuinely impress the PCs.** His surveillance skills — annoying and invasive when directed at the PCs — are EXACTLY what this heist needs. Thirty-six beholders mapped individually, blink patterns tracked, cone rotations catalogued. [[Snitch Ray]] is, for once, doing the thing he was built for, and he's the best in the room at it. The PCs should feel the uncomfortable realization that the Eyecon they hate most is the one saving the mission
- **[[Dazrathuur]] is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s dark mirror.** He tried to build something communal — a beholder society based on shared respect. He failed. Beholders can't cooperate because they can't see each other as real. The Conclave barely functions. [[Dazrathuur]] keeps it running through force and prestige, but it's a machine that fights itself every year. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is the opposite approach: instead of convincing beholders to respect each other, make one beholder so powerful that everyone else becomes irrelevant. [[Dazrathuur]] built a convention. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is building a god. One is noble and failed. The other is monstrous and succeeding

## Scene 4: The Keynote

### The Stage
The keynote stage is a floating platform at the Panopticon's "equator" — the widest point of the sphere, where the largest audience can observe from every angle. Viewing galleries tier upward and downward (in all directions; beholders don't care about "up") for hundreds of feet. The audience of sixty-plus beholders arranges itself in a sphere around the stage, floating at various heights, every central eye oriented toward the speaker.

The antimagic coverage map shifts exactly as [[Snitch Ray]] predicted. Cones converge on the stage. The southern approach to [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] thins.

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] rises to the stage.

### The Speech

He doesn't start with a pun. He doesn't start with a grill flash or a chain rattle or a goblet flourish. He starts with silence.

The room notices. Sixty beholders, each convinced they deserve the spotlight more, watching a peer take it. The silence stretches. One second. Two. Three. Long enough for discomfort to register.

Then:

> *"I know what you think when you look at me."*

Low. Conversational. Not performing.

> *"You think: 'loud.' You think: 'flashy.' You think: 'he's got good chains but his disintegration ray form is sloppy.'"*

A ripple of something that isn't quite laughter. Beholder humor. Recognition.

> *"And you're RIGHT. I'm loud. I'm flashy. My chains are INCREDIBLE."*

He rotates, letting the light catch every link.

> *"But I didn't come here to talk about chains. I came here to talk about what WE are."*

**The speech that follows is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] at his most honest.** He's not talking to servants. He's not threatening debtors. He's not performing for mortals. He's speaking to beings like himself — beings that share the fundamental beholder conviction that they are the center of everything — and he's saying things he'd never say to the PCs.

Key excerpts (DM: paraphrase or read selectively based on table energy):

> *"Every beholder KNOWS. From the moment of spawning, we know. We are the center. We are the point. Every eye we have confirms it. We see in ALL directions because nothing should exist without our observation. Other species need evidence. They need validation. They need FRIENDS to tell them they matter. We don't. We arrive KNOWING."*

A beat.

> *"So why are we sitting in a convention center arguing about who invented the disintegration ray?"*

Silence. Several beholders shift uncomfortably. He's touching a nerve.

> *"Because knowing isn't ENOUGH. Every one of you knows you're the center of the universe. And every one of you is RIGHT. But the universe doesn't care. It doesn't RECOGNIZE. The planes keep spinning. The gods keep ignoring us. Mortals keep treating us like MONSTERS instead of what we ARE."*

His voice rises. Not anger — conviction. Pure, terrifying conviction.

> *"What we ARE is the universe's CORRECTION. We are what happens when reality admits it needs someone in charge. Every race got their thing — elves got time, dwarves got stubbornness, humans got whatever it is humans have. BEHOLDERS got CERTAINTY. The one thing nobody else has. The absolute, unshakeable knowledge that we are right. About everything. Always."*

He lets this settle.

> *"The question isn't whether we're right. The question is: what do we DO with that certainty?"*

### The Hidden Layer
The PCs, knowing what they know about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], hear the speech differently. When [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] says "what do we do with that certainty," the PCs know his answer: you build a machine that makes your certainty into universal law. When he says "the universe doesn't recognize us," the PCs know he's building something that forces recognition. The speech is both genuine beholder philosophy AND a coded confession about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]].

**The most revealing moment:** Midway through, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] drops into something that is unmistakably personal.

> *"I was nobody. Lower than nobody. An enforcer for a syndicate that didn't know my name. I had one eye that worked right and eight that disagreed about which direction was 'up.' I owed money to a GOBLIN."*

The audience is rapt. Beholders don't share vulnerability. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sharing his origin story — the debt, the low status, the early years — is unprecedented. It's like watching a CEO strip naked at a shareholder meeting. It shouldn't work. It does.

> *"And I looked at the multiverse — at the gods, at the planes, at all the creatures that thought they mattered — and I said: 'You know what? I SEE better than all of you. All of you combined. And I'm going to PROVE it.'"*

The room is silent. Not awkward silence. Held-breath silence.

> *"I built something. Not a LAIR. Not a HOARD. I built a SYSTEM. Where vision is currency. Where seeing clearly is the only power that matters. Where the being with the most eyes — the BEST eyes — the eyes that see in ALL directions — is the one who writes the rules."*

This is it. Eighty percent of the room hears ambition. The PCs hear a confession. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is describing the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] in metaphor, and the metaphor is so close to literal that the distinction barely exists.

He rises. Every chain catches every light. Every eyestalk extends to maximum length. The golden circuits on his hide — visible now, undeniable — pulse once, in time with something deep below [[Offledger]]'s surface.

> *"I'm not the beholder of the year. I'm the beholder of the FUTURE. And the future is going to be BEAUTIFUL."*

**Standing ovation.** Or the beholder equivalent: every eyestalk in the room orients toward [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] simultaneously, a gesture of total attention that constitutes the highest praise a beholder can give. For twelve seconds, every central eye in the room is pointed at the stage.

The southern approach to [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] is completely clear.

### DM Notes
- **The keynote is the adventure's emotional centerpiece.** Play it as a real speech — read excerpts, let the table react, give it weight. This is the first time the PCs see [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] speak to beings he considers PEERS, and what he says is radically different from how he talks to them. He's honest in a way he can't be with servants. He's vulnerable in a way he'd never show mortals. He's also accidentally revealing more about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] than he ever has directly
- **"I owed money to a GOBLIN" is a bombshell the PCs may not fully process yet.** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s debt to [[Grindle Gruk'tak]] — the 49gp IOU — is the endgame weapon. He's mentioning it casually, as a shameful origin detail, in front of an audience. The PCs may register this as backstory color. But the debt is real. The IOU exists. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] just confirmed, in a room full of witnesses, that he was once a debtor. Under the Crown's own Law II (Debt Becomes Destiny), that matters. It matters enormously
- **The speech reframing is the campaign's key tonal moment.** The PCs have spent the whole campaign watching [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] be funny. Here, surrounded by his own kind, he's still funny — but the humor serves something real. He genuinely believes beholders are the universe's correction. He genuinely believes his certainty is a form of cosmic justice. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] isn't just ambition — it's PHILOSOPHY. It's a beholder's answer to an unfair universe. That makes [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] more sympathetic AND more dangerous, which is exactly the collision this campaign needs
- **Time the standing ovation to the heist.** The twelve seconds of total attention is the PCs' window. The comedy of a standing ovation at a beholder convention (every eyestalk at attention, central eyes forward, the most narcissistic species in existence momentarily unified in admiration for someone else) is also the heist's critical moment. The funniest beat is the most tense beat. That's the campaign in miniature

## Scene 5: The Heist

### The Window
[[Snitch Ray]]'s voice in the PCs' minds — he's using a **[[Whispering Eye]]**, a beholder-crafted telepathic relay that [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] provided:

> *"Southern approach clear. Cone count: two. Gap: twelve feet wide, expanding. You have thirty seconds starting... now."*

### The Approach
The PCs must cross approximately 200 feet of open air from the nearest mortal walkway to [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s central pedestal. The force walkways don't extend that far — the last 80 feet are open space. Options:

- **Fly spell/magic items:** Functional, but the two remaining antimagic cones create dead zones that flicker in and out. A PC flying through a cone drops. Fall distance: variable, but enough to hurt
- **Rope and grapple:** Low-tech, immune to antimagic. The pedestal has attachment points (maintenance hooks for the display's occasional servicing). Reliable but slow
- **Levitation from a beholder ally:** [[Snitch Ray]] can use his telekinesis eye ray to move PCs through open space. Fast and precise, but requires [[Snitch Ray]] to break position from his surveillance post, which leaves the team blind for the duration
- **The mundane path:** There IS a maintenance walkway — a narrow beam of force that extends from a service hatch to the pedestal. It's used by mortal workers who polish the case. It's 18 inches wide, 200 feet long, and has no railing. Balance check: DC 15 per round, DC 20 if hurrying. Failing by 5+ means falling

The PCs should combine methods. Creativity is rewarded.

### The Ego Lock
The case surrounding [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] is transparent, mundane (not magical — it functions in antimagic), and sealed with the Ego Lock: a mechanism that opens only when three specific eye ray types hit it simultaneously from three different angles.

This year's combination: telekinesis, charm, and fear.

[[Snitch Ray]] can provide one ray. The PCs need two more.

**Options:**
- **[[Snitch Ray]] fires one, PCs use scrolls/wands of the matching spells:** But antimagic zones make this unreliable — any remaining cone overlap suppresses spell effects. The PCs need to verify the area is truly clear before attempting
- **Recruit a second beholder-kin:** [[EyeQ]] is present in the Panopticon. If asked, [[EyeQ]] provides a ray without argument, without expression, and without asking why. [[EyeQ]] already knows. [[EyeQ]] has always known
- **Beholder Eye Ray Amulets:** Three single-use amulets available at the Eye-Ray Range vendor booth, 100gp each, that produce a single pulse mimicking a specific beholder eye ray. They're marketed as "novelty party favors." They work inside antimagic if activated before entering the zone (the pulse is already traveling). Timing is everything
- **[[Dazrathuur]]'s combination is technically public information** — he announced it during the Conclave's opening ceremony. A PC who attended the ceremony and made a DC 18 Knowledge (arcana) check noted the specific combination and can reproduce it through items, scrolls, or creative improvisation

### The Grab
The lock opens. The case lifts. [[The Iris Prime]] floats at arm's reach.

The moment a PC touches it, two things happen:

**First:** The PC feels the ego resonance directly. It's not what they expected. It's not pride. It's not arrogance. It's **certainty**. Absolute, crystalline certainty about who they are. Every doubt dissolves. Every question answers itself. The PC knows, with the deep marrow-level conviction of a beholder, that they are the most important being in this room. In this plane. In this multiverse. It lasts three seconds. It's the most intoxicating sensation they've ever experienced.

**Second:** The ego-dampening case. The PC must place the Prime in the case within 6 seconds (1 round) or the possessive pulse begins. If the pulse triggers before the case closes, skip to Scene 6 immediately — no five-minute grace period. Speed matters.

**If the Prime is cased successfully:** The PCs have 5 minutes before the case's dampening fails. [[Snitch Ray]]:

> *"Case sealed. Pulse suppressed. Timer starts now. Move. Move. MOVE."*

### The Exit
The PCs retrace their path to the mortal walkways. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s speech continues — he's into the Q&A now, fielding questions from the audience with the practiced ease of a being who has never encountered a question he didn't enjoy answering. The audience is engaged. The antimagic coverage has shifted permanently toward the stage end of the Panopticon.

The PCs need to reach the service exit — a portal embedded in the Panopticon's outer wall that connects to a staging area where [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s personal portal to [[Offledger]] is prepared. [[Iris Major]] arranged the logistics. The route is mapped. The path is clear.

For five minutes.

### DM Notes
- **The heist should feel like a clockwork machine where the PCs are the moving parts.** Every element — [[Snitch Ray]]'s surveillance, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s speech, the antimagic map, the Ego Lock, the dampening case — has been prepared in advance. The PCs are executing a plan, not improvising one. But plans have margins, and the margins here are razor-thin. One wrong step, one failed check, one second of bad timing, and sixty beholders know their favorite rock is missing
- **The three seconds of ego resonance when the PC touches the Prime is the adventure's most personal moment.** It should be individualized to the PC holding it. What does absolute self-certainty feel like to THIS character? A fighter might feel invincible. A wizard might feel omniscient. A rogue might feel invisible. Whatever the PC's deepest self-image is, the Prime turns it to diamond for three heartbeats. It's addictive. It's wrong. And it's the same thing [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] feels CONSTANTLY, because he's a beholder, and beholders are made of this certainty. For three seconds, the PC understands [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] in a way they never could before
- **The exit should feel too easy.** The five-minute window is generous. The path is clear. [[Iris Major]]'s logistics are flawless. The PCs should start to relax. They should almost make it. Then the case fails

## Scene 6: The Civil War

### The Pulse
Four minutes and thirty-seven seconds after the case sealed, it fails. [[The Iris Prime]]'s resonance burns through the dampening enchantment like acid through cloth, and the possessive pulse detonates.

Every beholder in the Panopticon feels it simultaneously. A psychic shockwave of LOSS — the sensation of something that belongs to them being ripped away. It hits like a physical blow. Eyestalks whip in every direction. Central eyes dilate. The ambient psychic static of the Conclave — always present, always buzzing — spikes into a wall of paranoid fury.

For three seconds, the Panopticon is silent. The kind of silence that happens before an avalanche.

Then every beholder in the room reaches the same conclusion: *one of them stole it.*

Not the mortals. Mortals aren't real enough to steal the Prime. Not an outside force — the Panopticon is warded. It was DEFINITELY one of the other beholders. Specifically, the one that each beholder individually considers their greatest rival. Which is every other beholder.

### The Chaos
The first disintegration ray fires at 4 minutes, 41 seconds. By 4 minutes, 45 seconds, the Panopticon is a war zone.

**The beholder civil war is not a combat encounter for the PCs.** It's an environmental hazard. Eye rays fly in every direction — disintegration beams carving trenches in the Panopticon's walls, charm rays bouncing off antimagic cones, fear rays creating pockets of panicked beholders who flee into DIFFERENT panicked beholders, telekinesis rays hurling furniture, vendor booths, and unfortunate mortal servants across the sphere.

**Hazard table (roll or select each round the PCs are in the open):**

| d6 | Hazard |
|----|--------|
| 1 | **Stray disintegration ray** — Reflex DC 18 or 5d6 damage (this is a glancing hit from a ray aimed at someone else) |
| 2 | **Telekinetic debris** — A vendor booth, panel stage, or large piece of furniture hurtles through the PCs' area. Reflex DC 16 or 3d6 bludgeoning and knocked prone |
| 3 | **Antimagic cone sweep** — A panicked beholder swings its central eye through the PCs' area. All magic items, spells, and magical effects suppressed for 1 round. Flying PCs DROP |
| 4 | **Fear ray splash** — Will DC 17 or *frightened* for 1d4 rounds. The fear isn't targeted — it's ambient, the psychic bleedoff of dozens of fear rays firing simultaneously |
| 5 | **Charm ray cascade** — Will DC 16 or *confused* for 1 round. Multiple charm rays from different beholders create a contradictory psychic signal — the PC's mind can't determine which beholder to obey |
| 6 | **Clear path** — a momentary gap in the chaos. The PCs can move double speed this round without hazard |

### The Escape Route
The PCs need to reach the service exit portal — roughly 300 feet from their current position through a Panopticon that has become a spherical war zone.

**[[Snitch Ray]]:** Still in position. Still watching. His voice in the PCs' minds is the only calm thing in the Panopticon:

> *"Cone sweep from the northwest, 3 seconds. Clear path along the ventral gallery. Go NOW. — Debris incoming from the art gallery. Duck. — Two beholders engaging at your 2 o'clock. They don't see you. They don't care about you. You are BENEATH their concern. Use that."*

His surveillance is keeping the PCs alive. Every blink pattern he mapped, every cone rotation he catalogued — it's all real-time data now, and he's feeding it to the PCs with the cold precision of a mission controller guiding a shuttle through debris. This is what [[Snitch Ray]] was made for. Not snitching. Not reporting. THIS. Keeping assets alive through total environmental awareness.

### The Accusation
Halfway to the exit, a beholder blocks the PCs' path. Not intentionally — it's fleeing from a disintegration duel and collides with the mortal walkway. It sees the PCs. It sees the case. Its eyestalks orientate.

> ***"YOU. MORTALS. WHAT IS IN THE CASE."***

This is the moment.

**If the PCs bluff:** Deception DC 22 (the beholder is paranoid, furious, and looking for someone to blame — but it also considers mortals beneath suspicion, giving a circumstance bonus)

**If the PCs fight:** The beholder is a standard beholder (CR 13). Fighting it is survivable for a level 10-11 party but will take rounds they don't have. Every round of combat increases the chance of other beholders noticing

**If [[Snitch Ray]] intervenes:** This is where [[Snitch Ray]] does something he has never done. Something that goes against every fiber of his being. Something that costs him more than the PCs will ever understand.

He lies.

> *"I saw the Modron delegation leave with it. Northeast exit. Three minutes ago."*

He broadcasts this telepathically — not just to the accusing beholder, but to every beholder within his range. It's a fabrication. A cold, deliberate, professional lie from the Eyecon whose entire identity is built on documentation, reporting, and truth through surveillance.

The accusing beholder turns. The northeast exit is 500 feet away. It rockets toward it, screaming about modrons.

[[Snitch Ray]] says nothing. His telepathic link to the PCs is silent for exactly four seconds. Then:

> *"Move."*

He doesn't explain. He doesn't acknowledge what he did. He goes back to feeding navigation data.

But the PCs know. The snitch lied. For them. And somewhere in the bitter, joyless architecture of [[Snitch Ray]]'s personality, something just shifted.

### Encounter: Panicked Gauths (Service Corridor)
The last 100 feet to the service exit portal pass through a maintenance corridor — narrow, dimly lit, designed for mortal workers. It should be empty. It isn't.

Two **gauths** — lesser beholder-kin, the bottom of the beholder social hierarchy — have fled the civil war into the service tunnels. They're not loyal to anyone. They're not brave. They're terrified, cornered, and running on pure survival instinct. When they see the PCs enter the corridor, they see:
1. Mortals (weak, killable)
2. Carrying something (valuable, stealable)
3. Between them and the exit (problem)

They attack.

**Panicked Gauths (x2)** — MM p.26, CR 6 each
- Medium Aberration | HP 27 each | AC 18 (+4 Dex, +4 natural) | Speed fly 20 ft. (good)
- **Bite +4 melee** (1d6)
- **Eye Rays (6):** Max 2 per round, ranged touch +7, range 60 ft.:

| Ray | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| Sleep Ray | Will DC 14 or *sleep* for 1d4 minutes |
| Inflict Moderate Wounds | 2d8+3 damage, Will DC 14 half |
| Dispel Magic | As *dispel magic* (CL 5) |
| Scorching Ray | Ranged touch, 4d6 fire |
| Paralysis Ray | Fort DC 14, paralyzed 2d4 rounds |
| Exhaustion Ray | Fort DC 14, *exhausted* |

- **Stunning Gaze (Su):** 30-ft. range, Will DC 14 or stunned 1 round
- All-Around Vision: cannot be flanked
- **No antimagic cone** — gauths lack the central eye ability of true beholders

**Tactics:** Panicked, not strategic. They fire randomly — sleep rays at whoever's closest, scorching rays at whoever's carrying the case. If one gauth is killed, the other attempts to flee past the PCs toward the main sphere (where it will immediately be disintegrated by the crossfire, but it doesn't know that). They do not coordinate. They do not plan. They're scared animals with eye rays.

**[[Snitch Ray]]'s guidance:**
> *"Two gauths in the service tunnel. Panicked. Firing blind. They're CR 6. You're level 10. Don't embarrass me."*

If the PCs take more than 3 rounds, [[Snitch Ray]] adds: *"The structural integrity of the Panopticon is degrading. The service portal will destabilize in approximately 90 seconds. I mention this only as context."*

**DM Notes:**
- This is the adventure's only direct combat, and it should feel like swatting flies compared to the environmental chaos the PCs just survived. Two panicked gauths versus a level 10-11 party is not a fair fight. That's the point — after navigating sixty beholders, antimagic cones, a timed heist, and a civil war, the final obstacle is two scared runts. The anticlimax IS the joke
- The real danger isn't the gauths — it's time. Every round spent fighting is a round the portal destabilizes. The encounter is a speed bump, not a wall
- If a PC gets sleep-rayed while carrying the case and drops it, [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s resonance bleeds through the cracks. Nearby beholders feel it. The timer accelerates. Suddenly two panicked gauths are a very big problem

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] on Stage
Through the chaos, the PCs catch a glimpse of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. He hasn't moved from the keynote stage. The civil war rages around him — disintegration rays, screaming beholders, exploding vendor booths — and he's floating at the center of it, goblet of purple fire in his tentacle, surveying the destruction with an expression of absolute contentment.

He's not surprised. He's not worried. He's not fleeing.

He *orchestrated* this. The theft. The pulse. The civil war. The convention's collapse into beholder-on-beholder violence. This was always the plan. Not just steal [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] — but destroy the Conclave of Eyefellows in the process. Break the one institution that proved beholders could cooperate. Shatter the proof that ego could be channeled into community. Leave nothing behind but the memory that when beholders tried to build something together, it ended in disintegration.

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] didn't just steal an artifact. He killed an idea. The idea that beholders could be anything other than what they are: solitary, supreme, and alone.

He catches the PCs' eyes across the chaos. One eyestalk. A wink.

He raises his goblet. Purple fire catches the light of sixty eye rays.

### DM Notes
- **The civil war should be SPECTACLE.** Describe it in cinematic terms: beholders dueling in three dimensions, vendor booths detonating, the Panopticon's architecture cracking under combined eye ray fire. The PCs aren't the heroes of this scene — they're witnesses to a catastrophe they caused, sprinting through the wreckage of something that took four hundred years to build
- **[[Snitch Ray]]'s lie is the adventure's emotional hinge.** He lied. The surveillance specialist. The documentarian. The being whose entire purpose is recording truth. He fabricated evidence to protect the PCs. This isn't heroism — [[Snitch Ray]] would be offended by the word. It's pragmatism. The mission matters more than his principles. But the PCs should feel the weight of it: [[Snitch Ray]] sacrificed the one thing he valued about himself (his reliability as a source of truth) for them. That's a kind of loyalty he'll never admit to
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] destroying the Conclave is the adventure's thematic gut punch.** The funniest mission ends with [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] committing cultural genocide. He didn't just steal — he burned down the house. [[Dazrathuur]]'s life's work, four centuries of trying to build beholder cooperation, destroyed in five minutes because [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] needed a distraction. The comedy was the weapon. The laughter was the cover. When the PCs look back at this mission, the joke should curdle. They helped destroy the only proof that beholders could be more than monsters. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] did it smiling

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal deposits the PCs in the back room. The matching uniforms are singed, torn, and smell like ozone and burned olive oil (a vendor booth casualty). The ego-dampening case is cracked but holding. [[The Iris Prime]]'s resonance bleeds through the fractures, filling the room with a low thrum that makes every PC feel slightly more certain about everything.

The egg's scream and the Prime's thrum create a harmony. Not a pleasant one — a structural one. Like two load-bearing walls leaning against each other. [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|The Coinspiratorium]] vibrates with a new frequency. Customers in the shop above pause, feeling something they can't name: a certainty that they should buy something, that the transaction is important, that spending gold HERE matters more than spending it anywhere else.

The Crown is practicing on them.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] Arrives
He's last through the portal. Chains intact. Grill gleaming. Goblet still half-full of something purple that is definitely still on fire. He floats into the back room with the energy of a being who just had the best day of his very long life.

> *"THAT. Was. ART."*

He's beaming. Every eyestalk radiates satisfaction.

> *"The speech? Flawless. The heist? Surgical. The civil war?"*

He laughs. The laugh of someone who just watched something beautiful happen.

> *"They're STILL fighting. They'll be fighting for WEEKS. Every beholder in that room thinks every other beholder stole the Prime. The accusations alone will generate seventeen new blood feuds. [[Dazrathuur]] is going to spend the next DECADE trying to hold the Conclave together."*

He pauses. The grin widens.

> *"He won't. It's over. The Conclave of Eyefellows is done."*

He says this with no remorse. Not cruelty — just the casual certainty of a being who treats other people's achievements as terrain features. [[Dazrathuur]]'s life's work? Obstacle. Removed.

### [[The Iris Prime]]
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] takes the cracked case. Opens it. [[The Iris Prime]] rises from the shattered dampening material and floats before him, iris-shaped, shifting through amethyst and gold and absolute black.

For a moment — just a moment — every eyestalk goes still. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] looks at [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] and [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] looks back, because that's what it does: it reflects the ego of whoever's watching.

What [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sees in it is private. But the golden circuits on his hide flare bright, brighter than the PCs have ever seen, and for one heartbeat the beholder crime lord and the crystallized fragment of beholder supremacy are the same color: pure, burning gold.

> *"...Yeah."*

That's all he says. Quiet. Not for the audience. For himself.

Then the showmanship returns:

> *"Yo [[EyeQ]]. We got a delivery."*

### The Workshop (Third Visit)
Nine artifacts orbit the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]. [[The Iris Prime]] takes its place and the orbital ring pulses — each artifact resonating with the new addition. The Halo burns. The Pearl hums. The Key rotates. The Egg screams. And now [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]] adds its own frequency: a low, steady throb of CERTAINTY that anchors the entire structure.

The Crown looks more like a crown now. Specifically, it looks like [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. The overall shape — the orbital ring, the central void, the radiating energy patterns — has resolved into something that resembles a beholder's eye socket. A throne for a single eye. A frame for a single identity. Nine artifacts serving one ego.

Three slots remain empty. The lattice is visibly incomplete — gaps in the orbital ring where the remaining artifacts will go. The Crown vibrates with anticipation. It's hungry. It's close. It can almost taste completion.

[[EyeQ]] hovers beside the device, eyestalks tracking [[The Iris Prime|the Iris Prime]]'s integration. The circuits on [[EyeQ]]'s shell are blazing — not pulsing anymore, but STEADY. A constant golden glow. [[EyeQ]] looks less like a beholder-kin and more like a component of the device. The distinction is collapsing.

> *"Nine of twelve. The identity architecture is now self-reinforcing. The Prime provides the... anchor."*

A pause. The longest pause [[EyeQ]] has ever taken.

> *"The Crown now knows who it serves. That knowledge is permanent. Even if the remaining components are never installed, the Crown will wait. For him. For as long as reality persists."*

[[EyeQ]]'s eyestalks retract.

> *"It is patient now. It was not patient before."*

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Countdown

> *"Nine down. Three to go."*

He says it looking at the Crown. At the shape of his own ego reflected in stolen artifacts and broken promises. The golden circuits on his hide are synchronized with the Crown's pulse. His body and the device are running on the same clock.

> *"Three more. Then it's finished. Then everything changes."*

He turns to the PCs.

> *"You know what the best part of today was? It wasn't the speech. It wasn't the heist. It was looking at that room — sixty beholders, all of them thinking they're the center of everything — and KNOWING that I'm the only one who's RIGHT."*

He lets that sit.

> *"Because I'm the one building the machine that proves it."*

He floats toward the Workshop stairs, toward the Crown, toward the future he's been building since before the PCs were born.

> *"Three more. Then nobody argues. Then it's FACT."*

### The Letter from the Director

Three days after the Conclave, a letter appears on the shop counter.

Not in anyone's quarters. Not slipped under a door. On the **counter**. In the middle of the day. While [[Snitch Ray]]'s surveillance network was active. While [[Iris Major]]'s security protocols were engaged. While the Crown Infinite's ambient detection field covered every inch of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]].

The letter is on paper that feels heavy — heavier than paper should. Holding it feels like holding a verdict. The handwriting is ancient, precise, and carries a faint radiance that makes the reader's eyes water. Not divine light, exactly. The afterimage of divine light. The memory of what it felt like to glow.

There is no envelope. No seal. No return address.

> *To the operatives of Vizzok the Opulent:*
>
> *I have watched your progress with professional interest. You are effective. You are complicit. These are not mutually exclusive.*
>
> *The device your employer is constructing will, upon completion, restructure the foundational relationship between debt and reality across all planes of existence. My agency has spent four centuries preventing outcomes of this magnitude.*
>
> *Agent Quill believes you can be reasoned with. Auditor Pentival believes the mathematics will convince you. Agent Docket has filed a report recommending immediate extraction and detention.*
>
> *I have not yet decided which of them is correct.*
>
> *You have until the device reaches twelve components. After that, the decision will no longer be mine.*
>
> *— Casseel*
> *Director, O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.*

The moment the last PC finishes reading, the letter self-destructs. Not with fire — with *light*. The paper brightens, slowly, like a sunrise compressed into three seconds, until the words dissolve into warm, tired radiance. The light fades. The letter is gone. What remains: a faint warmth on the reader's hands that lingers for hours, and the impression — not visual, not magical, just *felt* — of very old, very tired eyes that have seen too many people make the wrong choice.

**The Residue:**

The warmth on the reader's hands is divine residue — trace energy from a being that was once a deva. It's not detectable by *detect magic*. It IS detectable by anyone who knows what divine energy feels like.

[[Snitch Ray]] knows what divine energy feels like.

If the reader interacts with [[Snitch Ray]] within 24 hours of reading the letter, there is a chance he notices. **Spot DC 16** for [[Snitch Ray]] (his eyestalks are calibrated for exactly this kind of anomaly). On success, he stares at the PC's hands for two seconds too long. His eyestalks tighten. He says nothing — immediately. But within 1d4 hours, he reports to [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] that he detected "residual extraplanar energy consistent with a celestial-class entity" in the shop.

The consequences depend on [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s current paranoia level:
- **Phase 4 (The Vice):** [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] interrogates the PCs directly. Not aggressively — worse. Quietly. *"Somebody holy touched something in my shop. I don't got holy friends. Do you?"*
- If the PCs confess the letter's existence: [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s central eye locks on the speaker. *"Casseel. She used to be an angel. Gave it up to chase people like me. You know what that means? It means she cared enough to stop being divine. That's commitment I gotta respect. It's also commitment that's gonna get her killed."* He doesn't threaten the PCs. He threatens Casseel. Which is worse

**Avoidance:** The divine residue fades naturally within 24 hours. Cold water and salt accelerate the process (Knowledge [religion] DC 15 to know this). If the reader washes their hands with salt water within an hour, the residue dissipates completely. [[Snitch Ray]] detects nothing. The letter becomes a private matter between the PCs and the Director.

**DM Notes:**
- **This is [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]'s final patience.** The escalation is complete: Pentival was curiosity. Quill was warmth. Docket was procedure. Casseel is *judgment*. Each agent represents a different kind of pressure, a different appeal to the PCs' better nature. Casseel's letter says: I've watched my agents try everything. Now I'm deciding whether you're worth saving or whether you go down with your employer
- **"You have until the device reaches twelve components" is a deadline.** Three components remain. Three quests. The PCs now know the clock. After twelve, [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] stops asking and starts acting. What "acting" means is deliberately unspecified — it could be arrest, extraction, or something worse. The ambiguity is the threat
- **The letter bypassing all of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s security is itself a message.** [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] can reach them anywhere. Inside the shop. Inside [[Offledger]]. Inside the Crown's detection radius. If Casseel wanted [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], she could get to him. She hasn't — because she wants the case to be complete. But the *capability* is demonstrated
- **The self-destructing letter contrasts with Pentival's persistent letters.** Pentival sends paper that stays. Casseel sends paper that burns. Pentival wants a conversation. Casseel wants a decision. The medium is the message
- **The divine residue subplot creates immediate tension.** The PCs must decide within hours: wash their hands (literally), or risk [[Snitch Ray]] detecting the contact. This forces a choice before they've had time to process the letter's contents. Casseel planned this — the residue is not accidental. It's a test of operational security. If the PCs can't hide a letter from their employer's surveillance, they can't be trusted as informants

### DM Notes
- **"The Crown now knows who it serves" is the debrief's most chilling revelation.** [[EyeQ]]'s assessment means the device has achieved a form of... consciousness? Intentionality? It's not alive. But it has a preference. It waits for [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] the way a throne waits for a king. Even unfinished, the Crown has committed to its purpose. The remaining three artifacts will complete its power. But its loyalty is already resolved. This changes the endgame calculus: even sabotaging individual components won't change the Crown's fundamental alignment. It's [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s. Period
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s final speech — "I'm the only one who's RIGHT" — is the campaign's villain thesis crystallized.** Every beholder believes they're the center. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s the one building the infrastructure to prove it. He's not wrong about the belief — he's wrong about the solution. The Crown doesn't validate his certainty. It weaponizes it. The difference between confidence and tyranny is consent: a confident being believes they're important. A tyrant makes sure nobody else CAN be
- **The Conclave's destruction should haunt the PCs.** It was funny in the moment — beholders disintegrating each other over a missing rock. In retrospect, it was annihilation. [[Dazrathuur]] tried to build something. It was fragile, imperfect, and probably doomed. But it was TRYING. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] burned it to cover a theft. The PCs were the hands. The comedy was the weapon. This is how the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] operates: it makes destruction entertaining so you don't notice the cost until it's too late
