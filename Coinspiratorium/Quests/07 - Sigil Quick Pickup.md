# "Visit Sigil for a 'Quick Pickup'"

> *"I don't need infinite doors. I need the right door, one time."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs to Sigil — the City of Doors, the multiversal crossroads, the one place in existence that sits at the center of everything and belongs to no one — to acquire [[The Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once|the Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once]] from a reclusive collector. He calls it a "quick pickup." Nothing in Sigil is quick. Nothing in Sigil is simple. And the key has a property [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] neglected to mention: it enforces absolute honesty in a 10-foot radius. No lies. No bluffs. No comfortable fictions. Every word within arm's reach of this key must be literally, verifiably true.

For a group of indentured adventurers working for a beholder crime lord and lying to everyone — including themselves — about what they're building, this is a problem.

This is the honesty episode. The adventure's challenge isn't combat, traps, or puzzles — it's truth. The PCs must navigate Sigil's chaotic social landscape, convince a collector who has refused every buyer for three centuries, survive the attention of the Lady of Pain, and escape the City of Doors, all without speaking a single lie. In a campaign built on debt, deception, and contracts with fine print, this is the mission where the fine print becomes legible.

**Tone arc:** Unease (briefing without [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]) → Wonder/comedy (arriving in Sigil, culture shock) → Investigation/tension (finding the Collector) → Vulnerability/catharsis (the truth challenge) → Existential dread (the Lady's shadow) → Action/desperation (the exit) → Quiet revelation (the debrief)

**Primary challenges:** Social navigation under enforced honesty, confronting the truth about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]], evading the Lady of Pain's attention, escaping Sigil under pressure
**Combat:** One major encounter (Harmonium confiscation patrol), environmental hazards from the Lady's attention; this is a roleplay-heavy adventure with combat as a complication, not a centerpiece

## Target Item
**[[The Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once]]** — A brass key, four inches long, unremarkable in every visible way. It could be any key from any locksmith in any city on any plane. Tarnished. Slightly bent. The bow is shaped like a simple ring, no ornamentation. It weighs a few ounces. Looking at it, you'd walk right past it in a junk drawer.

That's the point. The most powerful key in the multiverse looks like nothing. Because truth doesn't need to look impressive. It just needs to work.

**Properties:**

- **Universal access:** When inserted into any lock, door, portal, gate, seal, ward, or barrier of any kind, the key opens it. Perfectly. Silently. Without resistance. Arcane locks dissolve. Divine seals break. Planar wards part. Doors that aren't even locked swing open, because the key doesn't just *unlock* things — it *opens* them. The distinction matters. An unlocked door is still closed. This key makes it *not closed*
- **One use:** After opening a single door, the key becomes inert brass. No restoration, no recharging, no wish spell. One door. One opening. Permanent. The key spent itself on truth, and truth, once spoken, cannot be unspoken
- **Truth field (10-foot radius):** No creature within 10 feet of the key can knowingly speak a falsehood. Attempts to lie cause the words to die in the speaker's throat — not painfully, not dramatically, they just *don't come out*. Written lies within the field blur and fade. Illusion spells suffer a 50% failure rate. Disguises feel *wrong* to observers (Spot DC to see through them reduced by 10). The field does NOT compel speech — creatures can remain silent. It does NOT detect lies — omissions, technically true statements, and misleading truths all function normally. It simply makes falsehood impossible. No save. No spell resistance. *Antimagic field* does not suppress it. The key's truth is not magical. It's ontological
- **Teleportation defense:** If a creature within 10 feet speaks a knowing falsehood (which should be impossible, but the multiverse has edge cases), the key teleports to a random location within 1 mile in Sigil. This has happened eleven times in three hundred years. The Collector always finds it again
- **The key does not hum, glow, or radiate.** It does nothing to announce itself. It simply *is*. The most honest object in the multiverse has no reason to advertise

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Dimensional pivot — the key gives the Crown the ability to open a single, permanent doorway between [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s domain and every other plane simultaneously. Not a portal — a *door*. One opening. Irrevocable. Through that door, the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s three laws flow outward into all of reality. The key doesn't need to open every door. It opens THE door — the final barrier between [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s rewritten reality and the existing multiverse. After that, the door stays open. The key is spent. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s laws become everyone's laws, everywhere, forever.

The key opens one door. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] only needs one.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The back room of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]. The PCs arrive to find something wrong: **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] isn't here.**

[[Iris Major]] stands at the head of the table. Composed. Polished. Eyestalks arranged in a precise radial pattern that communicates "I am in charge and this is not a discussion." She's holding a slim folio — operational details, already prepared, already approved.

To her left, [[Major Look]] hovers with visible agitation. His eyestalks keep darting toward the stairway that leads down to the Workshop — the restricted area the PCs have never been allowed to enter. From below, a faint hum. A vibration that wasn't there last month. The walls of the back room have developed new patterns — hairline fractures filled with faint golden light, like circuits warming up.

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is downstairs. With [[EyeQ]]. Working on the device.

### [[Iris Major]]'s Briefing

> *"Lord Vizzok is occupied with a critical integration phase and has delegated this contract to my authority. Before we begin: your questions about his availability will be noted and not answered. Proceed from that understanding."*

She opens the folio.

> *"The target is a planar artifact held by a private collector in Sigil. The City of Doors. You will be given ingress, a contact, and a deadline. The operation is categorized as 'acquisition through negotiation,' which is our way of saying: buy it, trade for it, or convince someone to give it to you. Do not steal it. Do not fight for it. The collector and the artifact have... properties that make conventional approaches inadvisable."*

**If PCs ask what properties:**
> *"The artifact enforces honesty within its immediate proximity. No creature near it can speak a falsehood. This includes you. This includes anyone you're speaking to. This includes—"*

She pauses. Something flickers across her expression — the nearest thing [[Iris Major]] ever gets to discomfort.

> *"—anyone who might ask you what you're acquiring this for, or who sent you, or what your employer intends to do with it."*

She lets that sit.

> *"You will need to be... careful with your words."*

### [[Major Look]]'s Image Consultation

[[Major Look]] inserts himself into the briefing with the urgency of a man whose professional reputation is at stake.

> *"Sigil. The most public location in the multiverse. Every faction, every plane, every species — they're all there. Watching. Judging. Forming opinions. And you're walking in representing the brand."*

He circles the PCs, eyestalks scanning for dress code violations.

> *"Rules. One: no starting fights. The Harmonium patrols Sigil and they are not the flexible, bribeable sort of law enforcement we prefer. Two: do not mention Lord Vizzok by name in public. Three: do not insult the dabus. Four: do not, under any circumstances, acknowledge or attract the attention of—"*

He stops. His eyestalks retract slightly.

> *"The Lady. You know who I mean. Do not look at her. Do not speak to her. Do not walk where her shadow falls. If you see a floating woman with blades in her hair, you walk the other way and you do it calmly, because running attracts attention and attention is the thing we are AVOIDING."*

His voice drops.

> *"She is not part of this operation. She is not a factor. She is the one variable in the multiverse that Lord Vizzok does not account for, because she cannot be accounted for, and that is all you need to know."*

### Logistics

[[Iris Major]] resumes, unfazed.

- **Portal:** A door in [[Offledger]]'s south wall — currently a broom closet — will, when opened with the provided portal key (a copper coin minted in the lost city of Moil — don't ask where [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] got it), deposit the PCs in the Clerk's Ward of Sigil. The door opens from the other side only — they'll need the exit key for the return trip
- **Exit portal key:** A tuning fork that vibrates at the resonant frequency of [[Offledger]]'s dimensional shell. Strike it against any door frame in Sigil within 24 hours, and the door becomes a portal home. After 24 hours, the fork goes dead. *"Do not lose it. Do not break it. Do not use it to clean your teeth."*
- **Currency:** Standard gold works in Sigil, but [[Iris Major]] provides 200gp in Sigil trade bars (universally accepted, untraceable). *"The petty cash fund. There will be an accounting."*
- **Contact:** A tout named [[Krix]], who can be found at the Fortune's Wheel tavern in the Lady's Ward. *"[[Krix]] is a distributed cranium rat collective operating in a stolen Harmonium overcoat. They are reliable, expensive, and deeply unpleasant. Do not call them 'rats.' They bill by the question: 5 gold per inquiry, 20 for a guided tour."*
- **Target name:** The Collector is known as **[[Rue Kethani]]**. She lives in the Clerk's Ward. She does not advertise. Finding her address requires local intelligence. *"This is what [[Krix]] is for."*
- **Timeline:** 24 hours. The exit tuning fork is calibrated to a specific planar alignment window. After that, the PCs are stranded in Sigil until alternative extraction can be arranged, which [[Iris Major]] describes as *"expensive, slow, and not your employer's problem."*

### The Absence

Before the PCs leave, [[Major Look]] adds one more thing:

> *"Lord Vizzok wanted me to tell you something. His words, not mine."*

He straightens, imitating [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s cadence with practiced precision:

> *"'Quick pickup. In and out. Don't overthink it.'"*

The hum from the Workshop below intensifies briefly. The golden circuits in the walls flicker. Something down there is being fed, built, grown. The PCs are standing in a building that is slowly becoming a machine.

### DM Notes
- **[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s absence is the first scene's most important detail.** He has NEVER missed a briefing. He lives for the pitch — the monologue, the showmanship, the moment where he gets to be the center of the room. The fact that he sent [[Iris Major]] means the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s construction has reached a phase that demands his personal attention. The PCs should feel the absence. It should feel wrong, the way a room feels wrong when someone who always fills it isn't there
- **[[Iris Major]]'s hesitation when explaining the truth field is critical.** She's the most composed Eyecon. She doesn't flinch. But the idea that the PCs might be forced to tell the truth about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] — to a stranger, in Sigil, where everyone is listening — makes even [[Iris Major]] pause. That tells the PCs everything they need to know about how dangerous this honesty mechanic is
- **[[Major Look]]'s briefing on the Lady of Pain is the campaign's first direct acknowledgment that something exists that [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] can't handle.** Every previous adventure positioned [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] as the smartest person in any equation. Now the PCs learn there's a being he "does not account for." This reframes [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]: he's powerful, but he's not omnipotent. The Lady of Pain is proof that the multiverse contains things his Crown cannot touch
- **The Workshop hum and the golden circuits are the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] becoming visible.** After six artifacts, the device is taking shape. The PCs' home base is changing. This should feel gradual but unmistakable — [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] is no longer just a shop. It's a component

## Scene 2: The City of Doors

### Arrival
The broom closet door opens into an alley. The PCs step through and Sigil hits them like a wall of *everything*.

**Immediate sensory overload:**
- **Visual:** The city curves *upward*. Sigil is built on the inside of a hollow ring — a torus — and looking up, the PCs can see buildings on the opposite side of the ring, hanging upside down, half a mile above their heads. Streets climb the walls. Rooftops face each other across a void of hazy amber light. There is no sun. The light comes from everywhere and nowhere. It's perpetual late afternoon
- **Sound:** Every language ever spoken, simultaneously. The clang of a fire genasi blacksmith. The chanting of a Bleaker street preacher. A bariaur hawking fried something on a corner. An argument between two tieflings in a dialect that makes the PCs' teeth itch. Under it all, a low hum — the city itself, vibrating at a frequency that communicates *I am old and I do not care about you*
- **Smell:** Incense, sulfur, bread, something floral and alien, ozone from a nearby portal, and the unmistakable scent of a city where ten thousand species live in close proximity and have very different opinions about hygiene
- **Population:** Within the first 30 seconds, the PCs see: a modron walking a dog-sized rust monster on a leash, a githyanki selling maps from a hovering carpet, two aasimar sharing a pipe outside a tavern with a sign in Infernal, a gelatinous cube wearing a sandwich board that reads "TOURS — 3 GP — I CONTAIN MULTITUDES," and a perfectly ordinary-looking human woman pushing a cart of turnips. The human woman is the most suspicious thing in the alley

**The PCs are "Clueless."** That's not an insult — it's Sigil slang for material plane visitors. Everyone can tell. The way they look up. The way they flinch at the geometry. The way they walk in a straight line instead of angling for the optimal route between portals. In Sigil, being Clueless means being a mark, a tourist, and a source of entertainment.

### The Clerk's Ward
The portal deposited them in the Clerk's Ward — Sigil's administrative district. It's the closest thing the city has to "orderly": rows of identical stone buildings housing bureaucratic offices, record halls, and legal practices. The streets are cleaner than most of Sigil. The architecture is merely improbable rather than impossible. The residents walk with purpose. Nobody makes eye contact.

This is where the factions process paperwork, file claims, and adjudicate disputes. It is boring on purpose. Boring is safe. Boring keeps the Lady's attention elsewhere.

### Retailable Loot — Sigil Trade Goods

*Available throughout the city exploration scenes. Sigil is the multiversal crossroads — its market produces the best side-loot variety in the campaign. Items reflect planar diversity.*

**Environmental detail:** Posted on a wall, a notice in Common, Planar Common, Infernal, Celestial, and six other languages:

> *BY ORDER OF THE CLERK'S WARD ADMINISTRATIVE COUNCIL: ALL PLANAR ARTIFACTS OF TRANS-DIMENSIONAL SIGNIFICANCE MUST BE REGISTERED WITH THE WARD OFFICE PRIOR TO REMOVAL FROM SIGIL. FAILURE TO COMPLY WILL BE REPORTED TO THE HARMONIUM. FINE: 500gp OR EQUIVALENT.*

This notice is relevant. The PCs will not register the key. The Harmonium will care.

### DM Notes
- **Sigil should feel like the most overwhelming place the PCs have ever visited — not because it's hostile, but because it's EVERYTHING.** The aboleth vault was alien. Mount Celestia was holy. Sigil is the entire multiverse compressed into a city. It's not dangerous the way a dungeon is dangerous — it's dangerous the way a major city at rush hour is dangerous to someone who's never seen a crosswalk
- **The "Clueless" label should sting.** The PCs are experienced adventurers who've robbed angels and faced aboleth memory sheriffs. In Sigil, they're tourists. Play up the locals' casual dismissal — not cruelty, just the indifference of people who see Prime visitors every day and are not impressed
- **The artifact registration notice is Chekhov's bureaucracy.** The PCs will leave Sigil with an unregistered artifact of trans-dimensional significance. The Harmonium will have something to say about that

## Scene 3: Finding the Collector

### [[Krix]]
Fortune's Wheel is an upscale tavern in the Lady's Ward — the kind of place where celestials, fiends, and mortals drink at the same bar and the house rules include "no summoning at the table" and "tips are mandatory across all planes of existence." The PCs find [[Krix]] at a corner booth.

[[Krix]] is a **cranium rat collective** — forty-seven telepathic rats sharing a single distributed intelligence, operating inside a hollowed-out Harmonium officer's greatcoat. Two rats form the "face," perched on each other's shoulders at collar height, whiskers twitching in synchronized patterns that approximate facial expressions. A tail occasionally emerges from a sleeve. The coat buttons are done wrong.

[[Krix]] speaks telepathically, broadcasting to all creatures within 15 feet. The "voice" has a committee-meeting quality — slight disagreements between individual rats resolving in real-time.

> *"Welcome to — wait — yes, welcome to Sigil. You are — they're Clueless, obviously — you are Clueless, yes? Looking for information? We sell information. Five gold per question. Twenty for a guided tour. No refunds. No — that's our policy — no refunds."*

**If PCs ask about [[Rue Kethani]]:**
> *"Rue. Yes. The honest woman. Everyone — most of us — yes, everyone knows Rue. She lives at 14 Quill Street, Clerk's Ward. Upstairs flat. Door's never locked because — tell them — because nothing near her CAN be locked. The key won't allow it. That'll be five gold."*

**If PCs ask about the Key That Opens Any Door:**
A brief internal disagreement. Several rats shift position inside the coat. Then:
> *"We know of it. We don't — we SHOULDN'T discuss it in public. Powerful people want that key. The Harmonium wants it registered. The Fated want to tax it. The — no, don't mention HER — certain other parties have... standing opinions about artifacts that affect doors in the City of Doors. Understand? Five gold."*

**If PCs ask what the "standing opinions" are:**
> *"We didn't — we shouldn't have said that. Forget we — yes, forget it. Some things in Sigil, you don't talk about. You definitely don't talk about a key that opens any door in the city where every door is a portal, because if you think about that for more than two seconds, you'll understand why SHE might have FEELINGS about it, and nobody wants to be nearby when SHE has feelings. Five gold. Last question on that topic."*

### En Route to Rue
The walk from Fortune's Wheel to 14 Quill Street takes the PCs through the Clerk's Ward at ground level. It's a 20-minute walk. Along the way:

**The Harmonium Patrol:** A squad of four Harmonium officers — the "Hardheads," Sigil's self-appointed police force — marches past in formation. Heavy armor. Matching tabards. Identical disapproving expressions. They're not looking for the PCs specifically, but they're looking at *everyone* with the intensity of people who believe order is the highest virtue and disorder is a personal insult.

One of them — the squad leader, a half-orc woman with a jaw like a fortress — pauses and looks at the PCs. Just looks. Then moves on.

*Spot DC 15:* She noted their equipment, their bearing, and the fact that they're armed Clueless moving with purpose through the Clerk's Ward. She'll remember them.

**Razorvine Ambush:** Three blocks from the Fortune's Wheel, the PCs cut through a narrow alley between a records office and a condemned tenement. The walls are thick with **razorvine** — the canonical Sigil hazard, a parasitic plant that grows on every surface and cuts like broken glass. It's everywhere in the Hive Ward. It shouldn't be this deep in the Clerk's Ward. And it shouldn't be *moving*.

Four masses of razorvine detach from the walls and drop onto the PCs. These are **razorvine blights** — animate plant creatures spawned by Sigil's wild magic, razorvine colonies that have achieved a crude, hungry intelligence.

**Razorvine Blights (x4)** — CR 3 each (custom plant creatures)
- Medium Plant | HP 30 | AC 15 (natural armor) | Speed 20 ft., Climb 20 ft.
- **Vine Lash +6 melee** (1d6+3 slashing plus bleed)
- **Constrict (Ex):** On a successful grapple (+8), deals 1d6+3 slashing per round and the target bleeds
- **Bleed (Ex):** 1 HP/round per hit, stacking. Stopped by DC 12 Heal check or any magical healing
- **Fire Vulnerability:** Double fire damage. Fire causes immediate grapple release
- **Plant traits:** Immune to mind-affecting, poison, sleep, paralysis, stunning, critical hits
- **Morale:** When 2+ blights are destroyed, the remainder flee into the walls and vanish

**Tactics:** The blights drop from above — PCs are flat-footed in the surprise round unless Spot DC 16 notices the vine movement. Two blights target the front and rear PCs with constrict; two lash from the walls at anyone who isn't grappled. The alley is only 5 feet wide — no flanking room, limited maneuvering. The blights use the terrain.

**DM Guidance:** This is a brief, violent resource tax — not a set-piece. The encounter should take 3-4 rounds and cost the PCs HP and healing before the social core of the adventure (Rue's truth field). The bleed mechanic means even a quick fight leaves the PCs visibly injured — walking into Rue's apartment with razorvine cuts and bloodstained robes undercuts their professional image. The razorvine is a canonical Sigil hazard made monstrous: the city itself biting at interlopers.

**After the fight:** The vine mass on the walls goes still. The blights' remains dissolve into dry husks within minutes. A local — a tiefling barber sweeping his stoop — glances at the PCs and shrugs. *"Razorvine got hungry. Happens more in the Hive, but it's been creeping into the Ward lately. Welcome to Sigil."*

**The Dabus:** Halfway down Quill Street, a **dabus** floats past. Dabus are the Lady of Pain's servants — humanoid figures that hover a foot off the ground, silent, communicating in visual rebuses that appear in the air around their heads like thought bubbles made of light. This one is repairing a section of wall. It doesn't acknowledge the PCs.

But as they pass, the rebus above its head shifts — for one instant, before returning to construction diagrams — into an image of a **key**. Then it's gone. The dabus continues working.

The PCs can't be sure they saw it. But they did.

**[[Agent Quill]]:** Three blocks from Rue's address, a familiar face steps out of a side street.

[[Agent Quill]] looks different in Sigil. Not physically — the changeling's form is still the "default" nondescript human, the forgettable face, the sensible clothes. But they're *comfortable* here. Relaxed. Sigil is neutral ground — no faction has authority, no god has jurisdiction. For a spy, it's paradise.

> *"Fancy seeing you here. Don't worry — I'm not following you. Well."*

A pause. That almost-sincere smile.

> *"I'm not ONLY following you. I'm also here on business. Sigil's a hub. Intel moves through here faster than anywhere else in the multiverse. I just happen to be in the right ward at the right time."*

**If PCs are hostile:**
> *"I'm not here to cause problems. We're on the same street. That's all. But since we're talking — your employer sent you to Sigil for something specific. I know it's a 'quick pickup.' I know it involves a collector. I don't know what the artifact is. If you wanted to tell me..."*

They let the sentence hang.

**If PCs share information:**
Quill listens. Nods. Files it. Doesn't react visibly, but something shifts behind the eyes.

> *"Interesting. I'll pass that along. Be careful in there — [[Rue Kethani]] doesn't play games. If you're not ready to hear yourself talk, you might not like what comes out."*

**If PCs refuse:**
> *"Fair enough. I respect boundaries. I'll be around if you change your mind. Sigil's a small city — we'll probably bump into each other again."*

They will.

### DM Notes
- **[[Krix]] should be fun but brief.** They're a comedic interlude and a navigation tool, not a major NPC. The "committee disagreements" in their telepathic voice should get a laugh without slowing the pace. 2-3 questions, then the PCs have what they need
- **The Harmonium patrol is setup for Scene 6.** The squad leader noting the PCs means the Harmonium is already aware of armed Clueless in the Clerk's Ward. When the PCs later leave with an unregistered artifact, the patrol will respond
- **The dabus rebus — the momentary image of a key — is the first sign that the Lady of Pain is aware of the artifact.** The dabus didn't choose to show that image. It leaked. The Lady's attention is already on the key, and her servants' behavior reflects it. This should be unsettling, not alarming — a whisper, not a shout
- **Quill's appearance here is about positioning.** [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is monitoring [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s artifact acquisitions. They know the PCs are in Sigil. They don't know what the target is. Quill is genuinely fishing for intelligence — but Quill is also, in their way, checking on the PCs. The "I'm not only following you" line is as honest as Quill gets. Remember: Quill will encounter the truth field later, and the contrast between this conversation and that one is the point

## Scene 4: The Truth

### 14 Quill Street
The building is unremarkable — a three-story stone structure in the Clerk's Ward, sandwiched between a records office and a locksmith (the irony is not lost on Rue). The door to the upstairs flat is, as [[Krix]] promised, unlocked. It couldn't be locked if someone tried. The key's proximity keeps every lock in the building permanently open.

The PCs climb narrow stairs to a landing. The door to Rue's flat is ajar. Warm light spills out. The smell of tea and old paper.

### Entering the Truth Field
The PCs feel it the moment they step through the door. Not pain. Not pressure. A *settling*. Like something in their chest unclenches. Like the low-level background hum of every social performance they've ever maintained — being polite, being confident, being fine, being okay with what they're doing for a living — just... stops.

The truth field doesn't hurt. It clarifies. And clarity, for people who've been lying to themselves for months, is devastating.

**Mechanical effect (immediate, no save):** Every PC within 10 feet of the key loses the ability to speak falsehoods. They can remain silent. They can speak partial truths, technically true statements, and misdirections. But they cannot say something they know to be false. The words simply will not form. Players should be informed of this mechanic clearly — what they say at the table IS what their characters say, and if the statement is a lie, it doesn't come out.

**The pearl's echo:** Any PC who touched [[The Primordial Memory Pearl|the Primordial Memory Pearl]] (Quest 6) experiences a secondary effect: the truth field amplifies truthful memories. The vision of young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] — *"I just need a little more time. I'll pay it back. I swear."* — resurfaces with new clarity. In the pearl's presence, the PC told themselves it was "noise," like [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] said. Here, they can't maintain that lie. The memory is real. It happened. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] was once a debtor. The truth field won't let them pretend otherwise.

### [[Rue Kethani]]
She's sitting at a desk covered in contracts, keys, and legal documents, holding a cup of tea that has gone cold. She's a human woman who looks seventy and has lived three hundred and forty years — Sigil's non-linear relationship with time preserving her body while her mind accumulated centuries of absolute honesty. Her hair is white, pulled back severely. Her features are sharp — not unkind, but precise. She looks like a woman who has never said an unnecessary word.

The key sits on her desk, among a dozen other keys. It is, as described, completely unremarkable. Brass. Tarnished. Slightly bent. The PCs might not even notice it if they weren't looking.

Rue looks up when they enter. She studies them with the calm assessment of a woman who has spent three centuries evaluating people's honesty and has become very, very good at it.

> *"You're here for the key."*

Not a question.

> *"Sit down. I'll make fresh tea. You'll need it."*

### The Negotiation
Rue already knows why people come to her. The key is the most famous artifact in Sigil that nobody talks about. She's been approached by fiends, celestials, archmages, planar merchants, faction leaders, and at least three beings that claimed to be gods. She's refused every one.

She explains this without pride. It's just a fact.

> *"In three hundred and twelve years, no one who's come for this key has been able to answer my question. Not because they lied — they can't lie here. Because they didn't KNOW. They wanted the key for its power without understanding what that power meant. A key that opens any door is not a tool. It's a commitment. You're choosing which door matters most. And if you choose wrong, you don't get another chance."*

She sets down tea. It's good tea. Sigil imports from every plane.

> *"So. Here's my question."*

Her eyes are steady. No hostility. No games. Just a woman who has lived in truth so long that she's forgotten how to do anything else.

> *"What door does your employer plan to open? And what comes through when it opens?"*

### The Challenge
This is the adventure's centerpiece. The PCs cannot lie. They can:

**Option A — "We don't know."**
If this is genuinely true — if the PCs honestly have no idea what [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] plans to open — the words come out. Rue accepts them. But she doesn't give them the key.

> *"Then go home. Ask your employer. Come back when you have an answer. I'll be here. I'm always here."*

This ends the negotiation. The PCs must either find another way to acquire the key (extremely difficult — see DM Notes) or return to the table with more honesty.

**Option B — Partial truth.**
The PCs tell Rue what they suspect without committing to certainty. "We believe our employer is building a device. We believe this key is a component. We believe the device will change reality." These statements, if genuinely believed, pass the truth field. But Rue is a contract lawyer. Partial truth is her native language, and she recognizes it instantly.

> *"You 'believe.' You 'suspect.' You think you can come to me with hedged language and lawyerly qualifications? I was a contract lawyer for sixty years before I came here. I wrote clauses that would make a devil weep. Don't try to out-hedge ME."*

She leans forward.

> *"I'm not asking what you believe. I'm asking what you know. There IS a difference. And if you're honest with yourself — really honest, the kind of honest this room forces on you — you know more than you're admitting. Not to me. To yourselves."*

This pushes the PCs toward Option C.

**Option C — The truth.**
The PCs tell Rue what they actually know. What they've seen. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] — its components, its purpose, its laws. The artifacts they've already delivered. The visions from the pearl. Young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s voice. "Above the gods." Everything.

The truth field means they can't exaggerate or embellish. They can only state what they genuinely know and believe. And stating it — out loud, in sequence, to a stranger — is the first time the PCs have ever assembled the full picture.

This is the moment the PCs hear themselves describe what they've been doing. Not in fragments. Not as individual missions. As a whole. They are building a device that will rewrite reality, elevate a beholder crime lord to godhood, and enslave every soul in the multiverse through weaponized debt.

They've known this. They've suspected. But saying it out loud, in a room where lying is impossible, makes it real in a way it never was before.

Rue listens to all of it. She doesn't interrupt. Her expression doesn't change. When they finish, she's silent for a long time.

> *"Thank you."*

She picks up the key.

> *"That's the first honest answer I've received in three hundred and twelve years."*

### Rue's Decision
Rue gives them the key. But not for free. She extracts a promise.

> *"I'm not sentimental about this artifact. It's a key. Keys are meant to be used. I've kept it for three centuries because nobody who wanted it understood what it was for. You do. You understand what door your employer plans to open. And that means you understand what it would mean if that door were closed."*

She holds the key out.

> *"Promise me. Not a contract — I'm done with contracts. Not an oath. Just a promise, spoken here, where you can't lie. When the time comes — and it will come — use whatever leverage you have to close the door your employer opens."*

She waits.

**If the PCs promise honestly:** The words come out. The truth field confirms the intention is genuine. Rue nods, once, and places the key in the nearest PC's hand.

> *"I don't need you to promise you'll succeed. I was a lawyer. I know the difference between a promise to try and a guarantee of outcome. The trying is enough."*

**If a PC tries to promise without meaning it:** The words won't come. The truth field blocks the false promise exactly as it blocks any lie. The PC opens their mouth and nothing happens. Rue watches this with the weary patience of someone who has seen it many, many times.

> *"Take a moment. Think about whether you mean it. I'll wait."*

**If the PCs refuse to promise:** Rue doesn't give them the key. She's not hostile — she's just done.

> *"Then I can't help you. The key deserves better than being used by someone who doesn't care what it opens. I'm sorry you came all this way."*

The PCs must find another approach (see DM Notes for alternatives).

### Additional Dialogue

**If PCs ask about Rue's history:**
> *"I was a contract lawyer from Amn. I came to Sigil three hundred years ago following a case. I found the key in a market stall — three gold pieces, the merchant didn't know what he had. When I picked it up, I felt the truth field for the first time. And I realized I'd spent sixty years of my life speaking words I didn't mean, arguing positions I didn't believe, defending contracts designed to trap people."*

A pause.

> *"I couldn't go back. Not wouldn't — couldn't. The key changed me. Or I changed myself and the key made it permanent. Either way. I haven't lied in three hundred and twelve years. I haven't missed it."*

**If PCs ask if anyone else has inquired about the key:**
> *"Many. Most recently, a Mercane trade consortium offered me seventeen thousand gold pieces and a seat on their board. Before that, a pit fiend offered me a layer of the Abyss — which tells you how much he understood about what I value. And before all of them..."*

She hesitates. Not from deception — from memory.

> *"A creature with many eyes visited me. Very polite. It didn't try to take the key. It didn't make an offer. It just... asked if the key was happy here."*

She looks at the PCs.

> *"Was your employer always a crime lord? When he visited me, he seemed... smaller. More careful. Like someone who'd been hurt and was trying to figure out how to make sure it never happened again."*

This was [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. Centuries ago. Before the empire. Before the Crown. He came to Sigil, found Rue, looked at the key, and asked if it was *happy*. The question of a being who once understood what it meant to need a door to open.

**If PCs ask whether Rue knows about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] specifically:**
> *"I didn't have a name for it until you told me. But I've known someone was building something. The key... vibrates. It has for decades. Something in the multiverse is pulling at it. Something that needs a door opened very badly. I assumed it was a god. Gods always need doors."*

A faint, tired smile.

> *"A beholder. That's new."*

### DM Notes
- **This scene is the emotional core of the entire campaign so far.** Not the combat. Not the heist. The moment where the PCs, unable to lie, describe what they've been building to a stranger. The truth field forces them to confront something they've been avoiding: they KNOW what the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is. They've known for quests. They've seen the visions, heard the warnings, noticed the changes in [[Offledger]]. Saying it aloud, in an honest room, is a confession — and confessions change people
- **Rue is the campaign's clearest moral mirror.** She's a woman who chose truth over convenience, permanently, and built a life around it. She represents the path the PCs could take: stop lying, stop serving, stop pretending. It costs everything. But it's real. She should feel like the most grounded, most *sane* person the PCs have met in the entire campaign — which, surrounded by beholder crime lords and planar spy agencies, she might actually be
- **"Was your employer always a crime lord?"** This line from Rue should hit like a truck. The PCs heard young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] in the pearl's visions — scared, begging for time. Now Rue confirms: she met [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], and he was different. Smaller. More careful. Hurt. The PCs are serving a villain who was once a victim. That doesn't make him less dangerous. But it makes him more human. More tragic
- **Rue asking about the key's "happiness" — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s question — is devastatingly revealing.** It shows that [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]], at some point, thought about artifacts as beings with feelings, not just tools. He cared. He's not the same person anymore. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] isn't just his ambition — it's his armor. He's building a device that will make him invulnerable because he once felt vulnerable, and he never, ever wants to feel that way again
- **If the PCs refuse the promise or can't make it honestly:** Alternative approaches to acquiring the key: (1) The PCs can leave and return later in the session after genuine character discussion about their intentions. (2) A single PC with genuine resolve can make the promise alone. (3) In extreme cases, the PCs can attempt to trade something of equivalent truth-value — a genuine secret, a real confession, something that costs them. Rue values honesty, not combat. But DO NOT let the PCs bypass this scene's emotional work through cleverness. The point is the honesty, not the key

## Scene 5: The Lady's Shadow

### The Shift
The moment the key leaves 14 Quill Street, Sigil changes.

Not dramatically. Not all at once. The PCs step into the street carrying [[The Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once|the Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once]], and the City of Doors... notices.

**Phase 1 — The Walls (Immediate):**
The stone walls along Quill Street develop a thin sheen of moisture. Not water. Something thicker. Darker. It catches the light wrong. It's coming from *inside* the stone.

The walls are sweating. In Sigil, when the Lady of Pain pays attention to a specific location, the city's architecture responds. Stone bleeds. Metal hums. Wood groans. The city is her body, in a sense, and the walls are her skin, and the PCs just picked up something that matters to her.

**Phase 2 — The Silence (Within 5 minutes):**
The ambient noise of the Clerk's Ward — the chatter, the footsteps, the mundane sounds of city life — fades in a 30-foot radius around the PCs. Not dramatically. Gradually. Conversations nearby trail off. Pedestrians adjust their paths to avoid the PCs without seeming to realize they're doing it. A shopkeeper closing his shutters pauses, frowns, and goes inside early.

The PCs are carrying a bubble of quiet through a noisy city. The silence isn't magical — it's instinctive. Everything in Sigil has evolved to recognize when the Lady is watching, and everything in Sigil knows to *get out of the way*.

**Phase 3 — The Shadow (Within 10 minutes):**
A shadow falls across the street ahead of the PCs. It shouldn't be there — Sigil's ambient light comes from everywhere, leaving no strong shadows. But this one exists: long, sharp-edged, thrown by nothing visible. It stretches across the cobblestones like a sundial marking an hour that doesn't exist.

The shadow is shaped like a tall figure with blades fanning from its head.

The PCs do not see the Lady of Pain. They see her shadow. That is enough.

**What happens if a PC steps into the shadow:** Nothing. Nothing visible, nothing mechanical, nothing painful. But the PC feels, for one heartbeat, an awareness so vast and so impersonal that their sense of self nearly collapses. They are *seen* — not as a person with a name and a history, but as a data point. A variable. An event occurring in a location the Lady monitors. The feeling passes in a second. The PC is shaken (no mechanical effect, purely narrative). They do not want to feel that again.

**What happens if the PCs linger:** The shadow doesn't move. The moisture on the walls darkens to red. A second dabus appears at the end of the street, floating silently, watching. Its rebus shows a simple image: a door, closing.

Time to leave.

### The Maze Threat
If the PCs stay in the Lady's shadow for more than one minute, or if they do anything aggressive in its presence — draw weapons, cast spells, run — a third dabus appears. Its rebus shows a labyrinth. In Sigil, this is the ultimate threat: the Lady of Pain can trap a creature in an extradimensional maze from which there is no escape. Gods have been mazed. Powers have been mazed. No one has ever returned from the Lady's maze through their own efforts.

The rebus lingers for five seconds. Then the dabus floats away.

**The dabus did not maze the PCs.** It *showed them the option*. This is the Lady's way of communicating: I see you. I know what you're carrying. I am choosing not to act. Do not give me a reason to reconsider.

### DM Notes
- **The Lady of Pain is not an encounter. She is a weather event.** You don't fight the weather. You don't negotiate with the weather. You adjust your plans and you move. The Lady's presence in this scene should feel like a natural disaster that's choosing, for now, to pass overhead. The PCs can't fight her, can't reason with her, and can't prepare for her. They can only leave
- **Why doesn't the Lady act?** She doesn't care about the PCs. She doesn't care about [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. She cares about Sigil. The Key That Opens Any Door is relevant to Sigil — every door in the city is potentially a portal, and a key that opens ANY door has implications. But the key is leaving. The PCs are taking it out of Sigil. That resolves the Lady's concern. She's not stopping them — she's escorting them out
- **The shadow should be the most frightening thing in the campaign.** Not because of what it does — but because of what it DOESN'T do. The PCs have faced vampires, aboleth memory sheriffs, and Solar angels. All of those were beings they could interact with — fight, negotiate, flee from. The Lady of Pain exists beyond interaction. She is not a being with motivations. She is a force. The shadow is her way of saying: I am here. You are small. Leave
- **The maze rebus is the scene's climax.** The dabus showing the labyrinth image is the Lady's equivalent of a drawn weapon. She didn't use it. But the PCs now know — viscerally, permanently — that they were one bad decision away from being removed from reality. This stakes-escalation is important: the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] wants to rewrite reality. The Lady of Pain IS reality, at least in Sigil. These forces are not in the same category
- **Do not have the Lady speak, appear, or directly interact with the PCs.** She communicates through her shadow, her dabus, and the city's architecture. The moment you give her dialogue or a face, she becomes a character. She's not a character. She's an axiom

## Scene 6: The Exit

### The Clock
The PCs need to get from Quill Street to any door frame where they can use the tuning fork to open their exit portal. Normally, this would take ten minutes. Currently, it will take longer, because:

1. The Lady's attention has destabilized nearby portals — doors that used to lead to other parts of Sigil now lead to random planes. Opening the wrong door could deposit a PC on Gehenna, the Beastlands, or a demiplane that consists entirely of filing cabinets
2. The Harmonium is mobilizing — the squad from Scene 3 reported armed Clueless in the Clerk's Ward, and the artifact registration notice means the Hardheads are looking for anyone removing unregistered items
3. The truth field is still active — every social interaction on the way out is constrained by honesty

### Running Through Sigil
The PCs need to find a stable door — one that hasn't been affected by the Lady's attention — and use the tuning fork. Stable doors can be identified by:

- **Knowledge (the planes) DC 15:** The PC identifies structural indicators of portal stability — door frames with even coloring, no vibration, no smell of ozone
- **[[Krix]] (if the PCs kept contact):** For 20gp, [[Krix]] provides a route to a stable door four blocks away: *"The Gatehouse District, west entrance. The door to the old Bleaker hospice. That portal frame hasn't shifted in decades — it's too boring for — yes, too boring for anyone to notice. Go. Quickly. Don't — hurry."*
- **Spot/Listen DC 18:** The PCs notice that the locals are avoiding certain streets — those streets have destabilized portals. Following the locals' paths leads to stable areas

### Encounter: Portal Spillover (Optional)
Two blocks from the stable door, one of the destabilized portals detonates. A door that previously led to a cobbler's shop now opens onto **Gehenna** — the plane of volcanic cruelty — and disgorges two **howlers** before slamming shut and sealing itself with a hiss of sulfurous air.

The howlers are confused, enraged, and in pain — Sigil's ambient energy burns them like sunlight burns vampires. They have approximately 5 minutes before Sigil's natural defenses eject them back to their home plane. They intend to spend those 5 minutes doing as much damage as possible.

**Howlers (x2)** — MM p.154, CR 3 each
- Large Outsider (Chaotic, Evil, Extraplanar) | HP 39 each | AC 17 (-1 size, +3 Dex, +5 natural) | Speed 60 ft.
- **Bite +8 melee** (2d8+5)
- **Quills:** Any creature striking a howler with a natural weapon or unarmed attack takes 1d6 piercing. Quills can also be fired as a volley — **+2 ranged** (1d6, 30 ft. range)
- **Howl (Su):** Continuous 60-ft. radius. Every creature in range must make **Will DC 12** each round or become *shaken* (cumulative — shaken creatures that fail again become *frightened*). This STACKS with the confession field — a frightened PC in the truth field will blurt their deepest fears while running
- Speed 60 ft. — they're fast. Faster than most PCs. In Sigil's narrow streets, they ricocheting off walls, snapping at pedestrians, and screaming at a frequency that makes stone crack

**Tactics:** The howlers don't target the PCs specifically — they target everything. They're portal-sick, disoriented, and lashing out. But the PCs are carrying a truth field, a stolen artifact, and the Lady of Pain's residual attention. They attract chaos the way lightning rods attract bolts.

One howler charges the PCs; the other rampages down a side street, drawing Harmonium attention. This actually HELPS: [[Drenna Valk]]'s squad diverts to contain the loose howler, thinning the checkpoint ahead (reduce Harmonium encounter by 2 officers if the PCs let the second howler run).

**Resolution:**
- **Fight:** The howlers hit hard (2d8+5 bite, 60 ft. speed) but have modest HP for level 8-9 PCs. The howl is the real threat — the will-sapping aura compounds with the truth field to create a nightmare where PCs are terrified AND involuntarily honest about it. *"I'M SCARED OF DYING IN AN ALLEY IN A CITY THAT ISN'T EVEN ON A REAL PLANE"* — confession roll 5
- **Run:** The howlers have Speed 60 ft. They pursue for 3 rounds, then get distracted by a fruit cart. Sigil adapts
- **Ignore:** If the PCs identify that the howlers are portal-sick (Knowledge [the planes] DC 14), they know the creatures will be ejected in 5 minutes. Avoiding them with Hide DC 15 or simply taking a different route costs time but no resources

**DM Notes:**
- This encounter is pure Sigil chaos — the kind of random planar emergency that happens when a city made of doors starts having door problems. The PCs didn't cause this (directly). Their presence with the key caused the Lady's attention, which destabilized the portals, which vomited out howlers. It's a chain reaction. In Sigil, everything is connected, and consequences ripple in ways nobody predicts
- The howl + truth field combo is the encounter's secret weapon. A PC who fails both saves is simultaneously frightened and unable to lie about it. The table should be laughing and panicking at the same time. "I THINK ABOUT DEATH CONSTANTLY AND THIS ALLEY SMELLS LIKE MY WORST CHILDHOOD MEMORY" is not a combat quip — it's a failed save
- If a howler runs loose and draws the Harmonium, this creates a tactical decision: the PCs can let it rampage (morally questionable, reduces Harmonium at checkpoint) or try to stop it (morally correct, preserves full Harmonium opposition). This is a small version of the campaign's central question: do you do the right thing when the wrong thing is easier?

### The Harmonium Confrontation
Three blocks from the stable door, the PCs encounter the Harmonium squad from Scene 3 — reinforced. The half-orc squad leader, **[[Drenna Valk|Measure-3 Drenna Valk]]**, has brought her full patrol: six Harmonium officers in formation, blocking the street.

> *"Halt. You are carrying an unregistered artifact of trans-dimensional significance. Under Sigil Regulatory Code Section 4, Subsection 12, you are required to submit the artifact for inspection and registration before removal from the City of Doors."*

She's not hostile. She's procedural. She genuinely believes she's doing the right thing.

> *"Surrender the artifact. Submit to processing. You'll be free to leave within... three to five business days."*

Three to five business days. The exit tuning fork expires in hours.

**The truth field complicates everything:**
- The PCs can't claim they don't have an artifact
- The PCs can't claim they've registered it
- The PCs can't claim to be anyone other than who they are
- The PCs CAN say: "We decline your request" (true), "We intend to leave peacefully" (true, if they do), "We are under a time constraint that makes processing impossible" (true)

**Diplomacy approach (DC 22):** Convince Drenna that the artifact's removal is time-sensitive and that registration can be handled retroactively. This requires a genuinely persuasive argument, not a lie — Drenna is Harmonium and respects honest conviction. If the PCs explain (truthfully) that they're operating under contract and the artifact must leave Sigil within hours, Drenna *might* accept a written acknowledgment of the violation and a promise to file the registration later. She's lawful, not inflexible.

**The key helps here:** Drenna is within the truth field. She can't lie either. If a PC asks her, "If we submit to processing, will we be free to leave with the artifact afterward?" she has to answer honestly. And the honest answer is: *"I... cannot guarantee that. The artifact may be flagged for further review by the Fraternity of Order."*

In other words: if the PCs surrender the key, they might never get it back. Drenna knows this. She can't pretend otherwise.

**If diplomacy fails — combat:**
Drenna gives the order. The Harmonium squad engages.

**[[Drenna Valk|Measure-3 Drenna Valk]]** — Fighter 9, Lawful Neutral
- HP 76, AC 22 (full plate + heavy steel shield), Init +1
- Attacks: +1 longsword +14/+9 melee (1d8+5)
- Power Attack, Cleave, Combat Expertise, Improved Disarm
- **Tactic:** Disarm the PC carrying the key. She wants the artifact, not blood
- Will save +7 (relevant for the truth field — she can't lie if she's within 10 feet)

**Harmonium Officers (x5)** — Fighter 5, Lawful Neutral
- HP 42 each, AC 20 (breastplate + heavy shield), Init +1
- Attacks: longsword +8 melee (1d8+3)
- Formation training: when adjacent to another officer, +1 AC and +1 to attack (stacks). A full formation of 5 grants +4/+4
- **Tactic:** Surround and grapple. They fight to subdue, not kill — lethal force requires Drenna's explicit authorization

**Important:** The PCs should NOT kill Harmonium officers. Killing law enforcement in Sigil draws significantly more attention — more patrols, faction involvement, and potentially the Lady's direct displeasure. Non-lethal takedowns, magical incapacitation, or escape are strongly preferred. If the PCs DO kill officers, Scene 6 escalates dramatically (see Scaling Notes).

**Escape:** If the PCs break through or bypass the patrol, they have a clear sprint (3 rounds of movement) to the stable door. The Harmonium pursues but can be outrun — their heavy armor and formation discipline make them slow. The PCs need to reach the door, strike the tuning fork against the frame, and step through before the patrol catches up.

### The Quill Moment
During the exit — either during the Harmonium confrontation or immediately after — [[Agent Quill]] appears.

Bad timing. The PCs are carrying the key. The truth field is active. And Quill walks into it.

The changeling's face flickers.

It's subtle. A shimmer, like heat haze over a road. Quill's "default" appearance — the nondescript, forgettable, trustworthy face — ripples. For a moment, the PCs see what's underneath: not a specific face, but an *absence* of face. Pale, smooth, featureless. The true changeling form. The mask that wears masks.

Quill realizes what's happening immediately. They step back — trying to get outside the 10-foot radius. But the damage is done. Their form is unstable. The truth field has disrupted whatever psionic or magical process maintains their disguise.

And Quill, for three seconds, is standing in the truth field. Unable to lie.

> *"I—"*

They stop. Recalibrate. Try again.

> *"I'm not here because they ordered me to follow you."*

A pause. Something raw passes across the featureless face.

> *"I'm here because I'm worried about you. What you're carrying — what you're building for him — it's bigger than you know. And I don't..."*

Quill steps out of the field. Their face reforms — the friendly mask, the warm eyes, the professional smile. But it's shakier now. Less convincing.

> *"...I don't want to see what happens when it finishes."*

They turn and walk away. Quickly. Before the truth field can pull anything else out of them.

### The Door
The stable door — an unremarkable wooden door in the wall of an abandoned Bleaker hospice — stands at the end of a narrow alley. The PCs strike the tuning fork. The door hums, then resonates, then opens into the familiar chaos of [[Offledger]].

They step through. The door closes behind them.

In Sigil, on Quill Street, the moisture on the walls dries. The shadow recedes. The ambient noise returns to normal. The Lady of Pain's attention moves on to other things.

The key is gone from Sigil. The City of Doors can relax.

### DM Notes
- **The Harmonium fight should be tense but not deadly.** Drenna and her squad are competent but not overwhelming for a level 8-9 party. The real tension is the constraint: don't kill them, don't linger, and don't forget the truth field means you can't bluff your way out. This fight rewards creative problem-solving — illusions (50% failure rate in the field), battlefield control spells, non-lethal damage, and flat-out running
- **The truth field turning on Drenna is the encounter's best trick.** When Drenna can't lie about whether the PCs would get the key back, she reveals the Harmonium's real agenda: confiscate, not process. The bureaucratic facade cracks. This mirrors the campaign's theme — every institution claims to serve you while actually serving itself
- **Quill's moment in the truth field is the scene's emotional payload.** It's the first time the PCs see Quill without the mask — literally and figuratively. Quill's genuine concern is REAL. The truth field confirms it. This complicates the PCs' relationship with [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] enormously: their handler isn't just using them. Quill actually cares. But caring doesn't stop Quill from doing their job. The warmth is real AND the manipulation is real. Both things are true. Welcome to the truth field
- **"I don't want to see what happens when it finishes" is Quill revealing they know more about the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] than they've let on.** [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] has been investigating. They know something bad is coming. And Quill — personally, genuinely — is afraid of it. This should plant the seed that [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] might become allies, not just handlers, if the PCs choose to work with them against [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

## Scene 7: The Debrief

### Back at [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]
The portal deposits the PCs in the back room. They're dry (a welcome change from Quest 6). They're uninjured (probably). They're carrying a brass key that looks like nothing and is worth more than most kingdoms.

The back room has changed since this morning. The golden circuit-lines in the walls are brighter. They pulse now — a slow rhythm, like a heartbeat. The air tastes faintly of copper. And the hum from the Workshop below is louder, constant, resolved from a vague vibration into something that sounds, unmistakably, like machinery. Something down there is *running*.

[[Iris Major]] meets them. She takes the key with two eyestalks, examining it with professional assessment. She is, as always, utterly composed.

> *"[[The Key That Opens Any Door But Only Once]]. Confirmed. Operational parameters match the target profile."*

She pauses. Looks at the PCs. Her composure doesn't crack, but something in her gaze shifts — the faintest acknowledgment that what they just did was more than a "quick pickup."

> *"Lord Vizzok will receive you. He's... available now. Downstairs."*

### The Workshop (First Visit)
For the first time, the PCs are allowed into the Workshop.

The stairway descends two flights below [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] into a vaulted chamber carved from the raw dimensional substrate of [[Offledger]]. The walls aren't stone — they're the plane itself, exposed, thrumming with golden energy. Every surface is covered in circuit patterns: spiraling, branching, interconnecting lines of light that pulse in coordinated rhythms.

And at the center of the room: **[[The Device|the Crown Infinite]]**.

It's not finished. But it's no longer abstract. Six artifacts orbit a central point in the air, each one held in a lattice of golden energy: [[The Vanishing Mirror|the Vanishing Mirror]], Lolth's Tapestry, [[Acererak]]'s Notebook, [[The Black Ledger|the Black Ledger]], the Halo, and [[The Primordial Memory Pearl|the Primordial Memory Pearl]]. They rotate slowly, leaving trails of light that weave together into a structure that looks, from certain angles, like a crown. From other angles, like an eye. From other angles, like a cage.

[[EyeQ]] hovers near the device, eyestalks extended to their maximum length, each one tracking a different artifact's orbit. [[EyeQ]] does not acknowledge the PCs. [[EyeQ]] is the most focused the PCs have ever seen it — the detached, analytical Eyecon utterly absorbed in the architecture of the thing it built.

And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is there.

### [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s Reaction
He looks different. Not physically — he's still the diamond-grilled, chain-draped, impossibly flamboyant beholder the PCs have always known. But something in his bearing has shifted. There's an intensity that wasn't there before. A focus. The showmanship is still present but it's thinner, a veneer over something deeper and more dangerous.

He's standing in front of the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s unfinished architecture, and for the first time, the PCs can see him in relation to the thing he's building. It's *his*. It looks like him — eyes, gold, geometric precision. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s self-portrait rendered in stolen artifacts and weaponized debt.

> *"The key."*

He holds out a tentacle.

[[Iris Major]] passes it to him. He examines it — turns it over, holds it up to the light. The truth field washes over him.

For exactly one second, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] goes still. Every eyestalk freezes. His central eye dilates. The truth field is touching him, and whatever it's showing him — whatever truth he's being forced to confront — it hits hard.

Then it passes. He exhales.

> *"Simple little thing, ain't it? Whole multiverse could turn on this. Whole REALITY could change because of a piece of brass that weighs less than a sandwich."*

He floats toward the device. The key lifts from his tentacle and takes its place in the orbital ring — the seventh artifact, joining the circuit. The golden lattice expands. The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]]'s structure gains a new dimension. The hum changes pitch. Something clicks into place that was missing before.

> *"Seven down. Five to go."*

**If the PCs mention Rue:**
> *"[[Rue Kethani]]. Yeah. I remember her."*

His voice softens. Just slightly.

> *"She still sitting in that apartment? Still drinking that tea? ...She was nice. Honest. Like, REALLY honest. First person I ever met who didn't want something from me."*

A beat.

> *"I asked if the key was happy. You know what she said? She said, 'Keys don't have feelings. But I take care of it.' Three hundred years later, she's still taking care of it. That's... that's something."*

He turns back to the device.

> *"Anyway. It's home now."*

**If the PCs mention the Lady of Pain:**
Every eyestalk retracts. The room temperature drops — not literally, but it feels like it. Even [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s performative confidence can't fully suppress his response.

> *"The Lady noticed? She... noticed the key moving?"*

He processes this.

> *"That's fine. She's a local problem. Sigil's her territory. The key's out of Sigil now. She got no jurisdiction here."*

He says this like a man reassuring himself.

> *"We're good. We're fine. She don't come to [[Offledger]]. She CAN'T come to [[Offledger]]. ...Right? [[EyeQ]]. She can't come to [[Offledger]]."*

[[EyeQ]], without looking up from the device:

> *"No data suggests the Lady of Pain's influence extends beyond Sigil."*

A careful, calibrated non-answer. [[EyeQ]] did not say "no." [[EyeQ]] said "no data suggests." Because [[EyeQ]] always speaks precisely, and precision, in the truth field's lingering aura, is a kind of honesty.

**If the PCs mention [[Agent Quill]]:**
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s reaction is immediate and volcanic — but cold. Not explosive. Controlled.

> *"[[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] is in Sigil. Of course they are. Sigil's an intel buffet. Question is: did they see you take the key? Do they know what it is? Do they know where it is NOW?"*

He looks at the PCs. Central eye. The anti-magic cone eye. The one that strips away enchantments, wards, and pretense.

> *"What did you tell them?"*

This is a dangerous moment. If the PCs shared intel with Quill, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] wants to know exactly what. If they didn't, he wants confirmation. And if they lie—

They can't. The truth field's residual effect lingers for 1d4 hours after contact with the key. The PCs are still in the honesty radius. Whatever they tell [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] right now has to be true.

### DM Notes
- **The Workshop reveal is the campaign's visual climax so far.** Six artifacts orbiting a central point, connected by golden light, forming the skeleton of a crown/eye/cage — this is what the PCs have been building. But in that second, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is forced to confront his own truth — whatever that is. He recovers fast, because [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] always recovers fast. He asked if the key was happy — a question that reveals more about young [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] than any vision. He thought about artifacts as things that could have feelings. - **"Seven down. Five to go."** The [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] is more than half complete. The countdown is real. Every remaining mission brings it closer to activation. The PCs are running out of time to decide what they're going to do about it

### Sigil Environmental Notes (3.5e Reference)

- **No deity can enter Sigil physically.** Divine spellcasters still receive spells (the connection to their deity functions, the deity simply cannot manifest)
- **Teleportation within Sigil works normally** — teleportation TO or FROM Sigil requires a portal
- **Portals:** Every door, archway, or opening in Sigil is potentially a portal. Portals require specific "portal keys" (items or actions) to activate. Without the correct key, a door is just a door
- **The Lady of Pain:** CR — (do not stat). Cannot be fought. Cannot be negotiated with. Cannot be detected by divination. Communicates through dabus and environmental effects. Can "maze" any creature (no save, no SR, no escape except DM fiat). Treat as a force of nature, not a creature
- **Dabus:** Construct-type servants of the Lady of Pain. Float 1 ft. off the ground. Communicate in visual rebuses. Do not speak. Do not fight unless defending themselves. Effectively unkillable (the Lady simply creates more). Attacking a dabus is a guaranteed way to attract the Lady's lethal attention

## Appendix: The Key That Opens Any Door — Ongoing Campaign Use

- **Between adventures:** The key's integration into the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] causes the most dramatic environmental change in [[Offledger]] yet. Doors in [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] begin *opening by themselves*. Not randomly — meaningfully. A door opens to reveal a room that wasn't there before. A cabinet opens to show a portal-like shimmer that disappears when investigated. The building's architecture is becoming fluid, responsive, alive. Doors that used to lead to storerooms now occasionally lead to other places — a glimpse of a frozen battlefield, a second of tropical heat, a flash of starlight. The key is teaching the Crown how doors work, and the Crown is practicing
- **Rue as intelligence asset:** If befriended, Rue contacts the PCs once between adventures 8 and 9 via a letter that arrives through a door that shouldn't exist (a manifestation of the key's influence). She's been researching planar contract law — specifically, the legal status of debts owed by entities who have ascended to higher metaphysical states. Her findings: *"Under the Axioms of Planar Commerce, any debt owed prior to ascension compounds at a rate proportional to the debtor's increase in power. If your employer activates his device while holding outstanding obligations, those obligations become infinite. The system cannot process infinite debt owed by its own architect. It's a divide-by-zero error in the fabric of reality."* She doesn't know about the IOU. But she's handing the PCs the mathematical framework to understand why it matters
- **The Lady of Pain's lingering attention:** The Lady noted the key's removal from Sigil. She doesn't pursue. But Sigil's portals to [[Offledger]] — which previously worked normally — become slightly less reliable. Doors that used to open smoothly now stick. Portal keys that used to work on the first try require a second attempt. It's subtle. It's not hostile. But it's the Lady's way of saying: I remember. [[Offledger]] is now on her radar. If the [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] ever threatens Sigil's sovereignty — ever tries to rewrite the rules inside the Lady's domain — she will respond. And her response will not be subtle
- **Quill's honesty moment:** The image of Quill's true face — featureless, pale, vulnerable — should resurface. Once in a dream. Once when the PCs next encounter Quill in their usual disguise (the contrast between the mask and the truth stings). And once at the endgame, if the PCs choose to work with [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]]: Quill drops the disguise voluntarily, as a gesture of trust. *"You've seen me already. Might as well be honest about it."* The callback to the truth field transforms a spy into a person
- **The promise to close the door:** This is the adventure's endgame hook. Rue asked the PCs to close the door [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] opens. The IOU — when placed on the counter — closes it. The PCs may not realize they're fulfilling Rue's promise when they do it. But they are. And if they remember, later, in the quiet after the Crown falls: they kept their word. In a campaign about debt and broken promises, keeping a promise might be the most radical act of all
