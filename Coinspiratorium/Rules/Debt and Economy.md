# Debt and Economy
*You owe money. A lot of money.*

## The Debt
The players owe [[Vizzok the Opulent|Lord Veezy]] more gold than the local economy technically contains. The exact amount should feel like a **real obligation** but not be so crushing that it **cripples progress**. It's narrative pressure with mechanical teeth.

### Starting Debt
*Exact amount TBD — guidelines:*
- Should be large enough that the PCs can't pay it off quickly
- Should be small enough that regular payments feel meaningful (not futile)
- Consider: **per-party debt** rather than per-player, so the group shares the burden
- The "freedom number" (full buyout) should feel impossibly distant at level 1 but theoretically reachable by endgame

### Veezy's Tribute Demands
Veezy's demands are **impulsive and inconsistent**. This is a feature, not a bug:
- He's just as likely to be **unfair** as he is to be **magnanimous**
- One week he demands an outrageous cut; the next he waves it off and gives them something generous
- His mood, his current schemes, and his ego all affect the number
- **This unpredictability IS the mechanic** — players can never fully plan around it

**DM Guidance:** Use tribute demands as a **pacing lever**:
- PCs doing too well? Veezy's feeling greedy this week.
- PCs struggling? Veezy's feeling magnanimous (or distracted by other business).
- Big plot moment coming? Veezy's demands spike to create pressure.
- PCs just pulled off something impressive? Veezy respects hustle — lighter tribute, maybe a bonus.

### Interest
*Consider:*
- Does the debt compound? If so, how often?
- Or is interest another tool in Veezy's impulsive toolkit — sometimes he remembers to charge it, sometimes he forgets?
- The threat of interest might be more useful than the math of interest

## Income Sources
- **Dungeon Loot** — the primary source of new wealth
- **Shop Sales** — selling inventory to customers at markup
- **Contracts & Side Jobs** — Veezy's assignments sometimes come with bonuses
- **Found Treasure** — not everything is on the books (but Veezy has eyes... many eyes, and [[Snitch Ray]] is always watching)

## Expenses
- **Leveling Up** — [[GP is XP]] (gold spent here is gone forever)
- **Tribute to Veezy** — regular payments, non-negotiable (amount variable)
- **Shop Overhead** — rent, restocking, repairs, security
- **Personal Gear** — sometimes you need to buy a sword before you can loot a dungeon
- **Bribes, Fines, and Mishaps** — the cost of doing business in [[Offledger]]

## The Core Tension
Every gold piece serves one of three masters:
1. **Power** (leveling)
2. **Profit** (shop inventory and sales)
3. **The Boss** (debt repayment)

Players must constantly choose which master to serve. Neglect any one for too long and consequences follow:
- Skip leveling? The dungeons get harder.
- Skip the shop? No income, no customers, no reputation.
- Skip Veezy? [[EyeRate]] shows up. Then it gets worse.

**The balancing act is the game.** Loot from dungeons must be split between these three demands, and there's never quite enough to go around.

## Enforcement Escalation
*What happens when you don't pay.*

Veezy is impulsive about tribute amounts, but he is SYSTEMATIC about enforcement. Missing a payment triggers a predictable escalation sequence. The PCs learn this sequence exists when they see it happen to someone else first. Then it happens to them.

### Tier Table

| Tier | Trigger | Response | Tone |
|------|---------|----------|------|
| **1 — The Reminder** | First missed payment | [[Snitch Ray]] delivers a memo. Polite. Professional. The memo uses phrases like "overdue balance" and "at your earliest convenience." It is printed on expensive paper | Bureaucratic annoyance |
| **2 — The Visit** | Second missed payment | [[Major Look]] arrives in person. He doesn't threaten. He *expresses concern about the brand*. He rearranges the shop. He critiques the PCs' inventory, appearance, and life choices. He leaves behind a revised tribute schedule with the amount increased by 15% | Passive-aggressive dread |
| **3 — The Audit** | Third missed payment | [[Iris Major]] conducts a full financial audit of the PCs' operation. She reviews every transaction, every expenditure, every gold piece. She finds discrepancies (she always finds discrepancies). The PCs owe more than they thought. Interest has been applied. Retroactively | Cold, clinical fear |
| **4 — The Conversation** | Fourth missed payment | Veezy himself. In person. In the back room. No chains, no grill flash, no showmanship. Just a beholder in a room, looking at you with eleven eyes, asking: *"We got a problem?"* This is the most frightening thing in the campaign because Veezy without performance is Veezy without the mask. The real one. The one who disintegrates | Existential terror |
| **5 — EyeRate** | Fifth missed payment | [[EyeRate]] shows up. He doesn't speak. He doesn't threaten. He hovers in the shop for 24 hours, following the PCs, watching everything they do. He doesn't blink. He doesn't leave. Customers stop coming. The PCs can feel his disintegration ray like a sunburn on the back of their neck. After 24 hours, he leaves. The next tribute is due immediately. It is double | Violence as atmosphere |
| **6 — The Example** | Sixth missed payment | The PCs never reach Tier 6. Tier 6 is what happened to the LAST crew that missed six payments. Nobody talks about what happened to them. Their shop is still in Offledger. It's empty. The shelves are bare. The ledger is open to the last page. The last entry reads: "BALANCE: SETTLED." There is no further explanation | The void |

### EyeRate — Stat Block

**[[EyeRate]]** — CR 8 (Beholder-kin Enforcer)
- Small Aberration, Chaotic Evil (loyal to Veezy)
- **HD** 8d8+24 (60 hp), **AC** 18 (+1 size, +3 Dex, +4 natural), touch 14, flat-footed 15
- **Speed:** Fly 30 ft. (perfect)
- **Attacks:** Bite +8 melee (1d6+1)
- **Eye Rays (4):** Each usable 1/round as a free action, max 2 per round. Range 60 ft., ranged touch +10:

| Ray | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| Disintegration Ray | As *disintegrate* (CL 8, 5d6 on failed Fort DC 18, or 30d6 to objects) |
| Fear Ray | As *cause fear* (Will DC 16, 1d4 rounds) |
| Telekinesis Ray | As *telekinesis* (CL 8, violent thrust — 250 lbs., 2d6 impact) |
| Inflict Ray | As *inflict serious wounds* (3d8+8, Will DC 16 half) |

- **Rage (Ex):** Below 30 hp: +2 attack, +2 damage, -2 AC for 5 rounds. Attacks nearest threat regardless of tactics
- **All-Around Vision:** Cannot be flanked
- **Saves:** Fort +5, Ref +5, Will +8
- **Skills:** Intimidate +16, Spot +14, Listen +12

**DM Guidance:** EyeRate is a THREAT, not a regular encounter. The PCs should never fight EyeRate unless the campaign has gone catastrophically off-rails. His purpose is to make the debt feel dangerous without ever actually collecting through violence. The THREAT of EyeRate is the enforcement mechanism. If EyeRate actually attacks, something has gone very wrong — either narratively or at the table. Use Tier 5 to scare. Use Tier 4 to terrify. Never reach Tier 6.

## The Hidden Fourth Drain
What the PCs don't know: every transaction through [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] — every sale, every purchase, every gold piece that changes hands — **nourishes [[The Device|the Crown Infinite]]** (Component III). Their economic suffering is literally fuel for Veezy's ascension.
