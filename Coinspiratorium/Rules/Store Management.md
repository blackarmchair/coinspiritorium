# Store Phase & Brand Economy
*"If it looks expensive, it is." — Veezy*

> Every gold piece that passes through [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]] is a performance. The Store Phase is where you rehearse.

---

## Purpose

The Store Phase is a structured mini-game that converts dungeon loot into liquidity through risk, presentation, and social maneuvering. It runs between dungeon contracts and serves as the party's primary income engine — and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s primary extraction tool.

---

## The Store Phase

The Store Phase occurs once between major dungeon contracts (or at DM discretion). It consists of four steps:

1. **Inventory Allocation**
2. **Bulk Sales Resolution**
3. **Auction Resolution**
4. **Tribute & Brand Mandates**

---

## Step 1: Inventory Allocation

After a dungeon, divide recovered wealth into:

- **Personal Gear** — kept by PCs (weapons, armor, consumables they intend to use)
- **Liquid Currency** — immediate gp (coins, gems, art objects)
- **Retailable Inventory Pool** — items to sell via the Store Phase

The *Retailable Inventory Pool* is resolved through the Bulk and Auction channels below. All item values reference the **full listed price** (DMG market price), not the standard 50% resale — the Store Phase replaces the default resale rules.

---

## Step 2: Bulk Sales (Stable Income)

The Bulk channel represents routine commerce — foot traffic, underworld buyers, casual mages, fences, and collectors. Reliable, but you're not getting rich.

### Base Conversion

All bulk inventory converts at **50% of listed price** before modifiers.

### Modifiers

**Store Tier Bonus:**

| Tier | Bonus |
|------|-------|
| 0    | +0%   |
| 1    | +5%   |
| 2    | +10%  |
| 3    | +15%  |

**Market Week Roll (1d6 + Store Tier):**

| Roll | Condition    | Modifier |
|------|--------------|----------|
| 1–2  | Slow week    | −5%      |
| 3–4  | Normal week  | +0%      |
| 5–6  | Strong week  | +5%      |
| 7+   | Surge week   | +10%     |

### Formula

`Bulk Revenue = Inventory Pool × (50% + Tier Bonus + Market Modifier)`

Bulk resale is **capped at 75%** unless extraordinary story conditions apply.

> **Design note:** At Tier 3, the Market Roll range is 4–9 (minimum normal week, likely surge). This is intentional — a Tier 3 store never has a slow week. The progression from "praying for a good roll" to "reliably strong sales" is part of the reward for investing in the brand.

---

## Step 3: Auction (Variance Engine)

*"Every item has a story. Some stories sell."*

The Auction channel lets PCs gamble on individual high-value items for potentially above-market returns. Each item is resolved with a separate roll.

**Auction Slots:**
- Tier 0: up to **1 item** per phase
- Tier 1–2: up to **2 items** per phase
- Tier 3: up to **3 items** per phase

### Auction Roll (1d20)

| Roll   | Result                      |
|--------|-----------------------------|
| 1–3    | No buyer — item retained    |
| 4–7    | 50% of listed price         |
| 8–12   | 75% of listed price         |
| 13–17  | 100% of listed price        |
| 18–19  | 125% of listed price        |
| 20+    | 150% of listed price + Complication |

### Modifiers

| Condition | Modifier |
|-----------|----------|
| Buyer is thematically matched to the item | +2 |
| Strong Diplomacy or Bluff roleplay during the sale | +2 |
| Item is suspicious, stolen, or illegal | −2 |
| Store is Tier 0 | −2 |

### Complications (Roll 20+)

A Complication means the sale succeeds at 150% — but something comes with it. Roll 1d4 or choose:

1. **The Buyer Returns** — They want more. They become a recurring NPC with escalating demands and possible quest hooks
2. **Unwanted Attention** — The sale was too visible. [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] agents, rival factions, or the item's original owner take notice
3. **Strings Attached** — The buyer pays in favors, IOUs, or trade goods instead of clean gold. The value is there, but it's inconvenient
4. **[[Snitch Ray]] Noticed** — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] hears about the premium sale. Next tribute demand increases proportionally. *"I heard business was good. Real good."*

**DM Guidance:** Auction revenue should not consistently exceed **40% of total Store Phase income**. If the PCs are over-relying on auctions, reduce buyer availability or introduce market saturation ("Your third +1 longsword this month? The collectors are full.").

---

## Store Tiers (Brand Progression)

The store's tier represents its reputation, reach, and the quality of clientele it attracts. Upgrades require gold investment and [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s approval — which he gives enthusiastically, since it's your gold improving his brand.

### Tier 0 — Backroom Pawn
*Where you start. Where [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] pretends you don't embarrass him.*
- 50% base resale
- 2 auction slots
- Low traffic, no specialty buyers

### Tier 1 — Curated Arcane Outlet
**Upgrade Cost:** ~5,000gp
- +5% resale bonus
- +1 to Market Week Roll
- Specialty buyers unlocked (collectors, guild agents, minor nobles)

### Tier 2 — Planar Brand
**Upgrade Cost:** ~15,000gp
- +10% resale bonus
- +2 to Market Week Roll
- Employee hires unlocked (see [[Debt and Economy]])

### Tier 3 — Interplanar Destination
**Upgrade Cost:** ~40,000gp+
- +15% resale bonus
- 3 auction slots
- VIP buyer table unlocked
- Increased [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] scrutiny

## Step 4: Tribute & Brand Mandates

### Tribute

After Store revenue is determined, [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] demands his cut.

Tribute is **impulsive and inconsistent** — this is a feature, not a bug. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s mood, his current schemes, and his ego all affect the number. Use the ranges below as guidelines, not formulas:

| Level Band | Recommended Tribute Range |
|------------|---------------------------|
| Low (1–5)  | 40–50% of net revenue     |
| Mid (6–10) | 30–40% of net revenue     |
| High (11+) | 25–35% of net revenue     |

**DM Guidance:** Treat tribute as a pacing lever (see [[Debt and Economy]] for full guidance). PCs doing too well? Veezy's feeling greedy. PCs struggling? He's feeling magnanimous — or distracted. The unpredictability IS the mechanic. Players should never be able to fully plan around it.

Failure to pay triggers the enforcement escalation sequence (see [[Debt and Economy#Enforcement Escalation]]).

### Brand Mandates

*"The store is not just a business. It is an aesthetic."*

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] may require mandatory cosmetic upgrades at any time. These cost 5–15% of recent net profit, provide minor mechanical bonuses, and reinforce his control over the operation.

| Mandate Level | Cost | Effect |
|---------------|------|--------|
| **Minor** | 5kgp | +2 to next Market Week Roll; +1 auction modifier for one phase |
| **Moderate** | 5–15kgp | Permanent +5% to resale cap; unlock new buyer types |
| **Major Rebrand** | 25k+ gp | Increase base resale from 50% to 55%; unlock additional auction slot; increase scrutiny and narrative risk |

### Brand Philosophy

2/3 of [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s aesthetic instincts should improve performance. 1/3 should be ego-driven and unnecessary. Occasionally one should backfire.

When [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is right, it reinforces his authority. When he is wrong, it reinforces his volatility. Both are desirable.

---

## Wealth Control Targets

Total gold drain across Tribute, Brand Mandates, and Store Upgrades should average **15–25% of expected Wealth-By-Level (WBL) gain per level**.

Party wealth should hover within:
- **90–110%** of standard WBL (DMG guidelines)
- Occasional spikes to **125%** are acceptable

Consistent inflation beyond that should trigger:
- Higher tribute demands
- Increased [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] scrutiny
- Expensive brand compliance mandates

---

## Narrative Purpose

The Store Phase is not bookkeeping. It is theater. And [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is always watching.

It serves to:
- Encourage theft and greed (loot selection becomes strategic)
- Reward social engagement (auction roleplay matters)
- Inject NPC story hooks (buyers, complications, mandates)
- Reinforce indenture (tribute is inescapable)
- Mirror [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]'s philosophy of imposed identity
- Act as a pacing lever between dungeon arcs
