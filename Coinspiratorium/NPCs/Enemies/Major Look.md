## Major Look
*Image / Discipline / Public Order*

Major Look is obsessed with appearances, optics, and "how things read." He believes disorder is weakness, embarrassment is failure, and anything sloppy reflects poorly on the brand. He handles punishments meant to be **seen**, not necessarily to kill — public examples, humiliations, disappearances with messaging.

Major Look genuinely thinks he's the good guy, because in his mind, chaos is the real villain.

**Vibe:** Authoritarian PR guy who thinks he's saving everyone from themselves.

**Role at the table:** He's the one who shows up when the PCs embarrass the brand. Sold a cursed item to the wrong customer? Major Look wants a word. Got into a public brawl? Major Look is *concerned about optics*. He makes the PCs feel like employees at a toxic company — which they are.

---

## Appearance
- Impeccably maintained — 3.5 feet in diameter, hide polished to a high sheen in alternating panels of deep crimson and gold
- Six eyestalks held in a precise fan arrangement, never overlapping, like a peacock's tail
- Central eye is a striking amber, always half-lidded, always *assessing*
- Wears miniature enchanted banners on each eyestalk — tiny Gold Standard pennants that flutter in nonexistent wind
- His teeth are capped in white enamel and gold — a perfect, terrifying smile
- Leaves a faint trail of perfume — something expensive, something you can't quite place, something that makes you check your own clothes

### Eye Rays (Su)
Six eyestalks, up to **two rays per round**, targeting different creatures within **120 ft.** Save DC: **20** (Charisma-based).

| Eye Ray | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Brand Correction** | *Baleful Polymorph* — target is transformed into something "more on-brand" (usually a gold-plated statuette, decorative lamp, or tasteful centerpiece) |
| **Image Consultant** | *Phantasmal Killer* — the target sees their worst public humiliation made manifest |
| **Makeover Beam** | *Polymorph Other* — cosmetic alteration; target's appearance changes to Major Look's specifications for 1d4 hours. Non-damaging but deeply unsettling |
| **Spotlight** | *Faerie Fire* + *Daylight* combined — target glows brilliantly, cannot hide, all attacks against them gain +2 |
| **Talking Points** | *Suggestion* — target must publicly praise the Gold Standard brand in their own words |
| **Blacklist Ray** | *Mark of Justice* — invisible brand appears on target; triggers *bestow curse* if target publicly embarrasses the organization |

### Special Abilities

**Antimagic Cone (Su)**
Central eye emits a 100-foot cone of antimagic, as *antimagic field* (CL 11th).

**Aesthetic Authority (Su)**
All creatures within 30 ft. must make a Will save (DC 20) or become **self-conscious**: -2 to Charisma-based checks and the creature compulsively adjusts their appearance, clothing, or posture (no mechanical effect beyond the penalty, but deeply annoying).

**Public Example (Su)** — *1/day*
Major Look designates one creature within 60 ft. and delivers a scathing public critique. All creatures within 30 ft. who can hear and understand him must make a Will save (DC 20) or be *frightened* for 1d4 rounds — not of Major Look, but of the idea of being next. The designated target is automatically *shaken* for 1 minute (no save).

**All-Around Vision (Ex)**
Cannot be flanked.

## Notes
- Major Look's punishments are never lethal — they're *visible*. He wants the brand protected, not bodies piling up. Bodies are bad optics.
- His Brand Correction ray is his signature move. Turning a disobedient contractor into a decorative lamp and placing it in the shop foyer is his idea of "a teaching moment"
- He maintains a mental list of every brand violation the PCs have committed. He will recite it. In full. With footnotes.
- The enchanted banners on his eyestalks are functional *prestidigitation* anchors — he can change their color and design at will, and frequently does to match the season, the mood, or "the current brand palette"
- He and EyeRate despise each other. EyeRate's methods are "unsightly." Major Look's methods are "useless." They're both right.
