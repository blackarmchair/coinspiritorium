# "The Debt Collector's Wake"

> *"I'm not going. You're going. Think of it as... a networking opportunity."*
> *"Wear something nice. These people notice shoes."*
> *"Oh, and bring the charter folio. Standard business stuff. You never know when someone wants to see your paperwork."* — [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]

---

## Adventure Summary
[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] sends the PCs to attend **The Recession**, an invitation-only salon hosted by a reclusive debt magnate named **[[Aldenmere Voss]]** at his private estate in [[The Superiority Complex]], [[Offledger]]'s most exclusive district. Veezy's stated reason is mundane: represent the brand, network, make nice with old money. He can't attend himself. Prior engagements. The PCs go in his stead.

During the gathering, [[Aldenmere Voss]] is murdered.

The investigation reveals that **every guest at the party independently attempted to kill Voss on the same night.** Five separate lethal actions, none coordinated, each sufficient to kill. The cause of death is genuinely ambiguous. A standard investigation catches the most proximate killer. A thorough one uncovers the full scope. A brilliant one traces every guest's fresh motivation back to a common source.

After the murder, [[Offledger]]'s debt law seals the estate and triggers a contestation period for Voss's most valuable holding: **[[The Instrument of Contested Debt]]**. During the adjudication process, an ancient claim surfaces in the PCs' own paperwork, the business documents Veezy told them to bring. The Coinspiratorium's charter folio contains, buried in layers of commercial filings, a chain of obligations predating every other claim. The Instrument falls into the PCs' hands before they understand what happened.

**The PCs were never told they were on a heist. They were on a heist.**

**Tone arc:** Casual unease (the briefing feels too simple) → Social tension (the party is a pressure cooker) → Shock (the murder is ugly and real) → Investigation (whodunit with escalating revelations) → Dawning horror (everyone did it, and someone made them) → Bureaucratic absurdity (the Instrument lands in their laps) → Moral weight (the debrief, where Veezy says nothing and everything)

**Primary challenges:** Social navigation, murder investigation with layered solutions, competitive claim adjudication, moral reckoning
**Combat:** One optional encounter (Creditor Zhaal's debt-combat challenge). This is an investigation adventure. Combat is escalation, not centerpiece.

## Target Item
**[[The Instrument of Contested Debt]]** — A scroll case made of bone and tarnished silver, sealed with a wax stamp shaped like a closed fist. Inside: a single sheet of legal vellum bearing a contract so old the ink has fused with the material. The text is in a dead language predating [[Offledger]]'s founding. It smells like old coins and regret.

The Instrument is a **meta-contract**: a binding framework that governs how debts between planes are adjudicated. Whoever holds it doesn't own a debt. They own the rules by which debts are settled.

**Properties:**
- **Custodial binding:** Cannot be sold, traded, gifted, or transferred while its custodian lives. Bound to the custodian's legal personhood under [[Offledger]] debt law. As long as they exist as a recognized entity, the Instrument is theirs.
- **Contestation clause:** Upon the custodian's death, claims may be asserted by any entity with a legitimate debt-obligation against the deceased or the Instrument itself. Claims are ranked by age, depth, and contractual proximity. The adjudication happens automatically; the Instrument *knows* who has the strongest claim. It vibrates in the presence of the rightful claimant. It goes still for pretenders.
- **Adjudication field (30-ft. radius):** While contested, creatures within 30 feet cannot misrepresent debts, obligations, or financial claims. Lies about money cause the speaker's voice to fade to silence. You can lie about anything else. You just can't lie about money.
- **Resonance with the Crown:** Hums in proximity to other [[The Device|Crown Infinite]] components. If the PCs are carrying anything from the Crown's circuit, they feel a sympathetic vibration between artifacts that were always meant to be part of the same system.

**[[The Device|Crown Infinite]] Purpose:** Contractual jurisdiction. The Instrument gives the Crown authority to adjudicate debts across all planes. Without it, the Crown can *create* universal debt (Law II) but cannot enforce it. With this component, every contract in the multiverse becomes subject to Veezy's court. Every promise becomes his to enforce. The Crown needs teeth. The Instrument is teeth.

## The Setup: What Veezy Actually Did

> **DM EYES ONLY**

The PCs know none of this going in. Veezy has told them nothing. His manipulation of the PCs is as deliberate as his manipulation of the guests.

Over several months (while the PCs were busy with Quests 8-10), Veezy has:

1. **Destabilized Voss's position.** Called in minor debts through Gold Standard subsidiaries. Spread solvency rumors through [[Snitch Ray]]'s network. Purchased Voss's outstanding obligations from third parties to create pressure from every direction at once. Voss was once untouchable. Veezy made him brittle.

2. **Curated the guest list.** The Recession is Voss's annual gathering, but this year, every invitation went out with encouragement from Veezy's network. Each guest was chosen because they could be provoked into action. Veezy didn't choose the killer. He chose five candidates and gave all of them reason enough to act.

3. **Armed each guest with fresh motive.** In the weeks before the gathering, each guest received specific, targeted intelligence designed to push them from resentment into action:
   - [[Meralda Thorne]] received leaked documents proving Voss's buyout clause was fraudulently inserted
   - [[Harath Kull]] had his minor debts quietly paid off, restoring enough standing to attend, and received intelligence about vulnerabilities in Voss's study protections
   - [[Tesslyn Voss]] learned through a "concerned friend" that her uncle had amended his will against her
   - [[Creditor Zhaal]] received anonymous intelligence that the custodial renewal ceremony created a window of vulnerability
   - [[Pettifer Groan]] was told, through three layers of intermediary, that Voss planned to release compromising documents about him at tonight's gathering as "entertainment." This was a lie. The most elegant manipulation of the five.

4. **Timed the gathering.** The Recession always falls on the anniversary of Voss's acquisition of the Instrument. If the custodian dies during the renewal window, the contestation period accelerates from thirty days to three hours. Veezy didn't choose this date. Voss did. Veezy just made sure the date mattered.

5. **Buried the claim.** Through a chain of purchased obligations, shell contracts, and inherited debt instruments, Veezy constructed the oldest and most deeply nested claim on the Instrument. Every link is legally sound. Then he wove the claim into the Coinspiratorium's commercial charter filings, layered into routine business documents, invisible unless activated by the Instrument's own adjudication process. He handed this folio to the PCs and called it "standard business stuff."

**The result:** A room full of people who each independently decided to kill the host. A custodian too weakened to survive any single attempt. A timing window that accelerates the legal process. A claim so old and clean it survives any audit. And a group of employees carrying it in their briefcases without knowing.

Veezy didn't kill the man. He didn't hire the killer. He built a world where five people killed the same man on the same night for five different reasons, and then he sent his employees to collect what fell loose.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

### Setup
The back room of [[The Coinspiratorium (Location)|the Coinspiratorium]]. The usual chaos of Veezy's briefings is absent. No goblet, no grill, no grandiose entrance. [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is already here when the PCs arrive, floating at the back of the room. Not performing. Waiting.

The circuits on the walls pulse slower than usual. Ten artifacts orbit in the Workshop below. [[Iris Major]] is present. [[Snitch Ray]] is absent. [[Major Look]] is absent. [[EyeQ]]'s usual perch is dark.

Veezy is wearing something new: a thin chain with a pendant shaped like a closed fist, cast in tarnished silver. It doesn't match anything else he's wearing. He has never worn anything that doesn't match.

### Veezy's Pitch

He's breezy about it. Almost dismissive.

> *"Tomorrow night there's a thing. The Recession. Fancy people in a fancy house comparing the size of their ledgers. Hosted by a man named [[Aldenmere Voss]] up in the Complex. Very old money. Very old everything, actually."*

He waves a tentacle.

> *"I got us an invitation. Well, the shop got an invitation. Voss is acknowledging our growth or something. Point is, someone needs to go and I'm busy."*

**If PCs ask what he's busy with:**

> *"Inventory."*

He doesn't elaborate.

> *"You go. You represent the Coinspiratorium. You eat the tiny food. You tell people we're a serious operation. You stay for the whole evening, because leaving early at these things is worse than not showing up."*

He pauses.

> *"Oh, and bring the charter folio from the filing cabinet. The big leather one with all our commercial registrations. Voss's people are formal. If someone asks for your bona fides, you want to have them."*

**If PCs ask why they'd need commercial papers at a party:**

> *"These people are creditors, darlings. They don't make small talk. They make inquiries. Having your paperwork ready is the difference between being a guest and being a curiosity."*

**If PCs press for more details about the gathering or Voss:**

> *"Voss is old school. Principled. Respected. The kind of man who remembers your name and your outstanding balance. Be polite to him. He matters in this city more than most people realize."*

Nothing about the Instrument. Nothing about claims, custodianship, or death. Just a party.

### Iris Major's Supplement

After Veezy leaves (and he leaves first, which never happens), [[Iris Major]] provides logistics with her usual efficiency.

> *"The Recession begins at eighth bell. Formal attire has been arranged. Weapons are permitted but should remain sheathed. You will arrive as representatives of the Coinspiratorium."*

She hands each PC a slim leather folder containing:

- **Invitation:** Embossed on dark vellum. The Coinspiratorium's seal alongside Voss's. Legitimate.
- **Guest list:** Fourteen names. Brief dossiers on each (see **The Guests** below).
- **The charter folio:** A thick leather binder of commercial documents. Articles of incorporation, trade licenses, supplier agreements, debt registrations. Boring. Voluminous. Legitimate. Buried in the middle, indistinguishable from routine filings: Veezy's chain-of-obligation claim on the Instrument.

**If PCs examine the charter folio (unlikely but possible):**
A Knowledge (Local) or Forgery check at DC 30 notices that one section of the filings has an unusual structure, more layered than standard commercial registration. At DC 35, a PC recognizes it as a chain-of-obligation document formatted to look like a trade license application. Below DC 30, it looks like paperwork. Because it is paperwork. That's the point.

### DM Notes
- **The tonal shift is the scene's most important signal.** Veezy has been a showman for ten quests. This briefing is casual, almost careless. When Veezy stops performing, pay attention to what he's not saying.
- **The missing Eyecons should register.** [[Snitch Ray]]'s absence means surveillance is already deployed. [[Major Look]]'s absence means image management isn't needed, because this isn't social for Veezy. It's operational. [[EyeQ]]'s silence speaks for itself.
- **The charter folio is the adventure's central trick.** Veezy told the PCs to bring a weapon and they picked it up without looking at it. If a PC examines it carefully, reward them, but don't expect it. The DC 30 check is deliberately high. This should only be catchable in retrospect.
- **Veezy's pendant is the Instrument's seal.** The closed fist. He's been wearing it for weeks. The PCs probably won't notice. When they see the seal on the Instrument later, let them connect it if they can.

## Scene 2: The Recession

### The Superiority Complex
[[The Superiority Complex]] is [[Offledger]]'s wealthiest district, a neighborhood that sits slightly above the rest of the plane in both altitude and attitude. The streets are paved with compressed contract paper, centuries of fulfilled obligations underfoot. The air smells like expensive parchment.

[[Aldenmere Voss]]'s estate occupies a corner that angles away from the rest of [[Offledger]] like a jaw set in disapproval. A three-story structure of dark wood and brass, older than Veezy's arrival in Offledger by at least fifty years. The front door is ironwood carved with interlocking debt-chains. It opens only for invited guests. It remembers.

### The Gathering

The Recession is a performance of wealth conducted in the key of restraint. The drinks are expensive but not showy. The food is rare but not indulgent. Conversation is measured and loaded with subtext.

**The room:** A high-ceilinged salon on the second floor. Dark paneling. Brass sconces. A fire in a massive hearth that burns without wood, fueled by something that smells like old ink. Leather chairs. Crystal glasses. No music.

**The Instrument:** Displayed on a pedestal near the hearth behind a glass case. The bone-and-silver scroll case sits on dark velvet. The wax seal, a closed fist, catches the firelight. If a PC recognizes the symbol from Veezy's pendant (Spot DC 20), they notice the match. No explanation is offered for why their boss wears the seal of another man's artifact.

### The Host

**[[Aldenmere Voss]]** is a human man in his late sixties, thin, with silver hair pulled back tight. He wears dark formal clothes with no ornamentation except a single iron ring. He looks like a man who spent fifty years turning other people's desperation into quiet comfort.

He greets the PCs with genuine courtesy. He's pleased the Coinspiratorium sent representatives. He's heard about their work.

> *"Your employer's methods are... energetic. But results are results, and yours have been notable. Welcome."*

He offers them drinks. The drinks are perfect.

**DM Notes:** Voss is principled, competent, and human. He has enemies because he has power, not because he's cruel. His death should matter. The PCs should respect him before he dies.

### The Guests

Fourteen guests besides the PCs. Five of them will kill the host tonight without knowing the others plan to do the same.

**[[Meralda Thorne]]** — *The Betrayed Partner*
Half-elf woman in her fifties, dressed in dark green. Co-founded Voss's debt-holding firm forty years ago. He bought her out using a fraudulent clause. She's never forgiven him. She attends every year. She sits closest to the Instrument.
- **What she plans tonight:** She's been slowly extracting drops from a vial of arcanotoxin into Voss's personal wine decanter over the course of the cocktail hour. A cardiac poison, slow-acting, designed to look like heart failure. She's been planning this for three years.
- **What pushed her over the edge:** Three weeks ago, documents leaked proving the buyout clause was fraudulent. Forty years of suspicion confirmed. She decided tonight was the night. *(The leaked documents came from Veezy's network.)*

**[[Harath Kull]]** — *The Ruined Debtor*
Dwarf, once wealthy, now hollow-eyed. Voss called in his debts two years ago and took everything. He shouldn't be at this party. His invitation arrived unexpectedly.
- **What he plans tonight:** He's carrying a Debtor's Nail, a cursed iron nail etched with insolvency runes. If pressed into someone's flesh or slipped into their clothing, it slowly drains the target's vitality until the heart stops. He plans to palm it into Voss's coat pocket during a handshake or close conversation.
- **What pushed him over the edge:** Someone paid off his remaining minor debts last month, giving him enough standing to attend. And a package arrived with the Nail and a note: *"He took everything from you. Take something back."* Unsigned. *(Veezy's doing.)*

**[[Tesslyn Voss]]** — *The Impatient Heir*
Aldenmere's niece, his only living relative. Human woman in her thirties, sharp-featured. She has spent her adult life waiting for her uncle to retire or die.
- **What she plans tonight:** She's already done it. Two days ago, she accessed the estate and swapped her uncle's emergency healing vial (kept in his study desk, as it has been for decades) with an identical vial containing a concentrated alchemical toxin. If Voss feels unwell and reaches for his medicine, it kills him faster.
- **What pushed her over the edge:** She recently discovered Voss amended his will, reducing her inheritance significantly. The information came through a "concerned friend." *(Veezy's network.)*

**[[Creditor Zhaal]]** — *The Ancient Rival*
Githyanki debt-knight, a warrior-class creditor whose people treat obligations as holy writ. Has attended the Recession for twenty years, waiting for a legal opening to challenge Voss's custodianship of the Instrument.
- **What he plans tonight:** Before the main gathering, during a private conversation in the study, Zhaal administered a "ritual acknowledgment" to Voss: a small cut on the forearm with his silver sword, the blade coated in githyanki debt-venom. In githyanki debt-court, this is a legitimate legal procedure, a formal registration of contested obligation. It also slowly paralyzes the nervous system over the course of an hour. Zhaal considers this justice, not murder. The distinction matters to him. It would not matter to a judge.
- **What pushed him over the edge:** He recently received intelligence that the custodial renewal ceremony creates a structural weakness in Voss's bond to the Instrument. Zhaal decided this year's acknowledgment would carry teeth. *(The intelligence came from an anonymous source. Veezy.)*

**[[Pettifer Groan]]** — *The Blackmailed* **(SURFACE SOLUTION — most proximate killer)**
Gnome information broker who has worked with everyone in [[Offledger]]. Voss holds compromising material on Pettifer, material that could end his career and possibly his life.
- **What he plans tonight:** After Voss retreats to his study, Pettifer uses the servants' passage to enter and stabs Voss behind the left ear with a needle loaded with Threader's Bane, a gnome-crafted paralytic that stops the heart within sixty seconds. This is the most direct, most physical, most traceable murder attempt. Pettifer left poison residue on the door handle. His hands were shaking.
- **What pushed him over the edge:** He was told, through three layers of intermediary, that Voss planned to release the compromising documents tonight as "entertainment." This was a lie. Voss had no such plan. Pettifer believed it completely. *(Veezy's most elegant manipulation.)*

**[[Envoy Sareth]]** — *The O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. Plant*
Presents as a minor debt-holder from a distant plane. Polite, unremarkable. Actually an [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] agent under deep cover, tracking Veezy's financial movements. She expects a financial maneuver tonight, not a murder. When it happens, she pivots.
- **Role:** She is not guilty. She did not attempt to kill Voss. She is a complication during the claim process and a potential source of deeper insight if the PCs earn her trust.
- **Reveal timing:** She approaches the PCs mid-investigation, after the lockdown. She drops cover when the situation moves faster than she can control alone.

### The Evening (Before the Murder)

The first hour establishes:

1. **Voss is respected.** Guests approach him with deference. He speaks to each one personally. He remembers details. He is good at this.

2. **The guests are tense.** Sense Motive DC 15: too many fixed smiles, too many glances at the Instrument, too many conversations that stop when someone approaches.

3. **The guest list is odd.** Knowledge (Local) or Gather Information DC 18 during the cocktail hour: several guests wouldn't normally be here. Harath Kull is too poor. Creditor Zhaal hasn't attended in five years. Pettifer Groan usually sends a proxy. The guest list has been *curated*. (First clue, though the PCs may not know what to make of it yet.)

4. **Zhaal and Voss have a private conversation.** Midway through the cocktail hour, Zhaal requests a brief private word with Voss in the study. They're gone for ten minutes. When they return, Voss is rubbing his left forearm absently. Zhaal looks satisfied. (Spot DC 20 to notice Voss's discomfort.)

5. **Voss makes a speech.** A toast to the gathering, an acknowledgment of shared history, a reference to the Instrument and its significance as tonight marks the anniversary of his custodianship. He raises a glass. Everyone drinks.

   **Spot DC 20 or Sense Motive DC 18:** Voss hesitates before the toast. His eyes move across the room, assessing. He sees something that troubles him. Too many enemies. Too many fresh wounds. But the Recession is tradition, and Aldenmere Voss does not break with tradition.

### DM Notes
- **The cocktail hour is the investigation's foundation.** Every observation the PCs make here is evidence they'll need later. Let them mingle. Let them watch. Don't rush.
- **Several murder attempts happen during this scene without the PCs realizing it.** Meralda poisons the wine decanter. Harath palms the Nail into Voss's coat. Zhaal administers the venom-cut in private. Tesslyn's swap happened before the party even started. Only Pettifer acts after Voss retreats to the study. If PCs are watching carefully, they might catch fragments: Meralda lingering too long near the drinks, Harath's odd handshake, Voss rubbing his arm. These become critical evidence in retrospect.
- **Envoy Sareth blends in perfectly.** Pleasant, forgettable. If the PCs have met O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. agents before, a very high Sense Motive (DC 25) catches familiar phrasing.

## Scene 3: The Murder

### The Discovery
Between the speech and the second service, Voss excuses himself. A moment's privacy, a visit to his study. Five minutes. Ten. A servant knocks. No answer. The door opens.

[[Aldenmere Voss]] is dead. Slumped in the chair behind his study desk. He sat down, began reviewing a document (his amended will, half-read), and died.

### What the Body Reveals

The body doesn't give the PCs answers. It gives them questions.

**Heal DC 15:** The obvious wound. A thin needle puncture behind the left ear, skin discolored by a fast-acting poison. This is what killed him. Or at least, this is the most visible thing that killed him. At this DC, the PCs have a murder and a cause of death. Investigation can proceed from here as a standard whodunit.

**Heal DC 20:** Something's wrong. The needle poison should have been sufficient, but there are signs of additional systemic distress. A second toxin in his bloodstream, ingested rather than injected, unrelated to the needle. A shallow cut on his left forearm, partially healed, coated in an unfamiliar translucent residue. His vitality seems drained beyond what the poisons explain. The body is telling the PCs that whoever stuck the needle in Voss wasn't the only one trying to kill him tonight.

**Heal DC 25:** The full picture, or at least the full scope. Five distinct lethal agents were active in Voss's system simultaneously: a fast paralytic (the needle), a slow cardiac toxin (ingested), a paralytic venom (the arm wound), necromantic energy drain (source unclear from the body alone), and his emergency healing vial in the desk drawer is not a healing potion at all (Craft [Alchemy] DC 18 to confirm). Any single one of these would have killed him. Which one actually stopped his heart is a question without a clean answer.

**The key insight:** The body doesn't identify the killers. It identifies *how many there were.* The PCs must search the manor, interview the guests, and examine the evidence to trace each cause back to a person. The body is the starting gun for the investigation, not the finish line.

### The Lockdown

The room erupts. Guests cluster, accuse, demand answers. Several move toward the front door. One reaches for the handle.

**[[Envoy Sareth]]** steps forward.

She's been unremarkable all evening. Polite, forgettable, asking nobody anything dangerous. Now she moves to the center of the room with a posture the PCs might recognize if they've encountered [[O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E.]] before. She produces credentials. Her voice cuts through the noise.

> *"My name is Envoy Sareth. I'm an agent of O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E., the Office of Planar Enforcement, Regulation, and Tactical Interdiction. A man has been murdered. Nobody leaves this building until I understand what happened."*

She doesn't have legal jurisdiction in [[Offledger]]. Everyone in the room probably knows that. But she's armed, she's trained, she's calm, and she's the only person present who isn't panicking, grieving, or calculating. In a room full of scared rich people with a dead host, nobody challenges the woman with the badge. Not yet.

She turns to the PCs.

> *"You're the Coinspiratorium's representatives. You have no obvious connection to Voss and no stake in his estate. I need capable people I can trust to help me work this scene. Are you willing?"*

This is how the PCs become investigators. Not because the plot demands it, but because the most authoritative person in the room asked them.

**Meanwhile, the Instrument awakens.** The bone-and-silver scroll case begins to vibrate. The wax seal cracks open. A pulse of contractual energy ripples through the room, and every creature in the estate feels it: a subtle pressure, like the moment before a judge speaks. Within 30 feet of the Instrument, no one can lie about money.

Sareth notices the Instrument's activation. She doesn't understand its full significance yet, but she's watching it carefully.

**The clock starts.** Because Voss died during the renewal anniversary, the contestation period is three hours, not thirty days. Claims must be asserted and validated before the window closes or the Instrument defaults to [[Offledger]]'s general treasury. Most of the guests don't know this. Zhaal does. Tesslyn might. The PCs probably don't, unless they've been reading up on Offledger debt law.

### Immediate Reactions

**Creditor Zhaal** moves to the Instrument and asserts a claim in the githyanki legal form, loudly and formally. The Instrument doesn't vibrate for him. His claim is valid but not the oldest. He's furious.

**Tesslyn Voss** demands to see her uncle's body. She doesn't cry. She asks Sareth what happens to the estate, the Instrument, the will.

**Meralda Thorne** goes pale. Sets down her drink with trembling hands.

**Harath Kull** backs into a corner. Says nothing. His hands shake.

**Pettifer Groan** begins talking rapidly to anyone who'll listen, establishing alibis, offering theories, directing attention away from himself.

### DM Notes
- **The body sets the scope, but the manor holds the answers.** A DC 15 Heal check gives the PCs a murder to solve. A DC 20 tells them the murder is bigger than one killer. A DC 25 gives them the full scope. But at every tier, the *investigation* is what connects causes to people. Don't let the body do that work.
- **The murder should feel real.** After nine quests of comedic heists and cosmic absurdity, this is a man dead in a chair. Let the weight land.
- **The lockdown creates the adventure's structure.** Closed room, ticking clock, contested prize. The PCs become the de facto investigators because they're the most capable and least obviously motivated people present.
- **PCs who rolled low on Heal can still reach Tier 2.** The body is the most direct route, but investigative work in the manor can surface the other murder attempts independently. A PC searching the wine decanter doesn't need a Heal check to find arcanotoxin. A PC searching Voss's coat pockets doesn't need medical knowledge to find a warm, glowing nail. The Heal check tells them how many things to look for. Thorough investigation finds them anyway.

## Scene 4: The Investigation

### Tier 1: The Surface (Standard Murder Mystery)

The most accessible investigation catches **[[Pettifer Groan]]** as the killer. This is a genuine, satisfying whodunit on its own.

**Physical evidence pointing to Pettifer:**
- **The needle wound** (Heal DC 15) is the most visible injury
- **Poison residue on the study's servants' passage door handle** (Search DC 18). The killer's hands were shaking
- **Threader's Bane is gnome-crafted** (Knowledge [Nature] or Craft [Alchemy] DC 20). Rare, expensive, requires specialized knowledge to procure. Only one gnome is at this party
- **The servants' passage** (Gather Information DC 15 from household staff) connects to the study through a concealed door. Several guests knew about it
- **Pettifer's alibi has holes** (Sense Motive DC 20). He claims he was in the library reviewing the wine selection. He describes a wine that wasn't in the library and mentions a book shelved in a different room. He's been rehearsing, but his nerve is fraying
- **The adjudication field catches him.** Within 30 feet of the Instrument, if Pettifer claims *"I had no reason to want Voss dead,"* his voice fades. He had a reason. It was fabricated by Veezy's network, but he doesn't know that.

**Witness testimony supporting the Pettifer theory (reported to Sareth):**
- Servants saw Voss enter his study. No one followed through the main door. But the servants' passage was unwatched during the critical window.
- Meralda was near the study but didn't enter. The adjudication field confirms her denial. Her voice holds.
- Zhaal was in the main room (confirmed by multiple witnesses after his earlier private meeting).
- Harath left the main room briefly. No alibi for the critical window. But he breaks down under pressure and admits he *thought* about killing Voss but couldn't do it. He's been carrying a knife for two years. It's clean.
- Tesslyn was in conversation with another guest. Alibi confirmed.
- Sareth herself was in the salon the entire time. She offers her own testimony freely and suggests lines of inquiry for the PCs to pursue.

**Why tonight? (Tier 3 seed, available even at Tier 1):** If the PCs ask Pettifer why he acted tonight, he's desperate enough to explain. He was told, through three layers of intermediary, that Voss planned to release compromising documents about him at the gathering as "entertainment." He believed it completely. *"I had no choice. If those documents went public, I'm finished. Worse than finished."* The threat was a lie. Voss had no such plan. But Pettifer can name the intermediary who contacted him most recently (Gather Information DC 18 to get a useful name). Tracing that chain is a Tier 3 activity.

**Tier 1 resolution:** The PCs identify Pettifer, confront him, and he either confesses or is exposed by the adjudication field. A complete, satisfying solution. They report to Sareth, she takes Pettifer into custody (or whatever passes for custody in a locked manor), and the murder is solved. The PCs can stop here and it works.

### Tier 2: The Depth (Everyone Is Guilty)

This tier opens when the PCs realize the needle wasn't the only thing killing Voss. Maybe a good Heal check told them. Maybe they found something strange while searching the study. Maybe they noticed the wine glass and got curious. However they arrive here, the question is the same: **how does one man die five different ways at the same party?**

Each of the four remaining murder attempts is uncovered through a different combination of searching the manor, interviewing staff and guests, and confronting the suspect. The PCs have to do the legwork. The body pointed them in a direction. The house holds the evidence.

---

#### Meralda Thorne — The Poisoned Wine

**The physical trail:**
- Voss's wine glass is on his desk, half-empty. A PC who tests it (Craft [Alchemy] DC 18) finds arcanotoxin, a slow cardiac poison. Expensive, alchemically sophisticated, completely unrelated to the needle's Threader's Bane.
- Following the wine back to its source: the salon has a sideboard with a crystal decanter reserved for Voss's personal vintage. Search DC 15 locates it. Craft [Alchemy] DC 18 confirms traces of arcanotoxin on the decanter's rim and stopper. Someone dosed the decanter itself, not just the glass.

**The interview trail:**
- A servant (Gather Information DC 15) noticed Meralda lingering near the sideboard during the cocktail hour. She was alone with the decanter for about a minute while the servant was fetching glassware.
- If the PCs observed Meralda near the drinks during Scene 2 (Spot DC 18 during the cocktail hour), they can recall this without the servant's testimony.
- Another guest mentions, unprompted during general conversation, that Meralda seemed "calmer than usual tonight. Almost relieved." (Gather Information DC 12. This is easy to find because it's not hidden. Nobody thought it was suspicious at the time.)

**The confrontation:**
Meralda holds her composure better than most. She's had three years to make peace with what she planned to do. But within the adjudication field, she can't lie about the financial motive. If asked directly whether she wanted Voss dead over the buyout, her voice holds steady. She did want him dead. She's not ashamed. If asked whether she poisoned the wine, she pauses. Outside the field's radius, she'd deny it. Inside, she can't misrepresent an action taken in service of a financial grievance. Her silence is the confession.

> *"He stole forty years of my life using a clause I didn't read. I've been carrying that vial for three years, waiting for the right evening. I thought I was the only one with the nerve to do it."*

She's shaken to learn she wasn't.

**Why tonight? (Tier 3 seed):** If pressed on what changed after three years of waiting, Meralda admits that three weeks ago she received a packet of documents proving the buyout clause was fraudulently inserted. Forty years of suspicion, confirmed in black and white. The documents arrived anonymously. She has them in her handbag (Search DC 12; she brought them tonight in case she needed to justify herself afterward). The documents are genuine, extracted from a private legal archive. Knowledge (Local) DC 22 or Forgery DC 20 notes that accessing this archive would require either Voss's own authorization or significant financial leverage over the archival firm.

#### Creditor Zhaal — The Venom Cut

**The physical trail:**
- The cut on Voss's left forearm is visible once a PC examines the body closely (Search DC 15 once they know to look, or included in the Heal DC 20 findings). The residue on the wound can be identified with Knowledge (Arcana) or Knowledge (The Planes) DC 20 as githyanki debt-venom, a paralytic compound applied to blades during formal debt-court proceedings.
- Examining Zhaal's silver sword (Search DC 15 with his consent or DC 20 without) finds matching residue along the edge. The sword hasn't been cleaned since the cut was administered.
- A guest who visited the washroom (Gather Information DC 18) saw Zhaal rinsing his hands there shortly after his private meeting with Voss. He wasn't cleaning the sword. He was washing Voss's blood off his fingers.

**The interview trail:**
- The PCs may have observed Zhaal's private meeting with Voss during the cocktail hour (Scene 2). Voss was rubbing his forearm afterward. Zhaal looked satisfied.
- A servant posted outside the study during the meeting (Gather Information DC 15) heard raised voices. Voss protested something. There was a sharp, brief sound, like metal on skin. Then quiet. When they emerged, Voss looked uncomfortable but composed. Zhaal looked like a man who had completed a task.
- If the PCs didn't observe the meeting directly, other guests can confirm it happened (Gather Information DC 12). "Zhaal asked for a private word. They were in the study for about ten minutes."

**The confrontation:**
Zhaal does not deny it. He has no interest in denial. He considers the cut a legitimate legal procedure.

> *"I administered a lawful acknowledgment of contested obligation. The debt-venom registers my claim physically, as is the tradition of the githyanki courts. That it carries physiological consequences is a feature of our jurisprudence."*

If a PC points out that "physiological consequences" means "slowly killing him":

> *"The acknowledgment is proportional to the obligation. Voss's debt to the githyanki courts is considerable. The venom reflects that."*

He's wrong, legally, by any standard except his own. But he believes it with absolute conviction. He does not consider himself a murderer, and no argument will change that tonight.

**Why tonight? (Tier 3 seed):** Zhaal has attended the Recession for twenty years without acting. If asked what changed, he produces a folded dossier (he's not hiding it; he considers it evidence supporting his legal position). The dossier details a structural weakness in Voss's custodial bond that only exists during the annual renewal ceremony. "The window was always there. I simply did not know until this intelligence reached me." The dossier arrived four weeks ago through a githyanki information network Zhaal trusts. It's professionally prepared, well-sourced, and accurate. But Knowledge (The Planes) DC 22 or Gather Information DC 25 reveals that the original intelligence was injected into the githyanki network from an outside source. Someone who understood githyanki information channels fed Zhaal exactly what he needed to act.

#### Harath Kull — The Debtor's Nail

**The physical trail:**
- Searching Voss's coat (Search DC 18, or automatic if the PCs are being thorough with the body) turns up a small iron nail in the interior pocket. Warm to the touch. Etched with faintly glowing runes. Detect Magic reveals a necromantic aura. It's still active even after Voss's death.
- Knowledge (Arcana) DC 18 identifies it as a Debtor's Nail, a folk-curse object from dwarven debtor traditions. When placed on or near a person's body, it slowly drains vitality until the heart stops. Typically activated by proximity over several hours. The nail would have killed Voss by morning even if nothing else had.
- Searching Harath's person or belongings (Search DC 15) finds traces of the same necromantic residue on his right hand and in his coat pocket, where he carried the nail before planting it.

**The interview trail:**
- A guest who was near the greeting line (Gather Information DC 18) noticed Harath's handshake with Voss was oddly long. Harath gripped Voss's hand with both of his, which seemed overly familiar for someone of his diminished social standing. "I assumed he was being obsequious. Trying too hard."
- If the PCs observed the greeting during Scene 2 (Spot DC 20), they saw Harath's hand move briefly toward Voss's coat during the handshake.
- A servant (Gather Information DC 15) mentions that Harath has been asking quiet questions about the estate all evening. Room layouts, where Voss keeps personal items, which doors are locked. "I thought he was just impressed by the house."

**The confrontation:**
Harath has no composure to speak of. He falls apart almost immediately under direct questioning. He can't look the PCs in the eye. He can't maintain a story. Within the adjudication field, any attempt to deny his financial motive against Voss causes his voice to fade. Outside the field, he just stammers.

> *"I didn't know anyone else was going to... I thought it was just me. He took my foundry. My contracts. My name. I've been carrying that nail for two months, turning it over in my pocket every night, trying to decide."*

He's horrified to learn four other people tried to kill Voss on the same night. The horror is genuine. He thought his act was unique, desperate, singular. Learning it was one of five breaks something in him.

If the PCs show him kindness, he cooperates fully. He tells them everything: where he got the nail (it arrived in a package with an unsigned note reading *"He took everything from you. Take something back."*), how he planned it, what he felt. He becomes a witness, not an obstacle.

**Why tonight? (Tier 3 seed):** Harath still has the note (Search DC 10 on his person; he kept it in his pocket like a talisman). The note is printed on high-quality paper. Knowledge (Local) DC 18 identifies the paper stock as coming from a specific Offledger supplier who serves commercial clients in the financial district. And someone paid off Harath's remaining minor debts last month, restoring enough social standing for him to attend the Recession without being turned away at the door. Harath doesn't know who. The payments were routed through a shell entity called "Auric Holdings." He mentions the name without knowing what it means.

#### Tesslyn Voss — The Swapped Vial

**The physical trail:**
- The emergency healing vial is in the top drawer of Voss's study desk (Search DC 12; it's not hidden, just in a drawer). Voss kept it there for decades. Household staff can confirm this if asked.
- The vial looks right. Correct color, correct consistency, correct stopper. But Craft [Alchemy] DC 20 reveals it's a concentrated belladonna extract designed to mimic the appearance and smell of a standard healing potion. If Voss had felt ill from any of the other four attempts and reached for his medicine, this would have finished him.
- Searching Tesslyn's guest room at the estate (she maintains a room here as family) requires either her permission or a justified reason under the lockdown's authority. Search DC 18 turns up alchemical supplies, an empty vial identical to the one in the desk, and a handwritten note with dosage calculations. The note is in Tesslyn's handwriting.

**The interview trail:**
- A maid (Gather Information DC 15) mentions that Tesslyn visited the estate two days ago. "She said she was leaving a birthday card for her uncle." The maid thought nothing of it. Tesslyn has a key and visits occasionally.
- There is no birthday card anywhere in the study (Search DC 10). Or anywhere else in the estate, for that matter.
- The estate steward (Gather Information DC 18) can confirm that Tesslyn has visited unannounced several times in recent weeks. More frequently than usual. "She said she was helping with preparations for the Recession."
- If pressed, the steward also reveals that Tesslyn asked detailed questions about her uncle's daily habits. Which rooms he uses. When he takes his medicine. Where he keeps his personal effects. "I assumed she was concerned about his health. He's not young."

**The confrontation:**
Tesslyn is the hardest suspect to crack, because she is the most composed and the most prepared. She anticipated the possibility of discovery and has already constructed her defense. She doesn't panic. She calculates.

> *"I swapped a vial. He didn't drink it. Whatever killed my uncle, it wasn't belladonna, and you know it."*

This is technically true. The swapped vial didn't kill Voss, because he never reached for it. Tesslyn's murder attempt is the only one of the five that didn't land. She'll argue she's guilty of intent, not action.

Within the adjudication field, she can't deny her financial motive (the inheritance, the amended will). But she's careful with her words. She doesn't claim she didn't swap the vial. She claims the vial didn't kill him. The field lets that stand because it's true.

If the PCs have found all five killers by this point, Tesslyn's reaction to learning about the others is the most telling. She doesn't crumble like Harath. She doesn't justify like Zhaal. She goes very quiet, and then:

> *"Five of us. Five. And none of us knew about the others."*

She's the first person at the party smart enough to hear that sentence and understand what it implies. If the PCs are paying attention to her face in this moment, they see her reach the same conclusion they're reaching: this was arranged. Someone wanted all of them in the same room on the same night with the same target.

She doesn't say Veezy's name. She doesn't know it's him. But she knows it's someone.

**Why tonight? (Tier 3 seed):** If asked what prompted the vial swap, Tesslyn is matter-of-fact. She learned two months ago that her uncle amended his will, cutting her inheritance by more than half. The information came through a letter from "a concerned friend of the family" who thought she deserved to know. She has the letter in her guest room (Search DC 15). The letter is handwritten in a careful, neutral hand, unsigned, on high-quality stationery. The information it contains is accurate, which means it came from someone with access to Voss's private legal filings. Forgery DC 18 determines the handwriting is deliberately anonymized, written slowly to disguise the writer's natural style.

---

**Tier 2 resolution:** The PCs understand that five people independently tried to murder Aldenmere Voss on the same night. None coordinated. Each thought they were acting alone. The cause of death is a pile-up of independent lethal actions, any one of which would have been sufficient.

This should feel like the floor dropping out from under a mystery the PCs thought they'd already solved. The whodunit answer isn't "Pettifer." The answer is "everyone." And the real question becomes: why did all of them decide to act *tonight*?

### Tier 3: The Architecture (Veezy Built the Machine)

This tier opens when the PCs connect the antecedents. They've been collecting "why now?" details from each suspect throughout Tier 2: Meralda's leaked documents, Harath's mysterious benefactor, Zhaal's anonymous dossier, Tesslyn's unsigned letter, Pettifer's fabricated threat. Each push arrived independently, through different channels, to different people. But the PCs have been reporting their findings to Sareth. And if they lay the evidence out together, the pattern becomes visible.

**The pattern:** All five pushes arrived within a three-week window before the Recession. All contained accurate information (except Pettifer's, which was fabricated). All came from anonymous or untraceable sources. All targeted a specific person with a specific grievance against Voss. All provided not just motivation but the means or opportunity to act.

The question isn't "who killed Aldenmere Voss?" anymore. It's **"who loaded the gun five times and pointed it at the same man?"**

#### Connecting the Antecedents

**Clue: The timing** (no check required, just attention)
If the PCs have collected three or more antecedent details from Tier 2, they can simply lay the evidence on a table and look at the dates. Meralda's documents arrived three weeks ago. Harath's debts were paid off last month. Tesslyn's letter arrived two months ago. Zhaal's dossier arrived four weeks ago. Pettifer's warning came through in the past two weeks. The window is tight. This is coordination, not coincidence.

**Clue: Auric Holdings** (two paths to discovery)
- Path 1: Harath mentioned that his debts were paid by an entity called "Auric Holdings." Search DC 22 in Voss's locked desk drawer (Open Lock DC 25) turns up correspondence with the same name. Auric Holdings was buying Voss's outstanding obligations, consolidating financial pressure against him. The same entity that enabled Harath to attend the party was simultaneously squeezing the host.
- Path 2: Voss's private ledger (Search DC 15 in the study desk) shows unusual financial pressure in recent months. Debts called in from multiple sources. Reserve funds declining. Contracts terminated. The pattern is systematic. Cross-referencing the creditor names with the locked drawer correspondence identifies Auric Holdings as the consolidator.
- Either way: Knowledge (Local) DC 18 connects Auric Holdings to [[The Gold Standard]]. The PCs have worked for the Gold Standard for ten quests. They know what that name means.

**Clue: The information sources converge** (Gather Information DC 22, or DC 18 if the PCs have already found Auric Holdings)
Meralda's leaked documents came from a private archive accessible only to major financial players. Tesslyn's letter contained information from Voss's private legal filings. Zhaal's dossier was injected into a secure githyanki network by an outside source. Pettifer's threat was fabricated entirely. Harath was bankrolled by Auric Holdings. Five different channels, five different methods, one common requirement: deep financial resources and access to private information networks across multiple planes. The list of entities in [[Offledger]] with that reach is very short.

**Clue: Snitch Ray's letter** (Search DC 20 in Voss's desk)
A letter received last week: *"The walls are closing in. Your friends have been turned. Your enemies have been armed. Leave Offledger tonight."* Written on Coinspiratorium stationery. The PCs recognize the paper. [[Snitch Ray]] tried to warn Voss. He failed. He stayed home tonight because he couldn't bear to watch.

This is the most direct link to the Coinspiratorium and the most emotionally loaded piece of evidence in the adventure. If the PCs have been building a relationship with Snitch Ray, this lands hard.

**Clue: EyeQ's transmission** (if the PCs can reach [[EyeQ]] through magical communication despite the lockdown)
A single message: *"The probability of this specific confluence of events occurring naturally is zero."*

#### Sareth's Role at Tier 3

If the PCs have been reporting honestly to Sareth throughout the investigation, she's been assembling the same picture from her side. She's been tracking Veezy's financial movements for months as part of her O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. assignment. She has pieces the PCs don't.

When the PCs bring her the connected antecedents, she fills in gaps:

> *"I've been following your employer's financial activity for six months. Shell purchases, information trades, debt consolidation through intermediaries. I thought he was building a market position. Now I understand what the position was for."*

She lays it out:

> *"Every person in this room was handed a reason to act and pointed at the same target. The guest list was curated. The timing was chosen. The leaks, the payments, the anonymous intelligence. It's a machine. Your employer built a machine, and five people's anger was the fuel."*

If the PCs have reached Tier 3, this is the moment they have to decide: do they share everything with Sareth? Doing so gives O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. significant evidence against Veezy. Withholding protects their employer but makes them complicit in the cover-up.

Sareth doesn't force the choice. She asks.

> *"I can use this. If you help me build the case, O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. has enough to move against him. If you don't..."*

She leaves the sentence unfinished. She knows the PCs are in debt to Veezy. She knows what that means in Offledger.

**Tier 3 resolution:** The PCs understand that Veezy engineered the murder without committing it. He curated the guest list, armed each guest with fresh motivation, destabilized the host, and sent his own employees to collect the proceeds. The murder was architecture. What the PCs do with that understanding is the adventure's final test.

### DM Notes
- **The three tiers should be discoverable organically, not presented as a menu.** Let the PCs' choices and dice determine how deep they go. Some groups will catch Pettifer and feel satisfied. Some will notice the body has too many wounds and keep digging. Some will trace the threads all the way back to their employer. All three outcomes are valid.
- **Each Tier 2 investigation thread uses different skills and locations.** Meralda's trail runs through the salon and servant testimony. Zhaal's runs through the washroom and his weapon. Harath's runs through the coat and the greeting line. Tesslyn's runs through the guest rooms and household staff. PCs who split up and cover more ground will uncover more. PCs who focus narrowly on the study will find Pettifer but may miss the rest.
- **The adjudication field is a powerful investigation tool.** Drag suspects within 30 feet of the Instrument and ask about their financial connections to Voss. Watch whose voice fades. The field doesn't detect guilt for murder. It detects lies about money. But since every motive here is financial, the distinction barely matters.
- **Tesslyn is the bridge between Tier 2 and Tier 3.** She's the smartest person at the party after the PCs, and she's the first NPC likely to voice the question: "Five of us, none coordinated, all on the same night. How?" If the PCs don't make the leap to Tier 3 on their own, Tesslyn's reaction can nudge them.
- **The Auric Holdings connection should land hard.** The PCs have worked for the Gold Standard for ten quests. Finding that name in a dead man's papers connects their employer to the conditions that produced his death.
- **Snitch Ray's letter is the most emotionally complex clue.** He tried to save Voss and couldn't. He stayed home because he couldn't watch. If the PCs have been building a relationship with Snitch Ray, this lands.
- **PCs don't need all five to reach Tier 2.** Finding any two additional killers beyond Pettifer should be enough to trigger the realization that the murder is bigger than one person. Three is confirmation. Four is thorough. All five is exceptional and should be rewarded accordingly.

## Scene 5: The Claim

### The Adjudication

While the investigation proceeds, the Instrument's contestation clock is running. Claims are asserted. Zhaal's was first. Tesslyn asserts a claim as heir. Other minor claimants among the guests may assert as well.

Then the Instrument does something no one expects.

The adjudication field expands. The scroll case pulses. The contract-veins on the vellum flare brighter and sweep the room like a searchlight, pulsing in response to the presence of valid obligation chains. The vellum's glow settles, brightens, and points toward the PCs.

Specifically, toward the charter folio in their bag.

Everyone stares.

An estate solicitor (or a guest with legal expertise, or the adjudication process itself through visible contractual resonance) examines the PCs' commercial documents. Buried in what looks like routine trade filings, layered into the charter paperwork with surgical precision, is a chain of obligations predating every other claim in the room. The chain is ancient, structurally sound, and leads back to the Coinspiratorium's principal: Vizzok the Opulent.

> The solicitor, pale: *"This... this predates the custodianship by decades. The chain is clean. Every link is legally valid. How long has your employer held this?"*

The PCs don't know. They didn't know it existed five minutes ago. They were told to bring "standard business stuff."

The Instrument vibrates in the charter folio's direction. The other claims go still. The adjudication is complete. The Coinspiratorium has the oldest, deepest, most structurally sound claim on the Instrument of Contested Debt.

The PCs are holding it.

### Complications

**Creditor Zhaal** does not accept. His silver sword clears its sheath. He challenges the claim through githyanki debt-combat, a tradition where contested claims can be settled through ritual combat. Under [[Offledger]] debt law, this is technically valid if both parties consent. If the PCs refuse, Zhaal's claim is dismissed and he becomes a permanent enemy. If they accept, one PC must fight Zhaal in single combat.

**Tesslyn Voss** challenges the chain of custody, arguing that "Auric Holdings" is a shell entity and the chain is therefore fraudulent. The PCs must counter this through Diplomacy or Knowledge (Local) DC 20, or allow the Instrument's own adjudication to settle it. The Instrument validates the chain. Auric Holdings is technically legitimate, even if it's a front.

**Sareth** has been running the investigation. Now the PCs' employer turns out to be the beneficiary of the murder she's investigating. She produces a planar warrant to seize the Instrument as evidence. The warrant is real, but [[Offledger]]'s sovereignty means it has no force here. She knows this. She's stalling, hoping to complicate the process until the clock runs out. If the PCs have been cooperating with her honestly, this moment is painful. She trusted them. Now she's wondering if she should have.

### The Extraction
Once the claim is validated, the PCs can physically take the Instrument. The scroll case closes with a soft click. The adjudication field fades.

But Sareth is still running the lockdown. She decides when people leave. If the PCs have solved the murder to her satisfaction (any tier), she'll release the guests. If she's also learned that the Coinspiratorium has the winning claim on the victim's most valuable artifact, she has questions. Good questions. Questions the PCs may not want to answer with the Instrument in their hands.

How the PCs navigate this depends on their relationship with Sareth. If they've been honest and cooperative, she may let them leave with a warning. If they've been evasive, she'll make the extraction difficult. If they've reached Tier 3 together and she understands Veezy's role, she'll try to keep the Instrument as evidence, and the PCs will have to decide whether to cooperate with OPERATIVE or walk out of a crime scene carrying the proceeds of an engineered murder.

> **HP:** 108 | **AC:** 24 (full plate + Dex + ring of protection)
> **Attack:** +1 Silver Sword +18/+13/+8 melee (2d6+7, 17-20/x2, severs astral cord on crit)

### DM Notes
- **The claim should blindside the PCs.** They didn't know. The charter folio was "standard business stuff." The realization that Veezy armed them with the weapon and they carried it without looking should register as its own kind of violation.
- **If the PCs have reached Tier 3 of the investigation, this moment confirms everything.** Veezy sent them to this specific party, on this specific night, with this specific document. Nothing about this evening was accidental.
- **Sareth has been the PCs' ally all evening. Now she's an obstacle.** She ran the investigation, she trusted the PCs, and now their employer's name is on the winning claim. The betrayal she feels (or doesn't, depending on how forthcoming the PCs were) shapes the extraction. This is a relationship scene disguised as a heist complication.
- **Sareth can't legally stop them.** She has no jurisdiction in Offledger. But she can make things socially and practically difficult, and she can take what she's learned back to O.P.E.R.A.T.I.V.E. How the PCs treat her here determines how aggressively the agency moves in future quests.

## Scene 6: The Debrief

### Return to the Coinspiratorium
The PCs return with the Instrument. The Coinspiratorium is quiet. The circuits on the walls pulse in a new rhythm, welcoming, like the building recognizes what they're carrying.

[[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]] is in the back room. The goblet is back. The grill is polished. The chains catch the light. He looks like himself again. The showman. The mogul.

> *"So. How was the party?"*

He already knows.

### The Conversation

He takes the Instrument. The scroll case fits naturally in his tentacle. The bone and silver catch the firelight from his goblet.

> *"Beautiful work. Clean. Professional."*

**If PCs report the murder:**

> *"Terrible. Just terrible. Voss was a good man."*

He means it.

> *"These things happen at gatherings. You put enough history in one room and something gives."*

**If PCs mention the claim in the charter folio:**

> *"Oh, that old thing? I forgot that was even in there. Lucky you brought the paperwork, huh?"*

He says it the way someone says "small world" when they run into the exact person they were looking for.

**If PCs confront him about the guest list or the destabilization (Tier 3 knowledge):**

> *"I may have suggested a few names. Networking. I wanted the right people in the room. As for the rest..."*

He shrugs. With tentacles, this is quite a production.

> *"Markets move. Conditions change. People make choices. I didn't choose for anyone."*

**If PCs press harder:**

His central eye dims. The grill stops catching the light. The mask slips, just for a moment.

> *"I did what needed to be done. For the vision. For everything we're building. I didn't choose who held the needle. Or the vial. Or the blade. I chose who was in the room. The rest was organic."*

Beat.

> *"You want me to feel bad? I do. Voss was better than most. But the Instrument had to transfer. And under the rules, MY rules, the rules I'm building into everything, debts get collected. Obligations get enforced."*

He looks at the PCs. All ten eyestalks.

> *"You carried the claim. You presented it. You walked out with the Instrument. You could have dropped the folio. You could have left. You didn't."*

He lets that sit.

**If PCs didn't reach Tier 3:**

Veezy gives nothing away. He's pleased with their work. He pays them. He moves on. The PCs leave with the uneasy feeling that the evening was too convenient, but no proof. The seeds are planted for later realization.

### Payment

He pays generously. Above the usual rate. The bonus is never explained.

> *"Eleven down. One to go."*

The Instrument joins the other artifacts in the Workshop. The hum changes pitch. The circuits brighten. The walls of the Coinspiratorium shift, fractionally, as if the building just took a breath.

### DM Notes
- **This debrief is the moral climax.** The moment the PCs sit across from their employer and understand (or begin to understand) what he did, and what it means that they helped.
- **Veezy never admits to planning the murder.** He admits to everything around it if confronted: the guest list, the financial moves, the "favorable conditions." But he never says "I killed him." Because he didn't. He built a world where five people did.
- **The charter folio detail is the cruelest part.** Veezy weaponized their professionalism. He told them to bring paperwork and they brought paperwork. He trusted that they wouldn't read it. He was right.
- **"Eleven down. One to go."** The Crown is almost complete. Whatever the final quest holds, the PCs now know what their employer is willing to do to finish.
- **If the PCs refuse to hand over the Instrument:** Valid choice. Veezy's reaction is controlled fury. He reminds them of the contract. The debt. The consequences. He's not threatening them; he's reading them their rights. If they persist, this becomes a campaign-altering decision.

## Crown Component Integration

When the Instrument is installed in the Workshop:

**Environmental effect:** Contracts in [[Offledger]] begin self-enforcing. Minor debts that were ignored suddenly activate. Spectral collectors appear at debtors' doors. Handshake agreements develop binding force. The PCs' own contract with Veezy feels heavier, not changed, just more *present*. Customers in the Coinspiratorium pay exact amounts. Tips disappear. Haggling becomes impossible. The economy becomes frictionless, efficient, and merciless.

**Crown behavioral note:** The hum acquires a percussive quality, like a gavel striking. The Workshop's vibrations follow the rhythm of contractual transactions across Offledger. Every sale, every payment, every debt honored or dishonored sends a pulse through the floor. The Crown is counting.

## Notes
- This adventure falls at position 11 in the quest sequence. The PCs have seen enough to suspect Veezy of terrible things but haven't confirmed the scope. Depending on their investigation tier, this quest either plants seeds of suspicion or confirms their worst fears about their employer.
- The murder mystery should work at every tier. Tier 1 is a satisfying whodunit. Tier 2 is a Knives Out gut-punch. Tier 3 is the campaign's thesis statement made literal: Veezy doesn't force outcomes, he builds systems where outcomes are inevitable.
- The charter folio trick is the quest's signature move. The PCs were the heist crew and they didn't know it. Their boss weaponized their trust, their professionalism, and their willingness to carry a briefcase without reading every page. Let that sting.
- The thematic connection to [[Veezy's IOU]] is deliberate: the IOU is a forgotten debt that becomes the most powerful object in the multiverse. The Instrument enforces debts across planes. Both operate on the same principle. Veezy doesn't wield swords. He wields receipts.
- Veezy's methodology mirrors the Crown Infinite itself: he builds systems and lets them run. The Crown doesn't rewrite reality through brute force. It rewrites the rules and lets reality comply. This quest is the PCs experiencing that methodology applied to a human life.
