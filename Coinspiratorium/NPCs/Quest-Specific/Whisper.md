# Whisper

> *"..."*
> *(Whisper said nothing. Whisper was already behind you.)*

---

## Overview
Whisper is the infiltrator of [[The Gilt Edge]], a changeling rogue whose real name, face, and gender are unknown to everyone — including, possibly, Whisper. They shift between identities the way most people shift between moods: constantly, unconsciously, and without apparent attachment to any of them. They are the only Gilt Edge member who seems to *genuinely enjoy* working for [[Vizzok the Opulent|Veezy]]. This is the most unsettling thing about them.

Where [[Kess Darrowmere|Kess]] is the scowl and [[Brother Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] is the smile, Whisper is the silence. They watch everything. They say almost nothing. They position for flanks the way water finds cracks.

---

## Appearance
- Different every time — that's the point
- The PCs will never see the same face twice unless Whisper wants them to
- Common tells across all forms: preternaturally still hands, eyes that track movement a beat too precisely, a habit of standing in peripheral vision rather than direct line of sight
- When relaxed (rare), defaults to a nondescript humanoid form — average height, average build, features that slide off memory like oil off glass
- Wears dark, layered clothing that accommodates rapid body shifts without tearing
- Carries no visible weapons. This does not mean unarmed

## Personality
- **Silent.** Not shy, not brooding — *efficient*. Whisper speaks when words accomplish something that silence doesn't. This happens rarely
- **Observant.** Whisper notices everything: the PC who favors their left knee, the trap that was disarmed wrong, the lock that was picked from the inside. They file it all and share none of it unless Kess asks
- **Unsettling.** There's something deeply wrong about someone who wears faces like clothing and seems comfortable in none of them. NPCs who interact with Whisper for extended periods report feeling "watched from the inside"
- **Loyal to the crew.** The only consistent thing about Whisper is their commitment to Gilt Edge. They have abandoned faces, names, and entire identities without hesitation. They have never abandoned Kess
- **Enjoys the work.** This is the problem. Kess resents the debt. Ashenveil regrets the debt. Torque doesn't understand the debt. Whisper *likes the job*. They like the infiltration, the competition, the game. Whether this makes them the healthiest or most broken member of the crew is an open question

## Role
Whisper is the Gilt Edge member the PCs feel but rarely see. They're the reason a dungeon room is already empty when the PCs arrive. They're the shadow at the edge of camp. They're the shopkeeper the PCs talked to yesterday who was, in hindsight, asking very specific questions.

**DM Guidance:**
- Whisper should be introduced through absence — the PCs encounter evidence of someone who was there and isn't anymore, locks picked with impossible precision, a face in the crowd that doesn't quite match
- In direct encounters, Whisper positions for flanks automatically and fights with terrifying economy. No wasted movement, no banter, no emotion
- Late game: Whisper has been spending time near Veezy's Workshop in [[Offledger]], watching the construction of [[The Device]]. They haven't told the rest of Gilt Edge what they've seen. They haven't told anyone. But they've started locking doors behind them — something they never used to do

## Stats

- **Class/Level:** Rogue 4 (Soulknife; scales with party)
- **Alignment:** True Neutral
- **Notable Abilities:**
  - Changeling shapeshifting — assume any humanoid appearance at will
  - Sneak Attack (2d6, scales)
  - Soulknife: Psionic Blades (psychic damage, no visible weapon), Psychic Whispers (telepathic communication with Gilt Edge)
  - Expertise in Stealth and Deception
  - Cunning Action (Dash, Disengage, Hide as bonus action)
  - Uncanny Dodge (at higher levels)

---

## Connections
- **Faction:** [[The Gilt Edge]], contracted to [[The Gold Standard]]
- **Allies:** [[Kess Darrowmere]] (follows without question), [[Brother Ashenveil]], [[Torque]]
- **Enemies:** Whoever Kess points them at
- **Relationship to Veezy:** Unclear. Whisper doesn't complain about the debt. Whisper doesn't complain about anything. Veezy calls Whisper his *"favorite employee"* because Whisper never argues, never negotiates, and never disappoints. Whether Whisper has their own agenda with Veezy is something nobody has thought to ask — because Whisper has never given anyone a reason to

## Notes
- The Soulknife subclass means Whisper's weapons are psychic constructs — blades of shimmering mental energy that appear and disappear with thought. This means Whisper is *never* unarmed and can never be disarmed. It also means detecting weapons on Whisper is impossible
- Whisper communicates with Gilt Edge telepathically during missions (Psychic Whispers feature). This makes them functionally impossible to ambush as a group
- The PCs should periodically wonder if an NPC they just spoke to was actually Whisper. The answer should usually be no. But not always
- What Whisper saw in the Workshop: the Crown Infinite's framework, half-assembled, humming with planar resonance. Whisper understood exactly enough to be afraid — and Whisper does not get afraid
- If the PCs try to recruit Whisper away from Gilt Edge, Whisper will refuse. Not because of the debt, not because of Veezy — because of Kess. Whisper will not leave Kess
